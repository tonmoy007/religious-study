# Hadith and the Qur'an — The Prophetic Voice on Revelation

> Part of [[Religion Study MOC]] → Sacred Texts & Their Connections

How do the **hadith** (prophetic traditions) relate to the **Qur'an** (recited scripture) in Islam? This note studies the relationship through the actual texts in the vault's [[Hadith Library]] and [[Quran Index|Qur'an]], showing the concrete ways hadiths quote, explain, contextualize, and derive law from the Qur'anic text.

For the overview of the hadith collections, see [[Hadith Library]]. For the broader scriptural context, see [[Sacred Texts Overview]] and [[Connections Between Religious Texts]].

---

## The Two Sources of Islam

Islamic theology recognizes **two primary sources** of divine guidance:

| Source | Nature | Status | Example in vault |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Qur'an** | The literal speech of God (*kalam Allah*), revealed through Angel Jibril | Primary, inimitable (*i'jaz*), uncreated | [[Quran Index]] (114 surahs) |
| **Hadith** | The recorded sayings, actions, and approvals of Prophet Muhammad | Explanatory, secondary to Qur'an in authority | [[Hadith Library]] (9 collections) |

The foundational principle is stated in the Qur'an itself:

> [!quote] Qur'an 33:21 — The Prophet as model
> "Verily in the messenger of Allah ye have a **good example** for him who looketh unto Allah and the Last Day, and remembereth Allah much."
> — [[033 Al-Ahzaab|Al-Ahzaab 33:21]]

> [!quote] Qur'an 59:7 — Obey the Messenger
> "And whatsoever the messenger giveth you, take it. And whatsoever he forbiddeth, abstain (from it)."
> — [[059 Al-Hashr|Al-Hashr 59:7]]

These verses establish the **Sunnah** (the Prophet's way) as binding alongside the Qur'an. The hadiths are the recorded vehicle of that Sunnah. As the scholars say: *al-Sunnah tufassiru al-Qur'an* — **the Sunnah explains the Qur'an**.

## How Hadiths Engage with the Qur'an — Evidence from the Vault

The hadith collections in the vault contain thousands of direct engagements with Qur'anic verses. These fall into several distinct categories:

### 1. Asbab al-Nuzul — The Occasions of Revelation

Many hadiths record the **specific circumstances** under which a Qur'anic verse was revealed. Understanding *why* a verse came down is essential to interpreting it correctly.

> [!quote] Bukhari 2146 — The revelation of 2:158 (Safa and Marwa)
> *Narrated 'Urwa:* I asked 'Aisha about the meaning of 2:158. She said: "This divine inspiration was revealed concerning the Ansar who used to assume Ihram for worshipping an idol called 'Manat'… and whoever assumed Ihram (for the idol), would consider it not right to perform Tawaf between Safa and Marwa. When they embraced Islam, they asked Allah's Messenger about it, saying, 'O Allah's Apostle! We used to refrain from Tawaf between Safa and Marwa.' So Allah revealed: 'Verily As-Safa and Al-Marwa are among the symbols of Allah.'"
> — [[01 Sahih al-Bukhari]]

> [!quote] Bukhari 397 — Umar's agreement (2:125 on the Station of Abraham)
> *Narrated 'Umar bin Al-Khattab:* "My Lord agreed with me in three things: 1. I said, 'O Allah's Messenger, I wish we took the Station of Abraham as our praying place.' So came the Divine Inspiration: 'And take you the Station of Abraham as a place of prayer' (2:125). 2. And as regards the veiling of women… 3. Once the wives of the Prophet made a united front against him… So this verse (66:5) was revealed."
> — [[01 Sahih al-Bukhari]]

> [!quote] Tirmidhi 3046 — The change of Qibla (2:144)
> *Narrated Al-Bara bin 'Azib:* "When the Messenger of Allah arrived in Al-Madinah, he performed Salat facing Bait Al-Maqdis (Jerusalem) for sixteen or seventeen months. The Messenger of Allah longed to face the Ka'bah, so Allah revealed: 'Verily, We have seen the turning of your face towards the heaven. Surely, We shall turn your face in the direction of Al-Masjid Al-Haram' (2:144). So he faced the direction of the Ka'bah… A man performed Salat Al-'Asr along with him, then passed by some people of the Ansar performing Salat while bowing toward Bait Al-Maqdis. He told them he testified that he had prayed with the Prophet facing the Ka'bah. So they turned while they were still bowing."
> — [[04 Jami\` at-Tirmidhi]]

### 2. Tafsir — Explaining What Verses Mean

Hadiths frequently explain the **meaning** of Qur'anic words and phrases — sometimes clarifying ambiguous terms, sometimes correcting misunderstandings.

> [!quote] Bukhari 1848 — Explaining "white thread and black thread" (2:187)
> *Narrated 'Adi bin Hatim:* "When the above verses were revealed: 'Until the white thread appears to you, distinct from the black thread,' I took two (hair) strings, one black and the other white, and kept them under my pillow and went on looking at them throughout the night but could not make anything out of it. So the next morning I went to Allah's Messenger and told him the whole story. He explained to me: 'That verse means the darkness of the night and the whiteness of the dawn.'"
> — [[01 Sahih al-Bukhari]]

> [!quote] Tirmidhi 3040 — Explaining "enter the gate in prostration" (2:58)
> "Regarding Allah's saying: 'Enter the gate in prostration' — 'They entered dragging their behinds,' meaning they distorted it. And regarding: 'But those who did wrong changed the word' (2:59) — 'They said: Habbah (a seed) in Sha'irah (in barely).'"
> — [[04 Jami\` at-Tirmidhi]]

> [!quote] Tirmidhi 3045 — Explaining "Wasata" (2:143)
> "Thus we have made you a Wasata nation — the Prophet said: 'The meaning of Wasata is just.'"
> — [[04 Jami\` at-Tirmidhi]]

> [!quote] Tirmidhi 3033 — Warning against interpreting without knowledge
> *Ibn 'Abbas:* the Messenger of Allah said: "Whoever says (something) about the Qur'an without knowledge, then let him take his seat in the Fire."
> — [[04 Jami\` at-Tirmidhi]]

### 3. The Qur'an as a Source of Law (Ahkam)

Hadiths often cite specific Qur'anic verses as the basis for legal rulings — showing how the Prophet derived law from revelation.

> [!quote] Bukhari 1313 — Forgiveness for pagans (9:113)
> "Then Allah's Messenger said, 'I will keep on asking Allah's forgiveness for you unless I am forbidden (by Allah) to do so.' So Allah revealed the verse concerning it: 'It is not fitting for the Prophet and those who believe that they should invoke (Allah) for forgiveness for pagans even though they be of kin, after it has become clear to them that they are companions of the fire' (9:113)."
> — [[01 Sahih al-Bukhari]]

> [!quote] Bukhari 45 — The perfected religion (5:3)
> *Narrated 'Umar bin Al-Khattab:* Once a Jew said to me, "There is a verse in your Holy Book which is read by all of you, and had it been revealed to us, we would have taken that day as a day of celebration." 'Umar asked, "Which is that verse?" The Jew replied: "'This day I have perfected your religion for you, completed My favor upon you, and have chosen for you Islam as your religion' (5:3)." 'Umar replied: "No doubt, we know when and where this verse was revealed. It was Friday and the Prophet was standing at 'Arafat (on the Day of Hajj)."
> — [[01 Sahih al-Bukhari]]

### 4. The Qur'an in Worship

Hadiths record how the Prophet **recited and used** the Qur'an in prayer — establishing the pattern for Muslim worship.

> [!quote] Tirmidhi 3036 — Fatiha divided between God and servant
> *Abu Hurairah:* The Messenger of Allah said that Allah said: "I have divided the Salat (i.e., Al-Fatiha) between Myself and My slaves into two halves. Half of it is for Me, and half of it for My slave, and My slave shall have what he asks for."
> — [[04 Jami\` at-Tirmidhi]]

### 5. The Qur'an's Superiority and Virtues

Separate sections in the hadith collections (*Fada'il al-Qur'an* — Virtues of the Qur'an) discuss the Qur'an's excellence, the rewards of reciting it, and proper etiquette toward it.

> [!quote] Bukhari 875 — The Qur'an given to the last community
> *Abu Hurairah:* Allah's Messenger said: "We are the last (to come amongst the nations) but (will be) the foremost on the Day of Resurrection. They were given the Holy Scripture before us and we were given the Qur'an after them."
> — [[01 Sahih al-Bukhari]]

## The Tafsir Genre in the Hadith Collections

Several of the nine hadith collections contain **dedicated Tafsir (Qur'anic exegesis) sections**:

| Collection | Tafsir section | Approx. hadiths |
|---|---|---|
| [[01 Sahih al-Bukhari]] | Book of Tafsir (Kitab al-Tafsir) | ~500+ |
| [[02 Sahih Muslim]] | Book of Tafsir | ~400+ |
| [[04 Jami\` at-Tirmidhi]] | Chapters on Tafsir (lines 3185-3608) | ~425 |
| [[05 Sunan an-Nasa'i]] | Book of Tafsir | ~300+ |
| [[06 Sunan Ibn Majah]] | Chapters on Tafsir | ~200+ |

These sections are arranged **surah by surah, verse by verse** — each hadith explaining a specific passage. For example, in Tirmidhi's "Chapters on Tafsir," hadith 3043-3048 all cluster around Surah Al-Baqarah 2:125-144, the verses on the change of Qibla direction.

## Three Levels of Qur'an–Hadith Relationship

### Level 1: The Qur'an Commands, the Hadith Details

The Qur'an often gives general commands; the hadith fills in the specifics:

| Qur'anic command | Hadith specification |
|---|---|
| "Establish prayer" (2:43) | How to pray, when to pray, what to recite |
| "Give zakat" (2:43) | Minimum threshold (*nisab*), what counts as wealth |
| "Fast the month of Ramadan" (2:185) | When the month begins, who is exempt, what breaks the fast |
| "Perform Hajj" (2:196) | The rites, the timing, the prohibitions |

### Level 2: The Hadith Explains the Qur'an

As seen above, the hadith clarifies ambiguous words, identifies the occasions of revelation, and explains the intended meaning.

### Level 3: The Hadith Supplements the Qur'an

Some rulings exist in the hadith without a direct Qur'anic verse — for example, the prohibition of combining a woman with her aunt (paternal or maternal) in marriage, and the details of the call to prayer (*adhan*). These are considered revelations through the Prophet's inspired speech (*al-wahy al-khafi*).

## Comparative Perspective

The Qur'an–Hadith relationship offers an instructive comparison with how other traditions relate their scriptures:

| Tradition | Primary scripture | Secondary authority | Relationship |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Islam** | Qur'an (God's speech) | Hadith (Prophet's words) | Sunnah explains and supplements Qur'an |
| **Judaism** | Tanakh (Written Torah) | Talmud (Oral Torah) | Talmud interprets and applies Tanakh |
| **Christianity** | Bible (Old + New Testament) | Creeds, Church tradition | Creeds summarize, tradition interprets |
| **Hinduism** | Shruti (Vedas, Upanishads) | Smriti (Gita, Puranas, epics) | Smriti explains shruti for wider audience |

In each case, the **secondary** corpus functions as a bridge between the foundational text and living practice.

### Deeper Look: Hadith–Qur'an vs. Talmud–Tanakh

The hadith's relationship to the Qur'an is structurally analogous to the Talmud's relationship to the Tanakh — an elaborated interpretive tradition that makes the primary text actionable. The parallels run deep:

| Dimension | Qur'an → Hadith | Tanakh → Talmud |
|---|---|---|
| **Source of authority** | Qur'an is God's speech; Hadith is divinely inspired but not recited as revelation | Tanakh is *Ketuvim* (written); Talmud is *Torah she-be'al Peh* (Oral Torah), traced to Sinai |
| **Interpretive goal** | *Tafsir* — clarifying ambiguous verses (*mujmal/mubayyan*), specifying general commands | *Midrash* — deriving law (*halakha*) and narrative (*aggadah*) from biblical text |
| **Occasions** | *Asbab al-nuzul* — historical circumstances of revelation | *Peshat* / *derash* — contextual and interpretive readings of verses |
| **Legal derivation** | *Ahkam* — extracting rulings via qiyas, ijma, usul al-fiqh | *Talmudic sugyot* — debate-driven legal reasoning using hermenutic rules (e.g., Hillel's 7 middot) |
| **Supplementary rulings** | Some rulings exist only in Hadith (e.g., *adhan* details, combining aunts in marriage) | *Gezerot* and *takkanot* — rabbinic enactments not explicitly in Tanakh |
| **Textual format** | Multiple independent collections (Bukhari, Muslim, etc.) — graded by authenticity | Single multi-volume corpus (Babylonian + Jerusalem) — layered (Mishnah → Gemara) |
| **Critical challenges** | Historical reliability of chains (*isnad*), the "Hadith rejection" debate | Dating, authorship, and the historical reliability of attributions to specific rabbis |

The vault currently contains the **[[Mishnah Index|Mishnah]]** (the foundational layer of the Talmud), but not the full Talmud (marked "available on request" in the [[Scriptures Library]]). Once the Talmud is added, this comparison can be made with verbatim text evidence — a promising future project.

## Open Questions

- How do hadith that seem to conflict with the plain meaning of Qur'anic verses get reconciled? (The science of *mukhtalif al-hadith*)
- Is the Sunnah "revelation" (*wahy*) in the same sense as the Qur'an? The hadith itself records debates on this.
- The Tafsir sections of the hadith collections are a rich but underused resource in comparative religion — how do they compare with patristic biblical commentaries or Vedantic commentaries on the Upanishads?
- What happens to the authority of hadith when critical scholars challenge their historical reliability? Does that affect how the Qur'an itself is interpreted?
