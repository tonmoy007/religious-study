# Quranic Tafsir — *A Surah-by-Surah Companion*

> Brief interpretive commentary for each surah, drawing on the hadith Tafsir sections in the [[Hadith Library]], classical exegetes (Ṭabarī, Ibn Kathīr, al-Qurṭubī, al-Rāzī), and key exegetical traditions. Part of [[Religion Study MOC]].

---

## About This Note

This companion provides, for each surah:
1. **Key tafsir insights** — major interpretive points from classical scholarship
2. **Hadith references** — links to the Hadith Tafsir sections in the vault
3. **Notable exegetical differences** — where the classical *mufassirūn* disagreed

For the historical context (*asbāb al-nuzūl*) of verses, see [[Asbab al-Nuzul]].

## The Surahs

### 001. Al-Fātiḥah — The Opening

**7 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 5**

Surah al-Fātiḥah is unique: it is both the opening of the Qur'an and the essential recitation in every unit of prayer. The hadith of the "divided prayer" (Muslim 395) explains that God divided its verses between Himself and His servant — each line is a dialogue.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- *Bismillāh* — the basmalah: whether it is a verse of the surah (Shafi'i view) or a separator (Maliki/Hanafi view) is a long-standing debate
- *Al-Ḥamdu lillāh* — "All praise belongs to Allah": Ḥamdu covers both praise (*madḥ*) and gratitude (*shukr*). Ṭabarī: it is God praising Himself, teaching His servants to do the same
- *Al-Raḥmān al-Raḥīm* — the most repeated pair of divine attributes in the Qur'an: mercy in essence and mercy in action
- *Māliki Yawm al-Dīn* — "Master of the Day of Judgment": the recitation *Maliki* (Master) vs. *Māliki* (King) — both are canonical qira'at
- *Ihdina al-ṣirāṭ al-mustaqīm* — "Guide us to the straight path": the prayer for which the entire Qur'an is the answer

**Hadith in vault:** [Muslim 395](scriptures/islam-hadith/02%20Sahih%20Muslim.md#The%20Book%20of%20Prayers) — the divided prayer hadith · [Tirmidhi 3036](scriptures/islam-hadith/04%20Jami%60%20at-Tirmidhi.md#Chapters%20on%20Tafsir) — Fatiha divided between God and servant

### 002. Al-Baqarah — The Cow

**286 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 87**

The longest surah of the Qur'an — a comprehensive legislative constitution for the Muslim community. Named after the cow sacrificed by the Israelites in the story of an unsolved murder (vv. 67–73).

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **Āyat al-Kursī (2:255)** — "the greatest verse in the Book of Allah" (Muslim 810). Its 50 words contain 17 references to God's attributes. Ibn Kathīr: no verse combines tawhid, divine sovereignty, and intercession so powerfully
- **The Throne Verse's virtues**: protection from Satan until morning (Bukhari 2311)
- **2:256 — "No compulsion in religion"**: exegetes debate whether this was abrogated by the sword verses (9:5, 9:29) or remains general. Qurtubī: it applies to People of the Book, not to apostates. Abrogation view: majority hold it is *mufassar* (clarified) not *mansūkh* (abrogated)
- **2:282 — The Debt Verse**: the longest single verse in the Qur'an — a precise contract law for loans. Ibn Kathīr: all commercial transactions should be written
- **2:158 — Safa and Marwa**: the Ansar's hesitation about the pre-Islamic idols explained in hadith
- **2:115 — "Wherever you turn, there is the face of Allah"**: revealed about the qibla controversy — both the direction toward Jerusalem (earlier) and the flexibility for voluntary prayer on travel

**Hadith tafsir in vault:** [Bukhari 1003–1020, 4484–4649](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Prophetic%20Commentary%20on%20the%20Qur'an) — extensive · [Tirmidhi 3040–3058](scriptures/islam-hadith/04%20Jami%60%20at-Tirmidhi.md#Chapters%20on%20Tafsir)

### 003. Āl-i-'Imrān — The Family of Imran

**200 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 89**

Named after the family of Imran (father of Mary), this surah addresses People of the Book and clarifies Christian doctrines about Jesus.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **3:7 — Muḥkam and Mutashābih**: "He it is who sent down the Book — in it are verses muhkamat (clear/firm) and mutashabihat (ambiguous/similar)." This verse is the foundation of Qur'anic hermeneutics. Tabari: the muhkam is what is clear in meaning; the mutashabih is what requires interpretation (e.g., the letters, attributes). Zamakhshari: the distinction is relative — a verse may be muhkam to one reader and mutashabih to another
- **3:42–55 — Mary and Jesus**: the Qur'anic annunciation differs from Luke's Gospel: Mary is informed directly (not through Joseph). Jesus speaks from the cradle (v.46)
- **3:61 — Mubāhala**: "Come, let us call our sons and your sons…" — the challenge of prayer-imprecation to the Christians of Najran. Shia tafsir highlights that "our sons" refers to Hasan and Husayn
- **3:84** — the prophetic creed: "We make no distinction between any of them"
- **3:103** — "Hold firmly to the rope of Allah": interpreted as the Qur'an (rope from heaven to earth), the covenant, or the community

### 004. Al-Nisā' — The Women

**176 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 92**

The surah of women's rights — marriage, divorce, inheritance, and family law.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **4:3 — Polygyny limited**: "Marry what seems good of women — two, three, or four, but if you fear you cannot be just, then one." Tabari: *ʿadl* (justice) here means equal treatment in material provisions. Qurtubi: a ruling permitting but restraining polygyny — the default is monogamy if justice is doubtful
- **4:11–12 — Inheritance**: the most detailed inheritance legislation in the Qur'an. The fixed shares (*furūḍ*) transformed pre-Islamic custom where only adult males inherited
- **4:34 — Qiwāmah**: "Men are protectors/maintainers of women…" — the most debated verse in modern tafsir. Classical tafsir: *qiwāmah* based on (1) men's spending, (2) men's physical strength. The "discipline" clause (*wa-ḍribūhunna*) — most classical exegetes interpret as a light symbolic tap that must not leave a mark or break a bone. Many modern exegetes (particularly from the *iṣlāḥ* tradition) interpret it as separation or arbitration
- **4:59 — Obedience to authority**: "Obey Allah, obey the Messenger, and those in authority among you." *Ūlū al-amr* = rulers (Sunni view) or the Imams (Shia view)
- **4:176 — Kalālah**: the last verse revealed (some scholars say). The death of a person with no direct heir

### 005. Al-Mā'idah — The Table Spread

**120 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 112**

The "conclusion of the Law" surah — one of the last full surahs revealed, containing the perfection of religion (5:3).

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **5:3 — Perfection of religion** revealed at 'Arafat during the Farewell Pilgrimage. 'Umar wept: "I know that when something is perfected, it begins to decline." Tabari: the verse completed the rules of ḥalāl and ḥarām
- **5:6 — Ablution (*wuḍū'*)** — "When you rise for prayer, wash your faces…" The word *ilā al-marāfiq* (to the elbows) — does *ilā* mean "including" or "up to"? Shafi'i: including the elbows. Hanafi: up to (but not including) the elbows. Wiping over socks (*khuff*) — debated among madhahib
- **5:32 — Killing one soul** "it is as if he killed all of humanity" — derived from the story of Cain and Abel (the first murder). Ibn Kathir: the most comprehensive statement on the sanctity of life in scripture
- **5:90 — Prohibition of alcohol**: "Wine, gambling, idols, and arrows are an abomination of Satan's handiwork — avoid it." The final and absolute prohibition after gradual revelation (2:219 → 4:43 → 5:90–91)
- **5:116 — Jesus denies his divinity**: "Did you say to people: take me and my mother as gods besides Allah?" — the eschatological interrogation scene unique to the Qur'an

### 006. Al-An'ām — The Cattle

**165 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 55**

A sustained monotheistic argument against polytheism, directed at the Quraysh.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **6:74–83 — Abraham's argument against idolatry**: the moon-sun-star analogy — a logical proof for tawhid. Tabari: the "argument We gave Abraham against his people" (v.83) is the *burhān* (proof) of reason itself
- **6:95–99 — Signs in nature**: the splitting of the seed, the dawn, the night for rest, the stars for navigation. Razi: the natural world is the first revelation
- **6:103 — "Vision comprehends Him not, but He comprehends all vision"** — the basis for the *ru'yat Allāh* (seeing God) debate. Ash'aris: believers will see God in Paradise. Mu'tazilis: God cannot be seen, ever
- **6:151–153 — The ten commandments of the Qur'an**: a list of prohibitions parallel to the Decalogue — don't associate partners, honour parents, don't kill children (infanticide), don't approach lewdness, don't take life unjustly, don't touch orphan's property, give full measure

---

### 007. Al-A'rāf — The Heights

**206 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 39**

Named after the wall/height between Paradise and Hell (v.46). The surah of prophetic narratives — Adam, Noah, Hud, Salih, Lot, Shu'ayb, Moses — the longest sequence of prophet stories.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **7:143 — Moses' request to see God**: "Show Yourself to me, that I may look upon You." When God revealed Himself to the mountain, it crumbled. Tabari: this proves God *can* be seen (by His power) but that created sight cannot endure it in this world
- **7:172 — The Covenant of Alast**: "Am I not your Lord? They said: Yes, we testify." — the primordial covenant taken from all of Adam's descendants. The *mīthāq* — theological implications: (1) all humans are born with innate knowledge of God (fiṭrah), (2) no one can say "we were unaware" on Judgment Day. Qurtubi: this is the basis of fiṭrah — every child is born upon this covenant
- **7:46–50 — The People of al-A'rāf**: those whose good and bad deeds are equal — the "men on the heights" who recognize both the people of Paradise and Hell. Are they the prophets (Tabari), the angels (some tradition), or those whose scales are balanced (Ibn Kathir)? Majority: those whose good and evil deeds are balanced, awaiting mercy

---

### 008. Al-Anfāl — The Spoils of War

**75 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 88**

Revealed after the Battle of Badr (2 AH) — the first war surah, dealing with spoils, divine intervention, and the ethics of combat.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **8:1 — The spoils belong to Allah and His messenger**: the companions disputed over the distribution of Badr's spoils. The Prophet distributed them equally. Tabari: this established that war spoils are a public trust, not private plunder
- **8:9–17 — Angelic assistance**: "Your Lord inspired the angels: I am with you, so strengthen the believers." At Badr, the angels fought alongside. Ibn Kathir: the 3,000 to 5,000 angels (3:124–125) were a morale booster as much as military reinforcement
- **8:50 — Angels striking the faces and backs of the disbelievers at death**: the graphic description of death for the wicked. Qurtubi: a warning that divine punishment can strike in this world as well as the next

---

### 009. At-Tawbah — The Repentance

**129 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 113**

The only surah without the basmalah — it begins with an ultimatum to the polytheists. The last major surah revealed, covering foreign relations, warfare, and the final break with Arabian polytheism.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **9:5 — The Sword Verse**: "Then when the sacred months have passed, kill the polytheists wherever you find them." The most cited abrogating verse in classical tafsir. Tabari: it abrogated 124 verses of peace/toleration. However, (a) it applies only to Arabian polytheists who broke treaties, not to all non-Muslims (Ibn Kathir), and (b) it is limited by verses of peace (8:61, 2:256) according to modern exegetes
- **9:40 — The second of two in the cave**: Abu Bakr's fear and the Prophet's reassurance. Bukhari's complete narration: spiders and doves protected the cave
- **9:60 — Categories of Zakat recipients**: the definitive eight categories — the poor, the needy, collectors, reconciled hearts, slaves, debtors, in the path of Allah, the wayfarer
- **9:100 — The first forerunners** (*al-sābiqūn al-awwalūn*): the Muhajirun and Ansar. Vault: "May Allah be pleased with them and they with Him"

---

### 010. Yūnus — Jonah

**109 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 51**

A Meccan surah named after the prophet who was swallowed by the fish — a paradigm of prophetic patience.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **10:99–100 — "If your Lord had willed, all on earth would have believed"** : the tension between divine will and human freedom. Razi: this verse shows that faith is a gift, not mere human effort. Mu'tazilis: the "willing" here is not compelling force but guidance
- **10:57 — The Qur'an as healing**: "O humanity, there has come to you an exhortation from your Lord, healing for what is in the hearts, guidance and mercy for the believers"
- **10:92 — Pharaoh's body preserved**: "Today We will preserve your body that you may be a sign for those after you." — interpreted as the mummified body of Pharaoh discovered in modern Egypt

---

### 011. Hūd — The Prophet Hud

**123 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 52**

A "consolation surah" for the Prophet — the cycle of prophetic rejection and punishment is at its most extensive.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **11:112 — "Be upright as you are commanded"** : a verse of such weight that one companion said it "aged" the Prophet (because it demands perfect constancy). Tabari: *istiqāmah* (uprightness) is the most difficult command in the Qur'an
- **11:37 — Noah building the ark**: "Fashion the ark under our eyes and by Our inspiration — and do not address Me concerning those who do wrong" — God's command despite the mockery of Noah's people

---

### 012. Yūsuf — Joseph

**111 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 53**

The most sustained narrative in the Qur'an — a single complete story from youth to old age. Called "the best of stories" (12:3).

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **12:3 — "We narrate to you the best of stories"** : why *aḥsan al-qaṣaṣ*? Ibn Kathir: because of its completeness — jealousy, dream, slavery, temptation, imprisonment, power, reunion, forgiveness — nothing like it in scripture for narrative sweep
- **12:23–34 — The temptation of Zulaykhā**: the Qur'an's most famous scene. Unlike the Bible (Genesis 39), Zulaykhā is named in extra-Qur'anic tradition (not in the Qur'an itself — she is "the wife of the Aziz"). The detail of the ladies cutting their hands (12:31) when seeing Joseph's beauty is unique to the Qur'an. Tabari: Joseph's beauty was a *fitnah* (test) — he was given half of all beauty
- **12:67 — From where you least expect**: "And I know from Allah what you know not." Ya'qub's insight — divine knowledge exceeds human calculation

---

### 013. Al-Ra'd — The Thunder

**43 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 96**

Named after the thunder that glorifies God (v.13). A Medinan surah with a Meccan feel — cosmic signs and the triumph of truth.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **13:11 — The guardians**: "For each one are angels in succession before him and behind him, guarding him by Allah's command." The personal guardians and recorders
- **13:28 — Hearts find rest in God's remembrance**: one of the most recited verses in Sufi dhikr circles

---

### 014. Ibrāhīm — Abraham

**52 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 72**

The surah of Abraham's prayer — the patriarch supplicates for Mecca and for his descendants.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **14:37 — Abraham's prayer for his offspring**: "Our Lord, I have settled some of my descendants in a barren valley near Your Sacred House" — the foundation of Mecca's sacredness and prayer for its fruitfulness
- **14:40–41 — "My Lord, make me steadfast in prayer and from my descendants"** : the triple prayer for self, children, and parents — the most comprehensive supplication for family in the Qur'an

---

### 015. Al-Ḥijr — The Rocky Tract

**99 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 54**

Named after the region of the Thamud (Petra/Mada'in Saleh). A brief, dense consolation surah.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **15:9 — Divine preservation**: "Indeed We, it is We who sent down the Remembrance, and indeed We will preserve it." — the proof text for the Qur'an's textual invulnerability. Ibn Kathir: no other scripture has this guarantee
- **15:87 — The Seven Oft-Repeated**: "We have given you seven of the *mathānī* and the Great Qur'an" — interpreted as al-Fatihah (its seven verses) or the seven long surahs (al-Baqarah through al-Tawbah)

---

### 016. Al-Naḥl — The Bee

**128 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 70**

Named after the bee (v.68–69) — a surah of natural signs and divine provision.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **16:90 — Justice, Ihsan, and giving to relatives**: "Allah commands justice, excellence, and giving to relatives, and forbids lewdness, evil, and transgression." — the "verse of comprehensive ethics." Qurtubi: three commands and three prohibitions that sum up the entire moral law
- **16:68 — The bee's inspiration**: "Your Lord inspired the bee." The bee's *waḥy* is instinctual guidance, not prophetic revelation — showing that *waḥy* has levels

---

### 017. Al-Isrā' — The Night Journey

**111 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 50**

Also known as Banī Isrā'īl (Children of Israel). Opens with the Isra (Night Journey) and contains the subhah of 17:22–39 — the Ten Commandments of the Qur'an.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **17:1 — Isra**: "Glorified be He who carried His servant by night from the Inviolable Mosque to the Al-Aqsa Mosque." — body and soul (Ṭabarī) or soul only (Aisha's view, per some narrations)? Majority: bodily journey while awake
- **17:22–39 — The Islamic Decalogue**: commands: worship God alone (23), honour parents (23–24), give relatives their rights (26), don't be extravagant (27), don't kill children (31), don't approach adultery (32), don't take life unjustly (33), don't touch orphan's property (34), fulfil contracts (34), give full measure (35), don't pursue what you don't know (36), don't walk arrogantly (37)
- **17:70 — Dignity of humanity**: "We have honoured the children of Adam and carried them on land and sea" — the basis of human dignity regardless of belief
- **17:85 — The spirit**: "They ask you about the spirit. Say: The spirit is of the command of my Lord, and you have been given but little knowledge"

---

### 018. Al-Kahf — The Cave

**110 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 69**

The most frequently recited surah on Fridays — four major stories: the Cave Sleepers, the Rich Man and the Poor Man, Moses and Al-Khidr, and Dhul-Qarnayn.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **18:9–26 — The People of the Cave**: young monotheists who fled persecution and slept for 309 years. Their number (3, 5, or 7) — the Qur'an declines to specify, saying "My Lord knows best their number" (v.22). Tabari: a lesson that God can preserve His servants beyond natural means
- **18:60–82 — Moses and Al-Khidr**: the most famous dialogue in the Qur'an between Moses and the mysterious teacher. Three incidents (the boat, the boy, the wall) teach that divine wisdom may exceed human understanding. Razi: the limits of prophetic knowledge (Moses knew only the Law; Khidr knew the hidden purpose)
- **18:83–98 — Dhul-Qarnayn**: a righteous king who travelled to the ends of the earth and built a wall against Gog and Magog. Identity: Alexander the Great (most Western scholars), Cyrus the Great (modern scholarship), or a pre-Islamic Arabian king (some classical tradition)
- **18:65 — "We taught him knowledge from Our presence"** : *min ladunnā ʿilman* — "knowledge from Our own" — the source of the term *al-ʿilm al-ladunnī* (divinely granted knowledge) in Sufi tradition

**Virtue:** The Prophet said: "Whoever recites Surah al-Kahf on Friday, light will shine for him between the two Fridays." — [Bukhari 2011](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Friday%20Prayer)

---

### 019. Maryam — Mary

**98 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 44**

The only surah named after a woman. It tells the story of John and Jesus — with a unique Qur'anic account of Jesus' birth under a palm tree.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **19:16–36 — Mary's conception and Jesus' birth**: unique elements: (a) the angel appeared as a "well-formed man" (v.17), (b) Mary gave birth alone under a palm tree (v.23), (c) Jesus spoke from the cradle (v.24–33) — his first miracle. The birth narrative is distinct from Luke's Gospel (no inn, no shepherds, no census)
- **19:56–57 — Idris raised to a high station**: understood as Enoch — the only figure in the Qur'an described as being raised alive to heaven

---

### 020. Ṭā-Hā

**135 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 45**

One of the two "mysterious letter" surahs that opens with *Ṭā-Hā*. The surah of Moses — his entire prophetic career from the burning bush to the exodus.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **20:7–8 — Theophanies**: "And has the story of Moses reached you?" — the burning bush scene. Moses is commanded to remove his shoes at the holy valley of Tuwa. Tabari: removing shoes symbolizes detachment from worldly concerns
- **20:9–40 — Moses' training as prophet**: a sequence of five divine gifts: (1) staff = miracles, (2) white hand = another sign, (3) loosening of the tongue = eloquence, (4) Aaron as helper = companionship, (5) the Mosaic covenant = revelation
- **20:55 — "From it (the earth) We created you, to it We return you, and from it We shall bring you forth another time"** : the cycle of creation–death–resurrection in a single phrase
- **20:25–28 — Moses' prayer**: "My Lord, expand my chest, ease my task, and untie the knot of my tongue so they may understand my speech." — the most famous prophetic supplication for eloquence

---

### 021. Al-Anbiyā' — The Prophets

**112 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 73**

A survey of 16 prophets in quick succession — the prophetic chain in summary form.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **21:30 — The heavens and earth were joined**: "Do not the disbelievers see that the heavens and the earth were a joined entity, and We split them apart?" — interpreted by some modern exegetes as the Big Bang (Sayyid Qutb, Tantawi)
- **21:107 — Mercy to the worlds**: "And We have not sent you except as a mercy to the worlds." — the most universal description of Muhammad's mission. Razi: *al-ʿālamīn* includes humans, jinn, animals, and all creation

---

### 022. Al-Ḥajj — The Pilgrimage

**78 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 103**

The pilgrimage surah — the only surah named after a pillar of Islam.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **22:27 — The call to Hajj**: "And proclaim the Pilgrimage to the people — they will come on foot and on every lean camel from every distant pass." Abraham's call echoed across centuries
- **22:46 — Hearts to reflect**: "It is not the eyes that go blind, but the hearts within the chests." — the blindness of the heart is the real blindness

---

### 023. Al-Mu'minūn — The Believers

**118 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 74**

A catalogue of the believers' qualities — opening with a litany of their virtues.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **23:1–11 — The nine qualities of the successful believers**: (1) humble in prayer, (2) avoid idle talk, (3) pay zakat, (4) guard their chastity, (5) keep trusts and covenants, (6) maintain prayers. The Prophet said: "The verse from the Book of Allah that gladdened me most is [23:1] because it mentions the success of the believers." — [Tirmidhi 3166](scriptures/islam-hadith/04%20Jami%60%20at-Tirmidhi.md#Chapters%20on%20Tafsir)
- **23:12–14 — The stages of creation**: "We created man from an extract of clay, then as a drop… a clot… a lump… bones… flesh… another creation." — the embryological stages that amazed 20th-century commentators

---

### 024. Al-Nūr — The Light

**64 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 102**

The surah of community ethics — revealed after the Slander of Aisha (Ifk affair, 627 CE).

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **24:11–20 — The Ifk (Slander)**: the longest single narrative occasion in the Qur'an. Tabari: the revelation exonerated Aisha completely after a month of community gossip
- **24:2 — The lashing verse**: "The female and male fornicator — lash each of them with one hundred lashes. Let no compassion prevent you from Allah's religion." — Qurtubi contrasts the harsh penalty with the requirement of four witnesses for conviction, making the threshold almost impossibly high
- **24:35 — The Light Verse** (*Āyat al-Nūr*): "Allah is the light of the heavens and the earth — His light is like a niche in which is a lamp…" — the most allegorical verse in the Qur'an. Over 30 different interpretations recorded by Tabari. Ghazali's *Mishkāt al-Anwār* (Niche of Lights) devotes an entire book to it: the niche = the believer's chest, the glass = the heart, the lamp = the spirit, the olive tree = revelation
- **24:31 — The Veiling verse**: "And say to the believing women to lower their gaze and guard their modesty… and draw their headcovers over their bosoms." Qurtubi: *khimār* (headcover) was already worn by Arab women but they would throw it behind them, leaving their necks and ears exposed; the verse commanded covering the *jayb* (opening of the dress)
- **24:58–59 — Permission to enter rooms**: the three times of day when even household members must seek permission

---

### 025. Al-Furqān — The Criterion

**77 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 42**

Al-Furqān = "the Criterion" — one of the Qur'an's names.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **25:63–74 — The 10 qualities of the servants of the Merciful** (*ʿIbād al-Raḥmān*): walk humbly, respond to ignorance with peace, pray at night, seek refuge from hell, spend moderately, avoid shirk and murder and adultery, do not bear false witness, respond to revelation attentively, pray for family, seek dignity. The most detailed ethical portrait of the believer in the Qur'an

### 026. Al-Shu'arā' — The Poets

**227 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 47**

The longest Meccan surah — seven prophet stories told in parallel structure.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **26:214 — "And warn your closest kindred"** : the Prophet climbed Mount Safa and called his clan — a pivotal moment in the Meccan da'wah
- **26:224–227 — The poets criticized**: the distinction between inspired poetry and Qur'an — the Qur'an is *not* poetry, and the *real* poets are those who do not follow their own words

### 027. Al-Naml — The Ant

**93 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 48**

Named after the ant that spoke to Solomon's army.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **27:15–44 — Solomon's kingdom**: the hoopoe bringing news of Sheba, the letter to Bilqis, the throne transported by the jinn in the blink of an eye (by 'Ifrit vs. by a man with knowledge of the Book). Zamakhshari: "the man with knowledge of the Book" = someone who knew Allah's greatest name
- **27:82 — The Beast of the Earth** (*Dābbat al-Arḍ*): an eschatological sign that will emerge at the end of time to speak to people

### 028. Al-Qaṣaṣ — The Stories

**88 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 49**

The "stories" surah — focused on Moses from birth to his flight from Egypt.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **28:24 — Moses' prayer for help**: "My Lord, I am in need of whatever good You send down to me." — the prayer of the believer in the state of absolute need
- **28:27 — Moses serving Shu'ayb for 8–10 years to marry his daughter**: the only work-for-marriage contract in scripture
- **28:77 — "Seek the abode of the Hereafter with what Allah has given you, but do not forget your portion of this world"** : the Islamic balance — neither worldly renunciation nor worldly indulgence

### 029. Al-'Ankabūt — The Spider

**69 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 85**

Named after the spider's fragile web (v.41) — a metaphor for the weakness of idols.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **29:2 — "Do people think they will be left to say 'We believe' without being tested?"** : the definitive statement that faith must be proven through trial. No amputation of tests from belief
- **29:41 — The spider's house**: "The likeness of those who take protectors other than Allah is like the spider that builds a house — but the weakest of houses is the spider's house." — the fragility of false refuges
- **29:50–51 — The Qur'an as the only miracle**: "Is it not enough for them that We have sent down to you the Book which is recited to them?"

### 030. Al-Rūm — The Romans

**60 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 84**

Prophesying the Byzantine-Persian war — a political prophecy fulfilled within a decade.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **30:2–5 — The Romans defeated… then victorious**: revealed when the Persians crushed the Byzantines (614 CE). The Quraysh rejoiced (they favoured the fire-worshipping Persians over the Christian Byzantines). The prophecy of Byzantine victory within a few years was fulfilled at the Battle of Issus (622 CE) or Nineveh (627 CE)
- **30:30 — The Fiṭrah**: "So set your face toward the religion, upright — the *fiṭrah* of Allah upon which He created mankind. There is no changing Allah's creation." — the innate disposition toward tawhid

### 031. Luqmān — The Wise Man

**34 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 57**

Named after the sage Luqman — a figure of wisdom, possibly a prophet, possibly a righteous sage.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **31:12–19 — Luqman's advice to his son**: a parenting manual in eight verses: (1) no shirk is the greatest wrong (v.13), (2) honour parents but don't follow them into shirk (v.14–15), (3) God knows even the mustard seed (v.16), (4) establish prayer (v.17), (5) enjoin good and forbid evil (v.17), (6) be patient (v.17), (7) don't turn your cheek in arrogance (v.18), (8) walk moderately, speak softly (v.19)

### 032. Al-Sajdah — The Prostration

One of the surahs containing a prostration (*sajdah tilāwah*).

### 033. Al-Aḥzāb — The Confederates

**73 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 90**

The surah of the Battle of the Trench (627 CE) and the Prophet's household.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **33:21 — "You have a beautiful example in the Messenger of Allah"** : the basis of *uswah ḥasanah* — the Prophet as model
- **33:33 — The verse of purification** (*Āyat al-Taṭhīr*): "Allah desires only to remove impurity from you, O People of the House, and to purify you completely." — the most debated exegetical verse in Sunni-Shia dialogue. Sunni: includes the Prophet's wives. Shia: restricted to the Ahl al-Bayt (Ali, Fatima, Hasan, Husayn)
- **33:37 — Zayd's marriage to Zaynab**: the Prophet's marriage to the divorced wife of his adopted son was to abolish the pre-Islamic legal fiction of adoption
- **33:40 — "Seal of the Prophets"** : finality of prophethood — the definitive statement
- **33:56 — Salawāt upon the Prophet**: "Allah and His angels send blessings upon the Prophet — O you who believe, send blessings upon him and greet him with peace." — the basis of *durood* / *al-salāt al-ibrāhīmiyyah*
- **33:59 — The Hijab**: "O Prophet, tell your wives, daughters, and believing women to draw their cloaks over themselves" — the garment of modesty

### 036. Yā-Sīn

**83 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 41**

Called "the heart of the Qur'an" — recited over the dying and in graves.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **36:1 — Yā-Sīn**: the mysterious letters. Interpretations: "O human" (some companions), "O Muhammad" (majority), an oath (Tabari)
- **36:12 — "We record what they send ahead and what they leave behind"** : the Book of Deeds
- **36:36 — Everything created in pairs**: "Glory be to He who created all kinds, from what the earth grows and from themselves and from what they do not know" — pairs in plants, animals, and even the unknown (subatomic?)
- The entire surah is traditionally recited over the deceased in many Muslim cultures

**Virtue:** "Recite Yā-Sīn over your dead." — [Abu Dawud 3121](scriptures/islam-hadith/03%20Sunan%20Abi%20Dawud.md#Funerals)

### 037. Al-Ṣāffāt — Those Who Set the Ranks

**182 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 56**

The surah of the "arrangers" (angels) — a parade of prophets in the second half.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **37:1–3 — The oath by the arrays**: angels in ranks (Ṭabarī), or the believers in prayer rows, or the rows of martyrs in battle
- **37:100–111 — Abraham's sacrifice**: "We ransomed him with a great sacrifice." Unlike the Bible (Genesis 22), the son is not named in the Qur'an. Classical tafsir: Ishmael (majority of early authorities, especially among the companions) or Isaac (some later authorities and biblical traditions)
- **37:139–148 — Jonah**: the fish swallowed him — he was guilty of impatience first, then pardoned. Allah caused a gourd vine to grow over him after he was cast out

### 038. Ṣād

**88 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 38**

The surah of David and Solomon — opening with an oath by the Qur'an.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **38:17–26 — David's repentance**: David was tested through two disputants who climbed the wall of his prayer chamber. The parable of the 99 ewes — his judgment was "fair but had he sought the whole truth, he would have seen it differently." He bowed and repented
- **38:32 — Solomon and the horses**: "I have preferred the love of good things over the remembrance of my Lord until the sun disappeared" — did Solomon miss prayer because of admiring horses? Tabari: he was testing his horses, not neglecting prayer. Other interpretations: he was deeply absorbed and asked forgiveness
- **38:71–85 — Iblis refuses to prostrate to Adam**: the origin of Iblis's enmity

### 039. Al-Zumar — The Groups/Throngs

**75 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 80**

The surah of pure devotion — divided into two halves: warning and hope.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **39:53 — The verse of hope**: "Say: O My servants who have transgressed against themselves, do not despair of the mercy of Allah. Allah forgives all sins." — the most hopeful verse in the Qur'an. Revealed about Wahshi (the killer of Hamzah) or about polytheists who had killed and despaired. Tabari: its scope is universal - no sin is beyond God's forgiveness if repented
- **39:67 — "The whole earth will be in His grip on the Day of Resurrection, and the heavens will be folded in His right hand"** : the majesty of God on Judgment Day

### 040. Ghāfir / Al-Mu'min — The Forgiver / The Believer

**85 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 60**

The first of the *Ḥawāmīm* (seven surahs beginning with Ḥā-Mīm). Named after the believing man in Pharaoh's court.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **40:1 — Ḥā-Mīm**: the seven Ḥawāmīm surahs (40–46) form a distinct Qur'anic corpus — all revealed in a cluster
- **40:3 — "Forgiver of sin, Accepter of repentance"** : the opening divine attributes
- **40:28–45 — The Believer of the family of Pharaoh**: a prototype of hidden faith who secretly defended Moses — a model of speaking truth to power

### 041. Fuṣṣilat — Clearly Detailed

**54 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 61**

Also known as Ḥā-Mīm al-Sajdah. The surah of revelation and creation.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **41:9–12 — Creation in six days**: the creation narrative — earth in two days, mountains and provisions in four, heavens in two. A schema of creation
- **41:37 — Prostration is for Allah alone**: "Do not prostrate to the sun or the moon, but prostrate to Allah who created them" — the polemic against celestial worship
- **41:53 — "We will show them Our signs in the horizons and within themselves"** : the dual proof — external (the universe) and internal (the self)

### 042. Al-Shūrā — Consultation

The surah of mutual consultation — the basis of Islamic governance in the Qur'an.

**Tafsir highlights:**

- **42:23 — Love for the Prophet's family**: "I do not ask you for any reward except love for my close relatives" — interpreted differently: (a) literal Ahl al-Bayt (Shia), (b) the Quraysh (some Sunni), (c) anything that brings you closer to God (Tabari)

### 046. Al-Aḥqāf — The Sand Dunes

Named after the sand dune region of the 'Ād people.

### 047. Muḥammad

The only surah named after the Prophet — revealed after the Hijrah.

### 048. Al-Fatḥ — The Victory

**29 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 111**

Revealed after the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah — declaring the treaty a "clear victory."

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **48:1 — "Indeed We have given you a clear victory"** : 'Umar was initially confused — this "victory" looked like a retreat. The treaty's long-term benefits (peace, propagation of Islam) proved its wisdom
- **48:10 — The Pledge of Riḍwān**: "Those who pledge allegiance to you pledge allegiance to Allah — Allah's hand is over their hands." The oath under the tree at Hudaybiyyah
- **48:29 — The description of the Prophet's companions**: "Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah — and those with him are firm against the disbelievers, compassionate among themselves. You see them bowing and prostrating, seeking bounty and pleasure from Allah. Their mark is on their faces from the trace of prostration."

### 049. Al-Ḥujurāt — The Chambers / The Private Apartments

**18 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 106**

The surah of etiquette and community ethics — revealed after the Year of Conquest (9 AH) when delegations flooded Medina.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **49:1 — "Do not put yourselves before Allah and His Messenger"** : the basis of prophetic authority and adab
- **49:6 — Verify news from a wrongdoer**: "If a wicked person brings you news, verify it" — the foundation of Islamic journalism and fact-checking
- **49:9 — Justice between conflicting parties**: "If two groups of believers fight, make peace between them. If one oppresses the other, fight the oppressor until it returns to Allah's command." — the methodology of conflict resolution
- **49:10 — "Believers are but brothers"** : the basis of Islamic fraternity
- **49:11 — Prohibition of mockery and name-calling**: "Do not let one people mock another — they may be better than them"
- **49:12 — Prohibition of suspicion and backbiting**: "Avoid much suspicion, for some suspicion is sin. Do not spy on each other, nor backbite each other. Would one of you eat the flesh of his dead brother?"
- **49:13 — The verse of human equality**: "O humanity, We created you from a male and a female, and made you peoples and tribes that you may know one another. The noblest of you in Allah's sight is the most God-conscious."

### 050. Qāf

A Meccan resurrection surah — short, intense.

- **50:37 — The heart that hears**: "Indeed in that is a reminder for whoever has a heart or gives ear while witnessing." The believers' heart is alive; the heart of the heedless is dead

### 051. Al-Dhāriyāt — The Scatterers

Oath by Mount Sinai — the mountain of Moses' revelation.

### 053. Al-Najm — The Star

**62 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 23**

The surah of the Prophet's visionary experience — contains the most vivid description of the angelic encounter.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **53:1–18 — The Prophet's vision**: the closest the Qur'an comes to describing the theophany — the Star (Sirius) oath, the vision of Jibril "with his own form" at the highest horizon, the Sidrat al-Muntahā (Lote Tree of the Utmost Boundary). Tabari: the Prophet saw Jibril in his true form (600 wings filling the horizon) twice — once during the first revelation and once during the Mi'raj
- **53:19–20 — The "Satanic Verses" incident**: "Have you considered al-Lat, al-'Uzza, and Manat, the third, the other?" Classical tafsir (Tabari, Ibn Kathir): a false report that Satan inserted words praising the goddesses. The surah continues with what was actually revealed: "Shall you have males and He females? That is an unfair division!" (53:21–22). Modern critical scholarship debates the historicity of the incident
- **53:24 — "Or shall man have whatever he wishes?"** : the refutation of wishful thinking in religion

### 054. Al-Qamar — The Moon

**55 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 37**

Named after the splitting of the moon — a miracle given to the Prophet.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **54:1 — "The Hour drew near and the moon split"** : the Moon Splitting miracle. Bukhari: the companions saw the moon split in two while the Prophet was in Mecca. Classical tafsir: a literal event witnessed by the Quraysh who called it "magic." Some modern exegetes interpret it as a future eschatological event or a metaphor
- **54:17 — "And We have made the Qur'an easy to remember — is there anyone who will remember?"** : the refrain that recurs four times in this surah

### 055. Al-Raḥmān — The Most Merciful

**78 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 97**

The "Bride of the Qur'an" — a litany of divine blessings, with the repeated refrain "Which of your Lord's favours will you deny?"

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **55:1 — "The Most Merciful — He taught the Qur'an"** : the opening links *al-Raḥmān* (the supreme divine name) to the teaching of the Qur'an
- **55:26–27 — "All that is on earth will perish, but the Face of your Lord will remain, full of Majesty and Honour"** : the most concise statement of divine permanence
- **55:31 — "We will attend to you, O two heavy ones (humans and jinn)"** : both species addressed — the only surah where jinn are directly addressed alongside humans
- **55:46–78 — The two gardens of Paradise**: two levels of paradise — one for the foremost (*al-sābiqūn*), one for the people of the right (*aṣḥāb al-yamīn*). Described in parallel

### 056. Al-Wāqi'ah — The Inevitable Event

**96 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 46**

The threefold division of humanity on Judgment Day.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **56:7–56 — The three classes**: (1) the Foremost (*al-sābiqūn*) — the elite, nearest to God, (2) the People of the Right — the righteous mass, (3) the People of the Left — the deniers. Each described in parallel
- **56:78 — "In a Book well-guarded"** : the *Kitāb maknūn* — either the Preserved Tablet or the collected Qur'an

**Virtue:** The Prophet said: "Whoever recites Surah al-Wāqi'ah every night will never be afflicted by poverty." — narrated but weak chain

### 057. Al-Ḥadīd — Iron

**29 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 94**

The first of the *Musabbiḥāt* (surahs beginning with God's glorification).

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **57:25 — "We sent down iron"** : *Anzalnā al-ḥadīd* — "We sent down iron." Literal: iron created and sent down from the sky (meteorite iron? Or revelation of its use?). Figurative: the creation of iron. The verse notes iron's "mighty strength and benefits for humanity"
- **57:1–6 — The opening tasbīḥ**: everything in creation glorifies God — the theme of the five *Musabbiḥāt* (57, 59, 61, 62, 64)

### 058. Al-Mujādilah — The Pleading Woman

**22 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 105**

Named after Khawlah bint Tha'labah, who pleaded about her husband's divorce formula.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **58:1–4 — Zihār abolished**: the pre-Islamic divorce formula "You are to me like my mother's back" is prohibited and replaced with expiation (freeing a slave, fasting, feeding the poor). Khawlah's direct dialogue with the Prophet recorded
- **58:7 — "There is no secret conversation between three but He is the fourth"** : God's omniscience — a refrain of divine presence

### 059. Al-Ḥashr — The Gathering

**24 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 101**

Revealed after the expulsion of the Banu Nadir Jewish tribe from Medina.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **59:7 — The principle of community wealth**: "So that it does not circulate only among the rich among you" — the principle of equitable distribution
- **59:21 — "If We had sent this Qur'an down upon a mountain, you would have seen it humbled, splitting apart from fear of Allah"** : the Qur'an's weight would crush a mountain, yet humans are unmoved
- **59:22–24 — The fifteen names of Allah**: the most concentrated sequence of divine names in the Qur'an: Al-Raḥmān, Al-Raḥīm, Al-Malik, Al-Quddūs, Al-Salām, Al-Mu'min, Al-Muhaymin, Al-'Azīz, Al-Jabbār, Al-Mutakabbir, Al-Khāliq, Al-Bāri', Al-Muṣawwir, Al-'Azīz, Al-Ḥakīm

### 060. Al-Mumtaḥanah — The Tested Woman

**13 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 91**

Rules about relations with non-believers and the treatment of women emigrants.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **60:8 — The verse of peace with non-Muslims**: "Allah does not forbid you from being kind and just to those who did not fight you in religion nor drive you from your homes — indeed Allah loves the just." — one of the most important verses for Muslim-non-Muslim relations
- **60:10 — The test for emigrant women**: believing women fleeing Mecca must be tested to confirm their faith — not returned to the Quraysh (but their dowries refunded)

### 061. Al-Ṣaff — The Ranks

**14 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 109**

The surah of fighting in ranks for God's cause.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **61:2 — "Why do you say what you do not do?"** : a rebuke of the gap between words and deeds
- **61:6 — Jesus foretells Ahmad**: "And when Jesus, son of Mary, said: O Children of Israel, I am the messenger of Allah to you, confirming the Torah before me and giving good news of a messenger after me whose name is **Ahmad**." — the most direct biblical prophecy understood as pointing to Muhammad (Qur'anic interpretation of John 14:16, 15:26 — the Paraclete)
- **61:8 — "They want to extinguish Allah's light with their mouths, but Allah will perfect His light"** : the triumph of truth over opposition

### 062. Al-Jumu'ah — Friday

**11 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 110**

The surah of the Friday congregational prayer.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **62:9 — "When the call to Friday prayer is made, hasten to the remembrance of Allah and leave aside trade"** : the obligation of Friday prayer. The Prophet said: "The best day on which the sun rises is Friday." — [Muslim 854](scriptures/islam-hadith/02%20Sahih%20Muslim.md#The%20Book%20of%20Faith)
- **62:11 — "When they see trade or amusement, they disperse toward it and leave you standing"** : a specific incident — a trade caravan arrived during the Friday sermon and the companions rushed out, leaving the Prophet standing on the pulpit

### 063. Al-Munāfiqūn — The Hypocrites

### 064. Al-Taghābun — The Mutual Loss and Gain / The Day of Cheating

### 066. Al-Taḥrīm — The Prohibition

### 067. Al-Mulk — The Sovereignty

**30 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 77**

The "Protector" surah — recited nightly for protection from the grave's punishment.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **67:1–5 — Perfect creation**: "Blessed is He in Whose hand is the Kingdom… who created seven heavens in layers. You see no imperfection in the creation of the Most Merciful. Turn your eyes — do you see any flaw?" — the scientific challenge to find imperfection in creation
- **67:12 — Forgiveness for the unseen**: "Indeed, those who fear their Lord unseen will have forgiveness and a great reward"

**Virtue:** The Prophet said: "There is a surah in the Qur'an of thirty verses that will intercede for a man until he is forgiven — it is *Tabārak alladhī bi-yadihi al-mulk*." — [Tirmidhi 2891](scriptures/islam-hadith/04%20Jami%60%20at-Tirmidhi.md#Chapters%20on%20Tafsir)

---

### 068. Al-Qalam — The Pen

**52 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 2**

The second surah revealed (directly after al-'Alaq). Named after the Pen — the first created thing.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **68:1 — "Nūn, by the Pen"** : *Nūn* = the inkpot (Ibn Abbas), the whale (some traditions), or a mysterious letter. The Pen = the first thing God created, which wrote the decrees of all creation. Tabari: God created the Pen and commanded it to write
- **68:4 — "And you are of a noble character"** : the single greatest character endorsement of the Prophet. Aisha: "His character was the Qur'an" — [Muslim 746](scriptures/islam-hadith/02%20Sahih%20Muslim.md#The%20Book%20of%20Prayers)

---

### 069. Al-Ḥāqqah — The Inevitable Reality

**52 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 78**

The surah of the unavoidable day.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **69:38–43 — "It is the word of a noble messenger, not the word of a poet"** : the Qur'an is not poetry, nor soothsaying — a definitive claim that must be accepted

---

### 070. Al-Ma'ārij — The Ascending Stairways

**44 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 79**

Named after the pathways of ascent — the angels' and the Spirit's ascent to God.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **70:4 — "The angels and the Spirit ascend to Him in a day whose measure is 50,000 years"** : cosmic time — the duration of Judgment Day or the measure of the divine day
- **70:19–35 — The impatient vs. the faithful**: humans are created anxious — except those who pray, give charity, believe in judgment, guard their chastity, keep trusts, stand by their testimony, and maintain prayer

---

### 071. Nūḥ — Noah

**28 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 71**

Noah's preaching — the longest sustained monologue of a single prophet (other than Muhammad) in the Qur'an.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **71:10–12 — Noah's call**: "Ask forgiveness of your Lord — He is Oft-Forgiving. He will send rain to you in abundance, give you wealth and children, gardens and rivers." — seeking forgiveness brings material as well as spiritual blessings
- **71:26–28 — Noah's prayer against his people**: "My Lord, leave not a single disbeliever on the earth" — the only prophet who prayed for the destruction of the disbelievers

---

### 072. Al-Jinn — The Jinn

**28 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 40**

The surah of the jinn — a group of jinn hearing the Qur'an and believing.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **72:1 — "Say: It has been revealed to me that a group of jinn listened and said: We have heard a wonderful Recitation"** : a delegation of jinn from Nasībīn heard the Prophet reciting at dawn near the marketplace of 'Ukāẓ. Tabari: they were sent to hear the Prophet and returned to their people as warners
- **72:26 — "He knows the Unseen and does not disclose His Unseen to anyone except a messenger He has chosen"** : the limits of human and even prophetic knowledge of the unseen

---

### 073. Al-Muzzammil — The Enshrouded One

**20 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 3**

The third surah revealed — addressed to the Prophet wrapped in his cloak during the pause in revelation.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **73:1–4 — The command to night prayer**: "O you enshrouded! Stand the night in prayer except a little — half of it, or a little less, or a little more, and recite the Qur'an in slow, measured tones." — the early obligation of *tahajjud*
- **73:20 — **Later abrogation**: the latter part of the surah (v.20) lightened the night prayer obligation — abrogating the earlier command to stand half the night

---

### 074. Al-Muddaththir — The Cloaked One

**56 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 4**

The fourth surah revealed — "Arise and warn!"

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **74:1–7 — The new mission**: "O you cloaked! Arise and warn! Your Lord magnify! Your garments purify! Shun all filth! And do not bestow favour seeking gain! And for your Lord be patient!"
- **74:11–30 — Al-Walīd ibn al-Mughīrah**: the Quraysh leader who rejected the Qur'an despite knowing its eloquence — "Let Me deal with the one I created alone… I gave him vast wealth… then he schemes… may he be destroyed!" The climax: "Over it are nineteen" (74:30) — the guardians of Hell

---

### 075. Al-Qiyāmah — The Resurrection

**40 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 31**

The eschatological oath surah — the resurrection of the bones.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **75:16–19 — The command to listen before the revelation finishes**: "Do not move your tongue with it to hasten it — it is for Us to collect it and its recitation." — Jibril's instruction to wait until he finishes reciting before repeating — the basis of not rushing Qur'anic revelation
- **75:22–23 — Seeing God**: "That day faces will be radiant, looking towards their Lord." — the proof text for *ru'yat Allāh* (seeing God in Paradise), which Ash'aris affirm and Mu'tazilis deny

---

### 076. Al-Insān / Al-Dahr — The Human / Time

**31 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 98**

The surah of the righteous — describing their reward in Paradise.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **76:5–22 — The drink of the righteous**: "They drink from a cup mixed with camphor (*kāfūr*)… a spring from which Allah's servants drink." The *abrar* (righteous) are described in exquisite sensory detail
- **76:8 — Food despite love of it**: "And they give food — despite their love of it — to the poor, the orphan, and the captive" — the highest form of charity: giving what you yourself desire

---

### 077. Al-Mursalāt — The Emissaries / Those Sent Forth

**50 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 33**

Oaths by successive messengers or winds.

---

### 078. Al-Naba' — The Great News

**40 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 80**

The "Great News" = the Resurrection, which the Quraysh disputed.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **78:1–3 — "What are they asking about? About the Great News — about which they differ"** : the resurrection as the central disputed question
- **78:18–20 — The Day of Decision**: "The Day the Trumpet is blown and you come in multitudes, the sky is opened and becomes gates, the mountains are set in motion and become a mirage"

---

### 079. Al-Nāzi'āt — Those Who Pull Out / The Snatchers

**46 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 81**

Oaths by the angels who pull out souls (of the disbelievers violently, of the believers gently).

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **79:27–33 — Creation is harder**: "Are you more difficult to create or the heaven? He built it." — the argument from creation to resurrection — if God could create the vast heavens, He can surely recreate the human body

---

### 080. 'Abasa — He Frowned

**42 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 24**

The Prophet's rebuke for the blind man.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **80:1–10 — The blind man**: 'Abdullah b. Umm Maktūm, blind, came seeking knowledge while the Prophet was in dialogue with the Quraysh elite. The Prophet frowned and turned away. The "rebuke surah" descended — one of the most remarkable passages in scripture where God corrects His prophet gently but publicly. The Prophet thereafter honoured Ibn Umm Maktūm immensely, making him governor of Medina twice

---

### 081. Al-Takwīr — The Folding Up / The Darkening

**29 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 7**

One of the earliest revealed surahs — cosmic collapse imagery.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **81:1–14 — The twelve signs of the end**: sun folded, stars fall, mountains move, pregnant camels neglected, wild beasts gathered, seas set ablaze, souls paired, the infant girl buried alive asked her crime, scrolls spread, sky stripped, Hell set ablaze, Paradise brought near
- **81:29 — "You do not will unless Allah wills"** : the Ash'ari pillar — human will is subordinate to divine will

---

### 082. Al-Infiṭār — The Splitting

**19 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 82**

The splintering of the sky on Judgment Day.

---

### 083. Al-Muṭaffifīn — The Defrauders / Those Who Cheat

**36 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 86**

Woe to those who cheat in trade — revealed in Medina about the traders of the Ansar.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **83:1–6 — "Woe to those who give short measure"** : economic justice at the micro-level — the most detailed condemnation of commercial fraud in the Qur'an. The punishment: resurrection for a "terrible day"

---

### 084. Al-Inshiqāq — The Rending

**25 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 83**

The sky rent open on Judgment Day.

---

### 085. Al-Burūj — The Constellations / The Mansions of the Stars

**22 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 27**

The story of the Persecutors of the Trench (*Aṣḥāb al-Ukhdūd*) — believers were thrown into a trench of fire.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **85:4–8 — The People of the Ditch**: the story of the believing boy and the magician — a Christian tradition (the martyrs of Najran, c. 520 CE) or a Jewish tradition. The believers were thrown into a fire-filled trench for refusing to renounce their faith. Tabari: the story of the king who tested a believing boy — the boy's persistence converted thousands

---

### 086. Al-Ṭāriq — The Nightcomer / The Piercing Star

**17 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 36**

Oath by the morning star — the soul's creation and the return.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **86:5–7 — "Let man consider from what he was created"** : "Created from a fluid gushing forth, issuing from between the backbone and the ribs." — pre-medieval understanding of the origin of semen from the male and the female, uniquely described

---

### 087. Al-A'lā — The Most High

**19 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 8**

One of the earliest revealed surahs — glorifying God's name.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **87:1 — "Glorify the name of your Lord, the Most High"** : the *Tasbīḥ* — the basis of the *Subḥāna rabbiyal-a'lā* in prostration
- **87:18–19 — "This is in the earlier books — the scrolls of Abraham and Moses"** : the early connection between the Qur'an and previous revelations

**Virtue:** The Prophet said he loved this surah more than anything under the sun — narrated by 'Ali

---

### 088. Al-Ghāshiyah — The Overwhelming

**26 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 68**

The "overwhelming" event = Judgment Day, described in vivid contrast.

---

### 089. Al-Fajr — The Dawn

**30 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 10**

Oaths by the dawn and the ten nights — the destruction of ancient peoples.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **89:27–30 — The soul at peace**: "O soul at peace! Return to your Lord, well pleased and well pleasing. Enter among My servants! Enter My Paradise!" — the *nafs al-muṭma'innah* — recited at funerals and the seal of the believer's journey

---

### 090. Al-Balad — The City (Mecca)

**20 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 35**

The surah of the "city" — Mecca — and the human struggle.

---

### 091. Al-Shams — The Sun

**15 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 26**

The oath surah of the sun and the soul — the Thamud's destruction of the she-camel.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **91:7–10 — The soul's inspiration**: "By the soul and what proportioned it — and inspired it with its wickedness and its righteousness. He succeeds who purifies it, and fails who corrupts it." — the basis of *tazkiyat al-nafs* (soul purification) in Islamic spirituality. Seven oaths before reaching the soul — the longest oath sequence in the Qur'an for the most important subject

---

### 092. Al-Layl — The Night

**21 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 9**

The surah of the two paths — those who give and those who withhold.

---

### 093. Al-Ḍuḥā — The Morning Light

**11 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 11**

The most intimate consolation surah — revealed during the pause in revelation.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **93:1–3 — "Your Lord has not forsaken you"** : the direct response to the polytheists' taunt. Ibn Kathir: the pause in revelation — the Prophet's enemies said "Muhammad's Lord has abandoned him." This surah refutes them with the tenderness of a divine lullaby
- **93:6–8 — Three blessings**: orphan → shelter, wandering → guidance, destitute → enrichment. The triple grace that demands triple response: don't oppress the orphan, don't repel the beggar, proclaim God's favour

---

### 094. Al-Sharḥ / Al-Inshirāḥ — The Expansion / The Comfort

**8 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 12**

"Did We not expand your chest?" — the spiritual expansion that accompanies the burden of prophethood.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **94:1 — "Did We not expand your chest?"** : *sharḥ al-ṣadr* — either the literal opening of the Prophet's chest by angels (purification of his heart as a child, per hadith) or the spiritual expansion of his capacity. Both readings are supported by early tafsir
- **94:5–6 — "With hardship comes ease"** : repeated twice — a law of existence: every difficulty has a path through it. The Prophet said: "One hardship cannot overcome two eases."

---

### 095. Al-Tīn — The Fig

**8 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 28**

The oath by the fig, the olive, Mount Sinai, and the secure city (Mecca).

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **95:4–6 — Best of creation → lowest of the low**: humans created in the best form (*aḥsan taqwīm*) but brought to the lowest except those who believe and do good. Tabari: the universal human potential for both perfection and degradation

---

### 096. Al-'Alaq — The Clot

**19 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 1**

**The first surah revealed** — the opening command "Read!"

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **96:1–5 — The first revelation**: the five verses that began Islam. The command *iqra'* (read/recite) — three times before the Prophet could respond. Tabari: the Prophet was not literate (*al-ummī*), so the command was a miracle — he "recited" what he did not know
- **96:6–19 — Abu Jahl's threat**: the later verses threaten Abu Jahl, who threatened to stamp on the Prophet's neck when he prayed at the Kaaba

---

### 097. Al-Qadr — The Power / The Decree

**5 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 25**

The Night of Power — the night the Qur'an was sent down.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **97:1–3 — Laylat al-Qadr**: "The Night of Decree is better than a thousand months." The exact night was shown to the Prophet, then withdrawn (to encourage seeking it in the last ten nights of Ramadan). Its blessings exceed 83 years of worship. The angels descend — peace until dawn

---

### 098. Al-Bayyinah — The Clear Evidence

**8 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 100**

The surah of the "clear proof" — the Prophet and the Qur'an as evidence to the People of the Book.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **98:1–3 — The clear evidence**: "Those who disbelieve among the People of the Book and the polytheists were not going to leave until clear evidence came to them — a messenger from Allah reciting purified scrolls."

---

### 099. Al-Zalzalah — The Earthquake

**8 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 93**

The surah of the great earthquake — the earth's final testimony.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **99:7–8 — The atom's weight**: "Whoever does an atom's weight of good will see it, and whoever does an atom's weight of evil will see it." — the ultimate accountability for every action, no matter how small

---

### 100. Al-'Ādiyāt — The Coursers / The Chargers

**11 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 14**

Oaths by the warhorses — and the ingratitude of the human being.

---

### 101. Al-Qāri'ah — The Striking Calamity

**11 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 30**

"Al-Qāri'ah" = the shattering blow of Judgment Day.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **101:6–8 — The Scales**: "Then whose scales are heavy — he will be in a pleasant life. But whose scales are light — his mother is *Hāwiyah* (the Abyss)." — the weighing of deeds determines the eternal outcome

---

### 102. Al-Takāthur — The Rivalry for More / Competition in Increase

**8 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 16**

The surah against worldly competition — "until you visit the graves."

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **102:1–2 — "Competition for more distracts you — until you visit the graves"** : the most concise critique of consumerism and status-seeking in scripture. *Takāthur* — competing in wealth, children, status — blinds humans until death

---

### 103. Al-'Aṣr — The Time / The Declining Day

**3 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 13**

The shortest complete surah — yet "the summary of the whole Qur'an" (Imam Shafi'i).

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **103:1–3 — The condition of humanity**: "By time — humanity is in loss, except those who believe, do righteous deeds, enjoin truth, and enjoin patience." — four conditions for salvation: faith, action, truth, patience. Shafi'i said: if people reflected on this single surah, it would suffice them

---

### 104. Al-Humazah — The Slanderer / The Backbiter

**9 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 32**

Woe to the slanderer who collects wealth and thinks it will immortalize him.

---

### 105. Al-Fīl — The Elephant

**5 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 19**

The Year of the Elephant — Abraha's attack on Mecca destroyed by flocks of birds.

---

### 106. Quraysh — The Quraysh

**4 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 29**

The surah of the Quraysh tribe — the custodians of the Kaaba — reminding them of God's provision that made their trade caravans possible.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **106:4 — "Who fed them against hunger and gave them security from fear"** : the two greatest gifts to the Quraysh — food and safety — both from God

---

### 107. Al-Mā'ūn — The Small Kindness / Assistance

**7 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 17**

The surah of the hypocrite who withholds basic kindness.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **107:4–6 — "Woe to those who pray but are heedless of their prayer, who do things to be seen"** : the condemnation of formalistic worship without sincerity — the *murā'ūn* (show-offs)

---

### 108. Al-Kawthar — The Abundance

**3 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 15**

The shortest surah of the Qur'an — revealed as consolation when the Prophet's enemies mocked his childlessness.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **108:1 — "We have given you al-Kawthar"** : interpreted as a river in Paradise (the most common), the Qur'an itself, abundant goodness, or the multitude of followers. The Prophet said of Kawthar: "It is a river my Lord has given me in Paradise, whiter than milk and sweeter than honey." — [Bukhari 6578](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Softening%20of%20Hearts)
- **108:3 — "Your enemy is the one cut off"** : *abtar* = without progeny — the surah refutes the taunt by giving the Prophet the *real* abundance that lasts

---

### 109. Al-Kāfirūn — The Disbelievers

**6 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 18**

The surah of religious boundaries — "To you your religion, to me mine."

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **109:1–6 — The declaration of separation**: "Say: O disbelievers! I worship not what you worship, nor do you worship what I worship. Nor will I worship what you worship, nor will you worship what I worship. To you your religion, and to me mine." — a definitive statement of religious pluralism (in practice) and exclusivism (in theology). The Prophet was told to recite this at times of compromise negotiations — refusing all syncretism

---

### 110. Al-Naṣr — The Victory / The Help

**3 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 114**

**The last surah revealed** — signaling the completion of the Prophet's mission.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **110:1–3 — The Farewell**: "When Allah's victory and the Conquest come, and you see people entering Allah's religion in throngs, then glorify the praise of your Lord and seek His forgiveness. Indeed, He is Oft-Returning." Ibn Abbas: this surah told the Prophet that his death was near. 'Umar said: after this, the Prophet recited *istighfār* (seeking forgiveness) constantly

---

### 111. Al-Masad / Tabbat — The Palm Fiber / Perish

**5 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 6**

The condemnation of Abu Lahab and his wife — the only contemporary of the Prophet named in the Qur'an.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **111:1–5 — Abu Lahab**: the Prophet's uncle who opposed him fiercely. "Perish the hands of Abu Lahab — his wealth and his earnings did not avail him." His wife carried firewood (thorns to throw in the Prophet's path) — she will have a rope of palm fiber around her neck. Despite being the only person cursed by name in the Qur'an, Abu Lahab was a relative — showing that the Qur'an does not spare family ties where truth is concerned

---

### 112. Al-Ikhlāṣ — The Sincerity / Purity of Faith

**4 verses · Meccan · Revelation order: 22**

The essence of tawhid in four lines — equivalent to one-third of the Qur'an in reward.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **112:1–4 — The four negations and affirmations**: (1) *Qul Huwa Allāhu Aḥad* — God is One, not multiple. (2) *Allāhu al-Ṣamad* — God is Eternal, Self-Sufficient, needing nothing. (3) *Lam yalid wa lam yūlad* — God does not beget nor is He begotten — rejects both Christian sonship and pagan genealogy of gods. (4) *Wa lam yakun lahu kufuwan aḥad* — nothing is comparable to Him. The Prophet said: "Whoever recites Qul Huwa Allahu Ahad ten times, Allah will build him a house in Paradise." — [Musnad Ahmad](scriptures/islam-hadith/08%20Musnad%20Ahmad.md)

**Virtue:** "It is equivalent to one-third of the Qur'an." — [Bukhari 5013](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Virtues%20of%20the%20Qur%27an)

---

### 113. Al-Falaq — The Daybreak

**5 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 20**

The first of the *Muʿawwidhatān* (two refuge surahs) — seeking protection from creation's evils.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **113:1–5 — The four protections**: (1) from the evil of what He has created, (2) from the darkness when it settles, (3) from those who blow on knots (sorcery), (4) from the envier when he envies. Revealed when a Jewish sorcerer (Lubayd ibn al-A'sam) cast a spell on the Prophet — the chapter of the Qur'an that broke the spell

---

### 114. Al-Nās — The People

**6 verses · Medinan · Revelation order: 21**

The last surah of the Qur'an — seeking refuge in God from the whisperer.

**Tafsir highlights:**
- **114:1–6 — The three refuges**: "Say: I seek refuge in the Lord of People, the King of People, the God of People — from the evil of the whispering retreating whisperer who whispers into the hearts of people — from among the jinn and mankind." — the symmetrical conclusion to the Qur'an. As the Qur'an began with seeking guidance in al-Fatihah, it ends with seeking protection in al-Nās. The three divine titles (Lord, King, God) match the three levels of relationship with humanity (sustenance, governance, worship). The final word of the Qur'an? *Al-nās* — humanity
