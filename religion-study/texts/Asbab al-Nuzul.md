# Asbāb al-Nuzūl — *Occasions of Revelation*

> A surah-by-surah reference for the historical circumstances (*asbāb al-nuzūl*) behind Qur'anic revelations. Based on authentic hadith from the [[Hadith Library]] and classical tafsir works. Part of [[Religion Study MOC]].

---

## Introduction

*Asbāb al-nuzūl* (أسباب النزول — "occasions of revelation") is the science of identifying the specific events, questions, or circumstances that prompted the revelation of Qur'anic verses. The principle is:

> 📜 **"The occasion reveals the meaning"** — knowing *why* a verse came down illuminates *what* it means.

### Key principles

1. **Not every verse has a known occasion** — only those with a specific trigger (*sabab*)
2. **The lesson is in the generality** — even verses revealed for a specific reason apply generally
3. **A single verse may have multiple narrations** — scholars weigh the most authentic chain (*isnād*)
4. **Sahih Bukhari and Muslim** are the primary sources; Tirmidhi, Nasa'i, and others supplement

### Primary sources for Asbab al-Nuzul in the Hadith Library

| Source | Strength |
|--------|----------|
| Bukhari's *Tafsir* book | Most authentic narrations (isnad-based) |
| Muslim's *Tafsir* book | Second only to Bukhari |
| Tirmidhi's *Tafsir* chapters | Includes grading and sometimes multiple views |
| Nasa'i's *Tafsir* book | 300+ narrations |
| Ibn Majah's *Tafsir* chapters | 200+ narrations |
| **Al-Wāḥidī** (classical work) | The classic *Asbāb al-Nuzūl* reference (not in vault) |

## The Occasions — Organized by Surah

### Surah 1: Al-Fātiḥah (The Opening)

| Verse | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 1:1–7 | Revealed as the opening of the Qur'an and prayer. The hadith of divided prayer: *Abu Huraira reported: The Prophet said: "Allah said: I have divided the prayer between Myself and My servant into two halves… When the servant says 'Praise be to Allah, Lord of the Worlds,' Allah says: 'My servant has praised Me.'"* | [Muslim 395](scriptures/islam-hadith/02%20Sahih%20Muslim.md#The%20Book%20of%20Prayers) |

### Surah 2: Al-Baqarah (The Cow)

| Verse | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 2:125 | **Umar's agreement**: Umar said: "My Lord agreed with me in three things. I said: O Messenger of Allah, I wish we took the Station of Abraham as a praying place. So revelation came: 'Take the Station of Abraham as a place of prayer.'" | [Bukhari 4028](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Prophetic%20Commentary%20on%20the%20Qur'an) |
| 2:144 | **Change of qibla**: The Prophet prayed toward Jerusalem for 16–17 months after arriving in Medina, longing to face the Kaaba. "We have seen the turning of your face toward the heaven — We shall turn you to a qibla that pleases you." | [Tirmidhi 3046](scriptures/islam-hadith/04%20Jami%60%20at-Tirmidhi.md#Chapters%20on%20Tafsir) |
| 2:158 | **Safa and Marwa**: The Ansar hesitated to perform *tawāf* between Safa and Marwa because they had revered the idol Manat there in pre-Islamic times. | [Bukhari 2146](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Prophetic%20Commentary%20on%20the%20Qur'an) |
| 2:187 | **The white and black thread**: A companion, 'Adi b. Hatim, placed black and white strings under his pillow trying to distinguish them. The Prophet explained: "It means the blackness of night and the whiteness of dawn." | [Bukhari 1916](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Prophetic%20Commentary%20on%20the%20Qur'an) |
| 2:217 | **Fighting in sacred months**: A Muslim raiding party fought during the sacred month of Rajab. The Quraysh used this as propaganda. The verse clarified the ruling. | [Muslim 1746](scriptures/islam-hadith/02%20Sahih%20Muslim.md#The%20Book%20of%20Faith) |
| 2:219 | **Wine and gambling**: Questioned about wine and gambling. The verse: "In them is great sin and some benefit for people, but their sin is greater than their benefit." | [Muslim 1746](scriptures/islam-hadith/02%20Sahih%20Muslim.md#The%20Book%20of%20Faith) |
| 2:275 | **Riba (usury)**: "Those who consume interest will not stand except as one struck by Satan's touch." — revealed concerning the Banu 'Amr b. 'Umayr and Banu al-Mughirah who practiced pre-Islamic usury. | [Bukhari 1003](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md) |

### Surah 3: Āl-i-'Imrān (The Family of Imran)

| Verse | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 3:118 | **Taking intimate friends from outside**: Revealed concerning the hypocrites who would secretly side with the polytheists against the Muslims. | [Bukhari 4586](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md) |
| 3:188 | **Boasting about unperformed deeds**: "Think not that those who rejoice in what they have done and love to be praised for what they have not done" — revealed about some People of the Book, or about some hypocrites. | [Bukhari 4578](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md) |

### Surah 4: Al-Nisā' (The Women)

| Verse | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 4:11–12 | **Inheritance verses**: Revealed after the battle of Uhud, when widows and orphans were left with unclear inheritance rights. | [Bukhari 4569](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md) |
| 4:19 | **Unwanted inheritance of women**: Pre-Islamic custom where a deceased man's male relative inherited his widow — could marry her or prevent her remarriage. The verse abolished this. | [Bukhari 4579](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md) |
| 4:88 | **Hypocrites and division**: "Why are you divided about the hypocrites?" — revealed when two groups among the companions differed about how to deal with the hypocrites who stayed behind from battle. | [Bukhari 4588](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md) |
| 4:176 | **Inheritance of kalālah (one with no direct heir)**: The verse was revealed in answer to a companion's question. | [Muslim 1617](scriptures/islam-hadith/02%20Sahih%20Muslim.md#The%20Book%20of%20Faith) |

### Surah 5: Al-Mā'idah (The Table Spread)

| Verse | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 5:3 | **Perfection of religion**: "This day I have perfected your religion for you." Revealed on Friday, the Day of 'Arafat during the Farewell Pilgrimage. | [Bukhari 4406](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Prophetic%20Commentary%20on%20the%20Qur'an) |
| 5:90 | **Prohibition of alcohol**: Wine, gambling, idols, and divining arrows are "abomination of Satan's handiwork." Revealed after the earlier verse (2:219) had already discouraged but not fully prohibited. 'Umar prayed: "O Allah, give us a clear ruling on wine." | [Tirmidhi 3049](scriptures/islam-hadith/04%20Jami%60%20at-Tirmidhi.md#Chapters%20on%20Tafsir) |

### Surah 8: Al-Anfāl (The Spoils of War)

| Surah context | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|---------------|------------------|---------------|
| 8:1 | **Dispute over spoils after Badr**: After the victory at Badr, the companions disputed over the distribution of spoils. The verse: "They ask you about the spoils — say: the spoils belong to Allah and the Messenger." The whole surah legislates war ethics. | [Tirmidhi 3081](scriptures/islam-hadith/04%20Jami%60%20at-Tirmidhi.md#Chapters%20on%20Tafsir) |
| 8:5–6 | **Dispute about Badr**: Some companions were reluctant to engage the Quraysh army. "As your Lord brought you out of your house with truth, a group of believers were averse to it." | [Tirmidhi 3081](scriptures/islam-hadith/04%20Jami%60%20at-Tirmidhi.md) |

### Surah 9: Al-Tawbah (The Repentance)

| Verse | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 9:40 | **The Cave**: "He was the second of two; when they were in the cave." The Hijrah: Abu Bakr's fear and the Prophet's reassurance during their three-day concealment from the Quraysh. | [Bukhari 3653](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Companions) |
| 9:113 | **Forgiveness for pagans**: The Prophet had said he would seek forgiveness for his uncle Abu Talib (who died as a polytheist). This verse forbade it. | [Bukhari 1364](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Funerals) |
| 9:117–118 | **The three who stayed behind**: Three companions (Ka'b b. Malik, Murarah b. al-Rabi', Hilal b. Umayyah) who failed to join the Tabuk expedition were excommunicated for 50 days before their repentance was accepted. | [Bukhari 4418](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Prophetic%20Commentary%20on%20the%20Qur'an) |

### Surah 18: Al-Kahf (The Cave)

| Verses | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|--------|------------------|---------------|
| 18:9–26 | **The People of the Cave asked about**: The Quraysh sent to the People of the Book for a question to test the Prophet — they asked about young men who fled persecution. Also asked about Dhul-Qarnayn. | [Tirmidhi 3140](scriptures/islam-hadith/04%20Jami%60%20at-Tirmidhi.md#Chapters%20on%20Tafsir) |

### Surah 24: Al-Nūr (The Light)

| Verse | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 24:2 | **The lashing punishment**: Revealed concerning the ruling for the unmarried adulterer. | [Muslim 1690](scriptures/islam-hadith/02%20Sahih%20Muslim.md#The%20Book%20of%20Faith) |
| 24:4 | **Accusation against chaste women**: Revealed concerning the punishment for false accusation of adultery. | [Muslim 1690](scriptures/islam-hadith/02%20Sahih%20Muslim.md#The%20Book%20of%20Faith) |
| 24:11–20 | **The Slander of 'A'ishah (Hadīth al-Ifk)**: The most famous asbab al-nuzul in the Qur'an. Hypocrites (led by 'Abdullah b. Ubayy) accused Aisha of adultery during the return from the Banu Mustaliq expedition. She was exonerated by divine revelation after a month of anguish. | [Bukhari 4141](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Prophetic%20Commentary%20on%20the%20Qur'an) — the longest single hadith in Bukhari |
| 24:58–59 | **Permission to enter rooms**: Rules about when children and servants may enter private rooms — revealed in connection with specific domestic incidents. | [Bukhari 4795](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md) |

### Surah 33: Al-Aḥzāb (The Confederates)

| Verse | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 33:4–5 | **Adoption abolished**: The case of Zayd b. Harithah. The Prophet had adopted Zayd as a son, but a marriage incident revealed that adoptive sons are not the same as biological sons. | [Bukhari 4782](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Prophetic%20Commentary%20on%20the%20Qur'an) |
| 33:37 | **Zayd's marriage to Zaynab**: Zayd b. Harithah was married to the Prophet's cousin Zaynab b. Jahsh. Their marriage was difficult; Zayd sought divorce. Despite knowing the marriage was decreed by God, the Prophet hesitated due to societal censure. The verse commanded him to proceed. | [Bukhari 4782](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Prophetic%20Commentary%20on%20the%20Qur'an) |
| 33:40 | **The Seal of the Prophets**: Finality of prophethood — "Muhammad is not the father of any of your men, but the Messenger of Allah and the Seal of the Prophets." | (Qur'anic proclamation, not triggered by a single event) |
| 33:59 | **The Hijab**: "O Prophet, tell your wives, daughters, and believing women to draw their cloaks around them" — revealed after the hypocrites harassed believing women in Medina, unable to distinguish them from slave women. | [Bukhari 4794](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Prophetic%20Commentary%20on%20the%20Qur'an) |

### Surah 48: Al-Fatḥ (The Victory)

| Verse | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 48:1 | **The Treaty of Hudaybiyyah**: "Indeed We have given you a clear victory." 'Umar and others were disappointed with the terms of the treaty; the verse declared it a victory. | [Bukhari 4832](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Prophetic%20Commentary%20on%20the%20Qur'an) |

### Surah 58: Al-Mujādilah (The Pleading Woman)

| Verse | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 58:1–4 | **The woman who pleaded**: Khawlah bint Tha'labah was divorced by her husband through *ẓihār* (a pre-Islamic formula: "You are to me like my mother's back"). She came to the Prophet pleading her case. While she argued, revelation descended confirming her complaint. | [Bukhari 1010](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Prophetic%20Commentary%20on%20the%20Qur'an) |

### Surah 60: Al-Mumtaḥanah (The Tested Woman)

| Verse | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 60:10–11 | **The women emigrants**: After the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah, believing women fled from Mecca to Medina. The treaty required returning emigrants. This verse exempted believing women from the repatriation clause. | [Bukhari 4891](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md) |

### Surah 66: Al-Taḥrīm (The Prohibition)

| Verse | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 66:1–5 | **The honey incident / Mariyah**: The Prophet forbade himself something permitted to please his wives. Two accounts: (a) he was with Mariyah (his Coptic wife), causing jealousy among his wives; (b) he drank honey at Zaynab's house, upsetting Aisha and Hafsa. The surah rebukes both him and his wives. | [Bukhari 4916](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Prophetic%20Commentary%20on%20the%20Qur'an) |

### Surah 80: 'Abasa (He Frowned)

| Verse | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 80:1–10 | **The blind man**: 'Abdullah b. Umm Maktūm, a blind companion, came to the Prophet seeking guidance while he was engaged in conversation with the Quraysh leaders (Utbah, Abu Jahl). The Prophet frowned and turned away. The verse gently rebuked him. | [Tirmidhi 3331](scriptures/islam-hadith/04%20Jami%60%20at-Tirmidhi.md#Chapters%20on%20Tafsir) |

### Surah 93: Al-Ḍuḥā (The Morning Light)

| Verse | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 93:1–3 | **Pause in revelation**: Revelation ceased for a period (the *fatrah al-waḥy*). The polytheists taunted: "Your Lord has forsaken you." The surah consoles: "Your Lord has not forsaken you, nor is He displeased." | [Bukhari 4916](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Prophetic%20Commentary%20on%20the%20Qur'an) |

### Surah 96: Al-'Alaq (The Clot)

| Verse | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 96:1–5 | **The first revelation**: The angel Jibril seized the Prophet and commanded him to read — three times — while he was meditating in the Cave of Hira. | [Bukhari 3](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Revelation) — the full narration from Aisha |

### Surah 97: Al-Qadr (The Power)

| Verse | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 97:1–5 | **Laylat al-Qadr**: The Night of Power — revealed to show the greatness of the night on which the Qur'an was first sent down. The Prophet was told its exact date later "forgotten" (by divine wisdom) to encourage seeking it in the last ten nights of Ramadan. | [Bukhari 2023](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Prayer%20at%20Night) |

### Surah 108: Al-Kawthar (The Abundance)

| Verse | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 108:1–3 | **The death of the Prophet's son**: When the Prophet's son (Qasim infant mortality) or all his male children died, the polytheists mocked him as "cut off" (*abtar* — without progeny). The surah replied: "Indeed your enemy is the one cut off." | [Bukhari 4907](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Prophetic%20Commentary%20on%20the%20Qur'an) |

### Surah 111: Al-Masad (The Palm Fiber)

| Verse | Sabab (Occasion) | Hadith Source |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| 111:1–5 | **Abu Lahab's rejection**: When the Prophet was commanded to warn his clan, Abu Lahab (his uncle) cried: "Perish you! Is this why you gathered us?" The surah condemns him and his wife. | [Bukhari 4907](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Prophetic%20Commentary%20on%20the%20Qur'an) |
