# Hadith Thematic Groups

A reference guide to the thematic groups across the nine canonical Hadith collections in the vault. Each group maps to the chapter (*kitāb*) headings found in the collections and links hadiths that share a common subject. Use this note to navigate the Hadith by theme and to find relevant narrations for study.

> [!note] How the groups were built
> The nine Hadith collections ([[scriptures/islam-hadith/Hadith Library|Hadith Library]]) are each organized into topical books (*kutub*, sing. *kitāb*). The chapter headings of [[01 Sahih al-Bukhari|Bukhārī]] (97 books), [[02 Sahih Muslim|Muslim]] (57 books), and [[04 Jami\` at-Tirmidhi|Tirmidhī]] (46 books) were cross-referenced to identify shared thematic clusters. The groups below consolidate these into navigable themes.

## How to Use These Groups

**To find hadiths by theme:** Browse the group headings above. Each group lists key chapters from the major collections and representative hadiths with their source and number.

**For study:** Read a group sequentially — for example, all the Prayer groups (3–7) show the comprehensive nature of prophetic guidance on worship.

**For cross-referencing notes:** When writing about a topic (e.g., "charity in Islam"), consult groups 10 (Zakat & Charity) and 18 (Manners & Character) for ethical and legal dimensions.

**To navigate the collections:** Each group links to the relevant *kutub* (books) within the [[scriptures/islam-hadith/Hadith Library|Hadith Library]]. Use the chapter headings to locate specific sections within each collection.

**For the Prophet's own figure study:** See [[Muhammad - A Figure Traced]] — which includes a detailed treatment of the Prophet in the Hadith alongside his Qur'anic portrayal.
