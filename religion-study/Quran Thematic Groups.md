# Quran Thematic Groups

A reference guide to the thematic groups used to cross-reference the 114 surahs. Each group links surahs that share a common subject, narrative pattern, or rhetorical strategy. Use this note to navigate the Quran by theme and to understand why particular surahs are connected.

> [!note] How the groups were built
> The subject and backstory fields of each surah were analysed and grouped by shared keywords, narrative patterns, and historical context. Most surahs belong to multiple groups, reflecting the Quran's layered composition.

---

## 1. Eschatology / Judgment Day

The "apocalyptic" surahs that describe the Day of Judgment in vivid, cosmic detail. Many of these are early Meccan surahs with short, rhythmic verses and powerful oaths.

| Surah | Contribution |
|-------|-------------|
| [[056 Al-Waaqia]] | The Inevitable — three classes of humanity (Foremost, Right-hand, Left-hand) |
| [[069 Al-Haaqqa]] | The Reality — the record in the right hand vs. the left hand |
| [[075 Al-Qiyaama]] | The Resurrection — the deathbed scene, the self-accusing soul |
| [[077 Al-Mursalaat]] | The Emissaries — 10-fold refrain "Woe unto the repudiators" |
| [[078 An-Naba]] | The Announcement — creation as proof of resurrection |
| [[081 At-Takwir]] | The Overthrowing — 11 cosmic signs cascading toward the end |
| [[082 Al-Infitaar]] | The Cleaving — "what deceived you about your Lord?" |
| [[084 Al-Inshiqaaq]] | The Splitting — the book in the right hand vs. behind the back |
| [[088 Al-Ghaashiya]] | The Overwhelming — diptych of Hell and Paradise |
| [[099 Az-Zalzala]] | The Earthquake — the atom's weight of good and evil |
| [[101 Al-Qaari'a]] | The Calamity — the scales, the Abyss |

**Core eschatology** (most intense): [[056 Al-Waaqia]], [[075 Al-Qiyaama]], [[081 At-Takwir]], [[101 Al-Qaari'a]]

## 2. Cosmic Collapse Imagery

A subset of the eschatology group — surahs that open with cascading images of the cosmos unravelling (the sun darkened, the stars falling, the sky split, the earth shaken). These share a distinct rhetorical pattern of rapid, vivid scenes.

- [[081 At-Takwir]] — sun coiled, stars fallen, seas boiled, souls paired
- [[082 Al-Infitaar]] — sky cleft, stars scattered, seas poured forth
- [[084 Al-Inshiqaaq]] — earth stretched, sky split, graves overturned
- [[099 Az-Zalzala]] — earth convulsed, burdens cast forth
- [[101 Al-Qaari'a]] — humanity like moths, mountains like wool

---

## 3. Scales & Record Books

Surahs that focus on the weighing of deeds and the books of record (*Sijjeen* and *'Illiyyun*) on Judgment Day.

- [[056 Al-Waaqia]] — the three classes divided by merit
- [[069 Al-Haaqqa]] — the book in the right hand vs. the left hand
- [[083 Al-Mutaffifin]] — *Sijjeen* (the prison-record) and *'Illiyyun* (the exalted record)
- [[084 Al-Inshiqaaq]] — easy reckoning vs. being given the book behind the back
- [[099 Az-Zalzala]] — the atom's weight
- [[101 Al-Qaari'a]] — heavy scales vs. light scales

---

## 4. Creation & God's Signs in Nature

Surahs that argue for God's power, unity, and wisdom by pointing to the natural world: the heavens, the earth, rain, cattle, bees, ants, the sun and moon, the stars, the mountains, the seas. These form the Quran's "natural theology."

Key surahs: [[006 Al-An'aam]], [[013 Ar-Ra'd]], [[016 An-Nahl]], [[022 Al-Hajj]], [[023 Al-Muminoon]], [[027 An-Naml]], [[029 Al-Ankaboot]], [[030 Ar-Room]], [[031 Luqman]], [[032 As-Sajda]], [[035 Faatir]], [[036 Yaseen]], [[041 Fussilat]], [[045 Al-Jaathiya]], [[050 Qaaf]], [[051 Adh-Dhaariyat]], [[055 Ar-Rahmaan]], [[067 Al-Mulk]], [[078 An-Naba]], [[079 An-Naazi'aat]], [[080 Abasa]], [[086 At-Taariq]], [[087 Al-A'laa]], [[088 Al-Ghaashiya]], [[091 Ash-Shams]]

**Highlights:**
- [[016 An-Nahl]] — the bee, rain, cattle, the sea as divine signs
- [[055 Ar-Rahmaan]] — 31-fold "Which of the favours of your Lord will ye deny?"
- [[067 Al-Mulk]] — the creation of death and life, the seven heavens
- [[091 Ash-Shams]] — the sun, the moon, the day, the night, the soul

## 5. Pure Monotheism (Tawhid)

Surahs focused on the oneness of God and the refutation of polytheism, shirk, and idolatry. These form the theological backbone of the Meccan period.

| Surah | Key Contribution |
|-------|-----------------|
| [[006 Al-An'aam]] | Abraham's argument against his father's idols; sustained monotheistic argument |
| [[016 An-Nahl]] | God alone creates and provides; false gods cannot |
| [[022 Al-Hajj]] | The Hajj as a monotheistic practice |
| [[025 Al-Furqaan]] | The Quran as the criterion between truth and falsehood |
| [[039 Az-Zumar]] | Pure devotion (*ikhlas*) belongs to God; mercy encompasses all |
| [[040 Ghafir]] | The believing man in Pharaoh's court argues for monotheism |
| [[041 Fussilat]] | The detailed signs of God in creation and revelation |
| [[109 Al-Kaafiroon]] | "To you your religion, to me mine" — definitive separation |
| [[112 Al-Ikhlaas]] | "Say: He is Allah, the One" — the essence of tawhid in four verses |

- [[026 Ash-Shu'araa]] — Abraham's argument against idolatry

- [[037 As-Saaffaat]] — the sacrifice of Abraham's son

- [[051 Adh-Dhaariyat]] — the honoured guests (angels) visit Abraham

## 14. Jesus & Mary

Surahs that discuss [[Jesus and Isa - A Figure Traced|Jesus]]'s birth, prophethood, miracles, and the Christian claims about his nature. Also those that honour [[Mary and Maryam - A Figure Traced|Mary]] as the mother of Jesus.

- [[003 Aal-i-Imraan]] — the family of Imran (Mary's lineage), Jesus's birth, the Trinity refuted
- [[004 An-Nisaa]] — Jesus's crucifixion and ascension
- [[005 Al-Maaida]] — the Table miracle, Jesus questioned about his followers
- [[019 Maryam]] — named after Mary, the most complete birth narrative
- [[021 Al-Anbiyaa]] — Jesus in the prophetic chain
- [[023 Al-Muminoon]] — Jesus and his mother as a sign
- [[043 Az-Zukhruf]] — Jesus as a servant of God, not a son
- [[061 As-Saff]] — Jesus's prophecy of Ahmad (the Praised One) to come

## 15. Solomon & David

Surahs that recount the stories of [[David and Dawud - A Figure Traced|David]] and [[Solomon and Sulayman - A Figure Traced|Solomon]]: David's judgment and repentance, Solomon's wisdom, the Queen of Sheba, and the jinn subject to Solomon.

- [[021 Al-Anbiyaa]] — David and Solomon in the prophetic chain

## 16. People of the Book / Interfaith

Surahs that directly address Jews and Christians, correcting theological claims and clarifying the Quran's relationship to earlier scriptures.

- [[002 Al-Baqara]] — the covenant with Israel, the change of qibla, Abraham as *hanif*
- [[003 Aal-i-Imraan]] — family of Imran, the Trinity refuted, Jesus's true nature
- [[004 An-Nisaa]] — the crucifixion clarified, People of the Book warned
- [[005 Al-Maaida]] — the Table, corruption of Torah and Gospel, the final covenant
- [[098 Al-Bayyina]] — the Clear Proof: the People of the Book divided after evidence came

## 17. Hypocrisy (Munafiqun)

Surahs that expose and condemn the hypocrites (*munafiqun*) of Medina — those who professed faith but secretly opposed the Prophet.

- [[002 Al-Baqara]] — the opening section on the three groups (believers, disbelievers, hypocrites)
- [[004 An-Nisaa]] — the hypocrites' double-speech and evasion
- [[008 Al-Anfaal]] — the hypocrites' fear and flight at Badr
- [[009 At-Tawba]] — the most detailed exposure of the hypocrites, the "mosque of dissent"
- [[029 Al-Ankaboot]] — trial separates the sincere from the hypocrite
- [[047 Muhammad]] — hypocrites' failure in battle
- [[048 Al-Fath]] — hypocrites who refused to pledge at Hudaybiyyah
- [[049 Al-Hujuraat]] — etiquette and sincerity, testing of faith
- [[057 Al-Hadid]] — the Day when the light of believers outshines the hypocrites
- [[058 Al-Mujaadila]] — secret counsels of the hypocrites
- [[059 Al-Hashr]] — hypocrites who secretly supported the Banu Nadir
- [[060 Al-Mumtahana]] — the test of the emigrant women reveals true faith
- [[063 Al-Munaafiqoon]] — named after the hypocrites, their false oaths exposed

## 18. Legal Legislation (Medinan Law)

The great Medinan surahs that establish Islamic law: worship, family, contracts, crime, war, and community governance.

- [[002 Al-Baqara]] — the largest legal collection (fasting, pilgrimage, marriage, divorce, loans, usury)
- [[004 An-Nisaa]] — women's rights, inheritance, marriage, orphans
- [[005 Al-Maaida]] — dietary laws, crime and punishment, contracts
- [[024 An-Noor]] — sexual ethics, the slander (Ifk), veiling, modesty
- [[033 Al-Ahzaab]] — the Prophet's household, hijab, divorce rules
- [[047 Muhammad]] — conduct of war and prisoners
- [[048 Al-Fath]] — treaty law and pledges
- [[049 Al-Hujuraat]] — social ethics (verifying news, no mockery, no spying)
- [[058 Al-Mujaadila]] — *zihar* (repudiation) and its expiation
- [[060 Al-Mumtahana]] — examination of emigrant women, treaties with non-Muslims
- [[065 At-Talaaq]] — divorce waiting period (*'iddah*) and provision
- [[066 At-Tahrim]] — oaths and their expiation, household matters

## 27. Night Prayer & Spirituality

Surahs that command or describe the night vigil (*tahajjud*) and the spiritual discipline of prayer.

- [[017 Al-Israa]] — the Night Journey, the five daily prayers established
- [[032 As-Sajda]] — the prostration of the faithful, who forsake their beds to pray
- [[073 Al-Muzzammil]] — the command to stand the night in prayer (the *tahajjud*)
- [[076 Al-Insaan]] — the righteous keep vows, fed the poor and pray

## 28. Mecca & Quraysh

Surahs that deal with the historical events surrounding the Kaaba and the Quraysh tribe — including the miraculous protection of Mecca in the Year of the Elephant.

- [[105 Al-Fil]] — the Year of the Elephant, Abraha's army destroyed by birds
- [[106 Quraish]] — the Quraysh's winter and summer caravans, gratitude to the Lord of the Kaaba
- [[108 Al-Kawthar]] — the Prophet's vindication against the Quraysh who taunted him
- [[110 An-Nasr]] — the conquest of Mecca, the mission complete

## 29. Al-Mu'awwidhatayn (The Protection Surahs)

The two closing surahs of the Quran, traditionally recited together for spiritual protection against evil, magic, and the whisperer.

- [[113 Al-Falaq]] — "Say: I seek refuge in the Lord of the Dawn" — from darkness, sorcery, envy
- [[114 An-Naas]] — "Say: I seek refuge in the Lord of Mankind" — from the whispering devil

**Often paired with:** [[112 Al-Ikhlaas]] (the three *Qul* surahs: 112, 113, 114)
**Also connected to:** [[001 Al-Faatiha]] (the essence of seeking guidance)

## 30. The Jinn

Surahs that mention the jinn — spirit-beings created from fire — and their relationship to revelation.

- [[046 Al-Ahqaf]] — a group of jinn heard the Quran and believed (v29-32)
- [[072 Al-Jinn]] — the only surah named after the jinn, entirely about their conversion

---

## 31. Hajj (Pilgrimage)

Surahs that discuss the rites and significance of the Hajj pilgrimage to Mecca.

- [[002 Al-Baqara]] — the rites of Hajj and *'umrah* (v158, v196-203)
- [[003 Aal-i-Imraan]] — the first House (Kaaba) established for humanity
- [[022 Al-Hajj]] — the only surah named after Hajj, the most detailed treatment of pilgrimage

---

## 32. Friday (Jumu'ah)

The only surah named after a day of the week, establishing the Friday congregational prayer.

- [[062 Al-Jumu'a]] — the call to Friday prayer, the parable of the donkey carrying books

## How to Use These Groups

**To find surahs by theme:** Browse the group headings above and follow the wikilinks to surahs that share that theme.

**For study:** Read a group sequentially — for example, all the eschatology surahs (group 1) show how the Quran's description of Judgment Day developed across the Meccan period.

**For cross-referencing notes:** When writing about a topic (e.g., "charity in Islam"), consult group 21 (Charity & Wealth Critique) to find all relevant surahs.

**To understand individual surahs:** Each surah's `related` field in its YAML frontmatter links to other surahs that share one or more of these thematic groups. The group above explains *why* they are connected.
