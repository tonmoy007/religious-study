## Additional Sikh Concepts

- **Nām Simran** — The practice of remembering God through repetition/meditation of the Divine Name (expanded in [[Sikh Scripture Thematic Groups]] group 2 and [[Sikh Figures MOC]]).
- **Haumai** — Ego, the root cause of separation from God (developed in [[Sikh Scripture Thematic Groups]] group 4 and across the Guru Granth Sāhib).
- **Hukam** — Divine will/order, the acceptance of God's cosmic plan (explored in [[Sikh Scripture Thematic Groups]] group 5 and the Japjī Sāhib).
- **Pañj Chor** — The Five Evils: lust, anger, greed, attachment, pride (systematised in [[Sikh Scripture Thematic Groups]] group 8).
- **Khaṇḍē dī Pāhul** — The Amrit initiation ceremony using the double-edged sword (detailed in [[The Creation of the Khalsa (Vaisākhī 1699)]]).
- **Pañj Kakke** — The Five Ks of the Khalsa identity (discussed in [[Sikh Scripture Thematic Groups]] group 18 and [[The Creation of the Khalsa (Vaisākhī 1699)]]).
- **Satsang** — The holy congregation; spiritual community (covered in [[Sikh Scripture Thematic Groups]] group 10).
- **Sevā** — Selfless service to others without expectation of reward (developed in [[Sikh Scripture Thematic Groups]] group 7).
