# The Birth of the Kuru Princes

> The founding of the Kuru dynasty — Shantanu and Ganga, Bhishma's terrible oath, and the divine births of the hundred Kaurava brothers and the five Pandavas.

---

## The Story (Mahabharata — Adi Parva)

### Shantanu and Ganga

King **Shantanu** of Hastinapura sees a beautiful woman by the Ganga — he asks her to marry him. She agrees on one condition: he must never question her actions. They have seven sons — each time, she drowns the newborn in the river. Shantanu says nothing.

The eighth son is born. He cannot contain himself: "Stop — this is monstrous!" The woman reveals herself as the **goddess Ganga** — the seven sons were the Vasus (celestial beings) cursed to be born as mortals; drowning them was liberation. The eighth son — **Devavrata** (later Bhishma) — will live a long life.

### Bhishma's Vow

Years later, Shantanu loves **Satyavati**, a fisherwoman. Her father demands: the son born to Satyavati must be king — not Devavrata. Shantanu is heartbroken.

Devavrata — the young prince — makes a **terrible oath**: "I renounce the throne forever, and I vow **celibacy for life** — so no child of mine can ever claim the throne." Shantanu grants him the boon of **death only when he wills it** (ichcha-mrityu). He is called **Bhishma** — "the Terrible."

### The Next Generation

Satyavati bears two sons — **Chitrangada** (slain in battle) and **Vichitravirya** (dies young, childless). The dynasty is at risk.

Satyavati summons her firstborn son — the sage **Vyasa** (born to her before her marriage) — to perform **Niyoga** (levirate) with the widows. From Ambika is born **Dhritarashtra** (blind). From Ambalika is born **Pandu** (pale-skinned).

### The Divine Births of the Pandavas

**Pandu** becomes king — but is cursed to die if he embraces a woman in love. He retires to the forest with his two wives, **Kunti** and **Madri**.

Kunti has a boon: she can call any god to father a child. She summons:
- **Dharma** (the god of justice) → **Yudhishthira**
- **Vayu** (the wind god) → **Bhima**
- **Indra** (king of the gods) → **Arjuna**

Madri has the twins:
- **Ashwini Kumaras** (the twin horsemen) → **Nakula** and **Sahadeva**

**Dhritarashtra** — blind — rules as regent. His wife **Gandhari** — who chose to blindfold herself for life in solidarity with her husband — gives birth to **100 sons** (the Kauravas) after two years in the womb, led by **Duryodhana**. The rivalry between cousins is seeded.

---

## Theological Significance

| Theme | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Bhishma's vow** | The ultimate sacrifice for a father's happiness — celibacy and renunciation |
| **Divine parentage** | The Pandavas are demigods — their abilities come from their celestial fathers |
| **Blind Dhritarashtra** | Both literal blindness and the blindness of a father's partiality |
| **Gandhari's sacrifice** | A wife's solidarity — choosing darkness with her husband |
