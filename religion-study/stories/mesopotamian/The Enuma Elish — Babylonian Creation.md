# The Enuma Elish — Babylonian Creation

### Historical Context
Composed during the reign of Nebuchadnezzar I (c. 1125–1104 BCE) or earlier in the Kassite period, the Enuma Elish is a theological-political document that elevates [[Marduk]], the patron god of Babylon, to the head of the Mesopotamian pantheon. The epic was recited annually during the Akitu (New Year) festival in Babylon. Its seven-tablet structure and 1,000+ lines make it the longest and most sophisticated Mesopotamian creation narrative.

## Summary
The Babylonian Creation Epic (*Enūma Eliš*, "When on High") recounts the rise of [[Marduk]] to kingship over the gods. Before creation, only the primordial waters [[Tiamat]] (salt) and Apsu (fresh) exist. They give birth to the first gods, whose noise disturbs Apsu. When Ea kills Apsu, [[Tiamat]] creates a monstrous army led by Kingu to avenge him. The terrified gods empower [[Marduk]] on condition he win supreme authority. He defeats [[Tiamat]], splits her body to form heaven and earth, establishes the celestial order, creates humanity from Kingu's blood, and is enthroned with the 50 names. The epic is the theological charter for Babylon's supremacy.

## The Story

### Act 1: Primordial Chaos and the First Gods (Tablet I, lines 1–20)
When heaven above and earth below were unnamed, only Apsu (fresh water) and [[Tiamat]] (salt water) mingled their waters. Within them emerged the first gods: Lahmu and Lahamu, then Anshar and Kishar, then [[Anu (An) — Sky God|Anu]], then [[Enki (Ea) — God of Wisdom|Ea]].

### Act 2: Apsu's Conspiracy and Death (Tablet I, lines 21–80)
The younger gods' din disturbs Apsu. He plots their destruction. [[Tiamat]] recoils, but Mummu supports Apsu. [[Enki (Ea) — God of Wisdom|Ea]] casts a sleep-spell, kills Apsu, and establishes his dwelling upon the abyss.

### Act 3: Tiamat's Vengeance (Tablet I–II)
[[Tiamat]] creates eleven monsters — horned serpents, dragons, scorpion-men, fish-men, lion-men — and sets Kingu as her general. She gives him the Tablet of Destinies. The gods are terror-stricken; none can face her.

### Act 4: Marduk's Investiture (Tablets II–III)
[[Marduk]], son of [[Enki (Ea) — God of Wisdom|Ea]], volunteers — but demands absolute authority. The gods feast, convene, and grant him kingship, proclaiming: "Your word shall be law."

### Act 5: The Cosmic Battle (Tablet IV)
[[Marduk]] fights [[Tiamat]] with bow, mace, lightning, and the four winds. He traps her in a net, drives the Evil Wind into her belly, shoots an arrow that splits her heart. He then splits her body like a flat fish — half becomes the sky, half the earth.

### Act 6: Creation of the Cosmos (Tablets IV–V)
[[Marduk]] establishes the constellations, the calendar, the moon's phases, and the boundaries of the waters. He sets the gods in their stations.

### Act 7: Creation of Humanity (Tablet VI)
[[Marduk]] creates humans from the blood of Kingu "to bear the burden of the gods." The [[Anunnaki]] build Babylon and Esagila, Marduk's temple.

### Act 8: The 50 Names of Marduk (Tablets VI–VII)
The gods proclaim fifty names of [[Marduk]], each establishing his power over every domain — from "Asaru" (cultivator) to "Nibiru" (the planet Jupiter, the star of Marduk).

## Key Themes
- Order from chaos through divine combat
- Theomorphic creation (humanity shaped from divine blood)
- The political theology of Babylon's supremacy
- Divine generational conflict and the transfer of power
- The power of naming and words
