# Birth of Jesus Across Scriptures

> Part of [[Cross-Scripture Connections MOC]]

Jesus (ʿĪsā) is one of the few figures whose **birth** is narrated in both the
Christian Gospels and the Qur'an. Both affirm a **virgin conception by divine
power** — then diverge sharply on who the child *is*. A **genealogical**
connection that marks the central Christian–Muslim theological fork.

## The Gospels: virgin birth, manger, "God with us"
From [[40 Matthew|Matthew]] and [[42 Luke|Luke]]:

> [!quote] Conception by the Holy Spirit
> **Matthew 1:18** — "…his mother Mary was espoused to Joseph, before they came together, she was found with child **of the Holy Ghost**."
> **Matthew 1:23** — "Behold, **a virgin shall be with child**… and they shall call his name **Emmanuel**, which being interpreted is, **God with us**."
> **Luke 1:35** — "The Holy Ghost shall come upon thee… therefore also that holy thing which shall be born of thee shall be called the **Son of God**."

> [!quote] The manger
> **Luke 2:7** — "And she brought forth her firstborn son, and wrapped him in **swaddling clothes, and laid him in a manger**; because there was no room for them in the inn."

## The Qur'an: the palm tree and the dates
From [[019 Maryam|Surah Maryam]] — Mary gives birth alone, sustained by a
miracle:

> [!quote] Maryam 19:23–26
> **19:23** — "And the pangs of childbirth drove her unto **the trunk of the palm-tree**. She said: Oh, would that I had died ere this…"
> **19:24** — "Then (one) cried unto her from below her, saying: Grieve not! Thy Lord hath placed **a rivulet beneath thee**,"
> **19:25** — "And **shake the trunk of the palm-tree** toward thee, thou wilt cause **ripe dates to fall** upon thee."

And the conception, by the same divine command found in [[Creation by Divine Word and Light]]:

> [!quote] Aal-i-Imraan 3:47
> "…How can I have a child when no mortal hath touched me? He said: …Allah createth what He will. If He decreeth a thing, He saith unto it only: **Be! and it is**."

## Side by side
| | Gospels | Qur'an (Maryam) |
|---|---|---|
| Conception | by the Holy Ghost | by God's command "Be!" |
| Virgin birth | **affirmed** | **affirmed** |
| Setting | a manger, in Bethlehem | alone, beneath a palm-tree |
| Miraculous provision | angels, shepherds, magi | the rivulet and ripe dates |
| Who is the child? | **Son of God, "God with us"** | a **prophet & Messiah, not divine** |

## The shared miracle, the divided meaning
Both scriptures insist the birth is **miraculous and the mother a virgin** —
remarkable agreement. But Matthew's "God with us" and Luke's "Son of God" are
precisely what Surah Maryam **denies** ("It befitteth not… that He should take
unto Himself a son," 19:35). The nativity is thus a perfect microcosm of the
whole Abrahamic relationship: **maximum shared narrative, maximum theological
divergence** (see [[Shared Abrahamic Figures]]).

## What kind of connection?
**Genealogical.** The Qur'an engages the Christian story directly, honoring Mary
(the only woman named in the Qur'an, with a surah bearing her name) while
recasting Jesus' identity.
