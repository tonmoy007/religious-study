# Cain and Abel

> The first murder in scripture — and the first illustration of how sin, once it enters the world, spreads through human relationships.

---

## The Story (Genesis 4:1–16)

Adam and Eve's first two sons: **Cain** (a farmer) and **Abel** (a shepherd). Both bring offerings to the Lord:

- **Abel** brings the firstborn of his flock — the fat portions. God looks on Abel's offering with favor.
- **Cain** brings the fruit of the ground. God does not look on it with favor.

God warns Cain: *"If you do well, will you not be accepted? And if you do not do well, sin is crouching at the door. Its desire is for you, but you must rule over it"* (4:7).

Cain lures Abel into the field and **kills him**. When God asks "Where is Abel your brother?", Cain replies: *"I do not know. Am I my brother's keeper?"*

God curses Cain: the ground will no longer yield for him, and he will be a **fugitive and wanderer**. Cain fears that anyone who finds him will kill him. God places a **mark on Cain** to protect him — promising sevenfold vengeance on anyone who kills him. Cain settles in the land of Nod, east of Eden.

---

## Theological Significance

| Theme | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **The spread of sin** | From disobedience (Adam) to murder (Cain) in one generation |
| **Acceptable worship** | Not the offering alone but the heart — Abel's faith (Heb 11:4) |
| **Brother's keeper** | The moral call to responsibility for others |
| **The mark of Cain** | Divine protection even for a murderer — God's mercy amidst judgment |

## Comparison with Qur'anic Account

| Element | Bible (Genesis 4) | Qur'an (5:27–32) |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Occupations** | Cain = farmer, Abel = shepherd | Implied but not specified |
| **The offering** | Fruit vs. fat portions | "Each offered a sacrifice" |
| **Why rejected** | Not explained — Cain's heart? | Only that one was accepted and one was not |
| **The murder** | Cain kills Abel in the field | No detail — Cain's soul prompts him |
| **The lesson** | God sends Cain into exile | **The raven** is sent — digs the ground to show Cain how to bury his brother |
| **Cain's fate** | Exile, protected by a mark | Remorse — not detailed |
| **The moral** | "Am I my brother's keeper?" | "Whoever kills a soul — it is as if he killed all of humanity" (5:32) |
