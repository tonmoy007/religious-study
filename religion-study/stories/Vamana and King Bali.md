# Vamana and King Bali

> The dwarf avatar of Vishnu — who asked for three paces of land and covered the universe in two — a story of pride, generosity, and the surrender of all possessions.

---

## The Story (Bhagavata Purana)

### Bali's Conquest

**Mahabali** — a powerful Asura king, grandson of Prahlada — conquers the three worlds. He is a just and generous ruler, beloved by his people. The devas, defeated, appeal to Vishnu.

### The Dwarf Brahmin

Vishnu incarnates as **Vamana** — a dwarf brahmin boy. He approaches Bali during a great sacrifice, where Bali has vowed to give any gift to any brahmin who asks.

Bali greets the small brahmin with respect. Vamana says: "O King, you are known for your generosity. I need only three paces of land — measured by my own foot."

The priest **Shukracharya** (guru of the Asuras) senses Vishnu's hand: "Do not give it — this is no ordinary brahmin." Bali refuses to break his vow: "Even if Vishnu himself asks — I will give."

### The Three Steps

Vamana grows to a **cosmic size**. His first step covers the **earth**. His second step covers the **sky**. For the third step — there is no more space.

Bali offers his own head: "Place your foot here."

Vamana's foot pushes Bali into the underworld (*Patala*) — but grants him rule there and the boon to return as the king of the coming age and to be present as Vishnu's doorkeeper.

---

## Theological Significance

| Theme | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Bali's generosity** | The Asura king who gave everything — even knowing the cost |
| **Vamana's deception** | The small request hides infinite demand — divine economy |
| **The third step** | The final inch that cannot be given — only surrendered |
| **Bali's reward** | Humility is rewarded — he is not destroyed but blessed |

---

## Comparison with Other Traditions

The motif of a deity asking for a small gift and then claiming everything resonates across traditions:
- **Jesus and the widow's mite** (Mark 12:41–44) — the smallest gift is the greatest
- **The merchant selling all for the pearl** (Matthew 13:45–46) — everything for the kingdom
- **Abraham's test** (Genesis 22) — giving what you love most
