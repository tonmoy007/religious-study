# The Ten Commandments

> The covenant at Sinai — God's law written on stone, forming the ethical foundation of Judaism, Christianity, and Western legal tradition.

---

## The Story (Exodus 19–20)

Three months after leaving Egypt, Israel camps at **Mount Sinai**. God descends on the mountain in fire, smoke, earthquake, and trumpet blast. The people are forbidden to touch the mountain on pain of death.

God speaks the **Ten Commandments** (*Decalogue*):

1. **No other gods** — "You shall have no other gods before me"
2. **No idols** — "You shall not make for yourself a carved image"
3. **No misuse of God's name**
4. **Keep the Sabbath** — "Remember the Sabbath day, to keep it holy"
5. **Honor your father and mother**
6. **Do not murder**
7. **Do not commit adultery**
8. **Do not steal**
9. **Do not bear false witness**
10. **Do not covet** — "You shall not covet your neighbor's house... wife... anything"

The people tremble and ask Moses to mediate — they are afraid to hear God speak directly. Moses enters the thick darkness where God is.

---

## The Two Tables

The commandments are written by the **finger of God** on **two tablets of stone**. Jewish tradition:
- **Tablet 1** (commandments 1–4): duties toward God
- **Tablet 2** (commandments 5–10): duties toward others

## Comparison with Islamic Law

| Commandment | Biblical Source | Islamic Parallel |
|-------------|----------------|------------------|
| No other gods | Exodus 20:3 | *Tawḥīd* — the first pillar: "There is no god but Allah" |
| No idols | Exodus 20:4–6 | *Shirk* — the unforgivable sin (Qur'an 4:48) |
| No misuse of God's name | Exodus 20:7 | Respect for God's names — 99 Names of Allah |
| Sabbath | Exodus 20:8–11 | **Friday** (*Jumu'ah*) as day of congregational prayer — but not a day of rest |
| Honor parents | Exodus 20:12 | One of the highest duties — "do not say 'uff' to them" (17:23) |
| Do not murder | Exodus 20:13 | "Whoever kills a soul — it is as if he killed all humanity" (5:32) |
| Do not commit adultery | Exodus 20:14 | Forbidden — *zinā* is a major sin |
| Do not steal | Exodus 20:15 | Theft punished by amputation (5:38) |
| Do not bear false witness | Exodus 20:16 | *Shahāda* — truthfulness is sacred |
| Do not covet | Exodus 20:17 | Covetousness is criticized — "Do not stretch your eyes" (15:88) |
