# The Exodus: Plagues and Parting

> The foundational story of Israel — deliverance from slavery through signs, wonders, and the greatest miracle of the Old Testament.

---

## The Story (Exodus 7–14)

### Background (Exodus 1–6)

A new Pharaoh who "did not know Joseph" enslaves the Israelites. He orders all Hebrew male infants killed. **Moses** is saved by his mother's basket in the Nile, adopted by Pharaoh's daughter, flees to Midian after killing an Egyptian, and encounters God at the **burning bush**. God reveals His name — **YHWH** — and sends Moses back to demand Israel's release.

### The Ten Plagues (Exodus 7–12)

Moses and Aaron confront Pharaoh. Each plague targets an Egyptian deity:

| Plague | Scripture | Egyptian Deity Challenged |
|--------|-----------|---------------------------|
| 1. Water to blood | 7:14–24 | Hapi (Nile god) |
| 2. Frogs | 8:1–15 | Heket (frog goddess) |
| 3. Gnats/lice | 8:16–19 | Geb (earth god) |
| 4. Flies | 8:20–32 | Khepri (scarab/creation) |
| 5. Livestock disease | 9:1–7 | Hathor (cow goddess) |
| 6. Boils | 9:8–12 | Sekhmet (plague goddess) |
| 7. Hail and fire | 9:13–35 | Nut (sky goddess) |
| 8. Locusts | 10:1–20 | Serapis (protector from locusts) |
| 9. Darkness | 10:21–29 | Ra (sun god) |
| 10. **Death of the firstborn** | 11:1–12:36 | Pharaoh himself (divine king) |

**Pharaoh's heart is hardened** — sometimes by God, sometimes by Pharaoh himself — and he refuses to let Israel go.

### The Passover (Exodus 12)

The tenth plague: every firstborn in Egypt dies. The Israelites are spared by marking their doorposts with **lamb's blood** — the "Passover" (*Pesach*). This becomes the foundational feast of Israel.

> [!quote] Exodus 12:13
> "When I see the blood, I will pass over you, and the plague shall not be upon you to destroy you."

### The Parting of the Sea (Exodus 13–14)

Pharaoh finally releases Israel. But he changes his mind and chases them with his army. Trapped between the sea and the Egyptian chariots, Moses stretches out his hand. God drives back the sea with a strong east wind — the waters divide. Israel crosses on **dry ground**. When the Egyptians follow, the waters collapse, drowning the entire army.

**Miriam's song** (Exodus 15): *"Sing to the Lord, for he has triumphed gloriously — the horse and his rider he has thrown into the sea!"*

## Comparison with Qur'anic Account

| Element | Bible | Qur'an |
|---------|-------|--------|
| **Pharaoh's heart** | God hardens it | Pharaoh's arrogance hardens it himself |
| **The plagues** | Ten detailed plagues | Five plagues mentioned in passing |
| **The magicians** | Copy Moses' signs, then concede: "This is the finger of God" | **Convert to Islam** — believe in the Lord of Moses, become martyrs |
| **Pharaoh's fate** | Drowned in the sea | Drowned — but his body is preserved as a sign (10:92) |
| **Aaron's role** | Moses' spokesman throughout | Active prophet alongside Moses |
| **Moses' infancy** | Basket in the Nile | Basket in the Nile — near-identical |
| **The burning bush** | "I AM WHO I AM" | "I am your Lord — take off your shoes" |

> [!tip] The most significant difference: the Qur'an's magicians are transformed into believers and martyrs. See [[The Magicians of Pharaoh — From Sorcery to Martyrdom]].
