# The Churning of the Ocean

> The gods and demons churn the cosmic ocean for the nectar of immortality — and bring forth treasures, poisons, and the goddess of fortune.

---

## The Story (Bhagavata Purana, Vishnu Purana)

### The Curse of Durvasa

The sage **Durvasa** gives a celestial garland to **Indra** — king of the gods. Indra's elephant grabs the garland and tramples it. Durvasa curses Indra: "You and all the devas shall lose your strength and immortality."

The enemies of the gods — the **Asuras** (anti-gods) — take advantage. The devas are defeated. They appeal to **Vishnu**.

### The Churning

Vishnu advises: "Churn the Ocean of Milk (*Kshira Sagara*) for **Amrita** — the nectar of immortality. The Asuras will help if you promise them a share. I will ensure the devas get it."

Mount **Mandara** becomes the churning rod. The serpent **Vasuki** becomes the rope — the devas hold his tail, the asuras his head (the poison scorches the asuras). Vishnu himself supports the mountain as **Kurma** (the Tortoise). The churning begins.

### What Emerged

| Treasure | Holder | Significance |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| **Halahala** (deadly poison) | Shiva drank it | The blue throat — Neelakantha |
| **Lakshmi** | Vishnu's consort | Goddess of fortune |
| **Dhanvantari** (with the Amrita pot) | The physician-god | Father of Ayurveda |
| **Apsaras** (celestial maidens) | Devas' court dancers | — |
| **Uchhaishravas** (the white horse) | Indra's steed | — |
| **Kaustubha** (the gem) | Vishnu's chest | — |
| **Parijata** (the wish-fulfilling tree) | Indra's garden | — |
| **Surabhi** (the wish-fulfilling cow) | — | — |
| **Chandra** (the moon) | Shiva's hair | — |

### The Trick

The Asuras seize the Amrita pot. Vishnu takes the form of **Mohini** — a beautiful enchantress. The Asuras, enchanted, agree to let her distribute the nectar.

She gives the nectar — only to the **devas**. The Asura **Rahu** disguises himself as a deva and drinks. The sun and moon expose him. Vishnu cuts off his head — but he has already drunk: his head is immortal (Rahu is the cause of eclipses).

### The Poison

Before the Amrita emerges, a cloud of poison arises — so toxic it threatens the universe. **Shiva** gathers it in his palm and drinks it. His throat turns blue — he is called **Neelakantha** (the blue-throated one). Parvati tightens her grip on his neck to stop the poison from spreading.

---

## Theological Significance

| Theme | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Cooperation of good and evil** | The devas and asuras must work together — but for opposite goals |
| **The poison** | Before immortality comes suffering — Shiva absorbs it |
| **Mohini's trick** | Divine illusion (*maya*) — the nectar of immortality is not won by force |
| **The many treasures** | Creation yields abundance — but the greatest is not material |
