# The Revelation of John

> The final book of the Bible — a vision of the cosmic battle between good and evil, the judgment of the world, and the promise of a new heaven and a new earth.

---

## The Vision (Book of Revelation)

Written by **John** (likely John the Apostle) while exiled on the island of **Patmos**. He receives a revelation of "what must soon take place."

### The Risen Christ (Chapters 1–3)

John sees the risen Christ — *"one like a son of man"* — among seven golden lampstands (the seven churches of Asia Minor). Christ dictates letters to each church: praise, correction, and promises.

### The Throne Room (Chapters 4–5)

John is taken up to heaven. He sees God on a throne — surrounded by 24 elders and four living creatures (lion, calf, man, eagle). A scroll sealed with seven seals — no one is worthy to open it. Then the **Lamb** (Jesus Christ, slain but standing) appears — worthy to open the seals.

### The Seven Seals, Trumpets, and Bowls (Chapters 6–16)

A cycle of three sevenfold judgments:

**Seven Seals:** War, famine, death, martyrs, earthquake — the **seventh seal** releases the seven trumpets.

**Seven Trumpets:** Hail and fire, the sea turned to blood, the star Wormwood, the Abyss opened — demon locusts, an army of 200 million, the **third** of the world destroyed.

**Seven Bowls of God's Wrath:** Sores on the beast-worshippers, sea turned to blood, rivers to blood, scorching heat, darkness, the Euphrates dried up (for the battle of Armageddon), and the final earthquake — Babylon the great falls.

### The Final Victory (Chapters 17–20)

- **Babylon** (the corrupt world system) falls
- The **Beast** and the **False Prophet** are thrown into the lake of fire
- **Satan** is bound for 1,000 years — the Millennium — then released, finally defeated
- The **Great White Throne Judgment**: the dead are judged according to their works

### The New Creation (Chapters 21–22)

> *"Then I saw a new heaven and a new earth — for the first heaven and the first earth had passed away"* (21:1).

The **New Jerusalem** descends from heaven — a city of gold, gates of pearl, foundations of precious stones. No night, no sun — the glory of God is its light. The Tree of Life is there — its leaves for the healing of the nations. The river of the water of life flows from the throne.

> *"He who testifies to these things says: Surely I am coming soon. Amen. Come, Lord Jesus!"* (22:20)

## Theological Significance

| Theme | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Christus Victor** | Christ wins — the Lamb conquers through sacrifice |
| **The problem of evil** | Evil is real, cosmic, and defeated |
| **Judgment and hope** | The world is judged — but a new creation follows |
| **The patience of the saints** | "Here is a call for the endurance of the saints" |
| **Apocalyptic genre** | Symbolic, not literal — visions of what *is* and what *will be* |

## Comparison with Islamic Eschatology

| Theme | Revelation | Islamic Tradition |
|-------|------------|-------------------|
| **The Antichrist** | The Beast / False Prophet | **Al-Dajjal** (the Deceiver) |
| **The final battle** | Armageddon | War with Gog and Magog (*Yājūj wa Mājūj*) |
| **Christ's return** | Jesus returns as conquering king | **Isa** returns, breaks the cross, defeats the Dajjal |
| **Resurrection** | General resurrection and judgment | *Yawm al-Qiyāmah* — the Day of Standing |
| **Judgment** | Books opened, judged by works | Scales (*Mīzān*), books, the Sirāt bridge |
| **Paradise** | New Jerusalem — Tree of Life, river | *Jannah* — gardens, rivers, houris |
| **Hell** | Lake of fire | *Jahannam* — seven levels of fire |
| **The saved** | Those written in the Lamb's Book of Life | Those whose good deeds outweigh their bad |
