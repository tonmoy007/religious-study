# The Passion and Resurrection

> The central event of Christianity — the death and resurrection of Jesus Christ, told across all four Gospels with a unity of core and diversity of detail.

---

## The Story (Gospels — Matthew, Mark, Luke, John)

### The Last Supper

Jesus shares a Passover meal with his twelve disciples. He **washes their feet** — a model of servant leadership. He institutes the **Eucharist**: *"This is my body — this is my blood of the covenant, poured out for many for the forgiveness of sins"* (Matthew 26:26–28). He predicts Judas's betrayal and Peter's denial.

### Gethsemane

In the Garden of Gethsemane, Jesus prays in agony: *"Father — if you are willing, remove this cup from me. Nevertheless, not my will, but yours, be done"* (Luke 22:42). His sweat becomes like drops of blood. Judas betrays him with a kiss. The disciples flee.

### The Trial

Jesus is arrested and tried:
- **Before the Sanhedrin** (Jewish council) — accused of blasphemy for claiming to be the Son of God
- **Before Pilate** (Roman governor) — accused of sedition, claiming to be king
- **Before Herod Antipas** — who mocks him and sends him back

Pilate finds no fault in him — but bows to the crowd: *"Crucify him!"* He washes his hands. Barabbas is released. Jesus is flogged, crowned with thorns, and mocked.

### The Crucifixion

Jesus carries his cross to **Golgotha** (the Place of the Skull). He is crucified between two thieves. From the cross:

> *"Father, forgive them — for they know not what they do."* (Luke 23:34)  
> *"Today you will be with me in Paradise."* (Luke 23:43, to the repentant thief)  
> *"My God, my God — why have you forsaken me?"* (Mark 15:34, quoting Psalm 22)

Darkness covers the land from noon to 3 PM. The temple curtain is torn in two. Jesus cries out: *"It is finished"* (John 19:30) — and gives up his spirit.

### The Resurrection

On the **third day** — Sunday morning — the women (Mary Magdalene, Mary the mother of James, Salome) find the **stone rolled away**. The tomb is empty. Angels announce: *"He is not here — he is risen!"* (Mark 16:6).

Jesus appears to:
- Mary Magdalene (John 20:11–18)
- Two disciples on the road to Emmaus (Luke 24:13–35)
- The disciples in the upper room — showing his wounds (John 20:19–29)
- **Thomas** — who doubted: *"My Lord and my God!"* (John 20:28)
- Over 500 people at once (1 Corinthians 15:3–8)

After 40 days, he ascends into heaven.

## Comparison with Islamic Tradition

| Element | Gospels | Qur'an |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **The crucifixion** | Jesus was crucified, died, and buried | **"They did not kill him, nor did they crucify him — but it was made to appear so to them"** (4:157) |
| **The substitution** | No substitute — Jesus himself died | Someone was made to resemble Jesus — tradition identifies **Judas** or **Simon of Cyrene** |
| **The resurrection** | Bodily resurrection on the third day | **God raised him up** to Himself — no bodily resurrection (4:158) |
| **Salvation** | Christ's death atones for sin | Each soul bears its own burden — no substitutionary atonement |
| **Jesus' return** | Ascended — will return at the end | **Yes** — Isa returns to defeat the Dajjal (Antichrist) |

> [!tip] This is the **single greatest theological divergence** between Christianity and Islam. The Qur'an explicitly denies the crucifixion — making the two traditions irreconcilable on this point. See [[Jesus and Isa - A Figure Traced]] for depth.
