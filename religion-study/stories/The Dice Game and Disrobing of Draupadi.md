# The Dice Game and Disrobing of Draupadi

> The most dramatic scene in the Mahabharata — a king's gambling addiction, a queen's public humiliation, and a divine intervention that saved her honor. The war begins here.

---

## The Story (Mahabharata — Sabha Parva)

### The Invitation

**Shakuni** — Duryodhana's maternal uncle, a master of loaded dice — arranges for the Pandavas to be invited to a gambling match. Yudhishthira, a king, cannot refuse a challenge. Duryodhana and Shakuni have prepared the dice.

### The Game

Yudhishthira plays — and loses, one throw after another. He stakes:
- His jewels — loses
- His treasury — loses
- His kingdom — loses
- His brothers — loses
- Himself — loses
- **Draupadi** — loses

Shakuni laughs: "He has staked everything — and lost."

### The Summoning

Duryodhana orders the messenger to bring Draupadi into the court. Draupadi — who is menstruating, in a single garment — refuses. The messenger reports. Duryodhana sends **Duhshasana** (his brother) — who drags Draupadi into the assembly by her hair.

In the open court — elders watching — Duhshasana tries to **strip her**. She cries to Krishna — and a miracle occurs: every time her garment is pulled, another appears. The pile of cloth in the assembly hall grows. Duhshasana tires. Draupadi remains clothed.

### The Oath

> "I will not bind my hair until it is washed in the blood of Duhshasana."

Draupadi stands in the court, hair unbound, and vows: "The Kauravas will die for this." Bhima swears: "I will drink Dushasana's blood and smash Duryodhana's thigh."

**Bhishma** sits silent. **Dronacharya** sits silent. **Karna** taunts: "A woman with five husbands is a whore."

### The Curse and the Exile

Dhritarashtra — fearing catastrophe — restores everything: "Return to your kingdom." But Duryodhana calls them back for one more throw. Yudhishthira plays again — loses again.

The Pandavas are exiled for **13 years** — 12 in the forest, 1 in disguise, unknown.

---

## Theological Significance

| Theme | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Draupadi's violated honor** | The humiliation of a woman in open court — the crime that justifies the war |
| **The silent elders** | Bhishma, Drona, and Vidura who do not intervene — a moral failure |
| **Krishna's grace** | The miracle of the endless sari — the divine protects the faithful |
| **Yudhishthira's flaw** | Gambling — the king's weakness that destroyed everything |
