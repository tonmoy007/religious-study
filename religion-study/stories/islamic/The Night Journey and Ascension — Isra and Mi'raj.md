# The Night Journey and Ascension — Isra and Mi'raj

> Surah [[017 Al-Israa]] · Verse 1 · Surah [[053 An-Najm]] · Verses 1–18 · Meccan · c. 620 CE (Year of Sorrow)

One of the most profound events in the Prophet's life — a single night in which he was transported from Mecca to Jerusalem, then ascended through the seven heavens to the presence of God, received the command of the five daily prayers, and returned. This event separates the Meccan period into before and after, and was the greatest test of faith for the early Muslims.

---

## The Qur'anic Account

> *"Exalted is He who took His servant by night from the Inviolable Mosque to the Farthest Mosque (al-Aqsa), whose precincts We have blessed, to show him some of Our signs. He is the All-Hearing, the All-Seeing."* — **17:1**

The Qur'an itself is brief about the Night Journey. The second reference comes in Surah An-Najm:

> *"And he saw him in another descent — at the Sidrat al-Muntahā (the Lote Tree of the Utmost Boundary), near which is the Garden of Refuge — when there covered the Lote Tree what covered it. His sight did not swerve, nor did it transgress. He saw the greatest signs of his Lord."* — **53:13–18**

---

## The Hadith Account (Full Narrative)

### The Journey Begins (Isra)

The Prophet (ﷺ) was sleeping near the Kaaba when Jibril (Gabriel) came to him. He opened the Prophet's chest, removed his heart, and washed it with Zamzam water — filling it with faith and wisdom — then returned it. (This was the third such purification, the first being as a child.)

Jibril brought **Buraq** — a white, winged creature, smaller than a mule but larger than a donkey, whose stride carried it as far as the eye could see. The Prophet mounted Buraq and they set off toward Jerusalem.

They stopped in **Medina** (Yathrib): Jibril told the Prophet to pray there — this would be his place of hijrah.

They arrived at the **Temple Mount** in Jerusalem. There, the Prophet found all the prophets gathered: **Abraham, Moses, Jesus**, and others. He led them in prayer — Muhammad leading the entire prophetic chain — at the site of the Al-Aqsa Mosque.

### The Ascension (Mi'raj)

The Prophet was then taken upward from Jerusalem, through the seven heavens:

| Heaven | Prophet Met | Notable |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| **First** | Adam | The souls of the righteous and the wicked on his right and left |
| **Second** | John (Yahya) and Jesus (Isa) | Cousins, both born miraculous |
| **Third** | Joseph (Yusuf) | Half of all beauty given to him |
| **Fourth** | Enoch/Idris | "Raised to a high station" |
| **Fifth** | Aaron (Harun) | Beloved by his people |
| **Sixth** | Moses (Musa) | Sorrowful that his people was not as great |
| **Seventh** | Abraham (Ibrahim) | Leaning against the *Bayt al-Ma'mur* (the celestial Kaaba) |

At the Lote Tree of the Utmost Boundary (*Sidrat al-Muntahā*), the Prophet witnessed:
- The tree covered with colours beyond description
- Four rivers flowing from its roots — two visible (the Nile and the Euphrates) and two hidden (in Paradise)
- The *Bayt al-Ma'mur* (the heavenly Kaaba, visited by 70,000 angels daily)

### The Gift of Prayer

Then the Prophet was brought into the presence of God — within the veil of divine Majesty. He received the command:

> *"Fifty prayers per day."*

As he descended, he met Moses, who asked:

> *"What did your Lord command?"*

Fifty prayers. Moses said: "Your community cannot bear fifty prayers. I tested the Children of Israel and I know people. Go back and ask for a reduction."

The Prophet returned — five times — until the command was reduced to **five prayers per day**, with the reward of fifty.

Moses still urged further reduction, but the Prophet was ashamed to ask again.

### The Return

Jibril and the Prophet returned to Mecca. The Prophet's bed was still warm. The journey had taken a single night.

---

## The Controversy

When the Prophet announced the journey the next morning, the Quraysh mocked him:

> *"This is impossible! A caravan takes a month to reach Syria and a month to return — and you claim you went to Jerusalem and back in one night?"*

Even some Muslims wavered. **[[Abu Bakr al-Siddiq|Abu Bakr]]** was told: "Your companion claims he went to Jerusalem last night." He replied:

> *"If he said so, then it is true. I believe him about what is even more astonishing — the news from heaven."*

For this, [[Abu Bakr al-Siddiq|Abu Bakr]] earned the title **al-Ṣiddīq** (the Truthful Confirmer).

The Quraysh questioned the Prophet: "Describe Jerusalem for us." The Prophet had never been there. But Allah showed it to him, and he described it perfectly — down to the door latches and the number of pillars. Those who had seen Jerusalem were astounded.

## Key Tafsir Insights

**Body and soul or soul only?** 
- **Aisha** said: "The Prophet's body remained where it was, and only his soul travelled" — but this is a minority view
- **Ibn Abbas** and the majority: the journey was physical, with the body awake
- The Qur'an says "took His servant by night" — *ʿabduhu* (His servant) refers to the whole person

**Why Jerusalem?** The journey linked the three sacred cities: Mecca (Kaaba), Jerusalem (Al-Aqsa), and the Heavenly House (Bayt al-Ma'mur). Jerusalem was the site of all previous prophets — Muhammad's journey there and leading them in prayer symbolized his succession to the entire prophetic tradition.

**The Sidrat al-Muntahā:** The Lote Tree marks the boundary of created knowledge — beyond it lies the divine presence that no angel or prophet can cross.

**Why five prayers?** The reduction through Moses' intercession shows God's mercy: 50 was the full reward, 5 was the manageable obligation. The hadith says "the five are fifty" — the reward remains fifty-fold.

**The significance:** 
- The Isra and Mi'raj was the last major event before the Hijrah
- It strengthened the Prophet after the **Year of Sorrow** (Khadijah's and Abu Talib's deaths, the Ta'if rejection)
- It established Jerusalem as the third holiest city in Islam
- It confirmed the five daily prayers as a direct gift from God
