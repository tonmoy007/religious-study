# Sulayman and Bilqis — The Queen of Sheba

> Surah [[027 An-Naml]] · Verses 15–44 · Meccan

The most vivid and detailed story of Prophet Solomon (Sulayman) in the Qur'an — a complete narrative of the queen who came to power, the hoopoe's report, the miraculous throne, and the queen's submission. It is the only place in the Qur'an where a female ruler is treated at length.

---

## The Story

### Solomon's Gift

> *"And We gave David and Solomon knowledge, and they said: Praise be to Allah who has favored us over many of His believing servants."* — **27:15**

[[Solomon and Sulayman - A Figure Traced|Solomon]] inherited from David not just kingship but a unique gift from Allah: he understood the language of birds, and the wind was subject to him. He commanded the jinn, the humans, and the birds in a vast kingdom.

### The Missing Hoopoe

One day, Solomon assembled his armies of jinn, humans, and birds. He noticed that the **hoopoe** (*hudhud*) was absent — and he was not pleased.

> *"I will surely punish him with a severe punishment, or slaughter him, unless he brings me a clear reason."* — **27:21**

The hoopoe arrived shortly after and brought extraordinary news:

> *"I have learned something you have not learned. I come to you from Sheba with certain news. I found a woman ruling over them — she has been given of everything, and she has a great throne. I found her and her people prostrating to the sun instead of Allah. Satan has made their deeds seem fair to them and turned them from the path."* — **27:22–24**

### The Letter

Solomon wrote a letter and sent it with the hoopoe:

> *"Go with this letter of mine and deliver it to them — then withdraw from them and see what they reply."* — **27:28**

The letter read: *"In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful. Do not be arrogant toward me, and come to me in submission."*

### The Queen's Council

The Queen ([[Bilqis — Queen of Sheba|Bilqis]], as she is named in Islamic tradition) read the letter and called her council:

> *"O chiefs, an honorable letter has been delivered to me. It is from Solomon, and it begins: In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful. It says: Do not be arrogant toward me and come to me in submission."* — **27:29–31**

She asked for their counsel — the Qur'an's only depiction of a female leader consulting her advisors:

> *"O chiefs, give me your opinion in this matter — I never decide a matter until you are present."* — **27:32**

They offered military support, but she wisely chose diplomacy:

> *"Indeed, when kings enter a land, they ruin it and humiliate its elite. Such is what they do. So I will send them a gift and see what the envoys bring back."* — **27:34–35**

### The Gift Rejected

Solomon received the queen's gift of gold and treasure. He was unmoved:

> *"Do you provide me with wealth? What Allah has given me is better than what He has given you. Rather, you rejoice in your gift. Return to them — we will surely come to them with armies they cannot withstand, and we will drive them out humiliated."* — **27:36–37**

### The Throne Transported

Solomon then commanded the jinn:

> *"O jinn, which of you will bring me her throne before they come to me in submission?"*

A powerful *'ifrit* (a strong jinn) said:

> *"I will bring it to you before you rise from your seat — I am strong and trustworthy."* — **27:39**

But a man who had *"knowledge of the Book"* said:

> *"I will bring it to you in the blink of an eye."*

And it was done. Solomon saw the throne before him:

> *"This is from the bounty of my Lord, to test me — will I be grateful or ungrateful?"* — **27:40**

### The Test of the Queen

Solomon ordered his court to disguise the throne:
> *"Disguise her throne for her — we will see whether she is guided or among those who are not guided."* — **27:41**

When the Queen arrived at Solomon's palace, she was asked: "Is your throne like this?"

She replied:

> *"It is as though it were the very same."* — **27:42**

Solomon then showed her the truth. She had been worshipping the sun, but he called her to the worship of Allah alone.

### The Palace of Glass

Solomon built a palace with a floor of transparent glass over flowing water. When the Queen entered, she thought it was deep water and **lifted her skirt**, exposing her legs.

> *"She was told: It is a palace paved with crystal."* — **27:44**

At that moment, she declared:

> *"My Lord, I have wronged myself, and I submit with Solomon to Allah, the Lord of the worlds."* — **27:44**

She accepted monotheism.

## Key Tafsir Insights

**Who was Bilqis?** The name *Bilqis* is not in the Qur'an but is given in Islamic tradition. She was the Queen of Sheba (Saba'), a kingdom in modern Yemen. Her father was a Himyarite king. The kingdom was famous for the Ma'rib Dam.

**The hoopoe (hudhud):** In Islamic tradition, the hoopoe can understand water underground — useful for finding water sources in the desert. Its crest is said to resemble a crown, and it was considered a wise bird.

**The man with knowledge of the Book:** This is a mysterious figure. Interpretations:
- **Asaf ibn Barkhiya** (Sulayman's vizier) — the most common identification
- Al-Khidr
- Sulayman himself
- The angel Jibril
- The name "the one with knowledge of the Book" suggests someone who knew Allah's Greatest Name (*ism Allāh al-aʿẓam*), by which prayers are instantly answered

**The Queen's conversion:** Her declaration uses *aslamtu* (I submit) — the verb from which *Islam* derives. She submitted not just to Solomon's authority but to God.

**The glass floor:** The palace of crystal/glass (*ṣarḥun mumarradun min qawārīr*) was a test of the queen's understanding of reality — mistaking the reflection for water, she exposed what she would normally hide. Symbolically, the truth cannot be disguised.
