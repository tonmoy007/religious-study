# The Sacrifice of Abraham — The Great Ram

> Surah [[037 As-Saaffaat]] · Verses 100–111 · Meccan

[[Abraham and Ibrahim - A Figure Traced|Abraham]]'s greatest test — commanded to sacrifice his beloved son, he submitted without question. At the moment of sacrifice, Allah ransomed the boy with a ram from Paradise. This story is commemorated annually by Muslims worldwide during **Eid al-Adha** (the Festival of Sacrifice), the most important holiday after Ramadan.

---

## The Story

> *"He said: Truly I am going to my Lord — He will guide me. My Lord, grant me a righteous son."* — **37:99–100**

Abraham — after surviving the fire — prayed for a son. Allah gave him good news of a gentle, forbearing boy.

> *"And when he reached the age of work with him, he said: O my son, I see in a dream that I must sacrifice you. So see — what do you think?"* — **37:102**

In the Qur'an, Abraham does not command — he consults. He asks his son's opinion.

The son replied:

> *"O my father, do as you are commanded. You will find me, if Allah wills, among the patient."* — **37:102**

### The Submission

Both father and son submitted to God's will. Abraham laid his son down — forehead to the ground.

> *"When they both submitted and he put him down on his forehead — We called out to him: O Abraham, you have fulfilled the vision."* — **37:103–105**

At the moment of the knife, Allah stopped him.

> *"This is indeed a clear trial. And We ransomed him with a great sacrifice."* — **37:106–107**

A ram was sent from Paradise — the ram of Habil (Abel), the same one that had been sacrificed by Adam's son. Abraham sacrificed the ram instead.

## Key Tafsir Insights

**Which son?** This is one of the most famous exegetical debates:

| View | Proponents | Reasoning |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **Ishmael** | Majority of early Muslims (Ibn Abbas, Qatada, Sa'id ibn Jubayr) | The Qur'an speaks of the son before Isaac is mentioned (37:99–101 → 37:112). The son was the "only son" for years before Isaac's birth |
| **Isaac** | Some later authorities (Tabari lists both views) | The biblical version, and the phrase "We gave him glad tidings of Isaac after him" (37:112) could mean the sacrifice son *became* Isaac |

The modern Sunni consensus: **Ishmael** (Isma'il).

**Why was the son not named?** The Qur'an deliberately omits the name. The focus is on the *act* of submission, not the identity of the son. Every believing father is to see himself in Abraham; every believing son is to see himself in the boy.

**The dream as revelation:** Abraham received the command in a dream — the dream of prophets is revelation. The story establishes that prophetic dreams carry divine authority.

**"A clear trial" (*al-balā' al-mubīn*):** This is described as the *greatest* of Abraham's tests. He endured the fire, exile, and rejection by his father — but the sacrifice of his own son was the ultimate test. The fact that he passed is why he is called *Khalīl Allāh* (the Friend of God).

**The ram:** Described as *ʿaẓīm* (great). Hadith says it was the ram of Habil, which had grazed in Paradise for thousands of years — fattened for this moment.

**Eid al-Adha:** The annual commemoration — Muslims slaughter a sheep, goat, or cow, distribute a third to the poor, a third to neighbours, and keep a third. The ritual echoes Abraham's submission.
