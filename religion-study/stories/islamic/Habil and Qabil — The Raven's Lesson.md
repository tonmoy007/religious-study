# Habil and Qabil — The Raven's Lesson

> Surah [[005 Al-Maaida]] · Verses 27–32 · Medinan · Revelation order: 112

The Qur'anic version of the Cain and Abel story — the first murder in human history, but told with a unique Qur'anic detail: **the raven**. The raven taught Cain how to bury his brother, a detail absent from the biblical account. More importantly, the Qur'an uses the story to establish the most far-reaching principle in criminal law: that killing one innocent soul is like killing all of humanity.

---

## The Story

### The Offering

> *"Recite to them the story of the two sons of Adam in truth — when they offered an offering, and it was accepted from one of them and not accepted from the other."* — **5:27**

[[Habil and Qabil|Habil]] (Abel) was a shepherd; [[Habil and Qabil|Qabil]] (Cain) was a farmer. Each offered a sacrifice to Allah. Habil offered his best sheep; Qabil offered poor crops. The fire from heaven descended and consumed Habil's offering, but left Qabil's untouched.

Qabil was enraged — his brother was chosen over him.

> *"He (Qabil) said: I will surely kill you."* — **5:27**

Habil's response is one of the most beautiful speeches in the Qur'an:

> *"Allah only accepts from the God-fearing. If you stretch your hand against me to kill me, I will not stretch my hand against you to kill you — indeed, I fear Allah, the Lord of the worlds. I want you to bear my sin and your sin, and become one of the companions of the Fire. That is the reward of the wrongdoers."* — **5:28–29**

### The First Murder

> *"His soul prompted him to kill his brother — so he killed him and became among the losers."* — **5:30**

Qabil killed Habil. But he had never seen a death before. He stood over his brother's body — it was the first human corpse in history — and did not know what to do.

### The Raven

> *"Then Allah sent a raven scratching the ground, to show him how to bury his brother's corpse."* — **5:31**

A raven — the same species traditionally associated with death — was sent by Allah. Another raven had died, and the living raven dug a hole and buried its companion in the earth. Qabil watched, understood, and buried his brother.

> *"He said: Woe to me — am I unable to be like this raven and bury the corpse of my brother? And he became among the regretful."* — **5:31**

### The Divine Law

Then the Qur'an establishes the most powerful principle of justice in scripture:

> *"Because of that, We decreed upon the Children of Israel that whoever kills a soul — unless it is for murder or for spreading corruption in the land — it is as if he had killed all of humanity. And whoever saves a life — it is as if he had saved all of humanity."* — **5:32**

---

## Key Tafsir Insights

**Names not given in the Qur'an:** The Qur'an does not name the two brothers as Cain and Abel (or Habil and Qabil). The names come from Islamic tradition. The Qur'an calls them *ibnay Ādam* (the two sons of Adam).

**Why Habil refused to fight:** Habil's refusal to defend himself is a model of passive resistance. He would not compound the sin by becoming a killer himself. The great tradition says: "The one who is killed is better than the one who kills."

**The raven as teacher:** The raven is the only non-human teacher in the Qur'an (other than the hoopoe). The lesson: even a bird can be wiser than a son of Adam when he is overcome by envy. The shame — "Woe to me, am I unable to be like this raven?" — is eternal.

**Why this revelation in Surah Al-Maaida?** The surah was revealed near the end of the Prophet's life. The story of the first murder at the end of revelation is a reminder: the final revealed surahs contain the ultimate lesson — the sanctity of human life.

**"Like killing all of humanity":** This verse (5:32) is often quoted in Islamic bioethics, peace-building, and criminal justice discussions. It establishes that one innocent life has infinite value.

## Cross-Scripture Comparison

The Qur'anic version has **three unique elements** compared to Genesis 4:

| Element | Genesis 4 | Qur'an 5:27–32 |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| The brothers' occupations | Cain = farmer, Abel = shepherd | Same, implied |
| The rejection reason | Not stated | "Allah accepts only from the God-fearing" |
| **The raven's lesson** | ❌ Not mentioned | ✅ The raven teaches burial |
| Cain's punishment | Curse, mark, wandering | "Became among the regretful" |
| **"Killing all of humanity"** | ❌ Not stated | ✅ The eternal law |
| Abel's response | Not given | "I will not stretch my hand…" |
