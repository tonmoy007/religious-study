# Dhul-Qarnayn — The Righteous King

> Surah [[018 Al-Kahf]] · Verses 83–98 · Meccan

The story of a righteous king — one of the four great narratives of Surah Al-Kahf — who travelled to the ends of the earth, administered justice, and built a wall of iron and copper to imprison Gog and Magog until the end of time.

---

## The Story

### The King of the Two Horns

> *"They ask you about Dhul-Qarnayn. Say: I will recite to you an account of him."* — **18:83**

The name *[[Dhul-Qarnayn]]* (ذو القرنين) means "Possessor of the Two Horns" — interpreted variously as a ruler whose authority reached both ends of the world (the "two horns" of east and west), a king with two plaits of hair, or a ruler whose crown had two horns.

### The Western Journey

Dhul-Qarnayn travelled westward until he reached a place where the sun appeared to set into a dark, murky spring. There he found a people. Allah gave him a choice: punish them or treat them kindly. He replied:

> *"As for one who does wrong — we will punish him, then he will be returned to his Lord, who will punish him with a terrible punishment. But as for one who believes and does righteousness — he will have the best reward, and we will issue him commands that are easy."* — **18:87–88**

### The Eastern Journey

He then travelled eastward until he reached a place where the sun rose upon a people for whom Allah had provided no shelter from its heat — a land of open plains where people had no buildings or cover.

### The Wall of Iron

Dhul-Qarnayn's third journey took him to a place between two mountain barriers. There he found a people who could barely understand him. They complained:

> *"O Dhul-Qarnayn, Gog and Magog are spreading corruption in the land. Shall we pay you a tribute so that you may build a barrier between us and them?"* — **18:94**

He refused their payment:

> *"What my Lord has given me is better. But help me with strength, and I will build a rampart between you and them."* — **18:95**

He commanded them to bring blocks of iron. He piled them between the two mountain cliffs until the iron filled the gap and was level with the peaks. Then he said:

> *"Blow!"*

When the iron was red-hot, he poured molten copper over it, fusing the iron blocks into an impenetrable wall.

> *"So they were not able to scale it, nor could they dig through it."* — **18:97**

Gog and Magog were sealed behind the wall — until the end of time.

### The Final Hour

Dhul-Qarnayn prophesied:

> *"This is a mercy from my Lord. But when the promise of my Lord comes, He will level it to dust. And the promise of my Lord is ever true."* — **18:98**

And the Qur'an links this to the final signs: on that day, the people of Gog and Magog will pour forth from every height — one of the major signs before the Day of Judgment.

## Key Tafsir Insights

**Identity of Dhul-Qarnayn:** This is one of the great debates in Qur'anic exegesis.

| Identification | Who | Evidence |
|---------------|-----|----------|
| **Alexander the Great** | Popular classical view (Ṭabarī, Christian legend) | His campaigns reached India and Libya; "two horns" = horns of Ammon |
| **Cyrus the Great** | Modern scholarly consensus | The Cyrus Cylinder, his justice, the tomb at Pasargadae shows a winged figure with two horns; conquers the east and west |
| **A South Arabian king** | Early Islamic view | Tubba' of Yemen or Sa'b Dhu Marathid — who built a wall in the Caucasus |
| **A pre-Islamic sage-king** | Another Muslim tradition | Not a conqueror but a prophet-king |

The Prophet (ﷺ) did not definitively identify him, saying: "I do not know whether he was a prophet or not." (Tirmidhi)

**Gog and Magog (Yajuj and Majuj):** Two corrupted tribes or nations. In Islamic eschatology:
- They will be sealed until near the end of time
- They will break through and spread across the earth, drinking rivers and lakes dry
- Jesus and the righteous believers will pray to Allah, who will destroy them by sending worms/nematodes into their necks
- This is one of the ten major signs of the Hour (alongside the Dajjal, the Beast, the smoke, etc.)

**The iron wall:** Described in the Qur'an as blocks of iron covered in molten copper — a forge-like structure that would have been technologically remarkable. The location is most commonly placed at the **Darial Gorge** in the Caucasus Mountains (between modern Russia and Georgia), or the **Iron Gates** near Derbent.
