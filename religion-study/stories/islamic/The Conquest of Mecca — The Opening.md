# The Conquest of Mecca — The Opening

> 20 Ramadan, 8 AH · 1 January 630 CE (approx.)

The single most dramatic event in the Prophet's lifetime — the bloodless conquest of the city that had persecuted, exiled, and fought him for 21 years. Mecca fell without a fight. The Prophet entered the city he had been driven from, and in an act that has no parallel in history, declared a general amnesty. "Go — you are free." This is *al-Fatḥ* (the Opening), the realization of the Prophet's mission.

---

## The Background

### The Truce of Hudaybiyyah (6 AH)

In 628 CE, the Prophet and 1,400 unarmed Muslims attempted to perform the pilgrimage to Mecca. The Quraysh blocked them. After tense negotiations, the **Treaty of Hudaybiyyah** was signed — a ten-year truce that allowed the Prophet to perform the pilgrimage the following year.

On the surface, the treaty favoured the Quraysh. But within two years, more people had entered Islam than in the previous 20 years. The treaty recognized the Muslims as a legitimate power.

### The Breach

In 8 AH, the **Banu Bakr**, allies of the Quraysh, attacked the **Banu Khuza'ah**, allies of the Muslims. The Quraysh provided weapons to the Banu Bakr — violating the treaty.

The Prophet sent an ultimatum: either the Quraysh disavow the aggression or the treaty is void. The Quraysh refused. The Prophet mobilized **10,000 men** — the largest Muslim army ever assembled — and marched on Mecca.

---

## The Conquest

### The March

The Prophet ordered fires lit on the hills — 10,000 men with torches. The Quraysh looked out and saw the entire horizon lit with approaching fire. They knew resistance was futile.

### The Entry

**[[Abu Sufyan]]**, the leader of the Quraysh, came out to meet the Prophet. He was brought before him.

> *"Woe to you, Abu Sufyan — has the time not come for you to know that there is no god but Allah?"*

> *"My mother and father be your ransom — how kind and gentle you are! I know that if there were another god besides Allah, he would have helped me by now."*

> *"Woe to you, Abu Sufyan — has the time not come for you to know that I am the Messenger of Allah?"*

Abu Sufyan hesitated. **Abbas** (the Prophet's uncle) warned him: "Submit before you lose your head." Abu Sufyan declared his faith.

The Prophet divided the army into four columns entering from four directions — all but one column commanded by **[[Khalid ibn al-Walid]]** (now a Muslim), with strict orders:

> *"Do not fight anyone — unless they fight you."*

### The Purification

The Prophet entered Mecca — riding his camel, his head bowed in humility to God. He went straight to the **Kaaba**, circled it seven times on horseback, then commanded the **360 idols** around the Kaaba to be destroyed. He pointed at each idol with his staff and recited:

> *"Truth has come — falsehood has perished. Indeed, falsehood is bound to perish."* — **17:81**

He ordered **[[Bilal ibn Rabah|Bilal]]** — the former Abyssinian slave who had been tortured by the Quraysh — to climb the Kaaba and call the **Adhan** (call to prayer). The sound of *Allahu Akbar* echoed through Mecca for the first time.

---

## The Amnesty

The people of Mecca gathered in the courtyard of the Kaaba, terrified — expecting mass executions. The Prophet asked:

> *"What do you think I will do to you?"*

They replied: "You are a generous brother — the son of a generous brother."

He said:

> *"Go — you are free. There is no blame on you today. May Allah forgive you."* — **12:92**

He declared a general amnesty covering all of Mecca. **Only 10 people were excluded** — those who had committed specific acts of murder, treason, or blasphemy — and even most of them were later pardoned.

Among the most dramatic pardons:

- **[[Hind bint Utbah]]** — the woman who had mutilated Hamzah — she covered her face and said: "Muhammad, I was a woman wronged." He forgave her.
- **Wahshi** — the slave who killed Hamzah — he fled but later converted and was forgiven.
- **Ikrimah ibn Abi Jahl** — the son of the Prophet's arch-enemy — he fled to Yemen but his wife brought him back; he converted and became a great companion.

---

## The Aftermath

The Prophet remained in Mecca for 19 days, consolidating the conquest and destroying the remaining idols in the region. The people of Mecca embraced Islam in waves:

> *"When the victory of Allah comes and the Opening — and you see people entering the religion of Allah in multitudes — then glorify your Lord with praise and seek His forgiveness. He is oft-Relenting."* — **110:1–3**

---

## Key Tafsir Insights

**Why amnesty?** The general pardon is the Prophet's greatest ethical legacy. He could have executed his enemies. He had every right. Instead, he forgave them — transforming enemies into allies. Many of the former Quraysh leaders became devoted Muslims after the conquest.

**The contrast with biblical conquests:** In the Hebrew Bible, the conquest of Canaan involved the destruction of cities and the execution of captives (Deuteronomy 20, Joshua 6). In Islam, the conquest of Mecca is virtually bloodless. No city in history of this size was ever conquered with so little bloodshed.

**"Truth has come — falsehood has perished":** The Prophet destroyed the idols with his staff — 360 deities that had been worshipped for centuries, shattered in minutes. The phrase became an Islamic slogan for the triumph of tawhid over shirk.

**Bilal on the Kaaba:** That the first call to prayer from the Kaaba — the spiritual centre of Arabia — was made by a former slave, a black Abyssinian, was the most powerful statement of Islam's spiritual egalitarianism.
