# Joshua and the Fall of Jericho

> The walls came tumbling down — Israel's first battle in the Promised Land, won not by sword but by faith and the sound of trumpets.

---

## The Story (Joshua 6)

After Moses' death, **Joshua** leads Israel across the Jordan River into Canaan. Their first target: **Jericho**, a fortified city with high walls.

God gives Joshua a strange battle plan: march around the city once a day for six days — seven priests carrying rams' horns before the ark. On the seventh day, circle seven times, and when the priests blow the trumpets and the people shout — the walls will fall.

Israel obeys. On the seventh day, the trumpets sound, the people shout, and **the walls collapse**. The army charges in and destroys everything — men, women, children, cattle. Only **Rahab** and her family are spared, because she hid the Israelite spies earlier. Joshua curses anyone who rebuilds Jericho.

---

## Theological Significance

| Theme | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Faith and obedience** | The battle is won by following God's instructions, not military strategy |
| **God as warrior** | YHWH fights for Israel — the conquest is holy war (*herem*) |
| **Rahab's faith** | A Canaanite prostitute is saved by faith — she appears in Matthew's genealogy of Jesus |
| **The ban (*herem*)** | Total destruction as judgment on Canaanite sin (Deuteronomy 9:4–5) |
