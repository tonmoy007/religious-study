## The Geography of Paradise

The Field of Reeds is situated in the **eastern part of the Tuat** — the region closest to the dawn. It is described as an **archipelago of canals and islands**, lush with vegetation, divided into four main regions:

## What the Justified Soul Finds

The soul who passes [[The Weighing of the Heart — Egyptian Story|the Judgment]] and survives [[The Journey Through the Tuat — Egyptian Story|the Journey]] enters a world of **perfected earthly existence**:

### 1. Abundant Food and Drink
- Fields of **emmer wheat and barley** seven cubits high (about 12 feet)
- The **Sycomore of [[Nut — Egyptian Goddess|Nut]]** — a tree goddess who offers cool water, bread, and dates
- The **Lake of the Gods** — the soul drinks from it and is refreshed forever
- The **seven cows and the bull** (Chapter 148) — celestial beings who provide endless milk and beer

> *"The justified soul eats of the food of the gods, drinks of the water of the Lake of the Field of Reeds, and rests in the shade of the sycomore."*

### 2. Eternal Work and Leisure
The Field of Reeds is not a place of idle rest — it is a **perfect version of Egypt**:

- The soul **ploughs the fields, sows seed, reaps the harvest**
- The soul **fishes in the canals, navigates the waterways**
- But the work is not *toil* — it is **blessed labour**, without exhaustion, without heat
- The **Soul Birds** (the *ba*) fly in the sky over the fields, seeing everything

For those who do not wish to work, Chapter 6 provides a *shabti* spell — the funerary figurines (shabtis) come to life and do the work for the deceased:

> *"O shabti, if I am called upon to do any work in the Field of Reeds — to plough, to sow, to reap, to dig canals — you shall do it for me."*

### 3. Family and Community
- The soul **reunites with** family members who have also been justified
- The soul **sits among the gods** — speaks with [[Osiris — Egyptian God|Osiris]], [[Ra — Egyptian God|Ra]], and [[Thoth — Egyptian God|Thoth]]
- The soul **worships the gods** — daily hymns and offerings, forever
- The soul **hunts, fishes, and feasts** with the blessed dead

### 4. Transformation and Power
In the Field of Reeds, the soul gains divine powers:
- The **soul (ba)** can fly between heaven and earth, visit the living in dreams
- The **spirit (akh)** shines like a star — the blessed dead are the **imperishable stars**
- The soul can **transform into any form** — a hawk, a lotus, a heron, a crocodile, a god (see [[The Transformations — Egyptian Story]])
- The soul **has power over the elements** — controls the wind, the water, the growth of grain

## The Field of Offerings (Sekhet-Hetepet)

A distinct but connected region, the Field of Offerings is where the soul receives **daily sustenance**:
- Tables of bread, beer, beef, fowl, alabaster vessels of wine
- Liturgical offerings made by priests in the earthly temples are **transmitted** to the soul here
- A magical spell (Chapter 105) ensures the soul receives offerings even if no one on earth remembers them:

> *"I am the soul who lives on offerings. I have come. Give me bread. Give me beer. Give me the food which is my due."*

## The Lake of the Gods

At the centre of the Field of Reeds is a **great lake** with multiple names:
- "The Lake of Two Fountains"
- "The Lake of the Sun's Rays"
- "The Pool of the Water of Life"
- "The Lake of the God's Boats"

The soul sails on this lake in the **Boat of Ra** — the culmination of the daily journey. The lake is the place of **final transformation** — the soul bathes in its waters and becomes a god.

## The Mountain of the Just

Some versions describe a **mountain** in the Field of Reeds — the "Mountain of the Just" where:
- The throne of [[Osiris — Egyptian God|Osiris]] is set
- The **Book of the Dead** is recorded on the leaves of the sacred *ished* tree
- [[Thoth — Egyptian God|Thoth]] inscribes the soul's name on the tree, granting eternal memory

## Who Lives in the Field of Reeds?

The Field is for those who:
- **Lived by [[Ma'at — Goddess of Truth|Ma'at]]** — ethical integrity in life
- **Passed the Judgment** — the heart balanced with the feather
- **Knew the spells** — studied the Book of the Dead
- **Were properly buried** — mummified with the correct rites

Those not admitted:
- The **unjust** (whose hearts were devoured by [[Ammut — The Devourer|Ammut]])
- Those **not buried properly** — the soul cannot enter the Tuat
- Those who **died before their time** (in some traditions, they wander as restless spirits)

## The Uniqueness of the Egyptian Paradise

| Feature | Egyptian (Field of Reeds) | Other Traditions |
|---------|--------------------------|------------------|
| **Physical labour** | Present (but pleasant) | Absent in most (heaven = rest) |
| **Earthly continuity** | Same activities as earthly life | Complete transformation |
| **Community** | Yes — family, neighbours, gods | Often individual or anonymous |
| **Geography** | Detailed — canals, fields, lakes | Often abstract (light, clouds) |
| **Time** | Cyclical (eternal day-night) | Static eternity |
| **Desire** | Fully present (hunger, thirst satisfied) | Often desireless |
| **Transformation** | Possible (become a god, a star, a bird) | Limited (becoming an angel) |
