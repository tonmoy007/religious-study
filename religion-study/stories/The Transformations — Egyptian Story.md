## What Are the Transformations?

Chapters 76 through 88 of the Book of the Dead are a group of spells called the **"Chapters of Transformation"** (*shetet kheperu*). After passing judgment and entering [[The Field of Reeds — Egyptian Story|the Field of Reeds]], the justified soul can **take on any form they desire** — a god, a bird, a plant, a celestial body.

These are not mere illusions — they are **real transformations of being**. The soul does not pretend to be a falcon; it *becomes* the falcon. It does not imitate Ra; it *is* Ra.

## Selected Transformations in Detail

### 1. Becoming a Golden Hawk (Chapter 77)

> *"I am the golden hawk. I am the phoenix of the gods. I have come from the island of fire. I have opened the sight of Horus. I shine as the sun."*

The hawk is the most powerful transformation — it combines:
- **Sight** — the hawk sees everything from the highest sky
- **Speed** — the hawk moves between heaven and earth
- **Golden colour** — the divine radiance
- **Nearness to Ra** — the hawk flies beside the sun

### 2. Becoming a Lotus (Chapter 81A)

> *"I am the lotus-soul who comes forth from the waters of Nun. I am the pure lotus that rises from the abyss."*

The lotus is the symbol of:
- **Rebirth** — the lotus closes at night and opens at dawn
- **Purity** — the lotus grows from mud but remains spotless
- **Primordial creation** — the first lotus that emerged from Nun and gave birth to Ra
- **The sun** — the petals are the rays of the sun

To become a lotus is to become the **first thing that ever existed** — the very moment of creation.

### 3. Becoming the Bennu (Phoenix) (Chapters 83, 81B)

> *"I am the Bennu, the heart of Ra. I am the guide of the gods to the Tuat."*

The Bennu is the Egyptian phoenix — a heron-like bird that represents:
- **Self-creation** — the Bennu was born from the fire of the dawn
- **Cyclical renewal** — the Bennu dies, rises, and returns as the new sun
- **The morning star** — the Bennu is the planet Venus, the signal of dawn
- **The soul of Ra** — "the heart of Ra"

### 4. Becoming a Crocodile (Chapter 88)

> *"I am the crocodile. I walk upon the land and I walk in the water. I have come forth from the body of the crocodile-god Sobek. My teeth are the teeth of the gods. I devour the enemies of Osiris."*

The crocodile transformation is about **power and fearlessness**:
- The crocodile walks on land **and** in water — mastery of both realms
- It is the mightiest predator of the Nile
- It devours the enemies of [[Osiris — Egyptian God|Osiris]] — it is not just power, it is *justified* power

### 5. Becoming the God of Light (Chapter 80)

> *"I am the god of light. I shine in the darkness. I am the flame that burns before the face of the Great God."*

The soul becomes the **source of light in the Tuat** — a star, a flame, a lamp for the dead. This is the most theologically audacious transformation: the soul *becomes a source of divine illumination*.

## The Meaning of Transformation

### 1. Egyptian Soteriology as Transfiguration

In most ancient religions, salvation means:
- **Going to a good place** (paradise, the Field of Reeds)
- **Being with a good god** (Osiris, Ra)

In Egypt, salvation means **becoming divine yourself**. The justified soul does not merely *enter* paradise — it *becomes* a god, a star, a source of light. This is **theosis** — the transformation of the human into the divine.

### 2. The Principle of Kheper

The word *kheper* means:
- "To become"
- "To transform"
- "To create"
- "To come into being"

The scarab beetle ([[Khepri]]) is the form of [[Ra — Egyptian God|Ra]] that rolls the sun across the sky. The scarab *becomes* what it does — it is action and identity fused together.

The Transformations are the soul's *kheper* — becoming what it desires through the power of spells, knowledge, and [[Ma'at — Goddess of Truth|Ma'at]].

### 3. Knowledge as the Key

Every transformation requires **knowing a secret name**:
- The name of the form the soul is assuming
- The name of the god who guards that form
- The name of the gate that opens to that form

This connects the Transformations to [[Religious Epistemology]]: salvation is achieved through **gnosis** — knowing the hidden names of reality.

### 4. The Democratization of Divinity

In earlier periods (Old Kingdom), only the pharaoh could transform into a god and join Ra. By the time of the Book of the Dead (New Kingdom), **anyone who could afford a papyrus** could recite these spells. The afterlife was democratized — but the cost of a Book of the Dead scroll remained high.

## The Psychic Architecture of Transformation

The Transformations reveal an understanding of the soul as **multi-layered**:

| Soul Element | Form | Transformation |
|-------------|------|---------------|
| **Ka** (life force) | The double | Sustained by offerings |
| **Ba** (personality) | The soul-bird (human-headed bird) | Becomes a swallow, hawk, heron |
| **Akh** (spirit) | The shining one / star | Becomes the god of light |
| **Heart (Ib)** | The moral record | Protected by the heart scarab |
| **Shadow (Sheut)** | The conjoined spirit | Remains in the tomb |

The Transformations allow each element of the soul to assume its perfected form.
