# Joseph in Egypt

> The longest continuous story in Genesis — a tale of jealousy, slavery, dreams, temptation, famine, and the most famous family reunion in scripture.

---

## The Story (Genesis 37–50)

### Part 1: The Dreamer (Genesis 37)

Jacob's favorite son, **Joseph** (age 17), receives a **coat of many colors** from his father. His dreams — sheaves bowing, sun/moon/stars bowing — foretell his future authority. His brothers hate him. They plot to kill him, but **Reuben** intervenes. Instead, they sell him to Ishmaelite traders for 20 pieces of silver. They dip his coat in goat's blood and tell Jacob he is dead.

### Part 2: The Rise (Genesis 39–41)

In Egypt, Joseph serves **Potiphar**, captain of the guard. God is with him — everything prospers. Potiphar's wife tries to seduce him. When he refuses, she falsely accuses him. Joseph is thrown into prison.

In prison, God gives Joseph the ability to interpret dreams. He interprets the dreams of Pharaoh's cupbearer and baker — the cupbearer is restored, the baker is hanged. Two years later, Pharaoh has disturbing dreams (seven fat cows devoured by seven lean cows; seven full ears of grain swallowed by seven thin ears). The cupbearer remembers Joseph.

Joseph interprets: **seven years of abundance** followed by **seven years of famine**. He advises Pharaoh to store grain. Pharaoh appoints Joseph as vizier — second only to Pharaoh. Joseph marries Asenath, daughter of an Egyptian priest, and has two sons (Manasseh and Ephraim).

### Part 3: The Brothers' Return (Genesis 42–45)

The famine strikes Canaan. Jacob sends ten sons to Egypt for grain — leaving Benjamin behind. They bow before Joseph (fulfilling his dream) without recognizing him. Joseph tests them:
- Accuses them of being spies
- Imprisons Simeon
- Demands they bring Benjamin
- When they return with Benjamin, Joseph frames him with a silver cup

**Judah's plea** — *"Let your servant remain instead of the boy"* — breaks Joseph. He reveals himself: *"I am Joseph! Is my father still alive?"* (45:3). He forgives them: *"You intended evil against me, but God intended it for good"* (50:20).

### Part 4: The Reconciliation (Genesis 46–50)

Jacob moves to Egypt with all his family (70 souls). Jacob blesses Joseph's sons and prophesies over his own sons — the **Blessing of Jacob** (Genesis 49). Joseph dies at 110, having seen his great-grandchildren. He makes the Israelites swear to carry his bones to the Promised Land.

## Theological Significance

| Theme | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Divine providence** | God works through human evil to accomplish salvation |
| **The testing of character** | Joseph's faithfulness in slavery, prison, and power |
| **Forgiveness** | "You intended evil, but God intended good" — the key verse |
| **Types of Christ** | Christian tradition: Joseph as a type of Christ — beloved son, rejected by brothers, exalted to save |
