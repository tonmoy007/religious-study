# The Great Flood and Manu

> The Hindu flood myth — the fish avatar of Vishnu who saves the first man and the seeds of all life from a cosmic deluge.

---

## The Story (Shatapatha Brahmana, Matsya Purana)

### Manu's Discovery

**Manu** — the first man, progenitor of humanity — is performing his ablutions. A tiny fish swims into his hands. It begs: "Save me from the larger fish — I will protect you in turn."

Manu places the fish in a pot. The fish grows — it needs a larger vessel. A jar. Then a well. Then a river. Then the ocean.

### The Flood Prophecy

The fish — revealed as **Matsya** (the fish avatar of Vishnu) — tells Manu: "In seven days, the ocean will rise and flood the entire earth. **Build a boat** — take the seven sages, seeds of all plants, and one of every species."

Manu builds the great ship. The flood comes — there is nothing but water. Manu ties the boat to Matsya's horn with the serpent Vasuki as a rope. The fish pulls the boat through the flood for many years.

### The New Beginning

When the waters recede, the boat comes to rest on the **highest peak of the Himalayas** (Mount Naubandhana). Manu is the sole human survivor.

He performs a sacrifice — and from the sacrifice emerges **Ida**, who becomes his wife. From them, the human race is reborn.

## The Flood Across Traditions

| Tradition | Survivor | Divine Agent | Vessel |
|-----------|----------|-------------|--------|
| **Hinduism** | Manu | Matsya (Vishnu) | Ship tied to fish's horn |
| **Bible** | Noah | God | Ark |
| **Qur'an** | Nuh | Allah | Ark |
| **Mesopotamia** | Utnapishtim | Ea | Cube-shaped boat |
| **Greece** | Deucalion, Pyrrha | Prometheus | Chest |
