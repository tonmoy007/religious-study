# Noah and the Flood

> Divine judgment and covenant mercy — the story of a single righteous man, an ark, and the renewal of creation.

---

## The Story (Genesis 6–9)

The earth becomes filled with violence and corruption. God decides to wipe out humanity but finds **Noah** — *"a righteous man, blameless in his generation"* (6:9).

God instructs Noah to build an **ark** of gopher wood — 300 cubits long, 50 wide, 30 high — with three decks, a window, and a door. Noah is to take **two of every living creature** (seven pairs of clean animals) into the ark, along with his family.

The **floodwaters** cover the earth for 150 days. Every living thing on dry land dies. When the waters recede, the ark rests on **Mount Ararat**.

Noah sends out a raven, then a dove — the second time the dove returns with an **olive leaf**. God makes a **covenant** with Noah: never again will a flood destroy the earth. The sign: a **rainbow** in the clouds.

> [!quote] Genesis 9:13
> "I do set my bow in the cloud, and it shall be for a token of a covenant between me and the earth."

Noah plants a vineyard, becomes drunk, and lies uncovered. His son **Ham** sees him naked — his brothers Shem and Japheth cover him without looking. Ham's son Canaan is cursed; Shem and Japheth are blessed.

---

## Theological Significance

| Theme | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Divine judgment** | God's response to pervasive evil |
| **The remnant** | Noah saved by grace — "Noah found favor in the eyes of the Lord" (6:8) |
| **Covenant** | The first biblical *berith* — unconditional promise sealed with a sign |
| **Creation re-creation** | The flood reverses creation (waters cover the land); the ark is a new creation |
| **Types and shadows** | Christian tradition: the ark as a type of the Church; baptism as the flood (1 Peter 3:20–21) |

## Comparison with Islamic Account

| Element | Bible | Qur'an |
|---------|-------|--------|
| **Why the flood** | Human violence and corruption | People reject Nuh's message |
| **The ark** | Detailed dimensions (300×50×30 cubits) | An ark built under divine guidance — no dimensions |
| **Duration** | 40 days of rain, 150 days of waters | Not specified |
| **The son who drowns** | All humans die — no specific son mentioned | **Nuh's son** drowns despite Nuh's plea — a poignant theological moment (11:42–46) |
| **The covenant** | Rainbow as sign | No rainbow — the covenant is not emphasized |
| **After the flood** | Noah's drunkenness and Ham's curse | Nuh is blessed, settled, and continues his mission |

> The Qur'an adds a striking detail: one of Nuh's sons refuses to board the ark, saying "I will take refuge on a mountain" — and drowns. When Nuh pleads with God, he is told the son is not of his family (because of his unbelief). This is absent from Genesis.
