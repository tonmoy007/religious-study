# Creation by Divine Word and Light

> Part of [[Cross-Scripture Connections MOC]]

Across very different traditions, creation begins with an **utterance** — a
word, a command, a name — and with **light**. Some of these links are
genealogical (Genesis → John); others are striking but *independent*
convergences (the Tao).

## "Let there be light" — Genesis
From [[scriptures/bible-kjv/01 Genesis|Genesis]]:

> [!quote] Genesis 1:3
> "And God said, **Let there be light**: and there was light."

Creation here is **spoken**: God *says*, and it is so. Light is the first thing
called into being.

## "The Word was God" — John
The Gospel of [[43 John]] opens by deliberately echoing Genesis ("In the
beginning…") and identifying the creative Word (*Logos*) with God:

> [!quote] John 1:1
> "In the beginning was the **Word**, and the Word was with God, and the **Word was God**."

This is a **genealogical** link: John knowingly rewrites Genesis 1, fusing the
Hebrew creative speech with the Greek philosophical *Logos* (see the Hellenistic
thread in [[Connections Between Religious Texts]]).

## "Be! and it is" — the Qur'an
The [[Quran Index|Qur'an]] makes creation-by-command a recurring refrain —
the same formula appears across multiple surahs:

> [!quote] The divine command *Kun fa-yakūn*
> "When He decreeth a thing, He saith unto it only: **Be! and it is.**"
> — [[002 Al-Baqara|Al-Baqara 2:117]], [[019 Maryam|Maryam 19:35]], Aal-i-Imraan 3:47, Yaseen 36:82

Notably, in Aal-i-Imraan 3:47 the very same formula explains the **virgin
conception of Jesus** — linking this note to [[Birth of Jesus Across Scriptures]].

## "The Originator of heaven and earth" — the Tao
From [[Tao Te Ching]], Chapter 1 — here creation flows not from a personal
command but from the unnameable **Tao**:

> [!quote] Tao Te Ching, Ch. 1
> "The Tao that can be trodden is not the enduring and unchanging Tao. The name that can be named is not the enduring and unchanging name. (Conceived of as) having no name, it is the **Originator of heaven and earth**; (conceived of as) having a name, it is the **Mother of all things**."

## Side by side
| Tradition | Source of creation | Key phrase |
|---|---|---|
| Judaism | God's speech | "Let there be light" |
| Christianity | the *Logos* | "the Word was God" |
| Islam | divine command | "Be! and it is" |
| Daoism | the nameless Tao | "the Mother of all things" |

## What kind of connection?
- **Genesis → John → (engaged by) Qur'an:** *genealogical* — a shared Abrahamic stream of "creation by word."
- **Tao Te Ching:** *independent convergence* — a non-theistic origin that resists naming, yet lands on the same intuition that reality issues from a single ineffable source. The contrast is as instructive as the parallel.
