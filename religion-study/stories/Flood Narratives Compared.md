# Flood Narratives Compared

> Part of [[Cross-Scripture Connections MOC]]

A righteous man, warned by God, builds a vessel; the world drowns; the ark
comes to rest on a mountain. This story appears in the Hebrew Bible, the
Qur'an, and — centuries *earlier* — in Mesopotamian myth. Comparing the
versions shows both a clear **genealogical** link (Bible ↔ Qur'an) and a
likely **diffusion/shared-source** link (Mesopotamia → Hebrew).

## Genesis: the deluge from the deep
From [[scriptures/tanakh-jps1917/01 Genesis|Genesis]] (text matches the [[Bible (KJV) Index|KJV]]):

> [!quote] The waters rise
> **7:11** — "…the same day were all the **fountains of the great deep** broken up, and the windows of heaven were opened."
> **6:19** — "…**two of every sort** shalt thou bring into the ark… they shall be male and female."
> **7:20** — "Fifteen cubits upward did the waters prevail; and the mountains were covered."
> **8:4** — "And the ark rested… upon the **mountains of Ararat**."

## The Qur'an: the oven gushes forth
From [[011 Hud|Surah Hud]] — the same beats, distinctly retold:

> [!quote] Nūḥ builds the ship
> **11:37** — "**Build the ship** under Our eyes and by Our inspiration…"
> **11:40** — "…when Our commandment came to pass and **the oven gushed forth water**, We said: **Load therein two of every kind**, a pair (the male and female)…"
> **11:44** — "…And it (the ship) came to rest **upon (the mount) Al-Judi**…"

## Side by side
| Element | Genesis | Qur'an (Hud) |
|---|---|---|
| Hero | Noah | Nūḥ |
| Command | Build an ark | "Build the ship" |
| Animals | "two of every sort" | "two of every kind, a pair" |
| Flood's onset | "fountains of the great deep" | "the oven gushed forth water" |
| Resting place | **Mount Ararat** | **Mount Al-Judi** |
| Who is saved | Noah's household | household + "those who believe" |

The shared skeleton is unmistakable; the **resting mountain differs**
(Ararat vs. Al-Judi), and the Qur'an adds a moral filter — the saved are not
just kin but *believers*, and one of Noah's own sons is **left to drown**
(Hud 11:42–43), a detail absent from Genesis.

## The older layer: Gilgamesh
The Mesopotamian [[Epic of Gilgamesh — Overview|Epic of Gilgamesh]] (Tablet XI)
and the earlier [[Atrahasis — The Creation of Humans and the Flood|Atra-ḥasīs]]
have **Utnapishtim** build a boat, survive a divine flood, and release birds to
find land — predating the Genesis text. This points to a **shared Near Eastern
source or diffusion** beneath the biblical account, not literary borrowing in
the other direction.

> **See :** [[Gilgamesh's Quest for Immortality]] (§ The Flood Story) · [[Atrahasis — The Creation of Humans and the Flood]] · [[Epic of Gilgamesh — Overview]]

## What kind of connection?
- **Bible ↔ Qur'an:** *genealogical* — the Qur'an consciously retells the prophet Nūḥ.
- **Mesopotamia → Bible:** *diffusion / shared cultural source* — strong historical channel.
- **Global flood myths** (Hindu Manu, Greek Deucalion, Chinese Gun-Yu): mostly *independent/archetypal* — caution against parallelomania (see [[Connections Between Religious Texts]]).
