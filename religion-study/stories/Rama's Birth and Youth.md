# Rama's Birth and Youth

> The divine prince is born — an avatar of Vishnu sent to earth to slay the demon Ravana. His youth foretells the hero he will become.

---

## The Story (Ramayana — Bala Kanda)

### The Childless King

**Dasharatha**, king of **Ayodhya** (the splendid capital of Kosala), has three queens — Kausalya, Sumitra, and Kaikeyi — but no son. He performs the great **Putrakameshti Yajna** (sacrifice for progeny), presided over by the sage **Rishyasringa**.

From the sacrificial fire emerges a divine being, who gives Dasharatha a bowl of **payasa** (rice pudding): "Give this to your queens — they will bear your sons."

Kausalya bears **Rama** (half the pudding). Kaikeyi bears **Bharata** (a quarter). Sumitra bears **Lakshmana** and **Shatrughna** (the remaining quarter, split). The children are born — Rama is the **seventh avatar of Vishnu**, sent to destroy the demon **Ravana**.

> "Rama, O best of men, will, by his own prowess, make all quarters of the earth his own, worshipped by all as the moon is worshipped." — Bala Kanda

### The Princes Grow

The four princes study the Vedas under **Guru Vashistha**. **Rama** excels in archery, virtue, and wisdom. **Lakshmana** is inseparable from Rama — he is his constant protector.

### Vishwamitra's Request

The sage **Vishwamitra** arrives at court. Demons (Rakshasas) are disrupting his sacrifices — he asks Dasharatha to send **Rama** to protect them. Dasharatha hesitates — Rama is only 16. But Vishwamitra insists.

Rama and Lakshmana go with Vishwamitra. Rama slays the demoness **Tataka** and defeats **Subahu** and **Maricha** (who flees). The sacrifices proceed.

### The Bow of Shiva

Vishwamitra takes the princes to **Mithila**, where King **Janaka** is hosting a **Swayamvara** for his daughter **Sita**. The challenge: string the bow of Shiva — a divine weapon so heavy that 5,000 men were needed to carry it.

Many kings try — they cannot even lift it. Rama casually picks it up, strings it, and bends it so hard that the **bow snaps in two** with a thunderous crack. Janaka awards Sita to Rama.

### The Wedding

Rama marries Sita. Lakshmana marries Sita's sister **Urmila**. Bharata marries **Mandavi**; Shatrughna marries **Shrutakirti**. The party returns to Ayodhya in triumph.

## Theological Significance

| Theme | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Avatar** | Rama is Vishnu incarnate — the divine descends when dharma declines |
| **The ideal prince** | Rama is the model of royal virtue: courage, wisdom, humility, duty |
| **The bow** | Rama proves his worth not by words but by the impossible feat |
| **Vishwamitra** | The sage's role in shaping Rama's destiny — spiritual authority over royal |
