# Pentecost

> The birthday of the Church — the Holy Spirit descends with wind and fire, and fishermen begin speaking in languages they never learned.

---

## The Story (Acts 2)

Fifty days after Passover (Pentecost = "fiftieth") — the Jewish Feast of Weeks. The disciples are gathered in one place.

Suddenly, **a sound like a violent wind** fills the house. **Divided tongues of fire** appear and rest on each of them. They are **filled with the Holy Spirit** — and begin speaking in other languages as the Spirit gives them utterance.

Jews from every nation under heaven — Parthians, Medes, Elamites, Cretans, Arabs — hear them speaking in **their own native languages**: *"We hear them telling in our own tongues the mighty works of God!"* (2:11). Some mock: *"They are filled with new wine!"*

### Peter's Sermon

Peter stands and declares — quoting the prophet Joel:
- *"In the last days, I will pour out my Spirit on all flesh"*
- Jesus of Nazareth — **attested by miracles**, **crucified** by lawless men, **raised by God** — is both Lord and Christ

The crowd is cut to the heart: *"Brothers — what shall we do?"* Peter: *"Repent and be baptized — every one of you — in the name of Jesus Christ for the forgiveness of your sins, and you will receive the gift of the Holy Spirit."*

**3,000 souls** are added that day.

---

## Theological Significance

| Theme | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **The reversal of Babel** | At Babel, language divided humanity. At Pentecost, language unites — the gospel transcends barriers |
| **The Spirit poured out** | Not just for prophets and kings — for all believers |
| **The birth of the Church** | The community of believers begins — teaching, fellowship, breaking bread, prayer |
| **Peter's transformation** | The man who denied Christ now preaches boldly |

---

## Connection with Islamic Tradition

The concept of the **Holy Spirit** (*Rūḥ al-Qudus*) in Islam is **different**:

- In the Qur'an, the Holy Spirit is identified with **Gabriel** (Jibril) — the angel of revelation
- The Qur'an does not have a concept of the Spirit indwelling believers
- However, the **revelation of the Qur'an** in Arabic is seen as a spirit-filled event — "The Trustworthy Spirit descended with it upon your heart" (26:193–194)
