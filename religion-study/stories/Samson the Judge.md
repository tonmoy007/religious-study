# Samson the Judge

> A Nazirite with superhuman strength, tragic flaws, and a death that destroyed his enemies — the most paradoxical of Israel's judges.

---

## The Story (Judges 13–16)

**Samson** is born to a barren woman — an angel announces his birth, and he is dedicated as a **Nazirite** (no wine, no razor, no contact with the dead). He grows up and the Spirit of the Lord stirs in him.

**His strength:**
- Tears a lion apart with his bare hands
- Kills 30 Philistines to settle a bet
- Slays 1,000 Philistines with the jawbone of a donkey
- Carries away the gates of Gaza

**His weakness:**
- Marries a Philistine woman who betrays him
- Visits a prostitute in enemy territory
- Falls in love with **Delilah**, who is bribed by the Philistines to discover the secret of his strength

After Delilah's repeated entreaties, Samson reveals: *"No razor has ever come upon my head — if I am shaved, my strength will leave me"* (16:17). She cuts his hair while he sleeps. The Philistines seize him, **gouge out his eyes**, and make him grind grain in prison.

But his hair begins to grow back. During a festival to the Philistine god **Dagon**, Samson is brought out to entertain the crowd. He prays: *"O Lord God, remember me — strengthen me only this once"* (16:28). He pushes apart the two pillars of the temple — killing himself and more Philistines in his death than in his life.

---

## Theological Significance

| Theme | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **God uses the flawed** | Samson is faithless, lustful, and vengeful — yet God uses him |
| **Strength from God** | His power is not his own — it leaves when the Nazirite vow is broken |
| **Tragic hero** | A life of potential wasted by undisciplined passion |
| **Typology** | Christian tradition: Samson's death foreshadows Christ's — sacrificing himself to destroy the enemy |
