# The Nativity of Jesus

> The birth of Christ — the central narrative of the Christian faith, told by two gospels with different emphases, and a parallel tradition in the Qur'an.

---

## The Gospel Accounts

### Luke 1–2 (The Humble King)

The angel **Gabriel** appears to **Mary** (a virgin betrothed to Joseph): *"You will conceive and bear a son — you shall call his name Jesus. He will be great and will be called the Son of the Most High"* (1:31–32). Mary asks how — she is a virgin. Gabriel: *"The Holy Spirit will come upon you"* (1:35).

Mary visits her cousin **Elizabeth** (pregnant with John the Baptist) — the *Magnificat*: "My soul magnifies the Lord" (1:46–55).

A decree from Caesar Augustus: all the world must be registered. Joseph and Mary travel to **Bethlehem**. There is no room in the inn — Jesus is born in a stable, laid in a **manger**.

Angels appear to shepherds: *"Glory to God in the highest, and on earth peace, goodwill toward men"* (2:14). The shepherds visit the infant.

**Simeon** and **Anna** recognize the baby as the Messiah in the Temple.

### Matthew 1–2 (The King of the Jews)

Joseph discovers Mary is pregnant — plans to divorce her quietly. An angel appears in a dream: *"Joseph, son of David — do not fear to take Mary as your wife. She will bear a son, and you shall call his name Jesus, for he will save his people from their sins"* (1:20–21).

**Magi** from the East follow a star to Jerusalem. King Herod is troubled. The Magi find Jesus in a house — worship him, present gifts (gold, frankincense, myrrh), and return by another way (warned in a dream).

Joseph is warned in a dream: flee to **Egypt**. Herod slaughters the infants of Bethlehem — the "Massacre of the Innocents." After Herod's death, the family returns and settles in **Nazareth**.

## Comparison with the Qur'an

| Element | Gospels | Qur'an |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **Annunciation** | Gabriel appears to Mary | Gabriel appears to Mary — "a perfect man" |
| **Mary's reaction** | "How can this be?" | "How can I have a son — no man has touched me?" |
| **The conception** | The Holy Spirit overshadows her | God breathes His spirit into her |
| **Joseph's role** | Present — anguished, then obedient | Entirely absent |
| **Birthplace** | Bethlehem — stable, manger | Beneath a **palm tree** — she shakes dates from it |
| **The birth** | Shepherds, angels, Magi | Mary is alone — the infant **Jesus speaks from the cradle** |
| **Herod's threat** | Flight to Egypt | Implied (not narrated) |
