# The Binding of Isaac

> The *Akedah* — the binding — is the most harrowing and theologically dense story in Genesis. Abraham's test, Isaac's faith, and the ram in the thicket form the spiritual core of Jewish, Christian, and Islamic tradition.

---

## The Story (Genesis 22:1–19)

God tests Abraham: *"Take your son, your only son Isaac, whom you love — and go to the land of Moriah, and offer him there as a burnt offering on one of the mountains of which I shall tell you"* (22:2).

Abraham rises early, saddles his donkey, takes two servants and Isaac, and cuts wood for the burnt offering. On the third day, he sees the place. He tells the servants: *"Stay here with the donkey — the boy and I will go yonder and worship, and **come again to you**"* (22:5) — a hint of faith that both will return.

Isaac carries the wood. He asks: *"Father — the fire and the wood are here, but where is the lamb for a burnt offering?"* Abraham replies: *"God will provide for himself the lamb"* (22:8).

Abraham builds the altar, binds Isaac, lays him on the wood, and **raises the knife**.

The angel of the Lord calls out: *"Abraham! Abraham! Do not lay your hand on the boy — for now I know that you fear God, seeing you have not withheld your son"* (22:12). Abraham looks up — a **ram caught in a thicket** by its horns. He offers it instead.

God swears: *"Because you have done this, I will surely bless you and multiply your descendants as the stars of heaven"* (22:17).

---

## The Akedah in Jewish Tradition

| Theme | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **The tenth test** | This is Abraham's tenth and final trial — the culmination of his faith |
| **Isaac's consent** | Jewish tradition (midrash) emphasizes that Isaac was 37 years old and consented — a willing participant, not a child |
| **Merit of the binding** | The *Akedah* becomes a source of merit for Israel — God remembers it and forgives |
| **The ram's ashes** | Midrash: the ram was created at twilight on the sixth day of creation — its ashes were the foundation of the altar |

## Comparison with Islamic Account

| Element | Bible (Genesis 22) | Qur'an (37:100–113) |
|---------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **The son** | Isaac (named, "your only son") | Unnamed — tradition identifies him as **Ishmael** |
| **The command** | Direct word from God | Abraham sees a **dream** |
| **The son's awareness** | Isaac asks about the lamb | The son says: "Do as you are commanded" |
| **The binding** | Elaborate — wood, altar, binding | Brief — both submit |
| **The ram** | Caught in a thicket by its horns | "A great ram" (*dhibḥ ʿaẓīm*) |
| **The blessing** | Descendants as stars | "We left blessings upon him" |

> [!tip] This is the **single most important divergence** between the biblical and Islamic accounts. See [[The Sacrifice — Isaac or Ishmael]] for the full analysis.
