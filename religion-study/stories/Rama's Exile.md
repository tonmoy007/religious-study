# Rama's Exile

> The crown prince is banished on the eve of his coronation — a stepmother's jealousy, a king's broken vow, and a son's flawless obedience to dharma.

---

## The Story (Ramayana — Ayodhya Kanda)

### The Eve of Coronation

Dasharatha decides to crown **Rama** as yuvaraja (crown prince). The entire city rejoices. Preparations begin. Sita stays awake all night, praying.

### Kaikeyi's Boons

**Manthara** — Kaikeyi's hunchbacked maid — poisons her mind: "If Rama is crowned, Bharata will be forgotten. Demand your two boons — the boons Dasharatha promised you years ago when you saved his life in battle."

Kaikeyi enters the **Kopa Griha** (anger chamber). When Dasharatha comes to her, weeping, she demands her boons:
1. **Bharata** shall be crowned king
2. **Rama** shall be exiled to the forest for 14 years

Dasharatha collapses in grief. He begs her — she refuses.

### Rama's Acceptance

Rama is summoned. He hears the decree. His response is **perfect dharma**:

> "Father, if you command it, I will go — I will dwell in the forest, subsist on fruits and roots, and return only after fourteen years."

Sita insists on accompanying him. Lakshmana insists too. They dress in bark garments and leave Ayodhya. The city follows them — weeping. Rama slips away at night, crossing the Ganga.

Dasharatha dies of a broken heart — unable to bear the separation.

### Bharata's Grief

Bharata returns from his uncle's kingdom. He is **furious** with his mother: "You have killed my father and exiled my brother — I want no part of this kingdom!"

He goes to the forest to find Rama. Rama refuses to return — a father's word must be honored. Bharata takes Rama's **sandals** and places them on the throne. He rules as regent — in Rama's name — living as an ascetic outside Ayodhya.

---

## Theological Significance

| Theme | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Obedience to dharma** | Rama could have fought — he chooses to obey, even when unjust |
| **Kaikeyi's tragedy** | She is not evil — she is manipulated, and later full of remorse |
| **Bharata's devotion** | The brother who refuses the throne — loyalty over ambition |
| **Kingship as service** | Rama accepts exile because a king's word is law |
