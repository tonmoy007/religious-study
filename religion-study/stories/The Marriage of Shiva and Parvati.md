# The Marriage of Shiva and Parvati

> From Sati's sacrifice to Parvati's penance — the story of how the ascetic god who renounced the world was finally won by the devotion of the goddess.

---

## The Story (Shiva Purana)

### Sati's Sacrifice

**Daksha** — one of Brahma's sons — has a daughter, **Sati**. She marries **Shiva** — against her father's wishes. Daksha hates Shiva (he did not bow to him at a sacrifice). He holds a massive **Yajna** (sacrifice) but does not invite Shiva and Sati.

Sati goes anyway. Daksha insults Shiva in public. Sati — unable to bear the insult — **leaps into the sacrificial fire** and immolates herself.

Shiva learns what happened. He is **inconsolable** — he lifts Sati's body and performs the **Tandava** (the cosmic dance of destruction). The universe begins to crumble. Vishnu cuts Sati's body into 51 pieces (the **Shakti Peethas** — sacred sites where they fell).

Shiva retreats to Mount Kailash. He enters the deepest meditation. The universe is without desire.

### Parvati's Penance

**Parvati** — the daughter of Himavan (the mountain king) — is the reincarnation of Sati. She determines: "I will win Shiva — or die trying."

She goes to the forest, eats nothing, stands in the summer heat, sits in the winter cold, bare on the ground. She meditates for years.

The gods are concerned — the demon **Taraka** can only be killed by Shiva's son — but Shiva is lost in meditation. They send **Kama Deva** (the god of love) to shoot his arrow at Shiva.

Kama's arrow strikes Shiva. Shiva opens his third eye — **Kama is burned to ash**. Parvati continues her penance.

### The Wedding

Finally, Shiva relents — "She has earned me." He visits Parvati in disguise — he tests her. He insults Shiva, hoping she will curse him. Parvati: "Do not speak of him that way — or I will curse you."

Shiva reveals himself. The wedding is celebrated: Shiva, the ascetic adorned with snakes and ash, marries Parvati, the goddess radiant in silk and gold. The gods attend. Ganga flows from Shiva's hair.

**Kama Deva** — who was burned to ash — is reborn as the son of Krishna and Rukmini (some versions say he remains formless, existing only as emotion).

---

## Theological Significance

| Theme | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Sati's sacrifice** | The wife who chose death over dishonor — the archetype of devotion |
| **Shiva's grief** | The ascetic who renounces the world — yet is undone by love |
| **Parvati's penance** | It is not beauty that wins Shiva — it is determination and devotion |
| **The burning of Kama** | Desire must be burned before true love can arise |
