# The Great Renunciation

> *"Now is the time for the Great Going Forth!"* — Siddhartha, Nidānakathā

---

## The Palace Pleasures

King **Suddhodana**, terrified his son might renounce the world, built Siddhartha three palaces filled with dancers and musicians. Guards blocked every gate. No sick man, old man, or ascetic could enter. The king hoped pleasure could chain what the heart might free.

---

## The Sleeping Women

Siddhartha awoke at midnight to find the dancing women sprawled in sleep — mouths open, spittle running, snoring, half-dressed. Where there had been grace, only animal flesh remained. He felt **saṃvega** — a spiritual shudder at the fragility of conditioned existence.

---

## The Decision

He resolved to leave that very night. He ordered **Channa** to saddle **Kanthaka**. At the nursery door, he saw his newborn son **Rāhula** asleep with **Yasodharā**. He did not enter — waking her would raise the alarm.

> *"When I attain enlightenment, I will return for you."*

---

## The Departure

The gods muffled Kanthaka's hoofbeats. The gates swung open. Siddhartha cut off his long hair with his sword — the **Great Cutting** — and sent it flying skyward.

Four **Māras** spoke from the sky: *"Turn back — you could be a universal emperor!"* *"You will die alone!"* *"Your desires will drag you back!"* *"Why reject the world?"* He answered: *"I want what is deathless."*

---

## The Crossing of the River Anomā

At the river **Anomā**, Siddhartha exchanged his princely silks for the **yellow cloth** of an ascetic — a *pabbajita*, one who has gone forth.

> *"Tell my father: I renounce not from anger or poverty, but from compassion — to find liberation for all beings."*

---

## The Return of Kanthaka

Kanthaka turned back toward Kapilavatthu. Halfway home, the horse **died of a broken heart** — his love for his master was greater than his body could bear. Channa returned alone with the empty saddle.

---

## The Period of Searching

Siddhartha mastered the **Sphere of Nothingness** with **Āḷāra Kālāma**, then the **Sphere of Neither Perception nor Non-Perception** with **Uddaka Rāmaputta** — the highest attainments known. Each time he saw:

> *"This does not lead to Nibbāna."*

He left them both. The attainments were not false — they were **not enough**.

---

## Comparison

| Parallel | Figure | The Pattern |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| **Cave of Hirā** | Muḥammad | Solitary retreat before revelation |
| **40 Days in Wilderness** | Jesus | Confrontation with the tempter |
| **Dark Night of the Soul** | Christian mystics | Stripping away sensory consolation |

Each is a **crisis of departure** — a founder stepping away from security to seek a truth the settled life cannot give. Siddhartha's renunciation was total: family, wealth, kingship, identity. That radical poverty was the condition for enlightenment.
