# 🕊️ Religion Study — Map of Content

A structured, academic-depth study of religion across four lenses:
**comparison**, **philosophy/theology**, **history**, and **the connections
between sacred texts**. This note is the hub — start here and follow the links.

## Cross-Cutting Themes
Threads that run through every section above:

- **Mysticism** — direct experience across traditions
- **Secularization & modernity** — religion in the contemporary world

## Method & Sources
This study leans on the academic *study of religion* (religious studies),
which describes and analyzes traditions rather than adjudicating their truth
claims. Key disciplinary lenses: history, philosophy, anthropology, sociology,
and textual/philological study.

Foundational scholars worth knowing: **Mircea Eliade** (sacred/profane),
**Émile Durkheim** (religion as social), **Max Weber** (religion & economy),
**William James** (religious experience), **Karl Jaspers** (Axial Age),
**Wilfred Cantwell Smith** (the concept of "religion" itself).
