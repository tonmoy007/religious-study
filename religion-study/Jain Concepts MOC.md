## See Also

Additional Jain concepts that are discussed in greater detail within other notes across the vault:

- **Guṇasthāna** — The 14 stages of spiritual development (discussed in [[Jain Figures MOC]] under figures who attained kevala jñāna and in [[Jain Scripture Thematic Groups]] for related canonical texts).
- **Sallekhanā** — The Jain practice of fasting unto death as a peaceful, voluntary end of life (covered in [[Jain Figures MOC]] under Ācārya Samantabhadra and other teachers; also touched on in [[Ahiṃsā]]).
- **Kevala Jñāna** — Omniscience or absolute knowledge, the ultimate goal of Jain spiritual practice (detailed in [[Jain Figures MOC]] under the Tīrthaṅkaras and in [[Karma Theory (Jain)]] as the result of shedding all eight karmas).
- **Tīrthaṅkara** — A "ford-maker" who revives the Jain path; 24 appear in each half of the cosmic time cycle (discussed extensively in [[Jain Figures MOC]]).
- **Navapada / Pañca-Parameṣṭhi** — The five supreme beings (Arhat, Siddha, Ācārya, Upādhyāya, Sādhu) venerated in Jain liturgy (covered in [[Jain Scripture Thematic Groups]] under the Ṇamōkāra Mantra).
- **Aparigraha** — Non-attachment / non-possessiveness, one of the five mahāvratas (expanded in [[Ahiṃsā]] and the ethical conduct section of [[Ratnatraya]]).
- **Samavasarana** — The divine preaching hall where Tīrthaṅkaras deliver sermons (referenced in [[Jain Cosmology]] and [[Jain Figures MOC]]).
- **Loka-Puruṣa** — The universe conceived as a cosmic man (the primary metaphor of [[Jain Cosmology]]).
- **Jīva / Ajīva** — The fundamental ontological duality: sentient soul vs. non-sentient matter (core to [[Karma Theory (Jain)]] and [[Ratnatraya]]).
- **Puṇya / Pāpa** — Auspicious and inauspicious karmas (detailed in [[Karma Theory (Jain)]] under the 8 karmic types).
