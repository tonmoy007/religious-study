## I. Pre-Prophethood (c. 570–610 CE)

### 570 CE — The Year of the Elephant (*Ām al-Fīl*)

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Birth** | Muhammad ibn Abdullah born in Mecca, of the Quraysh tribe (Banu Hashim clan) |
| **Abraha's attack** | In the same year, the Abyssinian viceroy Abraha marched on Mecca with elephants — destroyed by flocks of birds carrying stones of baked clay |
| **Orphaned** | Father **Abdullah** died before his birth; mother **Aminah** died when he was six |

📖 [[105 Al-Fil]] — the surah commemorating the Year of the Elephant
📚 See [[Muhammad - A Figure Traced#The Orphan]] for the Qur'an's tender reference

### 576 CE — Under the Care of His Grandfather

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **'Abd al-Muttalib** | His grandfather took custody, then died two years later |
| **Abu Talib** | His uncle became his guardian |

### 582 CE — Journey to Syria

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **With Abu Talib** | At ~12 years old, travelled to Syria with his uncle's trade caravan |
| **Bahira the Monk** | A Christian monk recognized signs of prophethood in the young Muhammad |

### 595 CE — Marriage to Khadijah

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Khadijah bint Khuwaylid** | A wealthy 40-year-old widow who employed Muhammad to trade her goods |
| **Marriage** | Impressed by his honesty, she proposed marriage. He was 25. |
| **Children** | All his children (except Ibrahim) were from Khadijah: Qasim, Zaynab, Ruqayyah, Umm Kulthum, Fatimah, and Abd Allah |

> [!tip] See Also
> 🔗 [[Muhammad - A Figure Traced]] — for the Prophet's character in the Hadith, including Khadijah's role in the first revelation

### 605 CE — The Black Stone

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Rebuilding the Kaaba** | The Quraysh rebuilt the Kaaba; conflict over who would place the Black Stone |
| **The Wise Judge** | Muhammad placed the Stone on a cloak and had each clan chief lift a corner — a diplomatic masterstroke |

## II. The Meccan Period (610–622 CE)

### 610 CE — The First Revelation

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Cave of Hira** | During his annual retreat on Jabal al-Nur (Mountain of Light), he was visited by the angel Jibrīl (Gabriel) |
| **"Iqra'!"** | "Read! In the name of your Lord who created…" |
| **Khadijah's comfort** | Terrified, he returned to Khadijah, who reassured him and consulted her cousin Waraqah ibn Nawfal, a Christian sage |

📖 [[096 Al-Alaq]] (96:1–5) — the first revelation
📖 [[074 Al-Muddaththir]] (74:1–5) — the second command: "Arise and warn!"
📚 [Bukhari 3](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Revelation) — the full narration from Aisha

### 613 CE — Public Preaching Begins

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **The command** | "And warn your closest kindred" (26:214) — Muhammad began calling his tribe publicly |
| **Mount Safa** | He stood on Safa and asked: "If I told you an army was behind this mountain, would you believe me?" |
| **Quraysh opposition** | Abu Lahab rejected him; the persecution of early Muslims began |

📖 [[026 Ash-Shu'araa]] 26:214
📖 [[111 Al-Masad]] — the condemnation of Abu Lahab and his wife
🔗 [[Quran Thematic Groups#26. Prophet's Biography & Consolation]]

### 615 CE — First Migration to Abyssinia (*Hijrah* to Habashah)

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Persecution** | Some early Muslims fled Quraysh persecution |
| **Negus (Najashi)** | The Christian king of Abyssinia granted them refuge; refused to extradite them |
| **Return** | Most returned in 618 CE after rumours of Quraysh conversion proved false |

### 617–619 CE — The Boycott of Banu Hashim

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Social/civil boycott** | The Quraysh imposed a total boycott against the Banu Hashim and Banu Muttalib, refusing to trade or intermarry with them |
| **Mountain retreat** | The clans lived in the Shi'b of Abu Talib for three years, suffering severe hardship |

### 619 CE — The Year of Sorrow (*Ām al-Ḥuzn*)

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Death of Khadijah** | His beloved wife and first supporter — the greatest personal loss |
| **Death of Abu Talib** | His uncle and protector — with his death, the Quraysh intensified their persecution |
| **The triple loss** | Left without his wife, his uncle, and his clan's protection |

[📖 Context: The Meccan consolation surahs — see below]

📖 [[093 Ad-Dhuhaa]] — "Your Lord has not forsaken you"
📖 [[094 Ash-Sharh]] — "Did We not expand your chest?"

### 620 CE — The Journey to Ta'if

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Seeking refuge** | Walking 60 km to Ta'if to seek support from the Thaqif tribe |
| **Rejection** | The leaders rejected him and set street urchins to stone him until his feet bled |
| **Prayer** | His famous plea: "If You are not angry with me, I do not care…"

### 620 CE — The Night Journey (*Isrā'*) and Ascension (*Mi'rāj*)

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Journey to Jerusalem** | In a single night, from Mecca to Masjid al-Aqsa (Jerusalem) on the winged steed *al-Burāq* |
| **Ascension through the heavens** | Met Adam, Jesus, Moses, Abraham, and other prophets in the seven heavens |
| **The Five Prayers** | Received the command for 50 daily prayers, reduced to 5 at Moses' advice |
| **The prayer gift** | "These are five prayers — and they are fifty. My word does not change." |

📖 [[017 Al-Israa]] — "Glorified be He who carried His servant by night from the Inviolable Place of Worship to the Far Distant Place of Worship" (17:1)
📖 [[053 An-Najm]] 53:13–18 — the vision of the heavenly ascent

### 621 CE — The First Pledge of Aqabah

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Medinese pilgrims** | 12 men from Yathrib (Medina) met Muhammad at Aqabah and pledged to worship God alone and avoid sin |
| **Mus'ab ibn 'Umayr** | Sent to Medina to teach Islam |

### 622 CE — The Second Pledge of Aqabah / The Hijrah

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **73 men, 2 women** | A larger delegation from Medina pledged to protect Muhammad as they would their own families |
| **Emigration begins** | Muslims secretly left Mecca for Medina over several weeks |
| **The Hijrah** | Muhammad and Abu Bakr took a longer route, hiding in the Cave of Thawr for three days |
| **Arrival in Yathrib** | Renamed **al-Madīnah al-Munawwarah** (the Illuminated City). The Islamic calendar begins. |

📖 [[009 At-Tawba]] 9:40 — "the second of two when they were in the cave…"
📚 [Bukhari 3653](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Companions) — Abu Bakr in the cave

## III. The Medinan Period (622–632 CE)

### 622–624 CE — Building the Community

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Masjid al-Nabawi** | Built the first mosque in Medina — the palm-trunk mosque |
| **Brotherhood** | Established *mu'ākhāh* — pairing each emigrant (*Muhājir*) with a helper (*Anṣār*) |
| **Constitution of Medina** | A multi-faith civic charter: Jews, Muslims, and pagans as one community (*ummah*) |
| **Qibla changed** | Direction of prayer changed from Jerusalem to the Kaaba (after ~16 months) |

📖 [[002 Al-Baqara]] 2:144 — the change of qibla
📚 [Tirmidhi 3046](scriptures/islam-hadith/04%20Jami%60%20at-Tirmidhi.md#Chapters%20on%20Tafsir) — the change of qibla narrated by al-Bara'

### 624 CE — Battle of Badr (17 Ramadan, 2 AH)

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Forces** | 313 Muslims vs. ~950 Meccans |
| **Outcome** | Decisive Muslim victory. 70 Quraysh killed, 70 captured. |
| **Significance** | Established Muslims as a military force; divine intervention — angels fought alongside |
| **Captives** | Ransom policy: literate captives could teach 10 Muslims to read and go free |

📖 [[008 Al-Anfaal]] — the entire surah concerns Badr (spoils, divine assistance, ethics of war)

### 625 CE — Battle of Uhud (7 Shawwal, 3 AH)

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Forces** | ~700 Muslims vs. ~3,000 Meccans led by Abu Sufyan |
| **Initial success** | Muslims initially prevailed — the archers abandoned their post to collect spoils |
| **Reversal** | Khalid ibn al-Walid (then fighting for Quraysh) led a cavalry charge from the rear |
| **Prophet wounded** | Muhammad injured, his front tooth broken, the rumour spread that he was killed |
| **Hamzah's martyrdom** | The Prophet's uncle was mutilated by Hind bint Utbah |

📖 [[003 Aal-i-Imraan]] — lessons from Uhud (v.121–179)
🔗 [[Quran Thematic Groups#26. Prophet's Biography & Consolation]]

### 627 CE — Battle of the Trench (*Al-Aḥzāb*) / *Al-Khandaq*

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Confederates** | 10,000-strong alliance of Quraysh, Bedouin, and Jewish tribes |
| **The Trench** | Salman al-Farsi suggested digging a trench around Medina's exposed flank |
| **Siege** | Lasted ~30 days; the confederates could not breach the trench |
| **Storm** | A violent windstorm scattered the confederate camp at night |
| **Banu Qurayzah** | The Jewish tribe that conspired with the enemy was judged after the siege |

📖 [[033 Al-Ahzaab]] — the entire surah covers the battle and its aftermath

### 628 CE — Treaty of Ḥudaybiyyah (Dhul Qa'dah, 6 AH)

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **March to Mecca** | 1,400 Muslims marched for *'umrah* (minor pilgrimage) — unarmed |
| **Meccan refusal** | The Quraysh blocked entry; negotiations followed |
| **The Treaty** | 10-year truce; Muslims would return without pilgrimage that year; anyone could join either side; Muslims could return the following year for 3 days |
| **'Umar's objection** | 'Umar initially protested the terms (which seemed unfavourable) |
| **The "Clear Victory"** | Despite its apparent humility, the treaty was later declared a victory |

📖 [[048 Al-Fath]] — "Indeed We have given you a clear victory" (48:1)

### 629 CE — Conquest of Khaybar (7 AH)

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Jewish fortress** | The Banu Nadir had fortified Khaybar and allied with neighbouring tribes |
| **Muslim victory** | Fortresses fell one by one; the Jewish farmers retained their land in exchange for half the produce |
| **Saffiyyah** | The Prophet married Safiyyah bint Huyayy, a Jewish noblewoman |

### 629 CE — The Expedition to Mu'tah

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Byzantine border** | A Muslim force of 3,000 sent to the Syrian border |
| **Overwhelmed** | Faced a massive Byzantine-Ghassanid army; three commanders (Zayd, Ja'far, Abdullah) were killed |
| **Khalid emerges** | Khalid ibn al-Walid took command and executed a tactical retreat — later called "the Sword of Allah" |

### 630 CE — Conquest of Mecca (Ramadan, 8 AH)

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Treaty broken** | The Quraysh's allies (Banu Bakr) attacked a tribe in the Muslim alliance |
| **March on Mecca** | 10,000 Muslims marched toward Mecca — the largest force yet |
| **Peaceful entry** | Abu Sufyan accepted Islam; Muhammad entered Mecca with his head bowed in humility |
| **The Amnesty** | "Go, you are free" — general amnesty for the Quraysh, even his worst enemies |
| **Idols destroyed** | 360 idols around the Kaaba were smashed while reciting "Truth has come, falsehood has vanished" |
| **Bilal's adhan** | Bilal, the formerly enslaved Abyssinian, climbed the Kaaba and called the adhan |

📖 [[110 An-Nasr]] — the Farewell surah
📖 [[017 Al-Israa]] 17:81 — "Truth has come and falsehood has vanished"

### 630 CE — Battle of Ḥunayn & Siege of Ṭā'if

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Hawazin tribe** | A massive confederation advanced toward Mecca |
| **Initial rout** | The Muslims were initially ambushed and scattered |
| **Rally** | The Prophet stood firm with the Emigrants and Helpers; the tide turned |
| **Spoils & hearts** | The spoils of Hunayn were distributed to new Meccan converts (the "hearts to be reconciled") |

📖 [[009 At-Tawba]] 9:25–27 — the turning of the tide at Hunayn

### 631 CE — Expedition to Tabūk & the Year of Deputations (9–10 AH)

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Byzantine threat** | Rumours that the Byzantines were amassing in the north |
| **Tabuk march** | The most difficult expedition — summer, drought, long distance |
| **No battle** | The Byzantines did not engage; the expedition was a strategic demonstration |
| **Deputations** | Tribal delegations from across Arabia came to Medina to declare allegiance — "Year of Deputations" |
| **Surah al-Tawbah** | The final major revelation — regulations for the unified Muslim state |

📖 [[009 At-Tawba]] — the "ultimatum" surah, covering Tabuk and the final break with polytheism

### 632 CE — The Farewell Pilgrimage (*Ḥajjat al-Wadā'*)

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **March** | Tens of thousands accompanied the Prophet to Mecca |
| **The Sermon** | On the Plain of 'Arafat, delivered while seated on his camel: "O people, listen to me… I leave you two things: the Book of Allah and my Sunnah… A believer is a brother to another believer… You will meet your Lord, and He will ask you about your deeds…" |
| **The Verse of Perfection** | "This day I have perfected your religion for you." |

📖 [[005 Al-Maaida]] 5:3 — the verse of perfection

### 632 CE — The Final Days

| Event | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Return to Medina** | After the Hajj, fell ill |
| **Surah al-Nasr** | "When Allah's victory and the conquest come" (110:1) — understood as farewell |
| **Abu Bakr leads prayer** | Instructed Abu Bakr to lead the prayers in his place |
| **Last words** | "With the highest Companion" (*al-Rafīq al-A'lā*) |
| **Death** | Monday, 12 Rabi' al-Awwal, 11 AH (8 June 632 CE) — in Aisha's chamber, with his head in her lap |

📖 [[110 An-Nasr]] — the farewell surah
📚 [Bukhari 4440](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Companions) — the Prophet's final illness and death, narrated by Aisha

## Meccan vs. Medinan Surahs — The Biographical Grid

| Period | Characteristics | Key Surahs |
|--------|----------------|------------|
| **Early Meccan** (610–615) | Short, rhythmic, eschatological, intensely poetic | 96, 74, 73, 53, 81, 82, 84, 88, 91, 92, 93, 94, 97, 99–114 |
| **Middle Meccan** (615–619) | Longer narrative surahs with prophetic stories | 18, 19, 20, 21, 23, 25, 26, 27, 28, 29, 30, 31, 32, 36 |
| **Late Meccan** (619–622) | Debates with polytheists, more detailed arguments | 6, 7, 10, 11, 12, 14, 15, 16, 17, 22, 40, 41, 42, 43, 44, 45, 46 |
| **Early Medinan** (622–628) | Legislation, community building, battles | 2, 3, 4, 8, 24, 33, 47, 48, 49, 57, 58, 59, 60, 61, 62, 63, 64, 65, 66 |
| **Late Medinan** (628–632) | Consolidation, foreign relations, perfection | 5, 9, 110 |
