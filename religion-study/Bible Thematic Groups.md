# Bible Thematic Groups

A reference guide to the major thematic groups that run through the 66 books of the King James Bible. Each group links books that share a common subject, narrative arc, or theological theme. Use this note to navigate the Bible by theme and to see how individual books contribute to the larger biblical story.

> [!note] How the groups were built
> The 66 books of the Protestant canon were grouped by their dominant theological themes, narrative function, and literary genre. Many books belong to multiple groups — for example, [[01 Genesis|Genesis]] is simultaneously a creation narrative, a patriarchal story, the foundation of covenant theology, and the beginning of salvation history. The groups below consolidate these into navigable themes.

---

## 1. Creation & The Fall

The opening act of the biblical story: God creates the heavens and the earth, humanity is made in His image, sin enters through the Fall, and the creation is cursed. This group establishes the foundational questions that the rest of the Bible answers.

| Book | Key Contribution |
|------|-----------------|
| [[01 Genesis\|Genesis]] (1–3) | The six-day creation, the garden of Eden, the serpent, the Fall, the first promise of redemption (3:15) |
| [[19 Psalms\|Psalms]] (8, 19, 104) | The heavens declare the glory of God; humanity crowned with glory; meditations on creation |
| [[18 Job\|Job]] (38–42) | God's speeches from the whirlwind — the majestic and untamable creation that humanity cannot comprehend |
| [[45 Romans\|Romans]] (5:12–21) | The parallel of Adam and Christ — sin entered through one man, righteousness through another |
| [[46 1 Corinthians\|1 Corinthians]] (15:21–22, 45–49) | In Adam all die, in Christ all are made alive — the first and last Adam |
| [[66 Revelation\|Revelation]] (21–22) | The new heaven and new earth — creation restored beyond the Fall |
| [[23 Isaiah\|Isaiah]] (65:17–25) | The prophecy of new heavens and a new earth — peace restored |

**Core text:** [[01 Genesis|Genesis 1–3]]

**See also:** [[Adam - A Figure Traced]], [[Eve and Hawwa - A Figure Traced]], [[Noah and Nuh - A Figure Traced]], [[First Murder - Cain and Abel]], [[Flood Narratives Compared]]

## 2. Covenant & The Law

God establishes His covenant with Israel, giving them the Law as the constitution of their relationship with Him. This group covers the giving of the Law at Sinai, its expansion, and its theological meaning.

| Book | Key Contribution |
|------|-----------------|
| [[02 Exodus\|Exodus]] (19–40) | The Sinai covenant, the Ten Commandments, the Tabernacle, the priesthood |
| [[03 Leviticus\|Leviticus]] | The holiness code, sacrifices, purity laws, the Day of Atonement (*Yom Kippur*) |
| [[04 Numbers\|Numbers]] | The census, the wilderness legislation, the Levitical duties |
| [[05 Deuteronomy\|Deuteronomy]] | The second law — Moses' farewell speeches, the covenant renewed, blessings and curses |
| [[58 Hebrews\|Hebrews]] (7–10) | Christ as the new high priest and the perfect sacrifice — the old covenant fulfilled |
| [[48 Galatians\|Galatians]] (3–4) | The Law as a tutor to lead us to Christ — faith, not works of the Law |
| [[45 Romans\|Romans]] (9–11) | Israel and the Law — the righteousness of God apart from the Law |
| [[24 Jeremiah\|Jeremiah]] (31:31–34) | The promise of a new covenant written on the heart |

## 3. The Patriarchs

The foundational family stories of Israel: Abraham, Isaac, Jacob (Israel), and Joseph. God's promise of land, descendants, and blessing passes through this single family.

| Figure | Key Chapters | Narrative |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Abraham** | [[01 Genesis\|Genesis]] 12–25 | Called from Ur, the covenant of circumcision, the near-sacrifice of Isaac, hospitality to angels |
| **Isaac** | [[01 Genesis\|Genesis]] 21–28 | The promised son, the blessing stolen by Jacob, the wells of Gerar |
| **Jacob / Israel** | [[01 Genesis\|Genesis]] 25–50 | The birthright, the ladder to heaven, wrestling with God, twelve sons, the move to Egypt |
| **Joseph** | [[01 Genesis\|Genesis]] 37–50 | The coat of many colours, sold into slavery, rise to power in Egypt, the reunion and forgiveness |
| **Abraham references** | [[45 Romans\|Romans]] 4, [[48 Galatians\|Galatians]] 3, [[58 Hebrews\|Hebrews]] 11 | Abraham as the father of faith — justification by faith before the Law |
| **Jacob references** | [[39 Malachi\|Malachi]] 1:2–3, [[45 Romans\|Romans]] 9:10–13 | Jacob loved, Esau hated — the doctrine of election |

## 4. Exodus & Wilderness

The defining event of Israel's identity: deliverance from Egypt, the crossing of the Red Sea, the wilderness journey, and the generation that failed to enter the Promised Land.

- [[02 Exodus\|Exodus]] (1–18) — The ten plagues, the Passover, the Red Sea crossing, manna and water in the wilderness
- [[02 Exodus\|Exodus]] (19–40) — The covenant at Sinai, the golden calf, the Tabernacle built
- [[04 Numbers\|Numbers]] — The twelve spies, Korah's rebellion, the bronze serpent, Balaam
- [[05 Deuteronomy\|Deuteronomy]] (1–4) — Moses' review of the wilderness journey: "These forty years the Lord your God has been with you"
- [[13 1 Chronicles\|1 Chronicles]] (1–8) — The genealogies that trace Israel from Adam through the Exodus generations
- [[06 Joshua\|Joshua]] (1–5) — The crossing of the Jordan, the conquest begins
- [[19 Psalms\|Psalms]] (78, 105, 106, 114) — The wilderness as a pattern of rebellion and deliverance
- [[58 Hebrews\|Hebrews]] (3–4) — The wilderness generation as a warning — "Today if you hear His voice"
- [[44 Acts\|Acts]] (7) — Stephen's speech: the Exodus as the backbone of Israel's history
- [[65 Jude\|Jude]] 5 — "The Lord saved a people out of Egypt, but afterward destroyed those who did not believe"

**See also:** [[Moses and Musa - A Figure Traced]], [[Aaron and Harun - A Figure Traced]]

## 5. Conquest & Settlement

Israel enters the Promised Land under Joshua, settles in the time of the Judges, and transitions from a tribal confederacy to a monarchy. This is Israel's "iron age" history.

| Book | Key Contribution |
|------|-----------------|
| [[06 Joshua\|Joshua]] | The conquest of Canaan, the battle of Jericho, the sun stands still, the division of the land |
| [[07 Judges\|Judges]] | The cycle of sin → oppression → cry → deliverance; Deborah, Gideon, Samson |
| [[08 Ruth\|Ruth]] | A story of loyalty and redemption set "in the days when the judges ruled" |
| [[09 1 Samuel\|1 Samuel]] (1–7) | Samuel the prophet-judge, the ark captured, the transition to monarchy |
| [[19 Psalms\|Psalms]] (78, 132) | The history of Israel from Egypt to David's throne |

## 7. The Divided Kingdom & The Prophets

After Solomon's death, the kingdom splits into Israel (north, 10 tribes) and Judah (south, 2 tribes). The prophets call both kingdoms back to covenant faithfulness, warning of judgment and promising restoration.

### The Historical Narrative
- [[11 1 Kings\|1 Kings]] 12–22 — The division, Jeroboam's golden calves, Elijah vs. the prophets of Baal, Ahab and Jezebel
- [[12 2 Kings\|2 Kings]] — The fall of Israel (722 BCE) to Assyria, the fall of Judah (586 BCE) to Babylon

### The Writing Prophets (Pre-Exilic)

| Prophet | Book | Target | Key Message |
|---------|------|--------|-------------|
| **Jonah** | [[32 Jonah\|Jonah]] | Nineveh (Assyria) | God's mercy extends even to Israel's enemies |
| **Amos** | [[30 Amos\|Amos]] | Israel (north) | Social justice — "Let justice roll down like waters" |
| **Hosea** | [[28 Hosea\|Hosea]] | Israel (north) | God as a betrayed husband — the marriage metaphor |
| **Isaiah** | [[23 Isaiah\|Isaiah]] | Judah | Holiness, the Servant Songs, the coming Messiah |
| **Micah** | [[33 Micah\|Micah]] | Judah | "What does the Lord require? Justice, mercy, humility" |
| **Jeremiah** | [[24 Jeremiah\|Jeremiah]] | Judah | The weeping prophet, the new covenant, the fall of Jerusalem |
| **Habakkuk** | [[35 Habakkuk\|Habakkuk]] | Judah | "The just shall live by his faith" — theodicy in the face of Babylonian invasion |
| **Zephaniah** | [[36 Zephaniah\|Zephaniah]] | Judah | The Day of the Lord as judgment and restoration |

### The Writing Prophets (Exilic & Post-Exilic)
- [[25 Lamentations\|Lamentations]] — Five poems mourning the destruction of Jerusalem (attributed to Jeremiah)
- [[26 Ezekiel\|Ezekiel]] — Visions of God's glory departing and returning, the valley of dry bones, Gog and Magog
- [[27 Daniel\|Daniel]] — The furnace, the lions' den, the four empires, the Son of Man, the resurrection
- [[37 Haggai\|Haggai]] — "Consider your ways" — rebuild the Temple
- [[38 Zechariah\|Zechariah]] — Visions of restoration, the coming King riding on a donkey
- [[39 Malachi\|Malachi]] — "Return to me" — the last prophetic voice before 400 years of silence

**See also:** [[Elijah and Ilyas - A Figure Traced]], [[Elisha and Al-Yasa - A Figure Traced]], [[Jonah and Yunus - A Figure Traced]], [[Flood Narratives Compared]] (the prophetic warning pattern)

The most traumatic event in Israel's history — the destruction of Jerusalem, the Babylonian captivity, and the eventual return under Persia.

| Stage | Key Books | Key Events |
|-------|-----------|------------|

## 9. Wisdom Literature

The Bible's wisdom tradition: poetry, proverbs, philosophical reflection, and meditation on the meaning of life, suffering, and the fear of the Lord.

| Book | Genre | Key Themes |
|------|-------|------------|
| [[18 Job\|Job]] | Philosophical dialogue | Theodicy — why do the righteous suffer? The speeches of God from the whirlwind |
| [[19 Psalms\|Psalms]] | Lyric poetry / hymnody | Praise, lament, thanksgiving, trust, imprecation — the prayer book of the Bible |
| [[20 Proverbs\|Proverbs]] | Aphorisms / sayings | Practical wisdom: the fear of the Lord, the sluggard, the wise woman, the fool |
| [[21 Ecclesiastes\|Ecclesiastes]] | Philosophical reflection | "Vanity of vanities, all is vanity" — meaning under the sun, the fear of God |
| [[22 Song of Solomon\|Song of Solomon]] | Love poetry | The celebration of romantic love, an allegory of God's love for His people |

**Highlights:**
- [[18 Job\|Job]] 38–42 — "Where were you when I laid the earth's foundation?" — the climax of biblical theodicy
- [[19 Psalms\|Psalms]] 23 — "The Lord is my shepherd" — the most beloved psalm
- [[19 Psalms\|Psalms]] 51 — "Create in me a clean heart" — David's repentance
- [[19 Psalms\|Psalms]] 22 — "My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?" — quoted by Jesus on the cross
- [[20 Proverbs\|Proverbs]] 31 — The virtuous woman — the ideal of womanhood
- [[21 Ecclesiastes\|Ecclesiastes]] 3 — "To everything there is a season" — time and eternity
- [[22 Song of Solomon\|Song of Solomon]] 8:6–7 — "Love is strong as death"

**See also:** [[Job and Ayyub - A Figure Traced]] (the archetypal sufferer in wisdom tradition)

## 10. The Gospels — Life of Jesus

The four accounts of Jesus's life, death, and resurrection. Each Gospel has a distinct emphasis, audience, and portrait of Jesus.

| Gospel | Symbol | Portrait of Jesus | Key Emphasis |
|--------|--------|-------------------|--------------|
| [[40 Matthew\|Matthew]] | Lion | The King — the promised Messiah of Jewish prophecy | Teaching: five major discourses, the Sermon on the Mount |
| [[41 Mark\|Mark]] | Ox | The Servant — action-oriented, urgent, "immediately" | Suffering: the cross, the messianic secret |
| [[42 Luke\|Luke]] | Man | The Son of Man — compassionate, inclusive, prayerful | Parables: the Good Samaritan, the Prodigal Son |
| [[43 John\|John]] | Eagle | The Son of God — divine, theological, "I AM" | Identity: seven signs, seven "I am" sayings |

**Key passages:**
- **The Birth Narratives:** [[40 Matthew|Matthew]] 1–2, [[42 Luke|Luke]] 1–2 (see [[Birth of Jesus Across Scriptures]])
- **The Sermon on the Mount:** [[40 Matthew|Matthew]] 5–7 — the ethics of the kingdom
- **The Parables:** [[42 Luke|Luke]] 15 — the lost sheep, the lost coin, the prodigal son
- **The Passion:** [[40 Matthew|Matthew]] 26–27, [[41 Mark|Mark]] 14–15, [[42 Luke|Luke]] 22–23, [[43 John|John]] 18–19
- **The Resurrection:** [[40 Matthew|Matthew]] 28, [[41 Mark|Mark]] 16, [[42 Luke|Luke]] 24, [[43 John|John]] 20–21

**See also:** [[Jesus and Isa - A Figure Traced]], [[Mary and Maryam - A Figure Traced]], [[John the Baptist and Yahya - A Figure Traced]], [[Zachariah and Zakariyya - A Figure Traced]], [[Birth of Jesus Across Scriptures]]

## 11. Christology — Who Jesus Is

The New Testament's sustained reflection on the identity and nature of Jesus Christ — His divinity, humanity, and unique role as mediator between God and humanity.

| Book | Key Contribution |
|------|-----------------|
| [[43 John\|John]] (1, 10, 14, 20) | "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God" — the Logos, the "I AM" sayings, "My Lord and my God" |
| [[50 Philippians\|Philippians]] (2:5–11) | The *kenosis* (self-emptying) — Christ, being in the form of God, took the form of a servant |
| [[51 Colossians\|Colossians]] (1:15–20) | The supremacy of Christ — "the firstborn over all creation," "all things hold together in Him" |
| [[58 Hebrews\|Hebrews]] (1–4) | Christ superior to the angels, to Moses, to the Levitical priesthood — "the same yesterday, today, and forever" |
| [[66 Revelation\|Revelation]] (1, 5, 19, 22) | "I am the Alpha and the Omega" — the risen, glorified Christ; the Lamb on the throne |
| [[45 Romans\|Romans]] (1:3–4, 9:5) | Christ as the Son of David according to the flesh, the Son of God by the resurrection |
| [[46 1 Corinthians\|1 Corinthians]] (15) | The resurrection as the cornerstone of Christian faith — "if Christ is not raised, your faith is futile" |
| [[62 1 John\|1 John]] (1–5) | The Word of life — fellowship with the Father and the Son, the test of true belief |

## 14. Pauline Theology: Justification & Grace

The core of Paul's theological contribution: how sinners are declared righteous before God through faith in Christ, not by works of the Law.

| Book | Key Contribution |
|------|-----------------|
| [[45 Romans\|Romans]] (1–8) | The righteousness of God revealed from faith to faith — the most systematic statement of the gospel |
| [[45 Romans\|Romans]] (9–11) | Israel's place in God's plan — the mystery of hardening and the fullness of the Gentiles |
| [[48 Galatians\|Galatians]] (1–6) | "A man is not justified by works of the Law but through faith in Jesus Christ" |
| [[49 Ephesians\|Ephesians]] (1–3) | "By grace you have been saved through faith" — the unearned gift of God |
| [[50 Philippians\|Philippians]] (3) | "Not having my own righteousness from the Law, but that which is through faith" |
| [[51 Colossians\|Colossians]] (2) | "In Christ dwells all the fullness of the Godhead bodily" — the sufficiency of Christ |

**Key passages:**
- [[45 Romans\|Romans]] 3:21–26 — "The righteousness of God through faith in Jesus Christ to all who believe"
- [[45 Romans\|Romans]] 5:1 — "Being justified by faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ"
- [[45 Romans\|Romans]] 8:1 — "There is therefore now no condemnation to those who are in Christ Jesus"
- [[45 Romans\|Romans]] 8:31–39 — "Nothing can separate us from the love of God in Christ Jesus"

## How to Use These Groups

**To find books by theme:** Browse the group headings above and follow the wikilinks to Bible books that share that theme. For example, group 18 (Eschatology) will take you from [[27 Daniel]] to [[40 Matthew]] 24–25 to [[66 Revelation]].

**For thematic study:** Read a group sequentially — for instance, trace the theme of covenant (group 2) from its establishment in [[02 Exodus]] through its prophetic renewal in [[24 Jeremiah]] to its fulfilment in [[58 Hebrews]].

**For cross-referencing notes:** When writing about a topic (e.g., "justification by faith"), consult group 14 (Pauline Theology) and group 12 (Atonement & Salvation) to find all relevant passages.

**To understand individual books:** Each book's `related` field in its YAML frontmatter links to other books that share one or more of these thematic groups. The group above explains *why* they are connected.

**Paired study:** Use alongside [[Quran Thematic Groups]] and [[Hadith Thematic Groups]] to compare how the Bible, Quran, and Hadith treat shared themes like creation, prophecy, ethics, and eschatology.
