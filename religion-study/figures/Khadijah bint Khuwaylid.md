# Khadijah bint Khuwaylid

The first wife of the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ), the first person to embrace Islam, and one of the four perfect women of Paradise alongside Asiyah, Mary, and Fatimah.

---

## Biography

**Born:** c. 555 CE, Mecca  
**Died:** 619 CE (Year of Sorrow), Mecca  
**Tribe:** Banu Asad, Quraysh  
**Married:** Muhammad (ﷺ) — 595 CE until her death (24 years)  
**Children:** Zaynab, Ruqayyah, Umm Kulthum, Fatimah, Qasim, Abd Allah

Khadijah was a wealthy businesswoman — the first client of the Prophet's career as a trade agent. She employed him, then proposed marriage.

She was 40, he was 25. She remained his only wife until her death — 24 years of monogamy.

---

## Role in Early Islam

### The First Believer

When the Prophet received the first revelation in the Cave of Hira, terrified, he ran home:

> *"Cover me! Cover me!"*

Khadijah wrapped him and calmed him:

> *"No, by Allah, Allah will never disgrace you. You uphold the ties of kinship, speak the truth, bear the burdens of the people, give to the destitute, and help the needy."*

She took him to her cousin **Waraqah ibn Nawfal**, a Christian scholar who confirmed the revelation.

Thus Khadijah became the **first person to embrace Islam** — before any man, child, or slave.

### The Support

For 23 years, Khadijah was the Prophet's emotional and material anchor. She:
- Dedicated her entire fortune to the cause of Islam
- Endured the boycott of the Banu Hashim (616–619 CE)
- Absorbed the death of every child she bore (only Fatimah survived)
- Never wavered in her faith during the worst persecution

### The Year of Sorrow

In 619 CE, two deaths shattered the Prophet:
1. **Abu Talib** — his protective uncle
2. **Khadijah** — his wife, his refuge

The year was named *Ām al-Ḥuzn* (Year of Sorrow). The Prophet said of Khadijah:

> *"She believed in me when no one else did. She accepted Islam when people rejected me. She comforted me when no one else did."*

---

## Legacy

- One of the **four perfect women** (Ahmad 2663)
- The Prophet's first love — he never remarried a virgin (all later wives were widows except Aisha)
- Her daughter **Fatimah** would continue her legacy as the "mother of her father"
- The Prophet continued to honour her friends even after her death — Aisha said: "He never left the house without mentioning Khadijah"
