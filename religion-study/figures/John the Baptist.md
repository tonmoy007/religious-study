# John the Baptist

> The voice crying in the wilderness — the last of the Old Testament prophets and the first to announce the coming of Christ.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Parents** | Zechariah (priest) and Elizabeth (Mary's cousin) |
| **Birth** | Miraculous — announced by Gabriel to Zechariah in the Temple |
| **Role** | Prophet — forerunner of the Messiah |
| **Ministry** | c. 28–29 CE — baptizing in the Jordan River |
| **Death** | Beheaded by Herod Antipas — at the request of Salome (Mark 6) |

---

## Key Events

| Event | Scripture | Significance |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **The birth announced** | Luke 1:5–25 | Zechariah is struck mute for his unbelief — restored when John is named |
| **Leaping in the womb** | Luke 1:39–45 | John leaps in Elizabeth's womb when Mary (pregnant with Jesus) arrives |
| **Preaching in the wilderness** | Matthew 3:1–12 | "Repent — for the kingdom of heaven is at hand!" |
| **Baptizing Jesus** | Matthew 3:13–17 | "I need to be baptized by you" — Jesus insists to "fulfill all righteousness" |
| **The testimony** | John 1:29 | "Behold the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world!" |
| **Imprisonment and death** | Mark 6:14–29 | Condemned Herod's marriage to Herodias — beheaded |

---

## Connection with Islam

John (*Yaḥyā*) is a prophet in Islam. The Qur'an mentions him in multiple places (3:39, 6:85, 19:7–15, 21:90). The Qur'anic account:
- Confirms the miraculous birth — "We gave him wisdom while still a child"
- Emphasizes his piety: "He was devout, kind to his parents, not arrogant"
- Does **not** narrate his preaching, baptism of Jesus, or his beheading
- Presents him as a prophet of God — but not as the forerunner of the Messiah in the Christian sense
