# Isis — Egyptian Goddess

## Summary
Isis (*Aset*, "Throne") is the great mother goddess of ancient Egypt — the wife of [[Osiris — Egyptian God]], mother of [[Horus — Egyptian God]], and the supreme practitioner of magic (*heka*). She is the most important goddess in the Egyptian pantheon, the one goddess whose cult spread across the entire Greco-Roman world. In [[The Egyptian Book of the Dead — Stories, Figures and Connections|the Book of the Dead]], she appears as the protective mourner and restorer, without whom Osiris could not be resurrected.

## Role in the Book of the Dead

| Occurrence | Context |
|------------|---------|
| **Chapter 1** | Recitation: "Isis comes to her brother Osiris, rejoicing over him" |
| **Chapter 18** | Isis is invoked as protector of the deceased |

## Iconography
- Throne hieroglyph on her head (her name *Aset* literally means "throne")
- Sun disk between cow horns (syncretized with [[Hathor — Egyptian Goddess]])
- Wings outstretched — protecting the dead or Osiris's body
- Tyet amulet (the "Knot of Isis") — red, worn for protection in the afterlife
- Nursing [[Horus — Egyptian God|Horus]] — the divine mother-and-child image

## The Search for Osiris

Isis is the protagonist of the search-and-recovery narrative at the heart of [[The Osiris Myth — Egyptian Story]]:

1. **After the murder**: Isis cuts off a lock of her hair and dons mourning garments
2. **She searches the Nile**, following the chest northward to Byblos (Phoenicia)
3. **At Byblos**: She becomes the nurse of the king's child, sitting by a tamarisk tree that had grown around Osiris's chest
4. **Recovery**: She reveals herself, claims the chest, returns it to Egypt
5. **The dismemberment**: When [[Set — Egyptian God|Set]] scatters the 14 pieces, Isis travels the length of Egypt in a papyrus skiff, recovering each piece with [[Nephthys — Egyptian Goddess|Nephthys]]
6. **The resurrection**: With [[Thoth — Egyptian God|Thoth's]] wisdom and her own magic, Isis restores Osiris long enough to conceive Horus
7. **Hiding Horus**: She raises Horus in the marshes of Chemmis, protecting him from Set

## Powers & Attributes

### Great of Magic (*Weret Hekau*)
Isis is the supreme magician. She:
- Knows the **secret name of [[Ra — Egyptian God|Ra]]** (obtained by making him reveal it through a venomous serpent bite)
- Uses magic to **reassemble Osiris's body**
- Protects Horus with **spells against scorpions and serpents** (these become protective spells for children)
- Has power over **fate itself** — she can lengthen life

### Maternal Protection
- The *tyet* (Knot of Isis) is buried with the dead for protection
- Spell 156 of the Book of the Dead says: "The blood of Isis, the magic of Isis, the power of Isis shall be a protection to this great and glorious body"
- She is the archetypal mother goddess — parallel to Mary in Christianity, Demeter in Greek religion

## Syncretism & Spread

Isis became the most prominent Egyptian deity in the Roman world:
- **Greco-Roman Isis**: Temple at Philae (last to close, ~550 CE under Justinian)
- **Identified with**: Demeter, Aphrodite, Hera, Cybele, Fortuna
- **Isis Mysteries**: A mystery cult with initiation, processions, and promises of afterlife — a major competitor to early Christianity
- **Black Madonnas**: Some scholars suggest medieval Black Madonnas in Europe carry Isian iconography (throne, child, dark skin)
