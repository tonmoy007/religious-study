# Esther

> The Jewish orphan who became queen of Persia and saved her people from annihilation — a story of courage, hidden identity, and divine providence.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Hebrew name** | Hadassah (myrtle) |
| **Persian name** | Esther (star) |
| **Guardian** | Mordecai (cousin) |
| **Husband** | King Xerxes (Ahasuerus) of Persia |
| **Role** | Queen of Persia |
| **Feast** | Purim (established in Esther 9) |

---

## Key Virtues

| Virtue | Evidence |
|--------|----------|
| **Courage** | Approaches the king unsummoned — "If I perish, I perish" (4:16) |
| **Wisdom** | Waits for the right moment — two banquets before revealing Haman's plot |
| **Sacrifice** | Risks her life for her people |
| **Faith** | Fasts for three days before approaching the king |

---

## The Hidden God

Esther is the only book of the Bible that **never mentions God** — yet His presence is everywhere:
- The "coincidences" that place Esther on the throne
- The timing of Mordecai's overheard plot
- The king's sleepless night that leads to Haman's downfall

---

## Connection with Islam

Esther does not appear in the Qur'an. The entire story is set in the Persian period — a historical context the Qur'an does not narrate in detail. However, the theme of a woman in a position of power who saves her people resonates with the story of **Asiyah** (Pharaoh's wife), who used her position to protect Moses.
