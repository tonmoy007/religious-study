# Bilal ibn Rabah

**The first mu'adhdhin** (caller to prayer) in Islam — a former Abyssinian slave who became one of the most honoured companions of the Prophet. His voice was the first to call the Adhan from the Kaaba.

---

## Biography

- **Born:** c. 580 CE, Mecca (of Abyssinian/Ethiopian origin)
- **Died:** c. 640 CE, Damascus or Medina
- **Mother:** Hamamah (a slave woman)
- **Status:** Slave → Free man → Companion → First mu'adhdhin
- **Known for:** Beautiful voice, unwavering faith under torture

---

## Key Moments

### The Torture

Bilal was a slave of **Umayyah ibn Khalaf**, a Quraysh leader. When Bilal declared his faith in Islam, Umayyah tortured him brutally: he was laid on the burning sand of Mecca with a massive stone on his chest, forced to renounce Islam. Bilal only repeated:

> *"Ahad, Ahad"* — "One (God), One."

**Abu Bakr** saw Bilal being tortured, bought his freedom from Umayyah, and emancipated him.

### The First Call to Prayer

When the Adhan (call to prayer) was established in Medina (1 AH), the Prophet chose Bilal as the first mu'adhdhin because of his beautiful, powerful voice. Bilal would climb the highest rooftop and call:

> *Allāhu Akbar, Allāhu Akbar…*

His voice became synonymous with the Muslim community.

### The Conquest of Mecca

When the Prophet conquered Mecca in 630 CE, he ordered Bilal to climb the **Kaaba** and call the Adhan. A former slave stood above the Kaaba — the spiritual centre of Arabia — and proclaimed the oneness of God. This was the most powerful statement of Islam's spiritual equality.

The Quraysh stood in disbelief. Some said: "Allah honoured Bilal above us?" Others wept.

---

## Legacy

- **First mu'adhdhin of Islam**
- Symbol of Islam's racial and social equality — an African former slave given the highest honour
- After the Prophet's death, Bilal stopped calling the Adhan, saying he could not bear it without the Prophet
- He migrated to Syria and died around 640 CE
- The Prophet said: "Bilal is from the people of Paradise."
