# Salman al-Farsi

**Salman the Persian** — the Persian companion whose ingenious suggestion to dig a trench saved Medina from the Confederates. His life was a remarkable spiritual journey across three continents in search of truth.

---

## Biography

- **Born:** c. 568 CE, Isfahan (Persia), in a wealthy Zoroastrian family
- **Died:** c. 657 CE (35–36 AH), Ctesiphon (Iraq) or Medina
- **Title:** *Salman al-Khayr* — Salman the Good
- **Known for:** The trench strategy, long search for truth, special closeness to the Prophet

---

## The Search for Truth

Salman was born into a devout Zoroastrian family. He was put in charge of the fire that his family worshipped — tending to it day and night.

One day, he noticed his father praying to something other than the fire. This planted a seed of doubt. He asked: "What is this?" and the question could not be suppressed.

He heard of Christianity and fled to Syria, joining a bishop's service. When the bishop died, he travelled to Mosul, then to Nusaybin, then to Ammuria, following a chain of scholars — each of whom told him before death: "Go to so-and-so."

The last scholar before his death told him:

> *"The religion you seek has come — there is no prophet now. But a prophet is about to appear in the land of the Arabs, who will migrate to a land of date-palms between two rocky plains. He will accept gifts but not charity, and the seal of prophethood is between his shoulders. There are many Jews there. Go if you can."*

Salman sold everything and joined a trade caravan heading to Arabia. The caravan betrayed him and sold him into slavery to a Jewish man in Medina.

## The Trench Strategy

When the Confederates marched on Medina (5 AH), the Muslims debated how to defend the city. The traditional Arabs had never seen siege warfare — city defence meant fighting in the streets.

Salman, with his Persian military knowledge, proposed:

> *"O Messenger of Allah, when we were in Persia and were besieged, we would dig a trench around the city."*

The Prophet immediately adopted the plan. The trench was dug — 5 kilometres long — and for the first time in Arabian warfare, an army was stopped by a ditch.

When the Confederates arrived and saw the trench, they were confounded. **Khalid ibn al-Walid** (then still a pagan) called it "a trick we Arabs never use."

---

## Legacy

- The Prophet declared: "Salman is from the family of the House" — honouring him as *Ahl al-Bayt*
- After the Prophet's death, he became governor of Ctesiphon (Mada'in)
- Known for his humility — he earned his living by weaving baskets
- The only companion from Persian origin — symbol of Islam's universalism
- The Prophet said: "If faith were at the Pleiades, the sons of Persia would reach it."
