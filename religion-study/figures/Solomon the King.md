# Solomon the King

> The wisest and wealthiest king of Israel — builder of the Temple, author of Proverbs and Ecclesiastes, whose heart was ultimately led astray by his love for foreign women.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Father** | David |
| **Mother** | Bathsheba |
| **Reign** | c. 970–930 BCE — 40 years |
| **Temple** | Built the **First Temple** in Jerusalem |
| **Wisdom** | Asked for wisdom — God gave him "a wise and discerning heart" (1 Kgs 3:12) |
| **Wealth** | Received 666 talents of gold per year — the richest king of his age |
| **Wives** | 700 wives + 300 concubines — many from nations God forbade |
| **Fall** | His foreign wives turned his heart to other gods (1 Kgs 11:1–13) |
| **Death** | Natural — succeeded by his son Rehoboam (whose folly split the kingdom) |

---

## Key Achievements

| Achievement | Scripture | Significance |
|-------------|-----------|--------------|
| **Judgment of the two mothers** | 1 Kings 3:16–28 | Wise as his wisdom — the famous "cut the baby in half" verdict |
| **Building the Temple** | 1 Kings 6–8 | The permanent dwelling place of God in Jerusalem — 7 years to build |
| **The Queen of Sheba** | 1 Kings 10 | International recognition of his wisdom and wealth |
| **Proverbs** | Book of Proverbs | Wisdom literature — "The fear of the Lord is the beginning of knowledge" |
| **Ecclesiastes** | Book of Ecclesiastes | "Vanity of vanities — all is vanity" — wisdom from the end of life's journey |

---

## The Fall

The Bible does not idealize Solomon. Despite his wisdom:
- He married **foreign women** (against Torah commands)
- They "turned his heart after other gods" — Ashtoreth, Chemosh, Molech
- The kingdom was torn from his son — judgment for his idolatry

His story is a cautionary tale: **wisdom does not guarantee obedience**.

---

## Connection with Islam

Solomon (*Sulaymān*) is a prophet in Islam. The Qur'an portrays him much more positively:
- He is a **prophet-king**, not a flawed monarch who fell into idolatry
- He commands the **jinn**, the **wind**, and speaks to **ants and birds** (27:15–44)
- The Queen of Sheba (Bilqis) submits to God through him
- The Qur'an specifically denies that Solomon was an idolater: "It was not Solomon who disbelieved — but the devils disbelieved" (2:102)
