# Sita

> The goddess Lakshmi incarnate — the daughter of the earth, the wife of Rama, the queen who walked through fire and was swallowed by her own mother.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Role** | Princess of Mithila, Queen of Ayodhya; Lakshmi's avatar |
| **Parents** | Found in a furrow by King Janaka — born of the earth |
| **Husband** | Rama |
| **Sons** | Lava and Kusha (twins, born in Valmiki's ashram) |
| **Previous birth** | Vedavati (some traditions) — Lakshmi's incarnation |

## Personhood

Sita is one of the most complex female figures in world scripture:

| Quality | Expression |
|---------|------------|
| **Loyalty** | Insists on sharing Rama's exile |
| **Compassion** | Steps outside the magic circle to help a beggar — the fatal error born of virtue |
| **Strength** | Refuses Ravana's advances for an entire year |
| **Dignity** | Speaks harshly to Rama during the Agni Pariksha — "you cannot doubt me" |
| **Autonomy** | Chooses the earth over Rama in the end — "take me back, mother" |

---

## Sita in Islamic and Jewish Traditions

Some scholars have noted parallels between Sita and **Hagar** (the abandoned mother in the desert), and between her fire trial and the story of **Abraham's ordeal in the fire** (found in Islamic tradition, where Nimrod throws Abraham into flames).
