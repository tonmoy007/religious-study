# Daniel

> The prophet in exile — statesman, interpreter of dreams, and seer of the end times. His life in Babylon and his visions of the future shape both Jewish apocalyptic and Christian eschatology.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Exile** | Taken to Babylon c. 605 BCE (first deportation) |
| **Name change** | **Belteshazzar** (Babylonian name) |
| **Role** | Advisor to Babylonian and Persian kings |
| **Status** | "Greatly beloved" by God (9:23, 10:11, 10:19) |
| **Death** | Tradition: died in Babylon, buried in Susa |

---

## Key Events

| Event | Scripture | Significance |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| **The king's dream** | Daniel 2 | Interprets Nebuchadnezzar's dream of the statue — four kingdoms |
| **The fiery furnace** | Daniel 3 | His friends refuse to bow — delivered from fire |
| **Nebuchadnezzar's madness** | Daniel 4 | The king becomes a beast for seven years — then humbled |
| **The writing on the wall** | Daniel 5 | "Mene, Mene, Tekel, Upharsin" — Belshazzar's fall |
| **The lions' den** | Daniel 6 | Daniel prays despite the decree — delivered from lions |
| **Vision of the four beasts** | Daniel 7 | Four kingdoms rising from the sea — the Son of Man |
| **The 70 weeks** | Daniel 9 | Prophecy of the Messiah's coming |
| **The final vision** | Daniel 10–12 | Kings of the north and south — the resurrection of the dead |

---

## Key Prophecies

| Prophecy | Passage | Interpretation |
|----------|---------|----------------|
| **The Son of Man** | 7:13–14 | "One like a son of man came with the clouds of heaven" — interpreted by Jesus as referring to himself (Mark 14:62) |
| **The 70 weeks** | 9:24–27 | 70 weeks of years (490 years) until the Messiah is cut off — debated between Christian and Jewish interpreters |
| **The resurrection** | 12:2 | "Many of those who sleep in the dust shall awake — some to everlasting life, some to shame" — the only clear OT reference to resurrection |

---

## Connection with Islam

Daniel (*Dānyāl*) is **not named in the Qur'an**, though some Islamic traditions regard him as a prophet. Hadith literature records that the Prophet ﷺ said Daniel was a prophet whose body was preserved by God. Some Islamic historians (Ṭabarī) narrate expanded versions of the lions' den story. The Qur'an does reference the **sleepers in the cave** (18:9–26) — a different story but one that shares the theme of divine protection through a supernatural sleep.
