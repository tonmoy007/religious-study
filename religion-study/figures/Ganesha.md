# Ganesha

> The elephant-headed god — the remover of obstacles, the patron of arts and sciences, and the most universally loved deity in Hinduism.

## The Story of the Elephant Head

Parvati, while bathing, creates a boy from the turmeric paste on her body and sets him to guard the door. Shiva returns — the boy blocks him. Enraged, Shiva cuts off the boy's head.

Parvati is devastated. Shiva sends his servants: "Bring the head of the first creature you find facing north." They bring the head of an elephant. Shiva places it on the boy's body and brings him back to life — Ganesha.

---

## The Tusk

The poet **Vyasa** needs a scribe for the Mahabharata. Ganesha agrees — on condition that Vyasa dictation never stops. Vyasa agrees — on condition that Ganesha understands every word before writing it. Ganesha's pen breaks mid-verse — he breaks off his **tusk** and uses it as a pen. The Mahabharata is completed.

---

## Worship

Ganesha is invoked at the beginning of every undertaking — prayers, ceremonies, journeys, new businesses. His elephant head symbolizes wisdom; his large belly, the capacity to digest everything — good and bad. He is the **lord of beginnings**.
