# Bhishma

> The grandfather of the Kuru dynasty — a warrior bound by a terrible oath, the commander who loved both sides, who chose the moment of his own death.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Role** | Grandfather of the Kurus, commander of the Kaurava army (Days 1–10) |
| **Birth name** | Devavrata |
| **Parents** | Shantanu and Ganga |
| **Celibacy** | Vowed lifelong — so no son of his could claim the throne |

---

## Bhishma's Tragic Position

Bhishma is **bound** — by his vow, he must serve whoever sits on the throne of Hastinapura, even if he knows they are wrong. He fights for the Kauravas — but he loves the Pandavas.

- He **knows** the dice game is wrong. He sits silent.
- He **knows** Draupadi's humiliation is wrong. He sits silent.
- He **knows** the Kauravas will lose. He fights anyway.

On his deathbed (a bed of arrows), he teaches Yudhishthira the art of kingship — the *Bhishma Parva* contains his wisdom lectures.

---

## Death at Will

Bhishma was granted **ichcha-mrityu** (death at will) by his father. He waits on the battlefield for 58 days — suspended on a bed of arrows — until the sun moves to the northern solstice (a favorable time to die). Then he releases his life.
