# Horus — Egyptian God

## Summary
Horus is the sky god, the son of [[Osiris — Egyptian God]] and [[Isis — Egyptian Goddess]], and the divine prototype of the pharaoh. He is the **avenger of his father** — the god who defeats [[Set — Egyptian God|Set]] and reclaims the kingship of Egypt. In [[The Egyptian Book of the Dead — Stories, Figures and Connections|the Book of the Dead]], Horus appears as the protector of the deceased, the intermediary with the gods, and the model of the justified heir.

## The Two Horuses

Egyptian tradition distinguishes two forms:

| Aspect | Horus the Elder (*Heru-ur*) | Horus the Child (*Heru-pa-khered*) |
|--------|---------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Nature | Primordial sky god, son of [[Nut — Egyptian Goddess|Nut]] and [[Geb — Egyptian God|Geb]] | Son of Osiris and Isis |
| Role | Brother of Osiris, Set, Isis, Nephthys | Avenger of Osiris, heir to the throne |
| Icon | Pure falcon, sun-disk wings | Child with side-lock, finger to mouth |
| Greek | Haroeris | Harpocrates |
| Key act | — | Defeats Set, reclaims the kingdom |

In the Book of the Dead, both forms appear — but the *younger Horus* (the avenger) is central.

## The Contendings of Horus and Set

See full treatment at [[The Osiris Myth — Egyptian Story]].

The Ennead (the nine great gods) must adjudicate the succession:

| Claimant | Argument | Outcome |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **Horus** | Son of the murdered king; rightful heir | Supported by Isis, Thoth, and eventually the Ennead |
| **Set** | Stronger; protected Egypt from Apepi nightly; older | Supported by [[Ra — Egyptian God|Ra]] (initially) because "a son of a dead man cannot rule" |

The contest includes:
- **Transformation into hippopotami** — whose can stay underwater longer
- **Theft of Horus's eye** by Set
- **Isis's clever intervention** — she throws a harpoon at Set, then feels pity and heals him, angering Horus
- **Horus's victory** — Set is defeated, Horus is crowned, and Set becomes the god of storms, foreign lands, and the retinue of [[Ra — Egyptian God|Ra]]

## Horus's Eye (*Wadjet*) — The Ultimate Symbol

The Eye of Horus is the most important protective symbol in Egyptian religion:

- **Myth**: Set tore out Horus's eye during the contest; [[Thoth — Egyptian God|Thoth]] restored it (the *Wadjet* — "the restored one")
- **Book of the Dead**: Spell 167 invokes the Eye for protection; it is painted on coffins and amulets
- **Mathematics**: Each part of the eye represents a fraction (1/2, 1/4, 1/8, 1/16, 1/32, 1/64) — the mythical basis of Egyptian fractions
- **Meaning**: Healing, sacrifice, restoration, offering

## Horus and the Pharaoh

Every pharaoh is the "Living Horus" in his lifetime — the god incarnate on earth. At death, the pharaoh becomes "Osiris N." and his successor becomes the new Horus. This cycle mirrors:
- Horus → Osiris → Horus (divine succession)
- Living king → Dead king → Living king (royal succession)
- Son → Father → Son (human succession)

- "Horus of the Horizon" (*Horakhty* — syncretized with [[Ra — Egyptian God|Ra]])
- "Lord of the Two Lands"
- "He whose eye is restored"
- "The Child who shines in the marsh"
