# Anubis — Egyptian God

## Summary
Anubis (*Inpu*) is the jackal-headed god of embalming, funerals, and the afterlife — the ultimate psychopomp. He guides the soul from the tomb to the Hall of Righteousness, oversees the mummification process, and weighs the heart against [[Ma'at — Goddess of Truth|Ma'at's]] feather in [[The Weighing of the Heart — Egyptian Story]]. Of all Egyptian gods, Anubis has the most constant presence in [[The Egyptian Book of the Dead — Stories, Figures and Connections|the Book of the Dead]], appearing in nearly every funerary spell.

## Iconography
- **Jackal / dog head** — black or blackish (jackals were observed in cemeteries, hence the association with death)
- **Full black body** — not the natural colour of a jackal; black symbolises the fertile Nile mud, regeneration
- **Was-sceptre** in one hand (power), *ankh* in the other (life)
- In the judgment vignette: **grey or black jackal-head**, wearing a red ribbon, holding the scales
- Sometimes shown as a **fully recumbent jackal** on a shrine (the "Jackal on his Hill")

## Roles

### The Embalmer
Anubis presides over every stage of mummification:
1. Washing the body with natron (salt)
2. Removing the organs (stored in canopic jars guarded by the four sons of Horus)
3. Wrapping the body in linen
4. The "Opening of the Mouth" ceremony — restoring the senses
5. Placing amulets (heart scarab, tyet, djed pillar) in the wrappings

He is the divine prototype of the *sem* priest who performs the rituals.

### The Psychopomp
Anubis leads the soul on the journey through the Tuat:
- Guards the entrance to the underworld
- Guides past the gatekeepers and guardians (who must be named — see [[The Journey Through the Tuat — Egyptian Story]])
- Presents the soul to Osiris in the Hall of Righteousness

### The Weigher of Hearts
In the judgment hall:
- Anubis brings the deceased's heart to the scales
- He places the feather of [[Ma'at — Goddess of Truth]] on the other side
- He adjusts the counterweight and watches the balance
- [[Thoth — Egyptian God|Thoth]] records the result
- If the heart is light as the feather, the soul passes to [[Osiris — Egyptian God|Osiris]]
- If heavy, [[Ammut — The Devourer]] consumes it

### The Guardian of the Mysteries
Anubis "knows what is in the Tuat." The spells of the Book of the Dead are repeatedly described as "the words which Anubis spake" — he is the source of the secret knowledge that enables the soul to navigate the underworld.

## Anubis and the Opening of the Mouth

The most important funerary ritual in Egypt, the "Opening of the Mouth" (*wpt-r*), is performed by Anubis:
- The *sem* priest (masked as Anubis) touches the mummy's mouth, eyes, nose, and ears with ritual implements (including the *pesh-kef* blade and the *ur-heka* adze)
- This restores the deceased's ability to speak, see, hear, and eat in the afterlife
- Without this ritual, the deceased cannot pronounce the passwords in the Tuat or claim justification in the Hall
