# Set — Egyptian God

## Summary
Set (also Seth, Sutekh) is the god of chaos, storms, violence, the desert, and foreign lands — the usurper and adversary in Egyptian myth. He murders his brother [[Osiris — Egyptian God|Osiris]], contests the kingship with [[Horus — Egyptian God|Horus]], and is the closest figure in Egyptian religion to a "villain" or "devil." Yet he is also a **necessary protector** — he rides with [[Ra — Egyptian God|Ra]] on the Solar Barque every night, defending the sun god against [[Apepi — Egyptian Serpent God|Apepi]], the chaos serpent. [[The Egyptian Book of the Dead — Stories, Figures and Connections|The Book of the Dead]] treats him with ambivalence: he is dangerous, but he is also a god who must be propitiated and at times invoked.

## Iconography

Set is the only Egyptian god with a **non-animal head** — the "Set animal" (sometimes called a *typhonic beast*):

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Head/Snout** | Curved, downward-turning snout (like an aardvark/anteater/okapi hybrid) |
| **Ears** | Square, upright, stiff |
| **Tail** | Forked (like a demon or monster in later tradition) |
| **Colour** | Red — the colour of the desert, of blood, of chaos |
| **Attire** | Wears the *deshret* (Red Crown of Lower Egypt) in some depictions |

No actual animal corresponds exactly to Set's form — he is deliberately *other*, a composite monster.

## Ambiguous Moral Status

Unlike Satan in Abrahamic religion, Set is not purely evil:

| Positive Role | Negative Role |
|--------------|---------------|
| **Protector of Ra** — stands on the solar barque, spearing [[Apepi — Egyptian Serpent God|Apepi]] | **Murderer of Osiris** — kills his brother through trickery |
| **God of strength** — the pharaoh sometimes associated himself with Set's power | **Usurper** — tries to take the throne illegitimately |
| **Storm god** — brings rain to the desert (life-giving in small doses) | **God of chaos** — represented the untamed, dangerous forces of nature |
| **Protector of caravans** — in the desert, Set is the one who knows the way | **Lord of foreign lands** — associated with invaders and foreigners |

In the earlier periods (Old Kingdom), Set was not an enemy god — he protected Ra. In the later periods (especially after the Hyksos and during the Ptolemaic period), he was increasingly demonized. The post-Exilic Jewish identification of Set with the Canaanite god Ba'al contributed to his negative image in Hellenistic sources.

## The Contendings of Horus and Set

See full treatment at [[The Osiris Myth — Egyptian Story]].

The legal contest before the Ennead:

| Round | Event | Significance |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | **Set proposes**: "Let me be judged stronger" — trial by combat | Set's argument is *might makes right* |
| 2 | **The hippopotamus contest**: Both transform into hippos underwater | Set tries to drown Horus |
| 3 | **The eye theft**: Set tears out Horus's eye | Horus is temporarily weakened |
| 4 | **Set's humiliation**: Horus beheads Set; Isis heals Set | The mother goddess's pity complicates the moral picture |
| 5 | **Judgment**: The Ennead awards the throne to Horus | Ma'at prevails; Set is relegated to the desert |

**Resolution**: Set does not lose. Rather, [[Ra — Egyptian God|Ra]] declares: "Let Set be given to me. He shall be with me as my son, and he shall thunder in the sky and be feared." — Set becomes the storm god in Ra's retinue.

## Set as a Figure in Comparative Religion

| Parallel | Tradition | Comparison |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Satan / The Adversary** | Christianity, Judaism, Islam | Both are accusers/adversaries who ultimately serve divine purpose. But Set doesn't rebel against the high god — he fights *alongside* Ra. |
| **Angra Mainyu / Ahriman** | Zoroastrianism | A genuine dualist adversary — Set is not dualist in the same sense; he is contained within the Egyptian cosmos |
| **Loki** | Norse | Both are tricksters who cause chaos and kill a beloved god (Osiris/Balder), yet are needed by the gods |
| **Typhon** | Greek | A chaos monster defeated by Zeus — Set was identified with Typhon by the Greeks (hence "Typhonic beast") |
| **Apepi/Apophis** | Egyptian | The *true* chaos serpent — Set fights *against* him, so Set is not equated with absolute chaos |
