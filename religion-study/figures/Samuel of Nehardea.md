# Samuel of Nehardea (שמואל)

Shmuel, known in Aramaic as Samuel Yarhina'ah ("the Astronomer"), was one of the two great first-generation Amoraim of Babylonia, alongside Rav. He headed the Nehardea Academy and was renowned for his expertise in both Jewish law (halakhah) and the natural sciences — particularly astronomy, medicine, and calendrics. His most famous legal principle, **Dina de-Malkhuta Dina** ("The law of the land is the law"), became a cornerstone of Jewish political theory.

Samuel was born in Nehardea, a major centre of Babylonian Jewry. Unlike Rav, he studied primarily in Babylonia, though he is believed to have spent some time in the Land of Israel. His father, Abba bar Abba, was a prominent scholar, and Samuel inherited both his father's position and his library. Samuel's academy in Nehardea rivalled Rav's academy in Sura, and the two sages — though often disagreeing — maintained a relationship of profound mutual respect. Samuel's expertise in astronomy allowed him to calculate the Jewish calendar independently, and he famously declared that he could determine the dates of festivals without relying on the Palestinian calendar.

## Key Contributions

- **Dina de-Malkhuta Dina (דִּינָא דְּמַלְכוּתָא דִּינָא):** The principle that the civil law of the host country is binding on Jews in all matters of property, taxation, and commerce. This became the foundational rule for Jewish life under non-Jewish governments throughout the diaspora (Bava Kamma 113a–b).
- **Astronomical Expertise:** Samuel was called Yarhina'ah ("the astronomer") because of his mastery of the lunar cycles. He taught that "the paths of the sky are as clear to me as the paths of Nehardea" (Berakhot 58b), and his calculations contributed to the development of the fixed Jewish calendar.
- **Medical Knowledge:** The Talmud records Samuel's medical teachings, including treatments for various ailments. He stated that a physician who cures for free is worth nothing (meaning the patient does not value the treatment), and his medical rulings often intersect with halakhic decisions.
- **Civil Law Specialisation:** Samuel was the leading authority on civil and monetary law in the Talmud. His rulings on property, loans, and business partnerships are foundational to Jewish commercial law.

## In Jewish Tradition

- The Talmud regularly frames debates as "Rav said" vs. "Shmuel said" — their disagreements are among the most fundamental in the Bavli
- His astronomical knowledge was so respected that the Palestinian sages deferred to him on calendar matters
- The principle of Dina de-Malkhuta Dina has been cited for nearly two millennia as the halakhic basis for Jewish loyalty to the state
- The Nehardea Academy was destroyed during the Palmyrene invasions (c. 260 CE), but its traditions were continued in Pumbedita
