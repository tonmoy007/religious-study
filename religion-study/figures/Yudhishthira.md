# Yudhishthira

> The son of Dharma — the eldest Pandava, the righteous king whose one weakness (gambling) nearly destroyed everything, and the only mortal to enter heaven in his physical body.

---

| Detail | Information |

| **Father** | Dharma (the god of justice) — thus born righteous |
| **Mother** | Kunti |
| **Wife** | Draupadi (shared) |

## The Yaksha Prashna

During the exile, Yudhishthira finds his four brothers dead by a lake. A voice (the Yaksha) asks him riddles — the answers determine whether his brothers live:

- "What is the greatest wonder?" → *Every day, people die — and those who remain act as if they are immortal.*
- "What is heavier than earth?" → *A mother.*
- "What is faster than wind?" → *The mind.*
- "What is the path to happiness?" → *Following dharma.*

---

## Yudhishthira's Flaw

Yudhishthira is the **dharma-king** — but he has a gambler's weakness. He knows he should not play the dice game — he plays anyway, because "a king cannot refuse a challenge."

His silent suffering after losing Draupadi is one of the most poignant elements of the epic.
