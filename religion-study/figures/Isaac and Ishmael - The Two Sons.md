# Isaac & Ishmael — The Two Sons

> Part of [[Cross-Scripture Connections MOC]] · a **single-figure deep dive**

Abraham's two sons are where the Abrahamic family literally **splits into two
peoples**. The Bible traces the covenant through **Isaac** (ancestor of Israel)
and sends **Ishmael** away; the Qur'an honours **Ishmael** as a prophet and the
co-founder of the Kaaba, ancestor of the Arabs — the line that leads to Muhammad.
Same two boys, two genealogies of election. A **genealogical** connection that
completes [[Abraham and Ibrahim - A Figure Traced]].

## The Bible: the covenant runs through Isaac
Genesis blesses Ishmael but is explicit that the **covenant** passes to Isaac:

> [!quote] Genesis — Ishmael blessed, Isaac chosen
> **16:11** — "thou shalt call his name **Ishmael**…"
> **16:12** — "he will be a **wild man**; his hand will be against every man… and he shall dwell in the presence of all his brethren."
> **17:20** — "as for **Ishmael**… I will make him **a great nation**." *(but 17:21: "my covenant will I establish with **Isaac**")*
> **21:18, 20** — "I will make him a great nation… and **God was with the lad**; and he… became an archer."

Ishmael is genuinely blessed — "twelve princes shall he beget" — but
**dismissed from the covenant line**, sent into the wilderness with his mother
Hagar. Isaac is the child of promise; through him come Jacob/Israel and the
[[Jacob and Yaqub - A Figure Traced|twelve tribes]].

## The Qur'an: Ishmael the prophet and Kaaba-builder
The Qur'an elevates **Ismail** to full prophethood and makes him central, not
peripheral:

> [!quote] Maryam 19:54–55 — Ishmael the prophet
> "make mention in the Scripture of **Ishmael**. Lo! he was a **keeper of his promise**, and he was a **messenger (of Allah), a prophet**. He enjoined upon his people worship and almsgiving."

> [!quote] Al-Baqara 2:127 — building the Kaaba (recalled)
> "when **Abraham and Ishmael were raising the foundations of the House** (at Makka)…"

And crucially, the Qur'an lists **both** sons together in its chain of patriarchs,
without ranking — "the god of thy fathers, Abraham and **Ishmael and Isaac**"
([[Jacob and Yaqub - A Figure Traced|Al-Baqara 2:133]]). Ishaq (Isaac) too is a
prophet (Maryam 19:49) — but Ishmael, the Bible's *outsider*, is the Qur'an's
**insider**.

## The altar question
The two sons are also the unresolved fork of the [[Abraham and Ibrahim - A Figure Traced|near-sacrifice]]:
- **Bible** — names **Isaac** as the bound son (Genesis 22:2).
- **Qur'an** — leaves the son **unnamed** (As-Saaffat 37:102); Muslim tradition
  identifies **Ishmael**.

So even the supreme test of faith points to a different son in each tradition.

## Same two boys, two peoples
| | Hebrew Bible | Qur'an |
|---|---|---|
| Covenant line | **Isaac** (→ Jacob → Israel) | both honoured; Arabs via **Ishmael** |
| Ishmael's status | blessed but **sent away**; "a wild man" | a **prophet**, builder of the Kaaba |
| The bound son | **Isaac** | unnamed → **Ishmael** (tradition) |
| Leads to | the people of Israel | the Arabs, and ultimately **Muhammad** |

This is the genealogical **root of the divergence** that every Abrahamic deep
dive keeps circling back to: one father, two sons, two chosen lines — and from
that fork, two of the world's great families of faith (see
[[Shared Abrahamic Figures]] and [[Abrahamic Traditions]]).
