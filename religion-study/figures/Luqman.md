# Luqman

The wise sage of the Qur'an — not a prophet, but a man to whom Allah granted profound wisdom (*ḥikmah*). An entire surah ([[031 Luqman]]) is named after him, and his advice to his son is one of the most beautiful passages in the Qur'an — a parenting manual in eight verses.

---

## Biography

- **Status:** Righteous sage (*walī*), given wisdom by Allah — NOT a prophet
- **Origin:** Nubian/Sudanese (some say Ethiopian) — a black man of humble origin
- **Appearance:** Described as thick-lipped, from the slave class
- **Known for:** Wisdom, right judgment, piety, profound counsel

---

## The Identity Debate

Classical exegetes differed:

| View | Proponents |
|------|-----------|
| A **righteous slave** of Nubian origin who became a judge | Ibn Abbas |
| A **wise man**, known for sound judgment among his people | Mujahid |
| A **judge** during the time of David | Ibn Kathir |
| A **prophet**? | Minority view — but Ibn Abbas firmly said: "He was not a prophet" |

Tirmidhi records: **"Luqman was not a prophet, but a man of deep reflection and certainty."**

## The Wisdom

See the full context: [[Luqman the Wise — Wisdom for His Son]]

The eight principles of his wisdom:

1. **No shirk** — the greatest wrong
2. **Honour parents** — but do not obey them in sin
3. **God's omniscience** — the mustard seed is not hidden
4. **Establish prayer** — foundation of faith
5. **Enjoin good, forbid evil** — with patience
6. **No arrogance** — don't turn your face away
7. **Walk moderately** — the golden mean
8. **Lower your voice** — the donkey's bray is the ugliest sound

---

## Legacy

- Patron saint of wisdom in Islamic tradition
- Subject of countless Arabic folk tales — "Luqman and the camel," "Luqman and the donkey"
- Used as a teaching text for children across the Muslim world for 1,400 years
- His method — move from theology to ethics to manners — is the template of Islamic parenting
