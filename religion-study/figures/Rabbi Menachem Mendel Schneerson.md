# Rabbi Menachem Mendel Schneerson (רבי מנחם מענדל שניאורסאהן — The Lubavitcher Rebbe)

The seventh Rebbe of the Chabad-Lubavitch dynasty and arguably the most recognisable Jewish religious leader of the twentieth century. Schneerson transformed a small, war-ravaged Hasidic sect into an international outreach movement with thousands of institutions worldwide. The messianic expectations that surrounded him in his later years remain one of the most controversial chapters in modern Jewish history.

## Key Contributions

- **Global Outreach (Shlichut):** The Rebbe created the network of *shluchim* (emissaries) — couples who establish Chabad Houses worldwide to serve Jewish communities of all affiliations. Today, over 5,000 shluchim serve in more than 100 countries, offering religious services, education, and social support without requiring prior commitment.
- **Educational Revolution:** He established a vast network of schools, yeshivas, and day camps under the banner of Chinuch Atzmai (independent education). His emphasis on Jewish education for every Jew — regardless of background or observance — was a core principle of his leadership.
- **Mivtza'im (Campaigns):** Schneerson launched a series of global campaigns to encourage specific mitzvot among all Jews: tefillin (Mivtza Tefillin), Shabbat candles (for women), Torah study, mezuzah, tzedakah, and Jewish books for every home. These campaigns dramatically changed the landscape of Jewish observance.
- **Maamarim and Likkutei Sichot:** The Rebbe delivered thousands of talks (sichot) and longer discourses (maamarim) that were transcribed and published. His *Likkutei Sichot* (38 volumes) and other writings present a vast Chabad philosophy covering Torah commentary, halakha, Jewish mysticism, and social issues from a Lubavitcher perspective.
- **Messianism:** In his later years, many followers proclaimed Schneerson as the Moshiach (Messiah), a view he neither fully endorsed nor explicitly rejected. After his death in 1994, a segment of the movement continued to believe he is the Messiah who will be resurrected — a theological position that created tensions with mainstream Judaism.

## Life & Legacy

- Born in Nikolaev, Ukraine, into the Schneerson dynasty; his father was the noted kabbalist Rabbi Levi Yitzchak Schneerson
- Studied at the University of Berlin and the Sorbonne in Paris, where he studied mathematics, physics, and philosophy alongside his Torah studies
- Escaped Nazi-occupied Europe in 1941 and settled in New York, initially working in a defence plant
- Assumed leadership of Chabad-Lubavitch in 1951, following the death of his father-in-law, the previous Rebbe, Rabbi Yosef Yitzchak Schneersohn
- Under his leadership, Crown Heights (Brooklyn) became the international headquarters of Chabad; he never visited Israel despite ardent Zionism
