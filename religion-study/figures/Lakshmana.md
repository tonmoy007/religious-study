# Lakshmana

> The ever-devoted brother of Rama — the incarnation of Shesha (the cosmic serpent who supports Vishnu) — who spent 14 years serving Rama without sleep.

---

| Detail | Information |

| **Role** | Brother of Rama, prince of Ayodhya |
| **Parents** | Dasharatha and Sumitra |
| **Wife** | Urmila (Sita's sister) |
| **Divine source** | Shesha / Adishesha — the serpent upon whom Vishnu rests |

---

## Key Role in the Ramayana

Lakshmana is **Rama's shadow** — he follows Rama into exile, builds their forest huts, guards Sita while Rama hunts. He is the one who draws the **magic circle** around the hermitage.

He cuts off **Shurpanakha's nose** — the act that triggers the entire crisis. He kills **Indrajit** (Ravana's son), the turning point of the war.

## Lakshmana's Sleep

Urmila — Lakshmana's wife — takes on his need for sleep so he can stay awake for 14 years. She sleeps for 14 years while Lakshmana serves Rama. This story — found in later lore — is a testament to the power of a wife's sacrifice.
