# Hamzah ibn Abd al-Muttalib

**Asad Allāh** (the Lion of Allah) and **Sayyid al-Shuhadā'** (the Chief of Martyrs). The Prophet's uncle, foster-brother, and one of the greatest warriors of early Islam. His martyrdom at Uhud — and the mutilation of his body by Hind bint Utbah — remains one of the most painful moments in Islamic history.

---

## Biography

- **Born:** c. 568 CE, Mecca
- **Died:** 23 March 625 CE (3 AH), Uhud
- **Father:** Abd al-Muttalib
- **Mother:** Halah bint Wuhayb
- **Relation:** Uncle of the Prophet (paternal)
- **Known for:** Strength, courage, hunting, ferocity in battle

---

## Key Moments

### Conversion

Hamzah was not an early Muslim. He converted in the 6th year of the Prophet's mission (c. 616 CE). When he heard that **Abu Jahl** had insulted and harmed the Prophet, Hamzah — returning from a hunt — stormed to the Kaaba, struck Abu Jahl with his bow, and declared:

> *"I am of his religion — I say what he says. Return my words if you can!"*

His conversion strengthened the Muslim community enormously — the Quraysh now knew the Prophet had a fierce protector.

### Badr

At Badr, Hamzah was the first to engage in single combat — he killed **Utbah ibn Rabi'ah**, the Quraysh champion, with a single stroke. His strength was decisive.

### The Martyrdom at Uhud

At Uhud, Hamzah fought so fiercely that no Quraysh could approach him. But **Wahshi**, an Abyssinian slave (a javelin-thrower known for his accuracy), had been promised freedom by **Hind bint Utbah** if he could kill Hamzah — Hind's father had been killed by Hamzah at Badr.

Hamzah stumbled over a root, and Wahshi's javelin struck him in the abdomen. Hamzah fell. He was 57 years old.

After the battle, Hind found Hamzah's body. She cut open his chest, tore out his liver, and tried to eat it — the act of ultimate vengeance.

When the Prophet saw Hamzah's body, he wept. He said:

> *"I have never suffered a loss like you, Hamzah."*

---

## Legacy

- Called **Sayyid al-Shuhadā'** (Chief of the Martyrs)
- The Prophet said: "Hamzah is of me and I am of him."
- His gravesite at Mount Uhud is still visited
- Among the most beloved companions — his courage and honour are legendary
