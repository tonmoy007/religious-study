# Abu Bakr al-Siddiq

The closest companion of the Prophet, the first adult male to embrace Islam (after Khadijah and Ali), the first Caliph of Islam, and the father of Aisha. Known as **al-Ṣiddīq** (the Truthful Confirmer) for his unwavering belief in the Prophet — most famously when he confirmed the Isra and Mi'raj instantly.

---

## Biography

- **Born:** 573 CE, Mecca
- **Died:** 634 CE (13 AH), Medina
- **Caliphate:** 632–634 CE (2 years)
- **Tribe:** Banu Taym, Quraysh
- **Name:** Abd Allah ibn Abi Quhafah
- **Known for:** Truthfulness, generosity, and calm wisdom

---

## Key Moments

### The Isra and Mi'raj

When the Prophet announced his Night Journey to Jerusalem and ascension to heaven, the Quraysh mocked him. The polytheists went to Abu Bakr saying:

*"Your companion claims he went to Jerusalem last night."*

Abu Bakr replied without hesitation:

*"If he said so, then it is true. I believe him about what is even more astonishing — the news from heaven."*

For this, he was named **al-Ṣiddīq**.

### Migration (Hijrah)

Abu Bakr was the Prophet's sole companion on the migration from Mecca to Medina. They hid in the **Cave of Thawr** for three days while the Quraysh searched for them. When Abu Bakr feared the pursuers would see them, the Prophet reassured him:

> *"Do not grieve — Allah is with us."* — **9:40**

### Battle of Badr

Abu Bakr fought at Badr and advised the Prophet on the ransom of prisoners — his counsel (ransom for teaching) was followed over Umar's (execution).

### The Caliphate

After the Prophet's death, when some tribes refused to pay Zakat, Abu Bakr declared:

> *"By Allah, if they withhold a single rope they used to give the Prophet, I will fight them for it."*

He fought the **Ridda Wars** (apostasy wars), unified Arabia, and began the expansion into Syria and Iraq.

---

## Legacy

- First Caliph of Islam (632–634 CE)
- Compiled the first written copy of the Qur'an (after the Battle of Yamamah)
- Freed **Bilal ibn Rabah** from slavery
- Buried beside the Prophet in Aisha's chamber
- Known for his simple lifestyle despite his power

The Prophet said:

> *"If I were to take anyone as a close friend (khalīl), I would take Abu Bakr — but Islam's brotherhood is sufficient."*
