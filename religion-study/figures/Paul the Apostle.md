# Paul the Apostle

> The Apostle to the Gentiles — formerly Saul of Tarsus, persecutor of the Church, then its most influential theologian. Author of 13 New Testament letters and the architect of Christian theology.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Born** | c. 5 CE — Tarsus (modern Turkey) |
| **Tribe** | Benjamin |
| **Citizenship** | Roman (by birth) |
| **Education** | Trained under Gamaliel (leading Pharisee) |
| **Conversion** | c. 34 CE — Road to Damascus (Acts 9) |
| **Missions** | Three missionary journeys across Asia Minor, Macedonia, Greece |
| **Death** | c. 64–67 CE — martyred in Rome (beheaded) |

---

## Key Theological Contributions

| Doctrine | Paul's Teaching |
|----------|----------------|
| **Justification by faith** | "A person is justified by faith apart from works of the law" (Romans 3:28) |
| **The Cross** | Christ's death as substitutionary atonement — "Christ died for our sins" (1 Cor 15:3) |
| **Grace vs. Law** | Salvation is a gift — not earned by observing the Torah (Galatians 3) |
| **The Body of Christ** | The Church as one body with many members — each with gifts (1 Cor 12) |
| **Eschatology** | The resurrection of the dead at Christ's return (1 Cor 15, 1 Thess 4) |
| **The universality of sin** | "All have sinned and fall short of the glory of God" (Romans 3:23) |

---

## Connection with Islam

Paul is **absent from the Qur'an**. This absence is significant because Islamic tradition holds that:
- The original *Injīl* (Gospel) revealed to Jesus was corrupted
- Paul is often identified by Muslim scholars as a primary agent of this corruption — introducing the divinity of Christ and abrogating the Law
- The Qur'an's denial of the crucifixion (4:157) directly contradicts Paul's central message: "I preach Christ **crucified**" (1 Cor 1:23)
