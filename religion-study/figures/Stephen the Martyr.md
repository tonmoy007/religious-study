# Stephen the Martyr

> The first Christian martyr — a deacon full of the Spirit who was stoned to death for preaching that Jesus fulfilled the Law. His murder set the stage for the gospel to go to the Gentiles.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Role** | Deacon — one of seven chosen to serve widows (Acts 6) |
| **Character** | "Full of faith and the Holy Spirit" — "full of grace and power" |
| **Accusation** | Blasphemy — speaking against the Temple and the Law |
| **Execution** | Stoned outside Jerusalem — c. 34 CE |
| **Witness** | **Saul of Tarsus** (later Paul) approved of his death |

---

## The Speech (Acts 7)

Stephen's defense before the Sanhedrin is the **longest speech in Acts** — a sweeping retelling of Israel's history:

> *"You stiff-necked people — you always resist the Holy Spirit. As your fathers did, so do you. Which of the prophets did your fathers not persecute? They killed those who foretold the coming of the Righteous One — and now you have become his betrayers and murderers."* (7:51–52)

As he is being stoned, Stephen sees a vision: *"Behold — I see the heavens opened, and the Son of Man standing at the right hand of God!"* (7:56). His last words: *"Lord, do not hold this sin against them"* (7:60) — echoing Jesus on the cross.

---

## Connection with Islam

Stephen is **not named in the Qur'an**. However:
- The theme of a **righteous person killed for their faith** — remaining faithful to death — directly parallels the **People of the Ditch** (*Ashab al-Ukhdud*)
- Stephen's forgiveness of his persecutors ("do not hold this sin against them") echoes the Qur'anic injunction to "repel evil with good" (41:34)
- The story of **Habib al-Najjar** (the believer in Surah Ya-Sin 36:20–27) — who calls his people to follow the messengers and is killed — is the closest Qur'anic parallel to Stephen
