# Inanna (Ishtar) — Goddess of Love and War

## Summary

Inanna (Sumerian) / Ishtar (Akkadian) is the goddess of love, beauty, sex, desire, fertility, war, justice, and political power — the most complex deity in the Mesopotamian pantheon. Associated with the planet Venus (morning and evening star). In [[Inanna's Descent to the Underworld]], she travels to the underworld, is stripped of her powers, judged and killed by the Anunnaki, and later resurrected. In the [[Epic of Gilgamesh]] Tablet VI, she proposes to [[Gilgamesh]], is rejected with a recitation of her disastrous lovers, and sends the Bull of Heaven in revenge. She is the daughter of [[Anu (An) — Sky God|Anu]] and patron of Uruk. Her cult involved sacred prostitution.
