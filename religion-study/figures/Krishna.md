# Krishna

> The eighth avatar of Vishnu — the cowherd who became the kingmaker, the playful child who became the teacher of the Gita, the divine who fully lived as human.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Role** | Prince of Dwarka, strategist, teacher of the Gita |
| **Parents** | Vasudeva and Devaki; raised by Nanda and Yashoda |
| **Consort** | Rukmini (chief queen); 16,108 wives (symbolic) |
| **City** | Dwarka |

## The Many Faces of Krishna

| Aspect | Story |
|--------|-------|
| **Child** | [[Krishna's Birth and Childhood]] — the butter thief, Govardhan, Kaliya |
| **Lover** | Rasa Lila with the gopis — divine love as the metaphor for the soul's longing |
| **Statesman** | The Mahabharata's diplomacy — tries to prevent the war; fails |
| **Teacher** | [[The Bhagavad Gita]] — the most important philosophical text of Hinduism |
| **Warrior** | Drives Arjuna's chariot; uses divine tactics to ensure victory |
| **Friend** | Draupadi's protector at the dice game |
| **Trickster** | Manipulates the war — advises Bhima to strike below the navel |

---

## Krishna's Death

Krishna dies at the age of 125 — shot in the foot by a hunter named **Jara**, who mistakes his foot for a deer. His death marks the beginning of the **Kali Yuga** (the current dark age).

---

## Comparison with Other Traditions

Krishna's dual nature — fully divine yet fully human — parallels the **Christological** debates in Christianity. His childhood miracles (lifting a mountain, killing demons) recall the **Infancy Gospels of Thomas**. His moral ambiguity (the trickster who breaks rules for higher dharma) parallels the **Yahweh** of the Old Testament who commands Abraham to sacrifice Isaac.
