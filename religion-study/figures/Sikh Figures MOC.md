# ☬ Sikh Figures — Map of Content

Key figures from the Sikh tradition — the ten Gurus who shaped the faith from 1469 to 1708, the martyrs who sealed it with their blood, the companions who sang beside the founders, and the rulers who built the Sikh Empire.

---

## The Ten Gurus

| # | Guru | Tenure | Key Contribution | Story Links |
|---|------|--------|-----------------|-------------|
| 1 | [[Gurū Nānak]] | 1469–1539 | Founder of Sikhism; composed the Japjī Sāhib; undertook four great journeys (Udāsīs); taught Ik Oaṅkār (One God) | [[Gurū Nānak's Birth and Early Life]], [[Gurū Nānak's Four Journeys]], [[Gurū Nānak and Bhāī Mardānā]] |
| 2 | [[Gurū Aṅgad]] | 1539–1552 | Developed the Gurmukhī script; collected Nānak's hymns; established the Guru's langar as a central institution | [[The Installation of Gurū Aṅgad]] |
| 3 | [[Gurū Amar Dās]] | 1552–1574 | Institutionalised langar with *peṅgh* (seated in rows); composed Anand Sāhib; appointed women preachers | [[Gurū Amar Dās and the Test of Humility]] |
| 4 | [[Gurū Rām Dās]] | 1574–1581 | Founded the city of Rāmdāspur (Amritsar); began construction of the Harimandir Sāhib (Golden Temple) | [[The Founding of Amritsar]] |
| 5 | [[Gurū Arjan]] | 1581–1606 | Compiled the Ādi Granth; built the Harimandir Sāhib; martyred by Emperor Jahāngīr — the first Sikh martyr | [[The Compilation of the Ādi Granth]], [[The Martyrdom of Gurū Arjan]] |
| 6 | [[Gurū Har Gobind]] | 1606–1644 | Introduced the concept of Mīrī-Pīrī (temporal & spiritual authority); wore two swords; built the Akāl Takht | [[Gurū Har Gobind and the Two Swords]] |
| 7 | [[Gurū Har Rāi]] | 1644–1661 | Maintained the Sikh army; sheltered Dārā Shikoh; known for his healing herb garden | — |
| 8 | [[Gurū Har Kriśan]] | 1661–1664 | The youngest Guru (age 5); blessed a smallpox-stricken Delhi; died of smallpox | [[Gurū Har Kriśan in Delhi]] |
| 9 | [[Gurū Tegh Bahādur]] | 1665–1675 | Martyred in Delhi for defending the religious freedom of Kashmiri Pandits; composed Baṛī Sāhib hymns | [[The Martyrdom of Gurū Tegh Bahādur]] |
| 10 | [[Gurū Gobind Siṅgh]] | 1675–1708 | Created the Khalsa (1699); finalized the Guru Granth Sāhib as eternal Guru; composed the Dasam Granth and the Zafarnāmā | [[The Creation of the Khalsa]], [[The Battle of Chamkaur]], [[The Zafarnāmā]] |
