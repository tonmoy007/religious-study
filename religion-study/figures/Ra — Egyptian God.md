# Ra — Egyptian God

## Summary
Ra (or Re) is the sun god of ancient Egypt — the supreme creator, king of the gods, and the source of all life. He sailed across the sky in his Solar Barque by day and through the Tuat (underworld) by night, battling [[Apepi — Egyptian Serpent God|Apepi]] at every sunset. In [[The Egyptian Book of the Dead — Stories, Figures and Connections|the Book of the Dead]], Ra is the ultimate sovereign: the deceased seeks to join his crew, pass through his gates, and be reborn with him at dawn.

## The Solar Barque

See full treatment at [[The Solar Barque — Egyptian Story]].

Ra sails in two boats:
- **Mandjet Barque** (the "Boat of Millions of Years") — day sky journey
- **Mesektet Barque** (the night boat) — underworld journey

The midnight struggle in the Tuat is the moment of cosmic tension: [[Apepi — Egyptian Serpent God|Apepi]] tries to swallow the barque, and the gods (especially [[Set — Egyptian God|Set]] and [[Thoth — Egyptian God|Thoth]]) defend it with magic and spears. If Ra does not emerge, creation collapses.

## Ra as Creator (Atum-Ra)

The Heliopolitan creation myth:

1. **The primordial waters** — Nun, the abyss
2. **Atum (Ra) rises** from Nun on the primeval mound (the *benben*)
3. **Self-creation**: Atum masturbates or spits to produce the first divine pair
4. **Shu** (air) and **Tefnut** (moisture) are born
5. **They produce [[Geb — Egyptian God|Geb]]** (earth) and **[[Nut — Egyptian Goddess|Nut]]** (sky)
6. **They produce Osiris, Isis, Set, Nephthys** — the Ennead is complete

## Ra and the Secret Name

The myth of Ra's secret name (which [[Isis — Egyptian Goddess|Isis]] tricks him into revealing) is one of the most important theological narratives:

1. **Isis grows tired** of mortals living among the unknowable gods
2. **She creates a serpent** from Ra's spit and earth
3. **The serpent bites Ra**, poisoning him with fire
4. **Isis offers to heal Ra** — but only if he tells her his *secret name*
5. **Ra resists**, but the pain is unbearable
6. **He reveals his name** — the knowledge passes to Isis
7. **Isis heals Ra**, but now has power over him (and thus over creation)

**Significance**: This is the *only* account of the supreme god being compelled — it establishes that divine knowledge (especially names) is power, and that even the creator is subject to the laws of magic (*heka*).

## Ra in the Funerary Context

For the deceased, Ra is:
- **The goal**: to be "justified" means to enter Ra's domain and see his face
- **The companion**: the soul travels with Ra's barque through the night
- **The rebirther**: to emerge from the Tuat with Ra at dawn is to achieve rebirth
- **The judge**: Ra, not Osiris, is sometimes the ultimate judge (especially in earlier periods)

## Key Epithets from the Texts
- "Lord of the Sun, the Great One who illumines the Two Lands"
- "The Great Disc"
- "He who creates all that exists"
- "The Hidden One" (as Amun-Ra)
- "The Lord of Eternity who traverses millions of years"
- "The Bennu Bird" (as the self-created one — the phoenix)
