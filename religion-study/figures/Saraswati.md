# Saraswati

> The goddess of knowledge, music, arts, and learning — the serene consort of Brahma, whose very name means "the flowing one."

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Role** | Goddess of knowledge, wisdom, music, arts |
| **Consort** | Brahma |
| **Vahana** | Hamsa (swan / goose) — also a peacock |
| **Symbols** | Veena (stringed instrument), Vedas, rosary |
| **Color** | White |
| **Festival** | Vasant Panchami |

---

## Origins

Saraswati is associated with the **Saraswati River** — a sacred river mentioned in the Rig Veda, long dried up but mythically preserved. She embodies the flow of knowledge — the river of wisdom.

---

## Key Associations

| Domain | Association |
|--------|-------------|
| **Music** | She plays the veena — the goddess of melody |
| **Learning** | Students pray to her before exams |
| **Speech** | She is *Vak* (speech personified) — the power of words |
| **Wisdom** | The Vedas are revealed through her grace |

---

## Worship

Saraswati is especially worshipped by students, scholars, and musicians. The festival **Vasant Panchami** marks the coming of spring and is dedicated to her — children are often taught their first letters on this day.
