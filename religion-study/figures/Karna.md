# Karna

> The son of the Sun god — abandoned at birth, raised as a charioteer's son, the greatest warrior of his age — who fought against his own brothers and died weaponless in the mud.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Role** | Eldest Pandava (by birth), King of Anga, greatest warrior of the Kaurava army |
| **Father** | Surya (the Sun god) |
| **Mother** | Kunti (before marriage — gave him away) |
| **Adoptive parents** | Adhiratha (charioteer) and Radha |
| **Wife** | Vrushali |
| **Weapon** | Vijaya (the bow of Shiva) |

---

## The Tragedy of Karna

Karna's life is one of the most tragic in all literature:

| Event | Significance |
|-------|-------------|
| **Abandonment** | Kunti places him in a basket in the river — the foundling motif |
| **The Swayamvara** | Draupadi refuses him: "I will not marry a charioteer's son" |
| **The secret** | Krishna and Kunti tell him he is a Pandava — he refuses to betray Duryodhana |
| **The curse** | His guru Parashurama curses him: "You are not a Kshatriya — when you need your knowledge most, it will fail you" |
| **The chariot wheel** | His wheel sinks in the mud — he descends — Arjuna shoots |
| **The death** | Kunti's secret son killed by his own brother — she hears the news and falls silent |

---

## Karna's Generosity (Dana)

Karna is famous for his **generosity** — he never refuses a request. Krishna, disguised as a brahmin, asks for Karna's earrings and armor (his divine protection, born with him). Karna cuts them off his body and gives them away.
