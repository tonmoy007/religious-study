# Indra

> The king of the gods — the thunderbolt-wielding wielder of rain and war — the most prominent deity of the Rig Veda, diminished in later Puranic literature.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Role** | King of the Devas; god of rain, thunder, war |
| **Consort** | Shachi (Indrani) |
| **Vahana** | Airavata (the white elephant) |
| **Weapon** | Vajra (the thunderbolt — made from the bones of the sage Dadhichi) |
| **Abode** | Amaravati (the capital of Svarga / heaven) |

---

## Key Stories

| Story | Role |
|-------|------|
| **The slaying of Vritra** | Kills the serpent-demon Vritra, releasing the waters — the central myth of the Rig Veda |
| **The Churning of the Ocean** | Participates — cursed by Durvasa, which begins the crisis |
| **Vamana and King Bali** | His kingdom is taken — he appeals to Vishnu |
| **Govardhan** | Krishna lifts Govardhan Hill — Indra's ego is humbled |
| **Ahalya's curse** | Seduces Ahalya (wife of sage Gautama) — cursed to wear a thousand vulvae, transformed into a thousand eyes |

---

## Theology

Indra is the **hero-god** of the Vedic period — the most invoked deity in the Rig Veda. But in the Puranas, he is depicted as a **troubled king**: powerful but insecure, noble but flawed, struggling to maintain his throne against Asura encroachments and the curses of sages.
