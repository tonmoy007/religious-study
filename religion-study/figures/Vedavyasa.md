# Vedavyasa

> The composer of the Mahabharata and the compiler of the Vedas — the sage who divided the eternal Veda into four, and who dictated the world's longest epic to Ganesha.

## The Division of the Vedas

Before Vyasa, the Veda was a single, vast body of oral tradition. He **divided** it into four — Rig, Yajur, Sama, and Atharva — and taught each to a different disciple, thus preserving the tradition across generations.

## The Mahabharata

Vyasa **dictated** the Mahabharata to Ganesha — who agreed to transcribe it only if Vyasa never paused. Vyasa, in turn, would compose complex verses that required Ganesha to pause and think — giving Vyasa time to compose the next verses in his mind.

The epic contains everything: "What is not in the Mahabharata does not exist anywhere."
