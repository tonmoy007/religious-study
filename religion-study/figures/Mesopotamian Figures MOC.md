# 🏛️ Mesopotamian Figures — Map of Content

A structured index of the gods, goddesses, heroes, and kings of ancient Mesopotamia — the earliest attested pantheon in world literature (c. 3200 BCE onward). Unlike scriptural traditions anchored by a single prophetic or revelatory figure, Mesopotamia offers a **decentralized cosmos**: multiple city-states, each with its patron deity, nested within an evolving divine hierarchy shaped by political fortunes.

---

## 1. Primeval Deities — Before the Cosmos

| Figure | Role | Domain / City | Status |
|--------|------|---------------|--------|
| **[[Tiamat]]** | Primordial salt-water chaos — dragon-goddess slain by Marduk | Cosmic ocean | ✅ |
| **Apsu** | Primordial fresh water — killed by Ea before the Enuma Elish begins | Underground sweet water | ⬜ |
| **Lahmu & Lahamu** | First divine pair — silt from the mingling of Apsu and Tiamat | Threshold deities | ⬜ |
| **Anshar & Kishar** | Sky- and earth-horizons — parents of Anu | Horizon | ⬜ |
| **[[Anu (An) — Sky God]]** | Sky-god — supreme authority, father of the gods | Uruk (Eanna precinct) | ✅ |
| **[[Ninhursag (Ninmah)]]** | Earth-goddess — mother of the land, creator of life | Keš, Adab | ✅ |
| **[[Enlil]]** | Air/storm-god — king of the gods in Sumerian tradition | Nippur (Ekur temple) | ✅ |
| **[[Enki (Ea) — God of Wisdom]]** | God of fresh water, wisdom, crafts, and magic | Eridu (E-abzu temple) | ✅ |

## 4. Mythical Kings & Heroes

| Figure | Role | City / Text | Status |
|--------|------|-------------|--------|
| **[[Gilgamesh]]** | King of Uruk — two-thirds divine, hero of the epic, seeker of immortality | Uruk | ✅ |
| **[[Enkidu]]** | Wild man created by the gods — Gilgamesh's companion and equal | Steppe | ✅ |
| **Utnapishtim (Ziusudra, Atrahasis)** | Survivor of the Great Flood — granted immortality | Shuruppak | ⬜ |
| **Enmerkar** | King of Uruk — early dynast; builds Uruk, contends with Aratta | Uruk | ⬜ |
| **Lugalbanda** | King of Uruk — father of Gilgamesh; miraculous journey through the mountains | Uruk | ⬜ |
| **Etana** | King of Kish — flew to heaven on an eagle to find the plant of birth | Kish | ⬜ |
| **Adapa (Uan)** | Sage of Eridu — first of the Seven Sages (abgallu); broke the South Wind's wing, refused the food of immortality | Eridu | ⬜ |
| **Humbaba (Huwawa)** | Guardian of the Cedar Forest — slain by Gilgamesh and Enkidu | Lebanon mountains | ⬜ |
| **Bull of Heaven** | Divine beast sent by Inanna/Ishtar to destroy Gilgamesh | Uruk | ⬜ |
| **The Anzû Bird (Zu)** | Storm-bird who stole the Tablet of Destinies — defeated by Ninurta | — | ⬜ |

## 5. Historical Figures (Scribes, Kings, Reformers)

| Figure | Role | Period | Status |
|--------|------|--------|--------|
| **Enheduanna** | Daughter of Sargon of Akkad — high priestess of Nanna at Ur; the world's first known author (c. 2285 BCE) | Akkadian | ⬜ |
| **Gudea** | Ruler of Lagaš — built the temple of Ningirsu; left detailed cylinder inscriptions | Neo-Sumerian | ⬜ |
| **Hammurabi** | King of Babylon — unified Mesopotamia, issued the Code of Hammurabi under Shamash's authority | Old Babylonian | ⬜ |
| **Sin-leqi-unninni** | Scribe/editor — standardized the Epic of Gilgamesh into the 12-tablet "Standard Babylonian" version | Middle Babylonian (c. 1200 BCE) | ⬜ |
| **Ashurbanipal** | King of Assyria — created the library at Nineveh, the largest surviving cuneiform archive | Neo-Assyrian (668–627 BCE) | ⬜ |
| **Sargon of Akkad** | Founder of the Akkadian Empire — first empire in history | Akkadian (c. 2334 BCE) | ⬜ |
| **Narām-Sîn** | Grandson of Sargon — deified himself; Curse of Agade blames his hubris | Akkadian | ⬜ |
| **Šulgi** | King of Ur III — patron of scribal arts, self-praising hymns | Ur III (c. 2094 BCE) | ⬜ |
