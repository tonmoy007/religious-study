# Valmiki

> The poet-sage who composed the Ramayana — the first poet (Adi Kavi) who transformed grief into verse, and the hermit who gave refuge to Sita.

---

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Role** | Author of the Ramayana |
| **Title** | Adi Kavi — "the first poet" |
| **Meter** | Anushtubh — the first meter of Sanskrit poetry |

## The Birth of Poetry

Valmiki was a bandit named **Ratnakara** — a robber who killed travelers. One day he tried to rob the sage **Narada**, who asked: "Why do you do this?" Ratnakara: "To feed my family." Narada: "Will they share your sin?" Ratnakara asked his family — they refused.

He meditated for so long that an **anthill** (*valmika*) grew around him. He emerged as Valmiki — "the ant-hill born."

### The First Verse

Valmiki saw a hunter kill a **crane** (*krauncha*) as it was mating. Overflowing with grief, he spontaneously uttered:

> *ma nishada pratishtham tvamagamah shashvatih samah / yat kraunchamithunadekam avadhih kamamohitam*

"You will find no rest for long ages, O hunter, because you killed a crane in love."

The god **Brahma** appeared: "You have composed the first verse — now sing the story of Rama."

---

## Sita's Refuge

When Rama exiles Sita, it is **Valmiki's ashram** where she finds shelter. Valmiki composes the Ramayana and teaches it to Sita's twin sons — **Lava and Kusha** — who sing it before Rama, reuniting the family.
