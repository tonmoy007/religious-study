# Parvati

> The Mother Goddess — the gentle wife of Shiva, the fierce Durga who slays demons, and the terrifying Kali who dances on corpses. She is all three.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Role** | Goddess of love, fertility, devotion, and power |
| **Consort** | Shiva |
| **Sons** | Ganesha, Kartikeya (Skanda) |
| **Father** | Himavan (the mountain king) |
| **Vahana** | Lion / tiger |

---

## Forms

| Form | Character |
|------|-----------|
| **Parvati** | Gentle, nurturing — the ideal wife and mother |
| **Durga** | Fierce warrior — created by the gods to slay the demon Mahishasura |
| **Kali** | Terrifying — dark, wild-haired, wearing a garland of skulls — the destroyer of evil |
| **Annapurna** | The goddess of food and nourishment |
| **Lalita** | The beautiful — presiding over the secret knowledge |

---

## Key Stories

| Story | Role |
|-------|------|
| **The Marriage of Shiva and Parvati** | Wins Shiva through penance |
| **The birth of Ganesha** | Creates Ganesha from the dirt of her body |
| **Durga's slaying of Mahishasura** | Fights the buffalo demon for nine nights |
| **Kali's dance** | Destroys the demon Raktabija (whose blood spawns duplicates) |

---

## Theology

Parvati completes the **Tridevi** (the three goddesses) with Lakshmi and Saraswati. The **Shakta** tradition worships her as the Supreme Reality — *Devi* — from whom all gods derive their power. Shiva without Shakti is a corpse (*Shava*).
