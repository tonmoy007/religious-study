# Thoth — Egyptian God

## Summary
Thoth (*Djehuty*) is the ibis-headed god of writing, wisdom, magic, measurement, and judgment. He is the divine scribe who records the [[The Weighing of the Heart — Egyptian Story|Weighing of the Heart]], the inventor of hieroglyphs, the lord of sacred books, and the keeper of time. In [[The Egyptian Book of the Dead — Stories, Figures and Connections|the Book of the Dead]], Thoth is the **mediator** — he speaks for the deceased, writes their name in the Hall of Righteousness, and ensures that justice (*[[Ma'at — Goddess of Truth|Ma'at]]*) prevails at the scales. In later Hellenistic tradition, he became **Hermes Trismegistus** ("Thrice-Greatest Hermes"), the legendary author of the Hermetic Corpus.

## Iconography
- **Ibis head** — long curved beak (the ibis was a sacred bird associated with wisdom)
- **Sometimes as a baboon** — the baboon was Thoth's sacred animal, often shown seated with a crescent moon on its head
- **Holds a reed pen and scribal palette** — the writing instruments of ancient Egypt
- **Moon disk on his head** — Thoth is also a lunar deity, the "reckoner of time"
- **Wears a kilt and sometimes the *atef* crown** when depicted as a full god

## Roles

### The Divine Scribe
Thoth records everything:
- The name of every person at birth (fixing their fate)
- The deeds of each life (the "book of the heart" that the heart will repeat at judgment)
- The **verdict** of the Weighing of the Heart
- The **name of the justified** deceased on the leaf of the sacred *ished* tree

**Theological significance**: Writing is not secondary to reality — it *constitutes* reality. What is not written has not happened. This gives Thoth a quasi-creative role.

### The God of Wisdom
Thoth is the source of all knowledge:
- Invented **hieroglyphs** (*medu netjer* — "the words of the gods")
- Author of the **42 Books of Thoth** (mythical texts containing all knowledge)
- Master of **secret spells** — many spells in the Book of the Dead are introduced as "thus said Thoth"
- **Geometer** — he established the measurements of the temples, the land, the stars
- **Astronomer** — he regulates the moon, the seasons, and the 365-day calendar
- **Creator of magic** (*heka*) — he is both the source and the wielder

### The Mediator and Advocate

Unlike [[Anubis — Egyptian God|Anubis]] who *weighs* and [[Ma'at — Goddess of Truth|Ma'at]] who *defines* the standard, Thoth *speaks for* the deceased:

> "I have come to you, O Lord of the Tuat, and I have not committed any sin. Give ear to me, O Thoth, and testify on my behalf before the Great God."

He tells Osiris: "The heart of N. is justified. His soul is righteous. Let him enter the Field of Reeds."

In this role, Thoth resembles a **defence advocate** or **paraclete** — one who speaks in favour of the soul based on its true state.

### The Lunar God
Thoth reckons time by the moon:
- He is "Lord of the Moon" (*Aah*)
- The waxing and waning of the moon represent Thoth's ongoing battle with darkness
- The lunar month is 29.5 days — 29 days of Thoth's manifest form, the 30th day is hidden (the invisible moon)
- Thoth's connection to the moon connects him to **measurement** (the calendar) and **hidden knowledge** (the new moon = the obscure wisdom)

## Thoth in the Hermetic Tradition

The most remarkable afterlife of any Egyptian deity: Thoth was syncretized with the Greek god Hermes by the Ptolemies, giving birth to **Hermes Trismegistus** ("Thrice-Great Hermes"):

| Egyptian Thoth | Hermes Trismegistus |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Scribe of the gods | Author of 42 sacred books |
| God of writing | Inventor of writing (alphabet, numbers) |
| The "Twice-Great" | The "Thrice-Great" |
| Author of funerary spells | Author of the Hermetic Corpus (philosophy, alchemy, astrology, theurgy) |
| Ibis-headed | Human, but with the *caduceus* (serpent staff) |
| Nightly defense of Ra's barque | Guide of souls (Hermes Psychopompos) |

The **Hermetic Corpus** (2nd–3rd century CE) — especially the *Poimandres* and the *Emerald Tablet* — became foundational for Western esotericism, alchemy, and Renaissance magic. Texts like *The Kybalion* (1908) are modern Hermeticism, claiming Thoth-Hermes as their source.
