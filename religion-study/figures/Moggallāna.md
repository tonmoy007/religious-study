# Moggallāna (Mahā Moggallāna)

> The Buddha's foremost disciple in psychic powers — the heavenly traveller, the tamer of demons, the chief disciple who searched the hells for his mother and died a martyr's death to pay an ancient karmic debt.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Personal Name** | Kolita (born in Kolita village) |
| **Title** | Mahā Moggallāna (The Great Moggallāna), Foremost in Psychic Powers (*Etadaggaṃ Iddhimantānaṃ*) |
| **Clan** | Brāhmaṇa (brahmin) |
| **Birthplace** | Kolita village, near Rājagaha (Magadha) |
| **Friend** | Sāriputta (Upatissa) — childhood friends, ordained together, chief disciples together |
| **Teacher** | The Buddha (previously Sañjaya Belaṭṭhiputta) |
| **Enlightenment** | Seven days after ordination — reached arahantship after a teaching on the elements |
| **Order** | Left brahmin life along with 250 followers (all of Sañjaya's students) |
| **Death** | Crushed by assassins (or a bandit) — killed at age ~84; a death attributed to past karmic debt |

## Key Powers (*Iddhi*)

Moggallāna's psychic powers are the most extensively catalogued in the Pāli Canon. He was the Buddha's go-to when a supernormal solution was needed.

| Power / Deed | Description | Source |
|--------------|-------------|--------|
| **Heavenly Travel** | Soared to deva-realms (Tāvatiṃsa, etc.) to question deities about their karma | Various Suttas |
| **Taming Nandopananda** | The mighty serpent-king (*nāga*) who threatened the Saṅgha — Moggallāna transformed into a Supaṇṇa (garuḍa) and subjugated him | Udāna Commentary |
| **Protecting from Māra** | When Māra possessed a young monk to disrupt a gathering, Moggallāna expelled him with a rebuke — Māra fled | Saṃyutta Nikāya |
| **Shaking the Mansion of Māra** | Struck the Peak of Mount Yugandhara with his toe, shaking Māra's entire palace | Saṃyutta Nikāya |
| **Visiting the Hells** | Descended to the hell-realms to witness suffering; taught hell-beings, comforted monks who feared hell | Various |
| **Searching for His Mother** | Traversed the deva- and hell-realms seeking his mother (reborn in hell for stinginess) — eventually guided her to a better rebirth | Petavatthu |
| **Moving Mount Vulture Peak** | Once moved the entire mountain with his finger as a display for a recalcitrant monk | Dhammapada Commentary |
| **Restoring a Monastery** | Rebuilt Jetavana's gate after Māra destroyed it — with his psychic power | Commentary |

> Moggallāna's powers were so formidable that the Buddha once warned him: "Refrain, Moggallāna, from displaying psychic powers among laypeople. It is not for their benefit." When a chariot-maker refused to believe, Moggallāna lifted him by his chariot, flew through the sky, and took him to meet a deva the man had been making offerings to.

## Relationship with Sāriputta

The friendship between Moggallāna and Sāriputta is one of the most touching relationships in the Pāli Canon.

| Aspect | Sāriputta | Moggallāna |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Birth Name** | Upatissa | Kolita |
| **Title** | Foremost in Wisdom (*Etadaggaṃ Paññāvantānaṃ*) | Foremost in Psychic Powers (*Etadaggaṃ Iddhimantānaṃ*) |
| **Rank** | First Chief Disciple (Dakkhinasāvaka) | Second Chief Disciple (Vāmasāvaka) |
| **Nature** | Gentle, analytical, loved teaching | Direct, powerful, loved protection |
| **Death** | Died naturally at his birthplace, surrounded by monks | Killed by bandits — violent, karmic death |
| **Past-life bond** | Vowed together a kalpa ago, under Buddha Anomadassī, to attain the chief discipleship | Same vow |

The two were childhood friends, each the son of the village head in their respective villages. They ordained together under Sañjaya, then left together to follow the Buddha after hearing Assaji's verse. They were inseparable — the Buddha called them the *Nāga Pair* (*Nāgayuga*), the twin pillars who upheld the Saṅgha after him. When Sāriputta died, Moggallāna was so distraught that he flew to him and cremated his body with his own hands. When Moggallāna died shortly after, the Saṅgha felt the loss of both pillars at once.

## His Death

Moggallāna's death is among the most dramatic in the Canon. Bandits (or hired assassins) ambushed his hut on the slopes of Mount Isigili (near Rājagaha). They beat him and crushed his bones, leaving him for dead — or, in another telling, came multiple times and he repelled them by psychic power until, on the final day, he accepted the attack, knowing his karmic debt was due.

The reason? In a past life, long ago, Moggallāna had killed his own parents — a crime carrying karmic weight so heavy that, even as an arahant (whose mind was utterly pure), the *result* of that karma (*vipāka*) had to be experienced before final Nibbāna. The bandits broke every bone in his body — and he, with his mind unshaken, used his psychic power to reassemble his body, climbed to the Buddha, paid his respects, and passed away into *parinibbāna*.

> "This is the ripening of karma, Moggallāna. Bear it. The ripening of karma that would have tormented you in hell for countless aeons — this brief bodily pain has exhausted it." — The Buddha, after Moggallāna's death (Dhammapada Commentary)

|--------|-----------|-----------|-------------|--------|
