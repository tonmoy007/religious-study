# David the King

> The shepherd boy who killed Goliath, became Israel's greatest king, wrote half the Psalms, and is called "a man after God's own heart" — despite spectacular failures.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Father** | Jesse of Bethlehem (tribe of Judah) |
| **Anointed** | By Samuel — as a young shepherd |
| **Reign** | c. 1010–970 BCE — 40 years (7 in Hebron, 33 in Jerusalem) |
| **Wives** | Michal, Abigail, Ahinoam, Maacah, Haggith, Abital, Eglah, **Bathsheba** — and many concubines |
| **Children** | Amnon, Absalom, Adonijah, **Solomon**, Tamar, and many more |
| **Death** | 70 years old — buried in the City of David |

---

## The Man After God's Own Heart

David's life is a study in contrasts:

| Virtue | Sin |
|--------|-----|
| Faith in God vs. Goliath | Adultery with **Bathsheba** |
| Repentance (Psalm 51) | Murder of **Uriah** (her husband) |
| Love for Jonathan | Failed as a father — Absalom's rebellion |
| Brings the Ark to Jerusalem | Numbers the people (census sin) |
| Writes the Psalms | Multiplied wives against Torah command |

His legacy: the **Davidic covenant** (2 Samuel 7) — God promises that David's throne will be established forever. Christians believe this is fulfilled in Jesus (the "Son of David"). Jews await the Messiah from David's line.

---

## Connection with Islam

David (*Dāwūd*) is a prophet in Islam. The Qur'an:
- Mentions David's defeat of Goliath (2:251)
- Says God gave him the **Zabūr** (Psalms) — 4:163, 17:55
- Narrates the story of David and the two disputants (38:21–25) — a parable about his sin with Bathsheba (though Islamic exegesis interprets it more gently than the Bible)
- Declares David a *khalīfa* (vicegerent) on earth (38:26)
