# Rabbi Joseph Karo (יוסף קארו — Maran)

**Rabbi Joseph Karo** (1488–1575) was the most influential codifier of Jewish law in the post-medieval period. His *Shulchan Aruch* (Set Table, 1565) — a concise, practical code of Halakha — became the definitive legal reference for traditional Judaism worldwide. Karo was also a profound kabbalist who recorded the teachings of a celestial *maggid* (angelic mentor) in his mystical diary *Maggid Mesharim*.

## Key Contributions

- ***Shulchan Aruch* (שולחן ערוך, 1565):** Karo's magnum opus — a clear, accessible code of Jewish law covering daily life, festivals, marriage, civil law, and dietary regulations. He based rulings primarily on three earlier authorities: Maimonides (Rambam), Rabbeinu Asher (Rosh), and Isaac Alfasi (Rif), deciding by majority among them.
- ***Beit Yosef* (בית יוסף):** The massive commentary that preceded the Shulchan Aruch. In it, Karo traced every halakhic ruling back to its Talmudic and medieval sources, explaining the reasoning behind each decision. The Shulchan Aruch is the abbreviated digest of this four-volume work.
- ***Maggid Mesharim* (מגיד מישרים, "Preacher of Righteousness"):** A mystical diary in which Karo recorded the teachings of a heavenly *maggid* — a personified voice of the Mishnah — that revealed esoteric secrets, reproved him for ethical failings, and instructed him in Kabbalistic practices. This work provides an extraordinary window into the inner spiritual life of a legal giant.
- **Halakhic Unity for a Dispersed People:** By distilling the vast corpus of Jewish law into a single, authoritative code, Karo gave Jews across the diaspora a shared legal framework — the Shulchan Aruch was accepted from Yemen to Poland, despite regional differences.

## Life & Legacy

- Born in Toledo, Spain in 1488, he was a child during the expulsion of the Jews from Spain (1492); his family settled in the Ottoman Empire
- Lived in Nikopolis (Bulgaria), then Adrianople (Edirne), and finally Safed (Tzfat) in the Galilee — the centre of 16th-century Kabbalah
- In Safed he studied alongside Rabbi Isaac Luria (the Arizal), Rabbi Moses Cordovero, and Rabbi Solomon Alkabetz (author of *Lecha Dodi*)
- His rulings were supplemented by Moses Isserles (Rema), whose *ha-Mappah* glosses added Ashkenazi customs; the two together became the standard text for all traditional Jewish practice
- Karo was ordained as a rabbi in the renewed semikhah (ordination) movement led by Rabbi Jacob Berab in Safed
- Died in Safed in 1575 and is buried in the ancient cemetery there
