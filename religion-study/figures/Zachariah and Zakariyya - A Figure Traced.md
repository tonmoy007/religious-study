# Zachariah & Zakariyya — A Figure Traced

> Part of [[Cross-Scripture Connections MOC]] · a **single-figure deep dive**

Zachariah — *Zakariyya* in Arabic — is the **aged priest** who is granted a son (John the Baptist / Yaḥyā) against all expectation. His story is the hinge that connects the Old and New Testaments in the Bible and opens the prophetic chain in the Qur'an's [[019 Maryam|Surah Maryam]]. A **genealogical** connection; a companion to [[John the Baptist and Yahya - A Figure Traced]], [[Mary and Maryam - A Figure Traced]], and [[Jesus and Isa - A Figure Traced]].

## The shared story: an angel promises a son to barren old age

Both scriptures begin with Zachariah ministering in the sanctuary when an angel appears with news that his elderly, barren wife will bear a son. He asks for a sign — and is struck silent until the child is born.

> [!quote] Luke 1 — the priest and the angel
> **1:5** — "There was in the days of Herod, the king of Judaea, a certain priest named **Zacharias**, of the course of Abia: and his wife was of the daughters of Aaron, and her name was **Elisabeth**."
> **1:7** — "they had no child, because that **Elisabeth was barren**, and they both were now well stricken in years."
> **1:11–13** — "there appeared unto him an **angel of the Lord**… Fear not, Zacharias: for thy prayer is heard; and thy wife Elisabeth shall bear thee a son, and thou shalt call his name **John**."
> **1:18–20** — "Whereby shall I know this?… I am **Gabriel**… thou shalt be **dumb, and not able to speak**, until the day that these things shall be performed."

> [!quote] Qur'an — the same tidings to Zakariyya
> **Maryam 19:2–4** — "A mention of the mercy of thy Lord unto His servant **Zachariah**. When he cried unto his Lord a cry in secret, saying: My Lord! Lo! the bones of me wax feeble and my head is shining with grey hair…"
> **Maryam 19:7–10** — "(It was said unto him): O Zachariah! Lo! We bring thee tidings of a son whose name is **John**… He said: My Lord! How can I have a son when **my wife is barren** and I have reached infirm old age?" He said: **"Thy sign** (shall be) that thou **speakest not unto mankind three nights**…"

The parallel is remarkably faithful: the aged priest, the barren wife, the angelic promise of a son named John, the request for a sign, and the **punishment of silence**. Even the detail that the sign is **three days/nights** of silence aligns with the Gospel's "until the day that these things shall be performed."

## Where Zachariah's story differs

### The Bible gives Zachariah his voice back at the naming ceremony
In Luke 1:57–80, the full arc plays out in a single narrative: the child is born, the relatives want to name him after his father, but **Elizabeth insists** on John. They ask Zachariah, who writes "**His name is John**" — and immediately **his speech returns**. He bursts into the *Benedictus* (Luke 1:68–79), a hymn that frames John as the forerunner of the Messiah.

### The Qur'an weaves Zakariyya into a tapestry of prophets
[[019 Maryam|Surah Maryam]] tells Zachariah's story, then immediately moves to **John, then Mary & Jesus, then Abraham, then Idris** — a chain of prophets. Zakariyya's prayer is a model of **persistent, secret supplication** (19:3 — "a cry in secret"), and he is mentioned in [[003 Aal-i-Imraan|Al-Imran 3:37–41]] as the **guardian of Mary** — a role the Bible does not give him. In the Qur'an, it is **Zachariah who is appointed to care for Mary** after her mother dedicates her to the temple, and whenever he visits her chamber he finds miraculous provision (3:37).

> [!quote] Al-Imran 3:37 — the guardian of Mary
> "Her Lord accepted her with a fair acceptance… and **Zachariah was her guardian**. Whenever Zachariah went into the sanctuary where she was, he found that she had food."

This connection — **Zachariah as Mary's guardian** — does not appear in the canonical Gospels. It is a Qur'an-unique detail that ties the two miraculous births together (John's and Jesus') under the same elderly guardian.

## The two portraits compared

| Aspect | Bible (Luke 1) | Qur'an (Maryam 19 / Al-Imran 3) |
|--------|---------------|----------------------------------|
| **Role** | Temple priest of the course of Abia | Prophet (nabī) and guardian of Mary |
| **His prayer** | For a son (implied by context) | "A cry in secret" for an heir (19:3–5) |
| **The angel** | Gabriel (named) | Unnamed "spirit" / angels |
| **The sign** | Muteness "until the day" | Silence "three nights" (19:10) |
| **Speech restored** | At the naming ceremony (Luke 1:64) | Not explicitly narrated |
| **His wife** | Named **Elisabeth** (Elizabeth), of Aaron's line | Unnamed, but described as barren and aged |
| **Connection to Jesus** | John is the forerunner of Jesus | Zakariyya is Mary's guardian; his story precedes the annunciation to Mary |

## What it means

Zachariah/Zakariyya is the **bridge figure**: his story, shared in detail across both scriptures, connects the prophetic tradition of the Old Testament (priest, temple, barren-wife motif) with the Gospel narrative of John and Jesus. In the Qur'an, he serves an additional role as **Mary's protector**, physically linking the house of 'Imran (Amram, the father of Moses and Aaron) to the birth of Jesus. His patient, secret prayer is held up as a model of **persistent faith** — "calling upon his Lord in secret" — a virtue that spans both traditions.
