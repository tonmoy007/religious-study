# Hanuman

> The monkey-god — the embodiment of unwavering devotion (*bhakti*), superhuman strength, and selfless service. The perfect servant of Rama.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Role** | Devotee of Rama, general of the monkey-army |
| **Father** | Vayu (the wind god) — also called Pavanaputra (son of the wind) |
| **Mother** | Anjana (a celestial nymph cursed to be born as a monkey) |
| **Form** | Vanara (monkey-like humanoid) |

---

## Key Stories

| Story | Role |
|-------|------|
| **The attempt to eat the sun** | As a child, leaps to eat the sun — Indra strikes him with the Vajra |
| **Hanuman's Search for Sita** | Leaps the ocean, finds Sita, burns Lanka |
| **The Sanjivani mountain** | Brings an entire mountain of healing herbs to revive Lakshmana |
| **The Great War** | Kills thousands of rakshasas |

---

## Powers

Hanuman has eight **siddhis** (supernatural powers):
- **Anima** — become infinitely small
- **Mahima** — become infinitely large
- **Laghima** — become weightless
- **Garima** — become infinitely heavy
- **Prapti** — travel anywhere
- **Prakamya** — fulfill any wish
- **Ishitva** — control nature
- **Vashitva** — control beings

---

## Hanuman as a Model

Hanuman is the **ideal bhakta** (devotee) — he serves without ego, without question, without expectation. He does not seek liberation — he only wants to serve Rama. The *Hanuman Chalisa* (40-verse hymn) is one of the most recited Hindu prayers.
