# Joshua

> Moses' successor — the general who led Israel into the Promised Land. His name (*Yehoshua* = "Yahweh saves") is the Hebrew original of "Jesus."

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Original name** | Hoshea (salvation) — renamed **Joshua** by Moses |
| **Father** | Nun (tribe of Ephraim) |
| **Role** | Moses' assistant, spy in Canaan, military commander |
| **Accomplishment** | Led Israel across the Jordan, conquered Jericho and the land of Canaan |
| **Death** | 110 years old — buried in Timnath-serah |

---

## Key Events

| Event | Scripture | Significance |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **Battle against Amalek** | Exodus 17 | Leads Israel's army while Moses holds up his hands |
| **The twelve spies** | Numbers 13–14 | One of two spies (with Caleb) who trust God — the only adults of the exodus generation to enter Canaan |
| **Crossing the Jordan** | Joshua 3–4 | The waters part — like Moses at the Red Sea |
| **The fall of Jericho** | Joshua 6 | Walls fall by faith, not force |
| **The sun stands still** | Joshua 10 | "Sun, stand still at Gibeon" — the longest day in scripture |

---

## Connection with Islam

Joshua (*Yūshaʿ ibn Nūn*) is **not named in the Qur'an** but is recognized in Islamic tradition as a prophet and the successor of Moses. The Qur'an alludes to him in 5:22–26 (the spies' report) and 18:60 (Moses' journey to the meeting of the two seas — a story involving Joshua as his servant).
