# Habil and Qabil

**Habil** (Abel) and **Qabil** (Cain) — the two sons of Adam and Eve, the first siblings in human history. Their story is the archetype of jealousy, murder, and its aftermath.

The Qur'an does not name them — it calls them *ibnay Ādam* (the two sons of Adam). The names Habil and Qabil come from Islamic tradition.

---

## The Story

See the full narrative: [[Habil and Qabil — The Raven's Lesson]]

Habil was a shepherd; Qabil a farmer. Each offered a sacrifice: Habil offered his best sheep; Qabil offered poor crops. The fire from heaven accepted Habil's and rejected Qabil's.

Qabil's jealousy consumed him. Habil refused to fight back:

> *"If you stretch your hand against me to kill me, I will not stretch my hand against you to kill you — I fear Allah, the Lord of the worlds."* — **5:28**

Qabil killed his brother — the first murder in human history. Not knowing what to do with the body, he saw a raven scratch the ground and bury another raven. He understood and buried his brother.

> *"Woe to me — am I unable to be like this raven?"* — **5:31**

---

## The Divine Law

The story establishes the most far-reaching legal principle in scripture:

> *"Whoever kills a soul — unless it is for murder or spreading corruption — it is as if he had killed all of humanity. Whoever saves a life — it is as if he had saved all of humanity."* — **5:32**
