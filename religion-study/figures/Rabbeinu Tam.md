# Rabbeinu Tam (רבנו תם — Jacob ben Meir)

The pre-eminent Tosafist of the 12th century and the spiritual leader of French Jewry. A grandson of Rashi, Rabbeinu Tam shaped the Tosafot — the critical glosses on Rashi's Talmud commentary — into a new method of Talmudic analysis that defined Ashkenazi learning for centuries. His rulings often deliberately diverged from Rashi's.

## Key Contributions

- ***Sefer ha-Yashar* (Book of the Upright):** Rabbeinu Tam's most important work, containing *Tosafot* (Talmudic glosses), *She'elot u-Teshuvot* (responsa), *Pesakim* (legal decisions), and *Chiddushim* (novellae). It showcases his method: rigorous dialectic that compares every relevant Talmudic passage, challenges apparent contradictions, and arrives at a novel synthesis.
- **Development of the Tosafot Method:** Rabbeinu Tam transformed Talmud study from Rashi's explanatory commentary into a dynamic, dialectical discipline. Tosafot do not simply explain the text — they question it, compare it with distant passages, and construct arguments that sometimes overturn the apparent meaning of a sugya.
- **Ruling Against Rashi:** In numerous cases Rabbeinu Tam explicitly rejected Rashi's interpretation or legal ruling, establishing the principle that the Tosafists — though descendants and students of Rashi — could disagree with him. Famous examples include the placement of the *shemoneh esreh* tefillin.
- **Communal Leadership:** Rabbeinu Tam was the acknowledged head of the Jewish communities of Northern France. He convened synods, issued takkanot (communal decrees), and corresponded with Jewish leaders across Europe. His authority was recognised even in Germany and England.
- **Victim of the Second Crusade (1147):** During the Crusader violence, Rabbeinu Tam was attacked and wounded in Ramerupt. According to the chronicler Ephraim of Bonn, he was saved by a crusader who recognised him as a scholar, but his fellow Jews were murdered.

## Life & Legacy

- Younger brother of Rashbam (Rabbi Shmuel ben Meir) and Rabbi Yitzchak ben Meir — all three grandsons of Rashi
- The name "Rabbeinu Tam" means "Our Perfect Teacher" — a reference to Jacob in Genesis 25:27, who is described as *ish tam* (a simple/perfect man)
- His rulings on *halitzah* (the ceremony of release from levirate marriage) created a distinct tradition that differs from Sephardi practice — the "shoe of Rabbeinu Tam"
- The "Rabbeinu Tam" tefillin (worn by many Ashkenazi Jews today) follow his opinion on the order of the scrolls inside the tefillin boxes, differing from Rashi's order
- His influence on Ashkenazi Talmud study is so profound that the standard page of the Talmud still includes Tosafot that largely follow his method
