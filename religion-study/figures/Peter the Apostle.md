# Peter the Apostle

> The fisherman who became the Rock — chief of the apostles, preacher at Pentecost, and the one who denied Christ three times before weeping bitterly.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Original name** | Simon (son of Jonah) — named **Cephas** (Peter = rock) by Jesus |
| **Occupation** | Fisherman on the Sea of Galilee |
| **Brother** | Andrew (also an apostle) |
| **Role** | Leader of the Twelve, preacher to the Jews |
| **Death** | c. 64–67 CE — crucified upside-down in Rome (tradition) |

---

## Key Moments

| Event | Scripture | Significance |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **The call** | Matthew 4:18–20 | "Follow me — I will make you fishers of men" — they leave their nets immediately |
| **The confession** | Matthew 16:13–20 | "You are the Christ, the Son of the living God" — the rock on which Jesus builds his church |
| **The transfiguration** | Matthew 17:1–8 | Witnesses Jesus' divine glory with James and John |
| **The denial** | Matthew 26:69–75 | Denies Jesus three times before the rooster crows — "I do not know the man!" |
| **The restoration** | John 21:15–19 | Jesus asks three times: "Do you love me?" — "Feed my sheep" |
| **Pentecost** | Acts 2 | Preaches the first Christian sermon — 3,000 converted |
| **The vision** | Acts 10 | Gentiles are welcomed into the church (Cornelius) |

---

## Connection with Islam

Peter is **not named in the Qur'an**. However:
- The disciples of Jesus (*al-Ḥawāriyyūn*) are mentioned in the Qur'an (3:52, 61:14) as helpers of God
- Islamic tradition names **Peter** (Sham'ūn al-Ṣafā / Simon the Pure) as the chief of the disciples
- The Qur'an does not narrate Peter's denial, confession, or role in the early church
- The Islamic view of the disciples is that they were faithful Muslims who believed in the message of Jesus
