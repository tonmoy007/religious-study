# Ravana

> The ten-headed demon-king of Lanka — a scholar of the Vedas, a devotee of Shiva, a formidable warrior, undone by his own lust and pride.

## Ravana's Greatness

Before his fall, Ravana is magnificent:

- **Scholar**: Master of all four Vedas, the six Shastras, and through his own *Ravana Samhita* (the most authoritative text on astrology)
- **Devotee**: He performed penance for 10,000 years — cutting off his heads one by one — until Shiva appeared. The name **Ravana** ("the roaring one") comes from the cries of the world when Shiva stepped on his fingers as he played the veena
- **King**: Lanka was a golden city — the Asura kingdom was wealthy, cultured, and powerful
- **Artist**: He played the veena with his sinews as strings

## The Undoing

> "Ravana knew the Vedas, but he did not know himself."

His flaw is **kama** (lust) and **ahamkara** (ego). The abduction of Sita is not just a crime — it is the one mistake that destroys his entire world: his sons, his brothers, his city, his kingdom, his life.

---

## Ravana in Other Traditions

In **Jainism**, Ravana is a tragic figure — he will be reborn as a Tirthankara (a liberated soul). In some **Buddhist** traditions (the *Dasarnha Jataka*), Ravana is a king who befriends the Bodhisattva.
