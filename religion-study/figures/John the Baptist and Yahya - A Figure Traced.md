# John the Baptist & Yahya — A Figure Traced

> Part of [[Cross-Scripture Connections MOC]] · a **single-figure deep dive**

John — *Yaḥyā* in Arabic — is the prophet of the **miraculous birth to elderly
parents**, the forerunner whose story sits right beside Jesus' in both
scriptures. The Qur'an even shares the Gospel's striking detail that **no one
before bore his name**. A **genealogical** connection (see
[[Shared Abrahamic Figures]]); a companion to [[Mary and Maryam - A Figure Traced]]
and [[Jesus and Isa - A Figure Traced]].

## The miraculous birth to barren parents
Both scriptures open the same way: the aged, childless priest **Zachariah**
(Zakariyya) prays, and an angel promises a son named John.

> [!quote] Luke 1 — Zacharias and Elisabeth
> **1:7** — "they had no child, because that **Elisabeth was barren**, and they both were now well stricken in years."
> **1:13** — "thy wife Elisabeth shall bear thee a son, and thou shalt **call his name John**."

> [!quote] Qur'an — Zachariah's tidings
> **Al-Imran 3:38–39** — "the angels called to him… Allah giveth thee glad tidings of (a son whose name is) **John**… lordly, **chaste, a prophet** of the righteous."
> **Maryam 19:7** — "O Zachariah! Lo! We bring thee tidings of a son whose name is **John**; we have given the same name to **none before (him)**."

## The shared "unique name" detail
The agreement is unusually specific. In Luke, when relatives object that *no one
in the family* bears the name, Zacharias writes "**His name is John**" and all
marvel (Luke 1:61–63). The Qur'an makes the same point divinely: "we have given
the same name to **none before him**" (19:7). Two scriptures independently
flag the **novelty of the name "John"** — a fine-grained parallel.

## John's character: ascetic forerunner vs. dutiful prophet
- **The Gospels** cast John as the **forerunner of the Messiah** — the wilderness
  preacher who baptizes Jesus and prepares his way: "he shall be great in the
  sight of the Lord, and shall drink neither wine nor strong drink… filled with
  the Holy Ghost, even from his mother's womb" (Luke 1:15). His role is
  *relational to Jesus*.
- **The Qur'an** praises Yahya for his **personal virtues** without the
  forerunner framing — wisdom given in childhood, devotion, and tenderness to his
  parents:

> [!quote] Maryam 19:12–15
> "O John! **Hold fast the Scripture**. And we gave him **wisdom when a child**, and compassion from Our presence, and **purity**; and he was devout, and **dutiful toward his parents**… **Peace on him** the day he was born, and the day he dieth and the day he shall be raised alive!"

That closing blessing — "**Peace on him** the day he was born, the day he dies,
the day he is raised" — is the **same formula** the Qur'an gives Jesus in his
cradle-speech (Maryam 19:33), linking the two prophets in parallel (see
[[Jesus and Isa - A Figure Traced]]).

| | Gospels (Luke) | Qur'an |
|---|---|---|
| Parents | barren Zacharias & Elisabeth | aged Zachariah |
| The name | "His name is John" — none in the family | "we have given the name to none before him" |
| His role | **forerunner** who baptizes Jesus | a **chaste prophet**, dutiful son |
| Distinctive | baptism, wilderness, "prepare the way" | "Peace on him" — born, dies, raised |
| His death | beheaded by Herod (Mark 6) | not narrated |

## Same prophet, different spotlight
The Gospels define John by his **mission toward Jesus** (the baptizer, the voice
crying in the wilderness); the Qur'an defines him by his **inner righteousness**
(wisdom, purity, filial duty). The miraculous birth and the unique name are
shared almost exactly — another case where the closeness makes the difference of
*emphasis* legible.
