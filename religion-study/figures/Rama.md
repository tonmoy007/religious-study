# Rama

> The seventh avatar of Vishnu — the ideal king, the perfect son, the faithful husband, and the tragic hero who could not escape his own dharma.

## The Ideal Man (Maryada Purushottama)

Rama is the *Maryada Purushottama* — the **Perfect Man**. He embodies every virtue:

| Virtue | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **Obedience** | Accepts exile without question — a father's word is law |
| **Courage** | Faces Ravana's entire army alone if needed |
| **Compassion** | Never kills a surrendering enemy |
| **Justice** | Enforces royal dharma even at personal cost — Sita's second exile |
| **Forgiveness** | Forgives Kaikeyi; accepts Vibhishana (Ravana's brother) as an ally |

---

## Rama as God

Though Rama is always depicted as aware of his divinity (he knows he is Vishnu), he rarely uses it. He behaves as a human — he weeps, grieves, doubts, and makes mistakes. This is the paradox of the avatar: the divine who chooses to live as a mortal.
