# Hūd — A Figure Traced

> Part of [[Cross-Scripture Connections MOC]] · an **Arabian-prophet deep dive**

Hūd is one of the three "Arabian prophets" — figures the Qur'an treats as major messengers but who have **no biblical counterpart**. He was sent to the ancient people of **ʿĀd** in southern Arabia, a powerful tribe dwelling in the region of *al-Aḥqāf* (the Sandy Plains, see [[046 Al-Ahqaf]]).

Unlike the shared Abrahamic prophets, Hūd belongs to a prophetic landscape rooted entirely in Arabian memory. (See [[The Arabian Prophets - Figures the Bible Lacks]] for the comparative framework.)

## The message

Hūd's call follows the Qur'an's signature "punishment story" template:

> [!quote] Hud 11:50
> "And unto (the tribe of) **A'ad** (We sent) their brother, **Hud**. He said: O my people! **Serve Allah! Ye have no other Allah save Him.** Lo! ye do but invent."

His people are condemned for their arrogance and their trust in their own strength:

> [!quote] Hud 11:52
> "And O my people! Ask forgiveness of your Lord, then turn unto Him repentant; He will cause the sky to rain abundance on you and add strength to your strength. Turn not away, guilty!"

## The destruction of ʿĀd

When ʿĀd rejected Hūd, they were destroyed by a **fierce, furious wind** that raged for seven nights and eight days — a punishment referenced across multiple surahs:

| Surah | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| [[007 Al-A'raaf]] | Hūd sent to ʿĀd; the people's arrogance |
| [[011 Hud]] | The most detailed account of Hūd's dialogue and the destruction |
| [[026 Ash-Shu'araa]] | Hūd's warning: "Will ye not ward off evil?" |
| [[046 Al-Ahqaf]] | The dunes of ʿĀd, the wind carrying them away |
| [[054 Al-Qamar]] | ʿĀd in the warning cycle: "How awful was My punishment" |
| [[069 Al-Haaqqa]] | ʿĀd destroyed by a raging wind for seven nights |
| [[089 Al-Fajr]] | Iram of the pillars — the lost city of ʿĀd |

### The city of Iram

The Qur'an mentions *Iram dhāt al-ʿimād* ("Iram of the pillars," 89:7), a legendary city unmatched in the land. It has been speculatively linked to the archaeological site of *Ubar* (also called "The Atlantis of the Sands") in Oman's Dhofar region.

## Why Hūd matters for cross-scripture study

Hūd is a key exhibit in the argument that **the Qur'an is not derivative of the Bible**. It asserts an independent line of Arabian prophets, making the universal claim that *every* nation received a warner (16:36). The consistency of the warn-reject-destroy template across Hūd, [[Noah and Nuh - A Figure Traced|Nuh]], [[Salih - A Figure Traced|Ṣāliḥ]], [[Shu'ayb - A Figure Traced|Shuʿayb]], and [[Lot and Lut - A Figure Traced|Lut]] reveals how the Qur'an *thinks* about prophecy as a unified pattern.
