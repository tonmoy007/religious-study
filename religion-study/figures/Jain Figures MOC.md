# Jain Figures — Map of Content

Key figures of the Jain tradition — the 24 Tīrthaṅkaras (ford-makers), the Ganadharas (chief disciples), the great Ācāryas (teachers), and the monks and laypeople who shaped Jain history.

---

## The Tīrthaṅkaras (Ford-Makers)

24 beings who have conquered saṃsāra and shown the path. The first, Ṛṣabha, is prehistoric; the last, Mahāvīra, is historical.

| Figure | # | Period | Known For |
|--------|---|--------|-----------|
| **[[Ṛṣabha]]** (Ādinātha) | 1st | Prehistoric | First Tīrthaṅkara; taught agriculture, writing, arts, law — the civilising hero |
| **[[Neminātha]]** (Aristanemi) | 22nd | Legendary | Kṛṣṇa's cousin; renounced on his wedding day when he heard the animals crying |
| **[[Pārśva]]** (Parshvanatha) | 23rd | c. 9th–8th c. BCE | Established the fourfold vow (non-violence, truth, non-stealing, non-attachment); protected by the serpent Dharaṇendra |
| **[[Mahāvīra]]** (Vardhamāna) | 24th | 599–527 BCE | The most recent Tīrthaṅkara; established the five great vows; contemporary of the Buddha |

---

## Ganadharas (Chief Disciples)

The 11 Ganadharas were Mahāvīra's first disciples, who understood the entirety of his teaching and founded the Jain scriptural tradition.

| Figure | Role | Key Contribution |
|--------|------|-----------------|
| **[[Indrabhūti Gautama]]** | Chief Ganadhara | The first disciple; Mahāvīra's foremost expositor; his teachings form the core of the Āgamas |
| **[[Sudharmā]]** | Ganadhara | Outlived all other Ganadharas; his student Jambūsvāmin became the first Śruta Kevalin; the Śvetāmbara Āgamas trace their lineage through him |
| **[[Jambūsvāmin]]** | First Śruta Kevalin | The last human to know the complete Jain canon by heart; bridge between the Ganadharas and the post-canonical age |

---

## Ācāryas & Philosophers

The teachers who preserved, systematised, and defended the Jain Dhamma across two millennia.

| Figure | Sect | Era | Key Work |
|--------|------|-----|----------|
| **[[Bhadrabāhu]]** | Unified (pre-schism) | c. 350 BCE | Kalpa Sūtra; prophesied the Great Famine; led the Digambara migration to Karnataka |
| **[[Sthūlabhadra]]** | Śvetāmbara | c. 310 BCE | Presided over the Pāṭaliputra Council; preserved the Śvetāmbara canon from extinction |
| **[[Kundakunda]]** | Digambara | c. 2nd c. CE | Samayasāra, Pravacanasāra, Pañcāstikāya — the greatest Digambara philosopher |
| **[[Umaswāmi]]** (Umasvāti) | Both sects revere | c. 2nd–5th c. CE | Tattvārtha Sūtra — the only Jain text accepted by all sects; the "Jain Gītā" |
| **[[Haribhadra]]** (Haribhadra Sūri) | Śvetāmbara | c. 8th c. CE | Ṣaḍdarśanasamuccaya, Yogabindu, Dharma Bindu — the philosopher who studied all 6 systems |
| **[[Hemacandra]]** | Śvetāmbara | 1089–1172 CE | Yogaśāstra, Triṣaṣṭiśalākāpuruṣacaritra (Lives of the 63 Illustrious Men), grammar, lexicography — the greatest Jain polymath |
| **[[Āryā Suhastin]]** | Śvetāmbara | c. 4th c. BCE | Early Śvetāmbara patriarch; stabilised the Saṅgha after the famine |

## Other Key Figures

| Figure | Distinction | Key Connection |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **[[Bāhubali]]** (Gommateshvara) | Son of Ṛṣabha; renounced after a war with his brother Bharata; stood in meditation for 12 years | 57-foot statue at Shravanabelagola — the tallest monolithic statue in India |
| **[[Cāmuṇḍarāya]]** | Digambara general (10th c.) | Commissioned the Bāhubali statue; patron of Jain literature and art |
| **[[Gosāla Maskarīputra]]** | Former disciple → rival | Left Mahāvīra and founded the Ājīvika sect; central antagonist in Jain narratives |
