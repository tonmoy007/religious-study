# Brahma

> The Creator — the first god of the Hindu Trimurti, who brings the universe into being and is the source of all knowledge.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Role** | Creator of the universe |
| **Consort** | Saraswati (goddess of knowledge) |
| **Vahana** | Hamsa (swan/ goose) |
| **Color** | Red |
| **Symbols** | Vedas, rosary, water pot, ladle (for sacrifice) |
| **Heads** | Four — facing the four directions |
| **Abode** | Brahmaloka / Satyaloka |

---

## Origin

Brahma is **svayambhu** (self-born) — born from a lotus that emerges from Vishnu's navel. He creates the universe, the gods, the sages, and the four varnas (social orders).

---

## Key Stories

| Story | Role |
|-------|------|
| **Creation** | Creates the universe, the Vedas emerge from his heads |
| **The birth of the sages** | Creates the Prajapatis (progenitors) from his mind |
| **Daksha's sacrifice** | His son Daksha holds the Yajna that leads to Sati's suicide |
| **Bhagiratha's penance** | Grants Ganga's descent to earth |
| **The boon to Hiranyakashipu** | Grants the near-immortality that leads to the Narasimha crisis |

---

## Worship

Brahma is the least worshipped of the Trimurti. Only one major temple exists (Pushkar, Rajasthan). The curse of the sage Bhrigu — "No one shall worship you" — is the traditional explanation.
