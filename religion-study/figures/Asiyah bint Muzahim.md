# Asiyah bint Muzahim

The wife of Pharaoh, the adoptive mother of Moses, and one of the four perfect women of Paradise. She is the archetype of the believer trapped in a household of unbelief — a woman who chose God over the most powerful man on earth and was martyred for it.

---

## Biography

- **Known as:** Asiyah bint Muzahim
- **Married to:** Pharaoh (the unnamed Pharaoh of the Exodus)
- **Status:** Queen of Egypt
- **Role:** Adopted Moses from the Nile
- **Faith:** Believed in the God of Moses (in secret)
- **Death:** Martyred by Pharaoh for her faith

---

## Key Moments

### The Adoption

When Moses was placed in the Nile, Asiyah found him and — against Pharaoh's instinct to kill him — pleaded:

> *"He is a comfort to the eye for me and for you. Do not kill him — perhaps he may benefit us, or we may adopt him as a son."* — **Surah 28:9**

She persuaded Pharaoh. The baby who would later bring down Egypt was raised in its most powerful palace.

### The Secret Believer

Asiyah believed in Moses' message in secret. She observed the miracles, the plagues, and her husband's growing fury — and remained steadfast in faith.

### The Martyrdom

When Pharaoh discovered her faith, he subjected her to torture — tying her to four stakes with a millstone. As she was being tortured, the angels shaded her, and the house she had prayed for was shown to her in Paradise. She prayed:

> *"My Lord, build for me near You a house in Paradise, and save me from Pharaoh and his deeds, and save me from the wrongdoing people."* — **Surah 66:11**

---

## Legacy

- One of the **four perfect women of Paradise** — alongside Mary, Khadijah, and Fatimah
- Her story in Surah 66:10–12 is paired with Mary as the two examples for believers
- She is the model of faith when surrounded by unbelief — the believer in the enemy's house
- Her feast day (conceptually) is commemorated in many Muslim cultures
