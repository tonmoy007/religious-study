# Duryodhana

> The eldest Kaurava — the jealous prince who could not bear his cousins' success, who conspired to destroy them, and who fought to the very end with unparalleled courage.

---

| Detail | Information |

| **Friend** | Karna |
| **Guru** | Shakuni (maternal uncle) |

---

## Duryodhana's Complexity

Duryodhana is not pure evil:

- **The good**: He is a generous friend — he makes Karna a king, he loves his brothers, he is brave in battle. He knows he is wrong about the dice game — but he cannot stop.

- **The flaw**: His jealousy is **boundless**. The sight of Indraprastha — the golden city the Pandavas built — drives him insane. "I cannot bear to see their prosperity."

- **The end**: On the battlefield, dying with his thigh shattered, he tells the Pandavas: "I know dharma — but I could not follow it. I knew adharma — but I could not leave it."
