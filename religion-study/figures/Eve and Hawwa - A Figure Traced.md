# Eve & Hawwa — A Figure Traced

> Part of [[Cross-Scripture Connections MOC]] · a **single-figure deep dive**

Eve — *Ḥawwā'* in Arabic — is the **first woman**, created from Adam's rib and responsible, with Adam, for the **Fall from the Garden**. The Bible and the Qur'an share the same core story — the tree, the temptation, the expulsion — but differ in where they place **blame** and **responsibility**, and in what they say about **original sin**. A **genealogical** connection; a companion to [[Adam - A Figure Traced]], [[First Murder - Cain and Abel]], and [[Creation by Divine Word and Light]].

## The shared story: creation, the tree, the expulsion

Both scriptures agree on the basic sequence: Adam and his wife dwell in a Garden, are forbidden one tree, are tempted by Iblis/Satan, eat from it, and are expelled from Paradise.

> [!quote] Genesis 2–3 — the biblical account
> **2:21–23** — "And the rib, which the LORD God had taken from man, made he a **woman**… She shall be called **Woman**, because she was taken out of Man."
> **3:1–6** — "the **serpent** said unto the woman… Ye shall not surely die… And when the woman saw that the tree was good for food… she **took of the fruit thereof, and did eat, and gave also unto her husband** with her; and he did eat."
> **3:16** — "Unto the woman he said, I will greatly multiply thy sorrow and thy conception; in **sorrow thou shalt bring forth children**; and thy desire shall be to thy husband, and he shall rule over thee."

> [!quote] Qur'an — the garden and the temptation
> **Al-Baqara 2:35** — "And We said: O **Adam**! Dwell thou and **thy wife** in the Garden, and eat ye freely (of the fruits) thereof where ye will; but **come not nigh this tree** lest ye become wrong-doers."
> **Al-A'raf 7:19–20** — "O Adam! Dwell thou and **thy wife** in the Garden… But **Satan whispered to them** that he might manifest unto them that which was hidden from them of their shame…"
> **Ta-Ha 20:120–121** — "But **Satan whispered to him**, saying: O Adam! Shall I show thee the tree of immortality… Then **they twain ate** thereof, so that their shame became apparent unto them, and they began to hide by heaping on themselves some of the leaves of the Garden."

## The key difference: who tempts, who is blamed?

### In the Bible, Eve is the primary actor and the one blamed
- The **serpent** (Satan's instrument) approaches Eve, not Adam (Genesis 3:1)
- Eve is **deceived** (1 Timothy 2:14 — "Adam was not deceived, but the woman being deceived was in the transgression")
- God addresses Eve first after the Fall, pronouncing **judgment on childbearing** (3:16)
- Adam's blame is for **listening to his wife** (3:17)
- The Western theological tradition (particularly Augustine and his successors) developed the doctrine of **original sin** heavily focused on Eve's role as the conduit of sin into the world

### In the Qur'an, both are tempted together, and both repent
- **Iblis (Satan)** whispers to **both** Adam and Hawwa — there is no separate conversation with the woman alone (7:20, 20:120)
- **Both eat** together — "they twain ate thereof" (20:121)
- **Both see their shame** and both try to cover themselves (7:22, 20:121)
- **Both are addressed** in the expulsion: "Go down, all of you" (2:38)
- **Both repent** and are **forgiven**: "Then Adam **received from his Lord words (of revelation)**, and He relented toward him" (2:37) — though the Arabic grammar allows the plural, including Hawwa
- There is **no concept of original sin** in Islam; each soul bears its own burden

## The naming of Eve

> [!quote] Genesis 3:20 — the name "Eve"
> "And Adam called his wife's name **Eve**; because she was the mother of all living."

> [!quote] Qur'an — "Hawwa" by tradition
> The name **Ḥawwā'** does not appear in the Qur'anic text itself — she is always referred to as "your wife" or "his wife" (***zawjuka*** / ***zawjahu***). However, Islamic tradition (Ḥadīth and tafsīr) names her **Hawwa**, from the same Semitic root *ḥ-y-y* ("to live"), the exact cognate of the Hebrew *Chawwah* / Eve.

Both names derive from the same root meaning "**life**" or "**living**" — she is the mother of all living.

## What it means

Eve/Hawwa is the figure where the **Bible and Qur'an diverge most sharply on gender and sin**. The Bible's narrative singles Eve out as the one approached by the serpent, the one deceived, the one whose punishment explicitly involves childbearing and subordination to her husband — a text that has profoundly shaped Western Christian attitudes toward women. The Qur'an distributes responsibility **evenly**: Iblis tempts both, both eat, both repent, and both are forgiven equally. There is no "curse on Eve" in the Islamic account — the consequences of the Fall fall on humanity collectively, and forgiveness is immediate for those who turn to God. Hawwa is not named in the text, but neither is she blamed.
