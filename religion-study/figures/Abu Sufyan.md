# Abu Sufyan

**Sakhr ibn Harb** — the leader of the Quraysh during the middle and late Medinan period. Abu Sufyan commanded the Quraysh armies at Uhud and the Trench, then submitted peacefully at the Conquest of Mecca and became a companion. His transformation from arch-enemy to loyal Muslim is one of the most consequential conversions in Islamic history.

---

## Biography

- **Full name:** Sakhr ibn Harb ibn Umayyah
- **Tribe:** Banu Abd Shams, Quraysh
- **Clan:** Umayyad
- **Married:** [[Hind bint Utbah]]
- **Children:** Muawiyah (founder of the Umayyad Caliphate), Yazid, others
- **Died:** c. 652 CE (32 AH), Medina

---

## Key Moments

### Before Badr (624 CE)

Abu Sufyan was leading a wealthy trade caravan from Syria when the Prophet set out to intercept it. Abu Sufyan evaded the Muslims. But the Quraysh army that came to protect the caravan was crushed at Badr — Abu Sufyan's father-in-law (Utbah) was killed, his son Hanzalah died, and his wife Hind's father was killed.

### Leader of the Quraysh

After Badr, Abu Sufyan became the supreme leader of the Quraysh. He swore:
> *"I will not touch oil or women until I have avenged Badr."*

He led the **Battle of Uhud** (625 CE) — a tactical draw that felt like a Quraysh victory. He led the **Battle of the Trench** (627 CE) — a failed siege that broke Quraysh morale.

### The Turning Point

After the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah, Abu Sufyan watched the Muslims grow stronger. He travelled to Medina for negotiations. By 630 CE, when the Muslim army of 10,000 marched on Mecca, he knew resistance was futile.

### The Submission

[[Abbas ibn Abd al-Muttalib]] brought Abu Sufyan to the Prophet's tent before the conquest. The Prophet said:

> *"Woe to you — has the time not come for you to know that there is no god but Allah?"*

Abu Sufyan submitted. He then requested:

> *"Grant honour to those who enter my house, those who enter the mosque, and those who stay in their homes."*

The Prophet declared: **"Whoever enters the house of Abu Sufyan is safe."**

---

## Legacy

- His conversion was the key to the peaceful conquest of Mecca
- Fought at the Battle of Yarmouk (636 CE) against the Byzantines
- Lost an eye in that battle, and the other at the Battle of Tawahin (later)
- His son **Muawiyah** founded the Umayyad Caliphate (661 CE)
- Seen as both a shrewd politician and a sincere convert
