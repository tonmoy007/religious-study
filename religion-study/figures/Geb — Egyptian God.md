# Geb — Egyptian God

## Summary
Geb is the earth god, the son of [[Shu — Egyptian God|Shu]] (air) and [[Tefnut — Egyptian Goddess|Tefnut]] (moisture), the consort of [[Nut — Egyptian Goddess|Nut]] (sky), and the father of [[Osiris — Egyptian God|Osiris]], [[Isis — Egyptian Goddess|Isis]], [[Set — Egyptian God|Set]], and [[Nephthys — Egyptian Goddess|Nephthys]]. In the Heliopolitan cosmology, he is the ground beneath all existence — the fertile earth that receives the dead and from which life springs. In [[The Egyptian Book of the Dead — Stories, Figures and Connections|the Book of the Dead]], Geb is the judge of the gods and the heir of [[Ra — Egyptian God|Ra]].

## Iconography
- **Reclining man** — often lying under Nut's arched body, sometimes with an erect phallus (fertility)
- **Green skin** — the colour of vegetation, life, the Nile delta
- **Goose on his head** — the "Great Cackler" who laid the cosmic egg (Geb is also the goose god)
- **Crown of Upper or Lower Egypt** — as one of the original divine kings
- In some depictions: a **serpent emerging from his body** — he is the source of both fertility and death

## The Separation of Heaven and Earth

The foundational myth of Geb and Nut:

1. **Geb and Nut are in constant embrace** — the sky is pressed against the earth, and no space exists between them
2. **Their children are born** (Osiris, Horus, Set, Isis, Nephthys) during the five epagomenal days won by [[Thoth — Egyptian God|Thoth]]
3. **[[Ra — Egyptian God|Ra]] commands Shu** (their father, the air) to **separate them**
4. **Shu lifts Nut** above him — creating the vault of the sky
5. **Geb remains below** — the earth, sometimes weeping (creating the oceans and lakes)
6. **Nut is held aloft** by the four pillars of the sky

This is the Egyptian version of the **sky-father / earth-mother** separation myth — though unusually, the earth is male and the sky female (inverted from most traditions).

## Geb as King

Geb inherited the throne of Egypt from [[Ra — Egyptian God|Ra]] and passed it to [[Osiris — Egyptian God|Osiris]], beginning the divine succession:
- **Ra** (first king of the gods)
- **Shu** (second king)
- **Geb** (third king)
- **Osiris** (fourth king)
- **Horus** (fifth king)
- **The Pharaoh** (the current "Living Horus")

This chain of succession legitimised pharaonic rule: every pharaoh was a direct descendant of [[Horus — Egyptian God|Horus]], who was the son of Osiris, who was the son of Geb, who was the son of Ra.

## Geb and the Dead

Geb is the **receiver of the dead** — the earth swallows the body, and Geb holds it safe:

- The tomb is the "house of Geb"
- The deceased lies in Geb's body, waiting for resurrection
- Geb "opens his mouth" to receive the dead — but also to release the justified soul to the sky
- At the judgment, Geb watches over the Weighing of the Heart as the representative of earthly justice
