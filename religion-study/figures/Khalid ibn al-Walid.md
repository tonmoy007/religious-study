# Khalid ibn al-Walid

**Sayf Allāh al-Maslūl** (the Drawn Sword of Allah) — the greatest military general in Islamic history. He fought against the Muslims at Uhud, converted after the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah, and went on to conquer Arabia, Iraq, and Syria. Never lost a single battle in his entire career — before or after Islam.

---

## Biography

- **Born:** c. 592 CE, Mecca
- **Died:** 642 CE (21 AH), Homs (Syria)
- **Tribe:** Banu Makhzum, Quraysh
- **Tribal role:** Leader of the Makhzum — the Quraysh's military class
- **Known for:** Genius in cavalry tactics, never defeated in battle

---

## Key Moments

### Against the Muslims

**Uhud (625 CE):** As a Quraysh cavalry commander, Khalid saw the archers abandon their post, wheeled his cavalry around the pass, and struck the Muslims from the rear — turning Quraysh defeat into a draw.

**The Trench (627 CE):** Khalid led the cavalry assault against the trench, but the ditch made his tactics useless.

### Conversion

Khalid converted after the **Treaty of Hudaybiyyah** (628 CE), along with **Amr ibn al-As** and **Uthman ibn Talhah**. He presented himself before the Prophet, who said:

> *"I have seen you fight against us many times."*

Khalid replied: "But I knew — you are the Messenger of Allah." The Prophet forgave him and called him **Sayf Allah** (Sword of Allah).

### The First Commander

Khalid's first campaign was the expedition to **Mu'tah** (629 CE) in Syria. After three Muslim commanders fell, Khalid took command and executed a brilliant tactical retreat that saved the army. The Prophet called him: "One of the swords of Allah."

During the **Ridda Wars** (632–633 CE), Khalid crushed the apostasy rebellions in Arabia — including the most dangerous rebel, **Musaylimah the Liar**, at the **Battle of Yamamah** (12,000 vs. 40,000).

### Conquest of Iraq

Khalid invaded **Sasanian Iraq** (633 CE), winning a series of battles against the Persian empire at **Ullays, Walaja, and Firaz** — each a masterpiece of tactical manoeuvre.

### Conquest of Syria

He then force-marched across the desert from Iraq to Syria — a six-day, 200-mile crossing — and arrived to take command of the Muslim armies. He defeated the **Byzantines** at:
- **Battle of Ajnadayn** (634 CE)
- **Battle of Fahl** (635 CE)
- **Siege of Damascus** (635 CE)
- **Battle of Yarmouk** (636 CE) — the decisive battle that gave Syria to the Muslims

### Dismissal

Caliph Umar removed Khalid from command — not for incompetence, but to prevent the army from treating him as a *cult figure*. Khalid accepted the dismissal without protest. He died in poverty, despite having conquered empires for Islam.

---

## Legacy

- **Never lost a single battle** — a record unmatched in military history
- Known as the greatest Muslim general of the "golden generation"
- Fought 100+ battles without defeat
- The Prophet's given title "Sword of Allah" sealed his reputation forever
- Despite Umar's dismissal, he remains one of the most revered companions
