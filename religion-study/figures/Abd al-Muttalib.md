# Abd al-Muttalib

**Shaybah al-Ḥamd** — the grandfather and guardian of the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ), the chief of the Banu Hashim clan, and a central figure in the Year of the Elephant. His encounter with Abraha is one of the most famous moments in pre-Islamic Arabian history.

---

## Biography

- **Full name:** Abd al-Muttalib ibn Hashim
- **Tribe:** Banu Hashim, Quraysh
- **Role:** Chief of the Quraysh, custodian of the Zamzam well
- **Died:** c. 579 CE, Mecca (before the Prophet's prophethood)
- **Known for:** Leadership, rediscovery of Zamzam, famous reply to Abraha

---

## Key Moments

### The Zamzam Well

Before Abd al-Muttalib, the Zamzam well had been buried and lost for generations. He saw a dream instructing him where to dig, and rediscovered it — making the Quraysh immensely wealthy from the pilgrims who came to drink. The Quraysh initially disputed his ownership, but withdrew when water gushed forth.

### The Year of the Elephant (570 CE)

When Abraha's army marched on Mecca with war elephants, Abd al-Muttalib went to negotiate. Abraha asked: "What do you want?"

The legendary reply:

> *"I want you to return my camels — two hundred that you have taken."*

Abraha was astonished: "You ask for your camels but not for your temple — the sanctuary of your religion?"

Abd al-Muttalib replied:

> *"I am the owner of the camels. The House also has an Owner who will protect it."*

Abraha returned the camels. The Quraysh evacuated Mecca. The army was destroyed by flocks of birds carrying stones of baked clay.

### Care of the Prophet

Abd al-Muttalib took care of the young Muhammad after his parents died. He loved him deeply and often said: "This son of mine will have a great future." When Abd al-Muttalib died (c. 579 CE), he entrusted the Prophet to his uncle Abu Talib.

---

## Legacy

- The Prophet was 8 when his grandfather died
- Abd al-Muttalib's trust in God's protection of the Kaaba prefigured the Islamic concept of *tawakkul* (reliance on Allah)
- He never worshipped idols — some traditions suggest he was a *ḥanīf* (a monotheist in the tradition of Abraham)
