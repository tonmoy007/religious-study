# Hathor — Egyptian Goddess

## Summary
Hathor (*Het-Heru*, "House of Horus") is the goddess of love, beauty, music, dance, fertility, and joy — the heavenly cow who nourishes the pharaoh and the dead. She is the **Lady of the West**, the goddess of the necropolis who welcomes souls into the afterlife. In [[The Egyptian Book of the Dead — Stories, Figures and Connections|the Book of the Dead]], she appears as the nourisher and the guide — the one who offers food, drink, and welcome to the arriving soul.

## Iconography
- **Cow horns holding a sun disk** — her most recognisable form
- **Cow ears** — even in human form she retains bovine ears
- **Tall plumed headdress** — sometimes with a vulture crown
- **Sistrum** (sacred rattle) — the instrument of her worship; music dispels chaos
- **Menat necklace** — a heavy bead necklace associated with fertility and joy
- **Sycomore tree** — she is the "Lady of the Sycomore," the tree that nourishes the dead
- In cow form: a **full cow** with the sun disk between her horns, often wearing a menat

## Dual Nature: The Distant Goddess

Hathor has a remarkable **dual aspect**:

| Hathor (Nurturing) | Hathor/Sekhmet (Destructive) |
|--------------------|------------------------------|
| Cow goddess, mother, nourisher | Lioness goddess of war and pestilence |
| Music, dance, love, beauty | Slaughter, plague, the heat of the sun |
| Welcomes the dead | Destroys the enemies of Ra |
| Gentle, joyful | Raging, uncontrollable |

The myth of the **Destruction of Mankind** (Book of the Heavenly Cow):

1. **Ra grows old**; humanity plots against him
2. **Ra sends his Eye** (*the Distant Goddess* — Hathor) to punish them
3. **Hathor becomes Sekhmet** and slaughters humanity
4. **Ra regrets** the slaughter — he dyes beer red (or mixes it with ochre) and floods the battlefield
5. **Sekhmet drinks the beer**, becomes drunk, and reverts to **Hathor**
6. Hathor returns to Ra, and humanity is saved

**Significance**: Hathor is not merely gentle — she *contains the destructive power of the sun's heat*. Her transformation illustrates that joy and destruction are two sides of the same divine power.

## Lady of the West

As the funerary goddess:
- "The West" (*Amentet*) is the land of the dead
- Hathor welcomes the soul as "the Lady of the West"
- The Theban necropolis is "the domain of Hathor"
- She appears as a sycomore tree offering the deceased a cool drink of water — the first refreshment in the afterlife
- She is the **mother of the dead** — who nourishes and protects them as she nourished Horus

## The Seven Hathors

In Egyptian folklore, the **Seven Hathors** appear at a child's birth to decree their fate:
- They are seven identical Hathors (cow-goddesses or women with cow horns)
- Each pronounces a different aspect of the child's destiny
- Their decrees are absolute — not even the gods can change them
- They appear in the Book of the Dead (Chapter 148) as the **seven cows and the bull** who provide sustenance to the dead

## Hathor as a Sky Goddess
Hathor is also a sky goddess:
- She is the **Milky Way** — the celestial cow whose milk is the stars
- She is "The Golden One" — the sun at dawn and dusk
- Her name, "House of Horus," means she is the **sky that contains the falcon-god** (Horus)
- She is closely connected to [[Nut — Egyptian Goddess|Nut]] and [[Ra — Egyptian God|Ra]] in solar theology
