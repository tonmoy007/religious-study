# Jeremiah

> The weeping prophet — who prophesied Judah's destruction, was rejected by his people, and watched Jerusalem burn.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Ministry** | c. 627–586 BCE — from Josiah's reign to the fall of Jerusalem |
| **Location** | Anathoth (near Jerusalem) → later forced to Egypt |
| **Role** | Prophet of judgment — "to pluck up and break down, to destroy and overthrow, to build and to plant" (1:10) |
| **Death** | Tradition: **stoned to death** in Egypt |
| **Book** | Jeremiah + Lamentations (attributed) |

---

## Key Themes

| Theme | Passage | Significance |
|-------|---------|--------------|
| **The new covenant** | "I will put my law within them — I will write it on their hearts" (31:33) | The promise of a covenant not on stone but on the heart |
| **The potter and the clay** | "Like clay in the potter's hand — so are you in my hand" (18:6) | God's sovereignty over nations |
| **The Temple sermon** | "Do not trust in deceptive words: The temple of the Lord!" (7:4) | Critique of empty religion |
| **Lament** | Lamentations — acrostic poems of grief over Jerusalem's fall | The poetry of trauma and hope |

---

## Key Events

| Event | Scripture | Significance |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **The call** | 1:4–10 | "I am only a youth" — "Do not say that — you will go to all I send you" |
| **The linen belt** | 13:1–11 | A buried, rotting belt — symbol of Judah's corruption |
| **The potter's house** | 18:1–12 | The potter remakes the spoiled vessel — God may relent |
| **The broken flask** | 19:1–15 | Symbol of Jerusalem's destruction |
| **The scroll burned** | 36 | King Jehoiakim burns Jeremiah's scroll — he rewrites it |
| **Thrown into a cistern** | 38 | Left to die in mud — rescued by Ebed-melech |
| **Jerusalem falls** | 39 | His prophecies fulfilled — he weeps over the ruins |

---

## Connection with Islam

Jeremiah (*Irmiyā*) is **not named in the Qur'an** but is recognized in Islamic tradition. Some scholars identify him with the prophet in Qur'an 2:259 — the man who passed by a ruined city and wondered how God would bring it back to life (the story of **Uzayr/Ezra** according to some, or Jeremiah according to others).
