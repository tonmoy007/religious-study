# Nephthys — Egyptian Goddess

## Summary
Nephthys (*Nebet-het*, "Lady of the House") is the funerary goddess who, with [[Isis — Egyptian Goddess|Isis]], mourns, protects, and restores [[Osiris — Egyptian God|Osiris]]. She is the sister of Osiris, [[Isis — Egyptian Goddess|Isis]], and [[Set — Egyptian God|Set]], the consort of Set, and the mother of [[Anubis — Egyptian God|Anubis]]. In [[The Egyptian Book of the Dead — Stories, Figures and Connections|the Book of the Dead]], she appears as one of the two protective kites (mourning birds) whose wings shield the dead. Though less prominent than Isis, she is essential to the funerary drama.

## Iconography
- **House hieroglyph** on her head (*nebet-het* = "lady of the house")
- **Wings outstretched** — protecting the dead or surrounding Osiris's body
- **Often paired with Isis** — one at the head, one at the foot of the coffin
- **Golden kite (bird of prey)** — the form she and Isis take when searching for Osiris
- In later art: **dark blue or black dress** — symbolising the night and the hidden

## The Lamentations of Isis and Nephthys

The most important ritual text involving Nephthys is the **Lamentations** (recorded on papyri from the Ptolemaic period, but older in origin):

> *"Come to your house, O beautiful youth! Come, that you may see your son Horus on his throne! Come, that you may see your daughter-in-law Isis! Come, that you may see your brother Set as a prisoner!"*

Nephthys and Isis would be impersonated by two priestesses in temple rituals. The liturgy:
1. **Summoning Osiris** — calling him back from death
2. **Lamenting the loss** — the grief of the goddesses
3. **Rejoicing at the restoration** — Osiris lives again through Horus
4. **Protecting the deceased** — the ritual transfers to the dead person

## The Osiris Myth: Nephthys's Role

See full treatment at [[The Osiris Myth — Egyptian Story]].

- She is **Set's wife** but helps Isis against Set
- She **mourns loudly** — her grief is the archetype of funerary lament
- She **helps search** for the dismembered pieces of Osiris
- She is associated with the **canopic jar of Hapi** (the baboon-headed son of Horus guarding the lungs)

## Ambiguity of Nephthys

Nephthys is an ambivalent figure:

| Positive | Ambiguous |
|----------|-----------|
| Protector of the dead | Consort of Set — associated with the adversary |
| Lover of Osiris (in some accounts, she bears Anubis by Osiris, not Set) | "Lady of the House" — but Set is the god of the desert/foreign lands |
| Sister and helper of Isis | Sometimes described as "the barren one" (yet mother of Anubis) |
| Guardian of the night | Represents the "empty house" — the tomb |

Her name, *Nebet-het* ("Lady of the House"), may refer to the "house of the dead" — the tomb. She is the goddess of the threshold: the space between life and death.
