# Rāhula

> The Buddha's only son — "The Fetter" — who was ordained as a novice monk at the age of seven and later became an arahant, foremost in eagerness for learning.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Name** | Rāhula ("Fetter" or "Obstacle") |
| **Mother** | Princess Yaśodharā (Bhaddakaccānā) |
| **Father** | Siddhartha Gautama (the Bodhisatta) |
| **Born** | On the night of the Great Renunciation (c. 528 BCE) |
| **Ordained** | Age 7 — became the first *sāmaṇera* (novice monk) |
| **Teacher** | Sāriputta (his preceptor) |
| **Death** | Died before the Buddha |

## Birth and Naming

Siddhartha named his son Rāhula — "fetter" — on the night he left the palace. The name carries a double meaning: (1) the child was a fetter tying him to household life, and (2) the child would be a fetter binding him to the world. In leaving, Siddhartha broke that fetter.

Later tradition offers a gentler etymology: Rāhula means "the moon's eclipse" — Yaśodharā gave birth during a lunar eclipse.

## The Buddha's Return

When the Buddha returned to Kapilavatthu seven years after leaving, Yaśodharā sent Rāhula to ask his father for his inheritance. The Buddha, instead of giving him worldly wealth, ordained him as a novice monk — making him heir to the Dhamma.

> "The child is mine — I will make him the heir of the Dhamma." — The Buddha, on ordaining Rāhula

## His Training

Sāriputta was appointed as Rāhula's preceptor. The Buddha gave him specific instructions on truthfulness (the *Ambalatthika Rāhulovāda Sutta* MN 61), mindfulness (*Mahārāhulovāda Sutta* MN 62), and breathing meditation (*Ānāpānasati*, MN 62). He attained arahantship as a young man.

## His Teaching

The name **Rāhulavāda** refers to the Buddha's discourses to his son — gentle, patient teachings full of fatherly care. These suttas are among the most accessible in the Pāli Canon.

## Comparison

Rāhula's role as the child who follows the founder into the religious life parallels:
- **Ismāʿīl** (Ishmael) — the firstborn who submits to the father's spiritual mission
- **Saṃnyāsa in Hinduism** — the householder who renounces, but Rāhula is unique in renouncing as a child
- **Biblical religious orders** — children given to monastic life
