# Anu (An) — Sky God

## Summary

An (Sumerian) / Anu (Akkadian) is the god of the sky, the supreme authority and father of the gods in the Mesopotamian pantheon. His name means "Heaven." Though remote and rarely intervening directly, he is the ultimate source of divine authority, the one from whom kingship descends. His temple is E-anna ("House of Heaven") in [[Uruk]]. In the [[Enuma Elish]], Anu is the son of Anshar and Kishar; in [[Atrahasis]], he is the king of the gods who receives appeals; in the [[Epic of Gilgamesh]], [[Inanna (Ishtar) — Goddess of Love and War|Ishtar]] appeals to him for the Bull of Heaven.
