# Bhima

> The son of the wind god — the strongest of the Pandavas, whose rage was the weapon that destroyed the Kauravas, and whose two oaths defined the war.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Role** | Second Pandava, protector of the family |
| **Father** | Vayu (the wind god) |
| **Mother** | Kunti |
| **Wife** | Draupadi (shared); Hidimba (rakshasi — mother of Ghatotkacha) |

---

## The Two Oaths

At the dice game, Bhima swears two oaths — and the entire Mahabharata resolves around them:

1. **"I will drink Duhshasana's blood."** — During the war, Bhima kills Duhshasana, drags him by the hair (as Duhshasana dragged Draupadi), drinks his blood, and dances on the battlefield.

2. **"I will break Duryodhana's thigh."** — On the 18th day, Bhima fights Duryodhana with maces. He strikes below the navel (on Krishna's guidance: "He who plotted to disrobe Draupadi has no right to fairness") and shatters Duryodhana's thigh.

---

## Character

Bhima is the **raw force** of the Pandava side — the muscle, the rage, the unstoppable violence. He kills every Kaurava brother on the battlefield (except Duryodhana, whom he faces last, and Karna, whom Arjuna faces). He is the one who personally fulfills Draupadi's vow.
