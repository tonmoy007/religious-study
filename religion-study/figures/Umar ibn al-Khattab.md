# Umar ibn al-Khattab

The second Caliph of Islam, one of the closest companions of the Prophet, and the greatest architect of the Islamic empire. Known as **al-Fārūq** (the Distinguisher between truth and falsehood). His conversion was a turning point for the early Muslim community.

---

## Biography

- **Born:** c. 584 CE, Mecca
- **Died:** 644 CE (23 AH), Medina (assassinated by a Persian slave)
- **Caliphate:** 634–644 CE (10 years)
- **Tribe:** Banu Adi, Quraysh
- **Known for:** Justice, strictness, administrative genius, piety

---

## Key Moments

### Conversion

Initially a fierce opponent of Islam, Umar set out to kill the Prophet. On the way, he was told his sister Fatimah had converted. He stormed her house, heard the Qur'an being recited (Surah Ta-Ha), and was transformed. He went directly to the Prophet and declared his faith.

The Muslims had been praying in secret. After Umar's conversion, the Prophet said:

> *"O Allah, strengthen Islam with the more beloved of these two men to You: Umar ibn al-Khattab or Amr ibn Hisham (Abu Jahl)."*

Islam was now preached openly.

### Badr

At Badr, Umar argued for executing the Quraysh prisoners. His counsel (execution of leaders) was overruled by Abu Bakr's (ransom). Revelations later suggested both positions had merit (8:67).

### Caliphate

Umar's caliphate saw the **greatest expansion of Islamic territory** in history:
- Conquest of Damascus (635 CE)
- Conquest of Jerusalem (638 CE)
- Conquest of Egypt (641 CE)
- Conquest of Persia (642 CE)
- Establishment of the Islamic calendar (Hijri)

### Justice

Umar was legendary for his justice. He established the **Diwan** (public treasury), laid the foundations of Islamic jurisprudence, and personally patrolled Medina at night to check on his people. The famous story: he found a woman cooking stones to feed her crying children, and personally carried a sack of grain to her house.

---

## Legacy

- Expanded Islam from Arabia to an empire spanning Persia, Egypt, and the Levant
- Organized the provinces, tax system, and military pensions
- The first to be called **Amir al-Mu'minin** (Commander of the Faithful)
- Assassinated by Abu Lu'lu'a (a Persian slave) while leading Fajr prayer
- Buried beside the Prophet and Abu Bakr in Aisha's chamber
- The Prophet said: "If there were a prophet after me, it would be Umar."
