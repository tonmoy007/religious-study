# Enkidu

## Summary

Enkidu is the wild man created by the goddess Aruru from clay in answer to the prayers of the people of Uruk. He is the equal and companion of [[Gilgamesh]]. Born in the steppe, he lives with wild animals until seduced by a temple prostitute who civilizes him. He fights Gilgamesh, becomes his closest friend, accompanies him against [[Humbaba]] and the Bull of Heaven, and is sentenced to death by the gods for these killings. His death sends Gilgamesh on his quest for immortality. In Tablet XII of the [[Epic of Gilgamesh]], his ghost is raised to describe the underworld.

### Key Transformation

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Wild Enkidu** | Lives with gazelles, protects animals from hunters |
| **Civilized by the harlot** | After six days and seven nights, the animals flee — he has lost his innocence |
| **The equal of Gilgamesh** | Fights at the gate of Uruk, becomes the king's beloved companion |
| **Condemned by the gods** | The Anunnaki decree his death for killing Humbaba and the Bull of Heaven |
