# Marduk

## Summary

Marduk is the patron god of Babylon who rises to supreme power in the Mesopotamian pantheon. In the [[Enuma Elish]], he is the son of [[Enki (Ea) — God of Wisdom|Ea]] and Damkina. He defeats [[Tiamat]] (the salt-water dragon) in cosmic combat, splits her body to create the heavens and earth, establishes the celestial order, creates humanity from the blood of Kingu, and is granted kingship by the assembly of gods. He assigns 300 Anunnaki to heaven and 300 to the underworld. The 50 names of Marduk are a theological statement of his supremacy.

## The Fifty Names of Marduk

The climax of the Enuma Elish is a hymn listing 50 names of Marduk, each conferring a divine attribute. Among them: Asarluhi (exorcist), Tutu (creator), Nabium (prophet), Nergal (warrior). This catalogue absorbs the identities of lesser gods into Marduk, establishing his sole supremacy — a proto-monotheistic move.
