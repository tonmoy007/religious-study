# Elisha & Al-Yasa — A Figure Traced

> Part of [[Cross-Scripture Connections MOC]] · a **single-figure deep dive**

Elisha — *Al-Yasa* in Arabic — is the **successor of Elijah**, a prophet whose ministry is recorded in the Bible with an abundance of miracles but who is barely mentioned in the Qur'an. His story nevertheless represents a clear **genealogical** connection: the same prophetic lineage, inherited from Elijah. A companion to [[Elijah and Ilyas - A Figure Traced]].

## The shared figure: Elijah's heir

In both scriptures, Elisha is named as a prophet who **follows in Elijah's footsteps**, though the Qur'an mentions him only in passing.

> [!quote] 1 Kings 19:19 — the mantle passes
> "So he departed thence, and found **Elisha the son of Shaphat**… and Elijah cast his **mantle** upon him."

> [!quote] Al-An'am 6:86 — in the list of the righteous
> "And **Ishmael and Elisha and Jonah and Lot** — each one of them We preferred above (all) creatures."

> [!quote] Sad 38:48 — among the company of the good
> "And make mention of **Ishmael and Elisha and Dhul-Kifl**. All are of the chosen."

In the Qur'an, Al-Yasa is simply **one of the righteous**, listed alongside other prophets without any narrative detail — similar to how Dhul-Kifl is mentioned. The biblical tradition contains his full story.

## The Bible's abundant miracles

The Bible devotes **2 Kings 2–13** to Elisha's ministry, recording **twice as many miracles** as Elijah's account. He inherits Elijah's mantle when the older prophet is taken to heaven in a whirlwind, and immediately parts the Jordan River to prove the spirit of Elijah rests on him.

| Miracle | Scripture | Detail |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| **Parts the Jordan** | 2 Kings 2:13–14 | With Elijah's mantle |
| **Heals the waters** | 2 Kings 2:19–22 | Jericho's spring made wholesome |
| **Curse on the mockers** | 2 Kings 2:23–25 | Youths mauled by bears for mocking his baldness |
| **Jar of oil never runs out** | 2 Kings 4:1–7 | The widow's debt paid |
| **Raises the Shunammite's son** | 2 Kings 4:8–37 | Resurrection miracle |
| **Cures Naaman of leprosy** | 2 Kings 5:1–19 | The Syrian general washed in the Jordan |
| **Gehazi's leprosy** | 2 Kings 5:20–27 | His servant punished for greed |
| **Floating ax head** | 2 Kings 6:1–7 | Iron swims |
| **The Syrian army blinded** | 2 Kings 6:8–23 | Elisha's servant sees the mountain full of horses and chariots of fire |
| **Siege of Samaria** | 2 Kings 6:24–7:20 | Elisha prophesies deliverance |
| **Elisha's bones raise a man** | 2 Kings 13:20–21 | A dead man revives on touching Elisha's tomb |

Elisha is thus a figure of **double-portion power** — having asked for "a double portion of thy spirit" (2 Kings 2:9), he performs precisely twice the recorded miracles of Elijah.

## What it means

Elisha/Al-Yasa illustrates the **asymmetry of preservation** across the two scriptures. The Bible preserves a rich cycle of miracle stories (waters healed, lepers cleansed, the dead raised, armies blinded) that mark him as the most miracle-dense prophetic career in the Old Testament. The Qur'an retains only his name in the company of the righteous, without narrative. The link is purely **genealogical** — Al-Yasa inherits the prophetic mantle of Ilyās in the Islamic tradition, but the details of that inheritance (the parting of the Jordan, the fiery chariot) are known only from the biblical text.
