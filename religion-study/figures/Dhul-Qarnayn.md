# Dhul-Qarnayn

**"The Two-Horned One"** — a righteous, divinely-established king who travelled to the ends of the earth, spread justice, and built the iron wall that penned Gog and Magog. His identity is one of the most debated questions in Qur'anic studies.

---

## Identity

The Qur'an does not name Dhul-Qarnayn. The title *Dhū al-Qarnayn* means "Possessor of Two Horns." Classical and modern identifications:

| Theory                                                  | Proponents                                      | Reasoning                                                                                              |
| ------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Alexander the Great**                                 | Some medieval exegetes, modern Western scholars | Alexander journeyed to the ends of the known world, built walls, is depicted with horns on some coins  |
| **Cyrus the Great**                                     | Modern scholars (Maududi, Azad), strongest case | Cyrus was a monotheist, just, built walls, and is described in the Bible (Isaiah 45) as God's anointed |
| **An ancient Arabian/South Arabian king**               | Some early exegetes                             | The wall of Gog/Magog is in the Caucasus or Central Asia                                               |
| **A pre-Islamic Himyarite king** (e.g., As'ad al-Kamil) | Ibn Kathir mentions this                        | Yemeni kings had the epithet                                                                           |

The **Cyrus theory** is the most academically compelling — Cyrus freed the Jews, restored the Temple, and was called by God in Isaiah 45 — fitting the Qur'an's description of a righteous ruler established by Allah.

## The Three Journeys

See the full narrative: [[Dhul-Qarnayn — The Righteous King]]

1. **Westward** — to where the sun sets in a muddy spring — judged justly
2. **Eastward** — to where the sun rises — found people with no shelter
3. **Northward** — to the pass between two mountains — people asked him to build a wall against Gog and Magog

He built the wall with iron and molten copper, holding back Gog and Magog until the end of time.

---

## Key Tafsir Insights

**"Two-Horned"** — Interpretations: (a) he ruled East and West, (b) he had two braids of hair like horns, (c) his crown had two horns, (d) an ancient symbol of divine authority.

**The wall of iron:** The description matches ancient fortifications in the **Derbent Pass** (Dagestan, Russia) — a massive iron gate built by the Sasanian Persians in the 6th century CE.

**Gog and Magog in eschatology:** They will break out at the end of time (21:96), and Allah will destroy them (18:99). This is one of the major signs of the Hour.
