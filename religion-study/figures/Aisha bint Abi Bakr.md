# Aisha bint Abi Bakr

**Mother of the Believers** (Umm al-Mu'minin), wife of the Prophet, daughter of Abu Bakr al-Siddiq, and the single greatest female scholar in Islamic history. Approximately **2,210 hadith** are narrated through her — more than any other companion. The Qur'anic verses of the **Hadith al-Ifk** (Surah 24:11–20) were revealed to exonerate her from slander.

---

## Biography

- **Born:** c. 614 CE, Mecca
- **Died:** 678 CE (56 AH), Medina
- **Father:** [[Abu Bakr al-Siddiq]] — the first Caliph
- **Married:** The Prophet (ﷺ) — c. 620 CE (1–2 years before Hijrah), wedding consummated in Shawwal, 1 AH (623 CE)

---

## The Slander (Hadith al-Ifk)

See the full account: [[Hadith al-Ifk — The Slander of Aisha]]

The most famous event in Aisha's life occurred during the return from the expedition against Banu al-Mustaliq (5–6 AH). Aisha was separated from the caravan, found by a young companion (Safwan ibn al-Mu'attal) who brought her back. The hypocrites and some Muslims spread slander questioning her chastity.

She was exonerated by direct revelation — verses 24:11–20 — which warned of severe punishment for slanderers and established the Islamic law of four witnesses for adultery accusations.

---

## Scholarship

Aisha was the most prolific narrator of hadith. Her legal opinions (*fatāwā*) were sought by the senior companions, including Umar and Abu Bakr. Her areas of expertise:

- **Fiqh** (jurisprudence) — particularly family law, inheritance, and purification
- **Hadith** — she corrected other companions' narrations when they conflicted with her memory of the Prophet
- **Tafsir** — she explained the context of many Qur'anic verses
- **Medicine** — known for medical knowledge, derived from observation of the Prophet's practices

The great companion **Abu Musa al-Ash'ari** said:

> *"Whenever we companions of the Prophet had difficulty with a hadith, we asked Aisha — we always found knowledge with her."*

---

## Legacy

- The only virgin the Prophet married (all his other wives were widows)
- Maintained her influence after the Prophet's death — narrated the most intimate details of his household life
- Defeated in the **Battle of the Camel** (656 CE) against Ali — but her participation was motivated by seeking justice for the murder of Uthman
- Died in 678 CE, buried in al-Baqi' cemetery
- Permitted for the Prophet to be buried in her room — his grave is in what was her chamber
