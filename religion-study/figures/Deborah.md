# Deborah

> The only female judge of Israel — a prophetess, military strategist, and poet who led Israel to victory when no man would step forward.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Role** | Prophetess — the fourth judge of Israel |
| **Location** | Between Ramah and Bethel — judged Israel under a palm tree |
| **Husband** | **Lappidoth** (mentioned once) |
| **Military** | Barak refuses to fight without her — she goes with him |
| **Poem** | Judges 5 — the Song of Deborah, one of the oldest Hebrew poems |
| **Title** | "A mother in Israel" (Judges 5:7) |

---

## Key Story (Judges 4–5)

Israel is oppressed by **Jabin** (king of Canaan) and his general **Sisera** — 900 iron chariots. Deborah summons **Barak**: *"Has not the Lord commanded you to take 10,000 men?"* Barak replies: *"If you will go with me — I will go. If you will not — I will not go."*

Deborah agrees — but prophesies: *"The honor will not be yours — the Lord will sell Sisera into the hand of a **woman**."*

Sisera's army is defeated. He flees and seeks refuge with **Jael** — who drives a tent peg through his temple. The prophecy fulfilled.

Deborah sings the victory — the **Song of Deborah** (Judges 5) — celebrating how the "stars fought from heaven" and the tribe of Ephraim, Benjamin, Machir, Zebulun, and Naphtali answered the call.

---

## Connection with Islam

Deborah does not appear in the Qur'an. Female leadership in war is rare in scripture — Deborah's dual role as prophetess and military commander is unique. The Qur'an does have female figures who exercise authority (Bilqis the Queen of Sheba) — but none who lead armies.
