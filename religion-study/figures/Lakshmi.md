# Lakshmi

> The goddess of wealth, fortune, and abundance — the radiant consort of Vishnu, who emerges from the ocean of milk and brings prosperity wherever she dwells.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Role** | Goddess of wealth, prosperity, fortune |
| **Consort** | Vishnu |
| **Vahana** | Lotus — she is seated on or holding a lotus |
| **Symbols** | Gold coins flowing from her palm, lotus |
| **Abode** | Vaikuntha (with Vishnu) |
| **Festival** | Diwali — lamps lit to welcome her |

---

## Origins

Lakshmi emerges from the **Churning of the Ocean of Milk** — seated on a lotus, radiant, carrying the choice of all the gods. She chooses Vishnu as her husband.

---

## Incarnations

As Vishnu descends in his avatars, Lakshmi accompanies him:

| Avatar of Vishnu | Incarnation of Lakshmi |
|------------------|------------------------|
| Rama | **Sita** |
| Krishna | **Rukmini** (and Radha in some traditions) |
| Venkateshwara (Tirupati) | Padmavati |

---

## Worship

Lakshmi is one of the most widely worshipped goddesses in Hinduism. The festival of **Diwali** is primarily dedicated to her — families light lamps, clean their homes, and open their doors to welcome her.
