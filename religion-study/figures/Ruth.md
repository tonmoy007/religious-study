# Ruth

> The Moabite woman who chose Israel's God and became the great-grandmother of King David — a model of loyalty and faith.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Origin** | Moab (modern Jordan) — a nation descended from Lot |
| **Family** | Married to Mahlon (Israelite exile); widowed; mother-in-law: Naomi |
| **Second marriage** | Boaz (kinsman-redeemer) |
| **Son** | Obed → Jesse → **David** |
| **Notable** | One of four women named in Matthew's genealogy of Jesus |

---

## Key Virtues

| Virtue | Evidence |
|--------|----------|
| **Loyalty (*ḥesed*)** | "Where you go, I will go — your people shall be my people" |
| **Conversion** | "Your God shall be my God" — a Gentile who joins the covenant |
| **Humility** | Gleans in the fields as a poor widow |
| **Faithfulness** | Follows Naomi's instructions with trust |

---

## Connection with Islam

Ruth does not appear in the Qur'an. However:
- The Qur'an values **loyalty and faithfulness** in the highest degree
- Ruth's conversion parallels the pattern of **Bilqis** (Queen of Sheba) — a foreign woman who submits to the one God
- The story of Ruth's *hesed* (covenant loyalty) resonates with the Qur'anic concept of **birr** (righteousness)
