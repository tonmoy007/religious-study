# Adam — A Figure Traced

> Part of [[Cross-Scripture Connections MOC]] · a **single-figure deep dive**

The first human is the *beginning* of the shared story — and the traditions
already diverge at the start of humanity. The Bible and the Qur'an agree that
Adam is formed by God from earth, placed in a garden, and falls by eating a
forbidden tree. But they part ways on **how the fall is read**: Genesis plants
the seed of what Christianity calls *original sin*, while the Qur'an has Adam
repent and be forgiven *immediately* — no inherited guilt. A **genealogical**
connection (see [[Shared Abrahamic Figures]]).

## Creation: dust vs. clay

> [!quote] Genesis 2:7 — dust and breath
> "And the LORD God **formed man of the dust of the ground**, and **breathed into his nostrils the breath of life**; and man became a living soul."

> [!quote] Qur'an — clay and God's spirit
> **Al-Hijr 15:26, 29** — "We created man of **potter's clay** of black mud altered… when I have made him and have **breathed into him of My Spirit**, do ye fall down, prostrating yourselves unto him."

The **earth + divine breath** formula is shared almost word for word. But the
Qur'an attaches a scene Genesis lacks entirely — the **angels commanded to bow**.

## What only the Qur'an tells: the names and the prostration
Before the garden, the Qur'an stages a drama of knowledge and pride:

> [!quote] Al-Baqara 2:30–34
> **2:30** — "I am about to place a **viceroy (khalifa) in the earth**…"
> **2:31** — "And He **taught Adam all the names**… Inform Me of the names of these, if ye are truthful."
> **2:34** — "Prostrate yourselves before Adam, they fell prostrate, **all save Iblis. He demurred through pride**, and so became a disbeliever."

This gives Adam a *dignity* Genesis doesn't dwell on — he is God's **viceroy**,
endowed with **language/knowledge** that even angels lack — and it introduces
**Iblis (Satan)** as the proud refuser. In Genesis the tempter is simply "the
serpent… more subtil than any beast" (3:1), with no backstory; the Qur'an makes
the fall part of a cosmic rebellion that began with Satan's refusal to bow.

## The fall: shared event, different aftermath

> [!quote] Genesis 3 — the serpent, the fruit, the curse
> **3:6** — "she took of the fruit thereof, and did eat, and gave also unto her husband… and he did eat."
> **3:13** — "The serpent beguiled me, and I did eat."
> **3:19** — "In the **sweat of thy face** shalt thou eat bread… for **dust thou art, and unto dust shalt thou return**."

> [!quote] Qur'an — the slip, then immediate mercy
> **Al-Baqara 2:36** — "But **Satan caused them to deflect** therefrom and expelled them from the (happy) state… Fall down… There shall be for you on earth a habitation and provision for a time."
> **2:37** — "Then Adam **received from his Lord words (of revelation), and He relented toward him**. Lo! He is the relenting, the Merciful."

| | Genesis | Qur'an |
|---|---|---|
| Who tempts? | "the serpent" (no origin given) | **Iblis/Satan**, the angel who refused to bow |
| Who eats first? | **Eve**, then gives to Adam | **both** "Adam and his wife" — blame is shared |
| The consequence | curse, painful toil, **death** ("dust… return") | expulsion to earth as a temporary "habitation" |
| What comes after | exile from Eden; sin enters the world | Adam **repents and is forgiven at once** (2:37) |

## The biggest divergence: original sin vs. immediate forgiveness
This is the theological payload. In the Christian reading of Genesis (developed
by Paul and Augustine), Adam's disobedience transmits a fallen nature to **all
humanity** — the doctrine of *original sin*, which frames why humanity needs
redemption (and connects to [[Jesus and Isa - A Figure Traced|Christ's
atonement]]).

The Qur'an has **no original sin**. Adam errs, repents, and is forgiven in the
same breath (2:37). Each person is born sinless and accountable only for their
*own* deeds. So the same garden, the same tree, and the same fall lead to
**opposite anthropologies**: humanity-as-fallen-and-needing-salvation
(Christianity) vs. humanity-as-originally-pure-and-personally-responsible
(Islam). Judaism, reading the same Genesis, also largely **rejects** inherited
guilt — closer to the Islamic reading than the Christian one.

## Same first man, three doctrines of human nature
| Tradition | Adam's fall means… |
|---|---|
| Judaism | a real disobedience, but **no inherited sin**; humans choose good or evil (*yetzer*) |
| Christianity | **original sin** — a fallen nature passed to all, requiring redemption |
| Islam | a forgiven slip — **no inherited guilt**; Adam is the first **prophet** and God's viceroy |

The very *first* story already encodes each tradition's view of what a human
being *is* — proof that shared narrative and shared meaning are not the same
thing (see [[Connections Between Religious Texts]]).
