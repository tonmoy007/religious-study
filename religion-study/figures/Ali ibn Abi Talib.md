# Ali ibn Abi Talib

The Prophet's cousin, son-in-law (husband of Fatimah), the fourth Caliph of Islam, and one of the greatest warriors in Islamic history. The first male to embrace Islam (age 10), he never worshipped an idol in his life. Known for his unparalleled courage, eloquence, and spiritual depth.

---

## Biography

- **Born:** c. 600 CE, Mecca
- **Died:** 661 CE (40 AH), Kufa (assassinated by Kharijite)
- **Caliphate:** 656–661 CE (5 years)
- **Father:** Abu Talib (the Prophet's uncle and protector)
- **Mother:** Fatimah bint Asad
- **Married:** Fatimah (daughter of the Prophet)
- **Sons:** Hasan, Husayn (progenitors of the Prophet's lineage)
- **Known for:** Bravery, justice, eloquence, spirituality

---

## Key Moments

### First Male Believer

When the Prophet gathered his clan and announced his mission, Ali — then a boy of 10 — was the first to declare: "I will be your helper." The Prophet said: "This is my brother, my successor — listen to him and obey."

### The Night of Migration (Laylat al-Mabit)

When the Prophet migrated to Medina, the Quraysh planned to assassinate him. Ali slept in the Prophet's bed as a decoy, knowing his life was forfeit. The Quraysh drew their swords but found Ali — and the Prophet had escaped.

### The Champion of Badr

At Badr, Ali killed **al-Walid ibn Utbah** in single combat. He fought with unmatched valour throughout the battle.

### The Protector at Uhud

When the Muslim line broke at Uhud and the Prophet was wounded, Ali was among the few who remained and fought to protect him. He brought water to the Prophet and helped him up the mountain.

### The Conquest of Khaybar

At the siege of Khaybar (7 AH), the Prophet declared:

> *"Tomorrow I will give the banner to a man loved by Allah and His Messenger, who will conquer."*

The next day, the banner was given to Ali. He fought through the gates of Khaybar — a gate so heavy it took 40 men to move — and conquered the fortress.

### Caliphate

Ali's caliphate was turbulent:
- Fought the **Battle of the Camel** (656 CE) against Aisha
- Fought the **Battle of Siffin** (657 CE) against Muawiyah
- Faced the **Kharijite** rebellion, whom he defeated at **Nahrawan** (658 CE)
- Assassinated in the mosque of Kufa by Abd al-Rahman ibn Muljam

---

## Legacy

- The progenitor of all **Sayyids** (descendants of the Prophet through Fatimah)
- Revered by **Sunni** as the fourth rightly guided Caliph
- Central figure in **Shia** Islam as the first Imam and rightful successor of the Prophet
- Known for his sermons and sayings compiled in **Nahj al-Balaghah** (Peak of Eloquence)
- The Prophet said: "I am the city of knowledge, and Ali is its gate."
- His son **Husayn** would later be martyred at Karbala (680 CE)
