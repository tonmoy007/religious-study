# Bharata

> The brother who refused the throne — who ruled as a regent for 14 years, placing Rama's sandals on the throne, and waiting for his brother's return.

---

| Detail | Information |

| **Parents** | Dasharatha and Kaikeyi |
| **Wife** | Mandavi |

---

## The Story

Bharata is the son of Kaikeyi — the queen whose boon sent Rama into exile. But Bharata is **not complicit** in his mother's scheme. He was away in his maternal uncle's kingdom when it happened.

When he returns and learns what his mother has done, he is **furious**: "You have killed my father and exiled my brother — I want no part of this kingdom!"

He goes to the forest to find Rama. He begs Rama to return. Rama refuses — "A father's word must be honored." Bharata takes Rama's **sandals** and places them on the throne.

For 14 years, Bharata rules as regent — from a village outside Ayodhya, clothed in ascetic's bark, eating simple food. "I am no king — I am only the caretaker of the sandals."

---

## The Archetype

Bharata embodies **selfless loyalty** — the brother who sacrifices his own ambition (and the kingdom) for his brother's honor. He is the foil to Duryodhana in the Mahabharata — a man who refuses power when offered unethically.
