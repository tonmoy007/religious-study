# Samuel

> The last judge, the first of the prophets after Moses — the man who anointed Israel's first two kings and served as the conscience of the nation.

---

## Biography

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Family** | Son of Elkanah and Hannah (barren woman who prayed for a child) |
| **Tribe** | Ephraim (but served at Shiloh) |
| **Role** | Judge, prophet, priest |
| **Anointed** | **Saul** (first king of Israel) and **David** (the shepherd king) |
| **Death** | 1 Samuel 25:1 — buried in Ramah |

---

## Key Events

### The Calling (1 Samuel 3)

As a boy serving the priest Eli, Samuel hears a voice in the night. Three times he runs to Eli — three times Eli says: *"I did not call"*. Finally, Eli recognizes: *"It is the Lord — speak, Lord, for your servant hears."*

Samuel becomes the first in a long line of prophets who "stand before the Lord" — receiving revelation, delivering judgment, and interceding for the people.

### The Warning about Kings (1 Samuel 8)

Israel demands a king — like the other nations. Samuel warns them: a king will take your sons, your daughters, your fields, your vineyards. They insist. God tells Samuel: *"They have not rejected you — they have rejected me."*

### The Anointing of David (1 Samuel 16)

God rejects Saul. Samuel travels to Bethlehem — Jesse's sons pass before him. The eldest — tall and strong — looks kingly. *"The Lord does not look at the things man looks at — man looks at the outward appearance, but the Lord looks at the heart"* (16:7). The youngest — **David** — is anointed.

---

## Connection with Islam

Samuel (*Sham'ūn* or *Samū'īl*) is **not named in the Qur'an**, though some Islamic scholars identify him with the prophet who anointed **Saul (Tālūt)** in the Qur'an (2:246–248). The Qur'anic account of the Israelites demanding a king and receiving **Tālūt** parallels 1 Samuel 8–10 — suggesting Samuel (unnamed) is the prophet who appoints him.
