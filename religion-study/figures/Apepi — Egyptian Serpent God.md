# Apepi — Egyptian Serpent God

## Summary
Apepi (also Apophis, Apep) is the primordial chaos serpent, the eternal enemy of [[Ra — Egyptian God|Ra]] and of cosmic order (*[[Ma'at — Goddess of Truth|Ma'at]]*). Every night he attacks the Solar Barque as it travels through the Tuat, attempting to swallow the sun and return the universe to the primordial abyss (*Nun*). He is the **only truly evil being** in Egyptian religion — not a god with temples and worship, but a force of uncreation that must be defeated nightly. In [[The Egyptian Book of the Dead — Stories, Figures and Connections|the Book of the Dead]], the deceased must help repel Apepi to ensure the sun rises again.

## Iconography
- **Gigantic serpent** — dozens or hundreds of cubits long
- **Coiled body** — filling the entire Tuat, threatening to block the barque's path
- **Knife-scarred body** — after each night's defeat, his body is cut and bound
- **Sometimes with multiple heads** — each head representing a different aspect of chaos
- **Under the earth** — he is the force that causes earthquakes

## The Nightly Battle

The cosmic cycle:

```
    SUNRISE ──────────────────────────────────── SUNSET
       │                                            │
       │  Ra emerges from the Tuat                 Ra enters the Tuat
       │  The Mandjet Barque (day boat)    │         The Mesektet Barque (night boat)
       │                                            │
       └────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                                                     │
                                          ┌──────────┴──────────┐
                                          │  THE TUAT (NIGHT)   │
                                          │                     │
                                          │  12 hours = 12 gates│
                                          │                     │
                                          │  7th hour: APEPI    │
                                          │  attacks the barque │
                                          │                     │
                                          │  Set + gods defend  │
                                          │  using spears +     │
                                          │  magic              │
                                          │                     │
                                          └─────────────────────┘
```

At the **7th hour** of the night (the darkest hour before the turnaround):
1. Apepi manifests in the Tuat, filling the entire cavern
2. He tries to **swallow the barque** — if he succeeds, the sun never rises
3. [[Set — Egyptian God|Set]] stands on the prow of the barque, **spearing Apepi** with a harpoon
4. [[Thoth — Egyptian God|Thoth]] recites **magic spells** that weaken the serpent
5. [[Isis — Egyptian Goddess|Isis]] and other goddesses **bind Apepi** with their magic
6. The gods **cut and dismember** Apepi — but he reforms every night
7. The barque passes, the sun rises, and the cycle repeats

This NEVER ends. Apepi is never finally defeated — he is always held at bay.

## Unique Status in Egyptian Religion

Apepi is **sui generis** in Egyptian theology:

| Aspect | Apepi | Other Gods (incl. Set) |
|--------|-------|----------------------|
| Worshipped? | Never — no temples, no priests, no offerings | Yes — every god has a cult |
| Can be propitiated? | No — he cannot be bargained with | Yes — offerings and prayers |
| Moral status | Pure evil — uncreated chaos | Ambivalent — agents of Ma'at |
| Origin | Born from the primordial waters (Nun) — older than creation | Born from the gods within creation |
| Defeated? | Every night — but never destroyed | Tricked, defeated, or reconciled |
| Can the deceased help? | Yes — by reciting spells, the deceased fights alongside Ra | Yes — by emulating them |

## The "Books of Overthrowing Apepi"

In the Late Period, priests developed elaborate rituals called the *Books of Overthrowing Apepi*:
- **Papyrus Bremner-Rhind** contains the longest surviving version
- The ritual involved:
  1. Drawing Apepi's image on papyrus in green ink
  2. Writing his name in red (the colour of chaos)
  3. Burning the papyrus
  4. Trampling the ashes
  5. Cutting up a wax effigy of a serpent
  6. Spitting on the remains
- These were performed **daily** in temples, especially in Thebes

Apepi's name was often **vowelled out** or replaced with euphemisms ("the Evil One," "the Enemy") because speaking the name of chaos gave it power.

## The True Adversary

Despite [[Set — Egyptian God|Set]] being the "god of chaos," Set fights *for* Ra against Apepi. This means:

> Set is the *controlled* chaos that defends order.
> Apepi is the *uncontrolled* chaos that seeks to destroy order.

In comparative terms:
- Apepi = genuine evil (chaos that must be eliminated)
- Set = manageable evil (chaos that can be harnessed for good)
