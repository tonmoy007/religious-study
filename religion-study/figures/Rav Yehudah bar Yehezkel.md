# Rav Yehudah bar Yehezkel (רב יהודה בר יחזקאל)

Rav Yehudah bar Yehezkel was a second-generation Babylonian Amora and the founder of the **Pumbedita Academy**, which would become one of the most prestigious centres of Jewish learning for centuries. He was the primary student of Rav and also studied under Samuel of Nehardea, synthesising the traditions of both great academies into his own rigorous legal methodology.

Rav Yehudah was known for his exceptional precision in legal formulation. The Talmud records that he would not allow his students to ask questions that were not directly relevant to the matter at hand, insisting on disciplined, focused study. His academy at Pumbedita emphasised sharp analysis and careful memorisation of baraitot (Tannaitic sources). After the destruction of Nehardea (c. 260 CE), Pumbedita became the principal centre of Babylonian Jewish scholarship alongside Sura, and it remained active well into the Geonic period. Rav Yehudah's students included many of the leading Amoraim of the next generation.

## Key Contributions

- **Founding of Pumbedita Academy:** After the fall of Nehardea, Yehudah established a new yeshiva in Pumbedita (modern-day Fallujah, Iraq), which became the second great academy of the Amoraic period alongside Sura. Pumbedita was known for its sharp dialectic and rigorous legal analysis.
- **Precise Legal Formulation:** Rav Yehudah insisted on precise wording in legal rulings, often rejecting formulations he considered imprecise. The Talmud quotes him saying: "We have a tradition from Rav that..." with scrupulous attention to accuracy.
- **Preservation of Rav's Teachings:** As Rav's foremost student, Yehudah became the primary transmitter of Rav's halakhic traditions, ensuring they were faithfully passed to subsequent generations.
- **Bridge between Generations:** He linked the first generation of Amoraim (Rav and Shmuel) with the later generations (including Abaye and Rava), forming a crucial chain in the transmission of the Oral Law.

## In Jewish Tradition

- Rav Yehudah's academy at Pumbedita became so famous that the phrase "in the study hall of Pumbedita" is synonymous with sharp, incisive Talmudic analysis
- The Talmud relates that Rav Yehudah would not sit on a chair while teaching if a pregnant woman was present, out of reverence for potential life (Shabbat 127a)
- He was known for his extreme piety and adherence to the letter of the law, sometimes at the cost of his own comfort
- His teachings are cited across the entire Babylonian Talmud
