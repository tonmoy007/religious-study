# Draupadi

> The daughter of fire — the queen who married five men, was humiliated in open court, and swore an oath that would destroy an entire generation.

| Detail | Information |

## Character

Draupadi is **fiery** (born from fire), proud, and relentless:

- **The vow**: "I will not braid my hair until Duhshasana's chest is ripped open." She keeps this vow for 13 years.

- **The question**: When dragged into the court, she asks: "Yudhishthira lost himself before he lost me — did he have the right to stake me?" The elders cannot answer. The question echoes: **can a man who has lost ownership of himself own another?**

- **The unreconciled**: Unlike Sita (who submits to the fire test) or Mary (who accepts her fate), Draupadi never forgives. She drives the war forward — she is the engine of justice.
