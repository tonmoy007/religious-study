# Risalah — *Prophethood & Messengership*

> The means by which God communicates His guidance to humanity — a chain of chosen messengers culminating in Muhammad. Part of [[Islamic Concepts MOC]].

---

## Definition

**Risālah** (رسالة) means "message" or "messengership." A **rasūl** (messenger) is a human being chosen by God to receive revelation (*waḥy*) and convey it to his people. Related: **Nubuwwah** (نبوة) — prophethood, from *nabī* (prophet).

| Term | Arabic | Meaning | Number in Qur'an |
|------|--------|---------|-----------------|
| *Nabī* | نبي | Prophet — one informed by God | Mentioned 43 times by name |
| *Rasūl* | رسول | Messenger — one sent with a scripture/mission | ~25 named messengers |
| *Ulū al-Azm* | أولو العزم | The five "mighty-willed" messengers | [[046 Al-Ahqaf]] 46:35, [[042 Ash-Shura]] 42:13 |

Every *rasūl* is a *nabī* but not every *nabī* is a *rasūl* — messengers receive a scripture and a mission to a people; prophets may be sent to confirm an existing message.

---

## Why Prophethood?

The Islamic argument for prophethood rests on God's **justice** (*ʿadl*) and **mercy** (*raḥmah*):

> [!quote] Qur'anic rationale
> - **Guidance**: "And We never punish until We have sent a messenger." ([[017 Al-Israa]] 17:15)
> - **Mercy**: "And We have not sent you except as a mercy to the worlds." ([[021 Al-Anbiyaa]] 21:107)
> - **Warning & Glad Tidings**: "Messengers as bearers of good news and warners, so that humanity might have no argument against Allah after the messengers." ([[004 An-Nisaa]] 4:165)
> - **Teaching**: "He it is Who sent among the unlettered a messenger from among themselves, reciting to them His verses, purifying them, and teaching them the Book and wisdom." ([[062 Al-Jumu'a]] 62:2)

## The Five Ulū al-Azm (Mighty-Willed) Messengers

Five messengers are singled out for their extraordinary perseverance (*ulū al-ʿazm*):

> [!quote] Qur'an 46:35
> > "So be patient, as the **mighty-willed** messengers were patient."
> > — [[046 Al-Ahqaf]]

These five are Noah, Abraham, Moses, Jesus, and Muhammad — the most tested and the most determined.

---

## Muhammad — The Seal of the Prophets

The distinctiveness of Muhammad's prophethood in Islamic theology:

| Doctrine | Evidence | Significance |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| **Finality** | "Muhammad is not the father of any of your men, but he is the Messenger of Allah and the Seal of the Prophets" ([[033 Al-Ahzaab]] 33:40) | No prophet after him; the message is complete |
| **Universality** | "And We have not sent you except to all of humanity, as a bearer of good news and a warner" ([[034 Saba]] 34:28) | Not limited to one people; sent to all nations |
| **Confirmation** | "He has sent down to you the Book with truth, confirming what was before it" ([[003 Aal-i-Imraan]] 3:3) | Confirms and supersedes all previous scriptures |
| **Aḥmad** | Jesus foretells "a messenger whose name is Ahmad" ([[061 As-Saff]] 61:6) | Fulfilment of biblical prophecy |

## Risalah in the Hadith

> [!quote] The Unity of Prophets
> *Abu Huraira reported:* The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said: "My example and the example of the prophets before me is like a man who built a house and beautified it, except for the place of one brick in a corner. People would walk around it and marvel, saying: 'Why has this brick not been laid?' I am that brick, and I am the Seal of the Prophets."
> — [Bukhari 3535](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Virtues%20of%20the%20Prophet)

> [!quote] Prophets are Brothers
> *Abu Huraira reported:* The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said: "The prophets are paternal brothers — their mothers differ, but their religion is one."
> — [Bukhari 3443](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Virtues%20of%20the%20Prophet)

> [!quote] The First of Prophets
> *Abu Huraira reported:* The Prophet (ﷺ) was asked about Adam: "Was he a prophet?" He said: "Yes, a prophet spoken to by Allah, created by His hand."
> — [Musnad Ahmad](scriptures/islam-hadith/08%20Musnad%20Ahmad.md)
