# Akhirah — *The Hereafter*

> The final destination of every soul — death, resurrection, judgment, and the eternal abode. Part of [[Islamic Concepts MOC]].

---

## Definition

**Al-Ākhirah** (الآخرة) — "the Last" or "the Hereafter" — is the permanent life that follows this temporary world (*al-dunyā*). Belief in the Hereafter is the second pillar of Islamic faith (after belief in God), and **nearly one-third of the Qur'an** addresses it.

> [!quote] Qur'an 57:20 — This world is transient
> > "Know that the life of this world is but play, amusement, adornment, boasting among yourselves, and competition in increase of wealth and children — like the example of a rain whose vegetation pleases the farmers, then it dries and you see it turned yellow, then it becomes debris. And in the Hereafter is a severe punishment and forgiveness from Allah and approval."
> > — [[057 Al-Hadid]]

---

## The Stages of the Hereafter

The Islamic eschatological timeline, as described in the Qur'an and Hadith:

### Stage 1: Death (Mawt)

Death is the separation of the soul from the body. It is the first stage of the Hereafter, experienced by every soul.

> [!quote] Qur'an 3:185
> > "Every soul shall taste death. And you will be paid your reward in full on the Day of Resurrection."
> > — [[003 Aal-i-Imraan]]

> [!quote] Hadith — Remembering Death
> *Abu Huraira reported:* The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said: "Remember often the destroyer of pleasures — death."
> — [Tirmidhi 2307](scriptures/islam-hadith/04%20Jami%60%20at-Tirmidhi.md#Chapters%20On%20Al-Fitan)

### Stage 2: The Grave / Barzakh (Intermediary State)

After death, souls enter **al-Barzakh** (the barrier) — a waiting state until the Resurrection. The grave experience is either a foretaste of paradise or a preview of punishment.

> [!quote] Qur'an 23:100
> > "And before them is a barrier (*barzakh*) until the Day they are resurrected."
> > — [[023 Al-Muminoon]]

> [!quote] Hadith — The Grave
> *Uthman reported:* When a burial is completed, the Prophet (ﷺ) would stand at the grave and say: "Ask forgiveness for your brother, and ask that he be made steadfast — for he is being questioned now."
> — [Abu Dawud 3221](scriptures/islam-hadith/03%20Sunan%20Abi%20Dawud.md#Funerals)

> *Anas reported:* The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "When the deceased is placed in his grave, he hears the footsteps of those who leave him. Then two angels come and make him sit and ask: 'What did you say about this man, Muhammad?' The believer says: 'I testify that he is Allah's servant and messenger.' Then it is said: 'Look at your place in the Fire — Allah has replaced it with a place in Paradise.' As for the disbeliever or hypocrite, he says: 'I do not know — I used to say what the people said.' Then it is said: 'You did not know and you did not follow.' Then he is struck with an iron hammer, and he screams so loudly that all creation hears except humans and jinn."
> — [Bukhari 1338](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Funerals)

### Stage 3: The Resurrection (Qiyāmah / Ba'th)

The Day of Resurrection begins when the Trumpet (Ṣūr) is blown by the angel Israfil. All creation — from the first to the last — is raised from the dead.

> [!quote] Qur'an 39:68
> > "And the Trumpet will be blown, and all who are in the heavens and the earth will fall dead, except whom Allah wills. Then it will be blown again, and behold, they will be standing, looking."
> > — [[039 Az-Zumar]]

The resurrection is accompanied by the **cosmic dissolution** vividly described in the Meccan surahs:

| Phenomenon | Qur'anic reference |
|------------|-------------------|
| The sun coiled up | [[081 At-Takwir]] 81:1 |
| Stars fall | [[081 At-Takwir]] 81:2 |
| Mountains scattered | [[101 Al-Qaari'a]] 101:5, [[056 Al-Waaqia]] 56:5–6 |
| Seas boiled over | [[081 At-Takwir]] 81:6 |
| Sky split open | [[082 Al-Infitaar]] 82:1 |
| Earth shaken | [[099 Az-Zalzala]] 99:1 |
| Graves overturned | [[082 Al-Infitaar]] 82:4 |
| The moon split | [[054 Al-Qamar]] 54:1 |

### Stage 4: The Gathering (Ḥashr)

All of humanity is gathered before God for judgment — on a transformed earth, under a sun brought near.

> [!quote] Qur'an 18:47
> > "And the Day We shall cause the mountains to pass away, and you will see the earth as a levelled plain, and We shall gather them all together, leaving none of them behind."
> > — [[018 Al-Kahf]]

> [!quote] Hadith — The Length of Standing
> *Abu Huraira reported:* The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "The people will sweat on the Day of Judgment until their sweat sinks seventy cubits into the earth, and it will reach up to their mouths."
> — [Bukhari 6532](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Softening%20of%20Hearts)

### Stage 5: The Judgment (Ḥisāb)

Each soul is judged with perfect justice. The **Books of Deeds** are distributed:

> [!quote] Qur'an 84:7–12
> > "Then as for he who is given his record in his right hand — he will be called to an easy account and return to his people happy. But as for he who is given his record behind his back — he will cry out for destruction and burn in a blazing Fire."
> > — [[084 Al-Inshiqaaq]]

The **Mīzān** (Balance/Scales) weighs deeds — not by quantity alone but by quality and sincerity:

> [!quote] Qur'an 101:6–9
> > "Then as for he whose scales are heavy — he will be in a pleasant life. But as for he whose scales are light — his abode will be the Abyss."
> > — [[101 Al-Qaari'a]]

### Stage 6: The Bridge (Ṣirāṭ)

A bridge spanning Hell, sharper than a sword and thinner than a hair, over which all must pass. The righteous cross swiftly; the sinful fall.

> [!quote] Hadith — The Crossing
> *Abu Huraira reported:* The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "The Bridge will be placed across Hell. I and my community will be the first to cross. On that day, only the messengers will speak, and their prayer will be: 'O Allah, grant safety!'"
> — [Bukhari 7439](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Tawhid)

### Stage 7: Intercession (Shafāʿah)

The Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) and other prophets, angels, and the righteous intercede for the sinful believers. The greatest intercession (*al-shafāʿah al-ʿuẓmā*) is Muhammad's, to begin the Judgment.

> [!quote] Hadith — The Prophet's Intercession
> *Anas reported:* The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "My intercession is for those of my community who committed major sins."
> — [Tirmidhi 2435](scriptures/islam-hadith/04%20Jami%60%20at-Tirmidhi.md#Chapters%20On%20Zuhd)

### Stage 8: Paradise (Jannah) & Hell (Jahannam)

The final, eternal abodes:

**Paradise (Jannah):**
- Gardens beneath which rivers flow ([[002 Al-Baqara]] 2:25)
- Immortal youth, pure spouses, delicious food and drink
- The greatest reward: **seeing God's Face** ([[075 Al-Qiyaama]] 75:22–23)

> [!quote] Qur'an 76:12–22 — The Rewards of Paradise
> > "And He will reward them for their patience with a Garden and silk. Reclining therein on couches, they will see neither sun nor bitter cold. Its shade will be near above them, and its fruit lowered in easy reach… And their Lord will give them a pure drink."
> > — [[076 Al-Insaan]]

**Hell (Jahannam):**
- Eternal fire ([[002 Al-Baqara]] 2:24)
- Seven levels, each for a category of sin
- Intense descriptions in the Meccan surahs: boiling water, scorching wind, dark smoke, chains

> [!quote] Qur'an 104:4–9 — Al-Humaza
> > "Nay! He will surely be thrown into the Crusher. And what can make you know what the Crusher is? It is the Fire of Allah, kindled, which rises over the hearts. It will be closed in upon them in extended columns."
> > — [[104 Al-Humaza]]

## The Purpose of Reminding of the Hereafter

The Qur'an repeatedly turns to the Hereafter for ethical and spiritual reasons:

- **To humble the arrogant**: "Do they not know that they will be resurrected for a Great Day?" ([[083 Al-Mutaffifin]] 83:4–6)
- **To console the poor**: "And the Hereafter is better for you than the first" ([[093 Ad-Dhuhaa]] 93:4)
- **To restrain the oppressor**: "Does he think that none has power over him?" ([[090 Al-Balad]] 90:5)
- **To motivate charity**: "Who will lend Allah a goodly loan?" ([[057 Al-Hadid]] 57:11)
- **To give hope**: "Say: O My servants who have transgressed against themselves, do not despair of the mercy of Allah" ([[039 Az-Zumar]] 39:53)
