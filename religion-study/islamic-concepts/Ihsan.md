# Ihsan — *Spiritual Excellence*

> The summit of the religion: to worship Allah **as if you see Him** — and if you do not see Him, **to know that He sees you**. Part of [[Islamic Concepts MOC]].

---

## Definition

**Iḥsān** (إحسان) literally means "doing what is beautiful" — from *ḥusn* (beauty, goodness). It is the highest of the three levels of the religion (*islām → īmān → iḥsān*), defined by the Prophet in the famous Ḥadīth of Jibrīl:

> [!quote] The Ḥadīth of Jibrīl — Ihsan Defined
> *'Umar bin al-Khattab reported:*
> > "The man [Jibrīl] said: 'Tell me about **iḥsān**.' The Prophet (ﷺ) replied: '**It is to worship Allah as if you see Him — and if you do not see Him, then truly He sees you.**'"
> — [Bukhari 50, Muslim 8](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Belief)

This definition contains two complementary spiritual states:
- **The Station of Seeing** (*maqām al-mushāhadah*): worship with such presence and love that God is, as it were, before you
- **The Station of Vigilance** (*maqām al-murāqabah*): if you cannot attain that vision, worship in the awareness that God is watching you

## Ihsan in the Qur'an

| Qur'anic reference | The dimension of ihsan |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| "Allah is with those who are *al-muttaqīn* (the God-conscious) and those who are *al-muḥsinīn* (the doers of good)" ([[016 An-Nahl]] 16:128) | God's presence with the righteous |
| "And do good (*aḥsinū*) — indeed Allah loves the doers of good (*al-muḥsinīn*)" ([[002 Al-Baqara]] 2:195) | The command to excel in goodness |
| "Indeed, Allah commands justice and *iḥsān* (excellence)" ([[016 An-Nahl]] 16:90) | Ihsan as a divine command alongside justice |
| "And whoever submits his face to Allah while he is a *muḥsin* — he has grasped the most trustworthy handhold" ([[031 Luqman]] 31:22) | The combination of submission and excellence |
| "They are those who spend in ease and hardship, who restrain anger, and forgive people — Allah loves the *muḥsinīn*" ([[003 Aal-i-Imraan]] 3:134) | Ihsan as the highest ethical character |
| "Surely the mercy of Allah is near to the *muḥsinīn*" ([[007 Al-A'raaf]] 7:56) | Divine mercy drawn by spiritual excellence |

## Ihsan in the Hadith — The Prophet as the Living Model of Ihsan

> [!quote] The Perfecter of Character
> *Abu Huraira reported:* The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said: "I have been sent only to perfect good character."
> — [Muwatta Malik 1614](scriptures/islam-hadith/07%20Muwatta%20Malik.md#The%20Mudabbar)

> [!quote] Ihsan to All Creation
> *Abu Huraira reported:* The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "While a man was walking, he became very thirsty. He found a well and went down to drink, then came out and saw a dog panting and eating the moist earth from thirst. The man said: 'This dog is suffering what I was suffering.' So he filled his shoe with water and held it in his mouth until he climbed out and gave it to the dog. **Allah thanked him and forgave him.**" They asked: "O Messenger of Allah, is there reward for us in animals?" He said: "**In every living being there is reward for doing good (iḥsān).**"
> — [Bukhari 2363](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Drinks)

> [!quote] Ihsan in Slaughter
> *Shaddad bin Aws reported:* The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said: "**Allah has prescribed iḥsān in everything.** So when you kill, kill well. When you slaughter, slaughter well. Let each of you sharpen his blade and spare the animal suffering."
> — [Muslim 1955](scriptures/islam-hadith/02%20Sahih%20Muslim.md#The%20Book%20of%20Prayers)

> [!quote] The Prophet's Own Ihsan
> *Anas reported:* "I served the Prophet for ten years, and he never said 'uff' to me. He never said about anything I did, 'Why did you do that?' — nor about anything I left, 'Why didn't you do that?'"
> — [Bukhari 6038](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Asking%20Permission)

## Ihsan and Tasawwuf (Sufism)

The science of iḥsān developed into the Islamic spiritual tradition known as **Taṣawwuf** (Sufism). Classical Sufi masters understood their path as the practical cultivation of iḥsān:

| Station (Maqām) | Description | Qur'anic Basis |
|-----------------|-------------|---------------|
| **Tawbah** (Repentance) | Turning from sin to God | [[024 An-Noor]] 24:31 |
| **Wara'** (Scrupulosity) | Abstaining from the doubtful | (Hadith-based) |
| **Zuhd** (Asceticism) | Detachment from the world | [[057 Al-Hadid]] 57:20 |
| **Faqr** (Spiritual Poverty) | Recognition of utter need for God | [[035 Faatir]] 35:15 |
| **Ṣabr** (Patience) | Steadfastness in difficulty | [[002 Al-Baqara]] 2:153 |
| **Shukr** (Gratitude) | Thankfulness for all states | [[014 Ibrahim]] 14:7 |
| **Tawakkul** (Trust) | Reliance on God alone | [[065 At-Talaaq]] 65:3 |
| **Riḍā** (Contentment) | Acceptance of God's decree | [[005 Al-Maaida]] 5:119 |
| **Maʿrifah** (Gnosis) | Direct knowledge of God | (The fruit of the path) |

> [!quote] The Hidden Hadith — Ihsan as the Inner Dimension
> *Abu Huraira reported:* The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Allah said: **'My servant draws near to Me through nothing I love more than that which I have made obligatory. And My servant continues to draw near to Me through supererogatory acts until I love him. And when I love him, I am the hearing with which he hears, the sight with which he sees, the hand with which he strikes, and the foot with which he walks.** If he asks Me, I give him. If he seeks refuge in Me, I grant it.'"
> — [Bukhari 6502](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Softening%20of%20Hearts)

This ḥadīth (*ḥadīth al-nawāfil*) is the spiritual charter of iḥsān — the path from mere obedience to intimate love of God.

## Ihsan vs. Islam vs. Iman

| Level | Focus | Definition | Deficiency |
|-------|-------|------------|------------|
| **Islam** | Body | Outward submission (the Five Pillars) | Hypocrisy (*nifāq*) |
| **Iman** | Heart | Inner belief (the Six Pillars) | Doubt (*shakk*) |
| **Ihsan** | Soul | Spiritual presence — "as if you see Him" | Heedlessness (*ghaflah*) |

Each level includes the ones below it. A *muḥsin* (one who practices iḥsān) is necessarily a believer and a submitter, but not every submitter or believer has attained iḥsān.
