# Iman — *Faith*

> The inner conviction that gives Islam its spiritual substance — belief in God, His angels, His books, His messengers, the Last Day, and divine decree. Part of [[Islamic Concepts MOC]].

---

## Definition

**Īmān** (إيمان) comes from the root *a-m-n* — safety, security, trust. It is **firm belief in the heart**, affirmed by the tongue, and demonstrated through action. In the famous Ḥadīth of Jibrīl (Gabriel), the Prophet defines iman as six specific beliefs:

> [!quote] The Ḥadīth of Jibrīl — The Six Pillars of Faith
> *'Umar bin al-Khattab reported:* 
> > "One day, while we were sitting with the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ), a man with very white clothes and very black hair appeared… He sat close to the Prophet and said: 'Tell me about **īmān** (faith).' The Prophet replied: '**It is to believe in Allah, His angels, His books, His messengers, the Last Day, and to believe in al-qadar (divine decree), both its good and its evil.**' The man said: 'You have spoken truly.'"
> — [Muslim 8](scriptures/islam-hadith/02%20Sahih%20Muslim.md#The%20Book%20of%20Faith)

---

## The Six Pillars of Iman

### 1. Belief in Allah (God)

The first and greatest pillar — the object of all faith. See [[Tawhid]].

> "The Messenger believes in what was revealed to him from his Lord, and so do the believers. Each one believes in Allah, His angels, His books, and His messengers." ([[002 Al-Baqara]] 2:285)

### 2. Belief in the Angels (Malā'ikah)

Created from light, the angels are obedient servants of God with specific duties:

| Angel | Role | Qur'anic reference |
|-------|------|-------------------|
| **Jibrīl** (Gabriel) | Conveys revelation to prophets | [[002 Al-Baqara]] 2:97 |
| **Mīkā'īl** (Michael) | Provides sustenance, controls rain | [[002 Al-Baqara]] 2:98 |
| **Isrāfīl** | Blows the Trumpet on Judgment Day | (mentioned in hadith) |
| **Malak al-Mawt** (Azra'īl) | Takes souls at death | [[032 As-Sajda]] 32:11 |
| **Munkar & Nakīr** | Question the dead in the grave | (mentioned in hadith) |
| **Mālik** | Keeper of Hell | [[043 Az-Zukhruf]] 43:77 |
| **Riḍwān** | Keeper of Paradise | (mentioned in hadith) |
| **Kīrāman Kātibīn** | Recording angels (on shoulders) | [[082 Al-Infitaar]] 82:10–12 |

> "And each person has angels in succession before him and behind him, guarding him by the command of Allah." ([[013 Ar-Ra'd]] 13:11)

### 3. Belief in the Books (Kutub)

The revealed scriptures sent to the prophets:

| Scripture | Prophet/People | Qur'anic reference |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Ṣuḥuf** (Scrolls) | Abraham, Moses | [[087 Al-A'laa]] 87:19 |
| **Tawrāh** (Torah) | Moses | [[003 Aal-i-Imraan]] 3:3 |
| **Zabūr** (Psalms) | David | [[017 Al-Israa]] 17:55 |
| **Injīl** (Gospel) | Jesus | [[005 Al-Maaida]] 5:46 |
| **Qur'an** | Muhammad | Confirms and supersedes all previous books |

> [!quote] Qur'an 5:48 — The Book confirming the Books
> > "And We have sent down to you the Book with truth, **confirming** the Scripture that came before it and **safeguarding** it (*muhaymin*). So judge between them by what Allah has revealed."
> > — [[005 Al-Maaida]]

See the vault's [[Scriptures Library]] for the full texts of the Qur'an, Bible, Tanakh, and other scriptures.

### 4. Belief in the Messengers (Rusul)

Belief in all prophets and messengers sent by God — without discrimination between them. See [[Risalah]] for the full list.

> "We make no distinction between any of His messengers." ([[002 Al-Baqara]] 2:285)

### 5. Belief in the Last Day (Yawm al-Ākhir)

The Day of Resurrection, Judgment, Paradise and Hell. See [[Akhirah]].

### 6. Belief in Divine Decree (Al-Qadar)

The belief that God's knowledge encompasses all things — past, present, and future — and that everything occurs by His will and decree. This is the most nuanced pillar, subject to centuries of theological debate.

> [!quote] Qur'anic foundation
> - "Indeed, We have created all things with *qadar* (measure/destiny)." ([[054 Al-Qamar]] 54:49)
> - "No calamity befalls the earth or yourselves except that it is in a Book before We bring it into being." ([[057 Al-Hadid]] 57:22)
> - "And you do not will except that Allah wills — Lord of the worlds." ([[081 At-Takwir]] 81:29)

> [!quote] Hadith — The Pen Dried
> *Abdullah bin 'Amr reported:* The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Allah wrote the measures of creation fifty thousand years before He created the heavens and the earth."
> — [Muslim 2653](scriptures/islam-hadith/02%20Sahih%20Muslim.md#The%20Book%20of%20Zakat)

> [!quote] Hadith — The Womb Decree
> *Abdullah bin Mas'ud reported:* The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Each one of you is gathered in his mother's womb for forty days as a drop, then a clot for forty days, then a lump of flesh for forty days — then an angel is sent to write four things: his provision, his lifespan, his deeds, and whether he is wretched or blessed."
> — [Bukhari 6595](scriptures/islam-hadith/01%20Sahih%20al-Bukhari.md#Divine%20Will)

## The Relationship Between Iman, Islam, and Ihsan

The Ḥadīth of Jibrīl presents the three levels of the religion as a hierarchy:

```
              ┌──────────────────┐
   Level 3:   │     IHSAN        │  ← Worship Allah as if you see Him
              │ (spiritual       │
              │  excellence)     │
              └────────┬─────────┘
                       │
              ┌────────▼─────────┐
   Level 2:   │     IMAN         │  ← Inner faith: six pillars
              │ (faith, belief)  │
              └────────┬─────────┘
                       │
              ┌────────▼─────────┐
   Level 1:   │     ISLAM        │  ← Outward submission: Five Pillars
              │ (submission)     │
              └──────────────────┘
```

Each level includes and builds upon the previous: a Muslim submits outwardly; a mu'min believes inwardly; a muhsin does both with spiritual presence.

## Iman in the Qur'an — Key Themes

| Theme | Key Verses |
|-------|-----------|
| **Iman increases and decreases** | "The believers are only those whose hearts tremble when Allah is mentioned, and when His verses are recited to them, it increases them in faith" ([[008 Al-Anfaal]] 8:2) |
| **Iman requires action** | "And those who believe and do righteous deeds — We will surely admit them among the righteous" ([[029 Al-Ankaboot]] 29:9) |
| **Iman is tested** | "Do people think they will be left to say 'We believe' without being tested?" ([[029 Al-Ankaboot]] 29:2) |
| **Iman brings peace** | "Those who believe and whose hearts find rest in the remembrance of Allah — verily in the remembrance of Allah do hearts find rest" ([[013 Ar-Ra'd]] 13:28) |
