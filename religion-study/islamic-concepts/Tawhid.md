# Tawhid — *The Oneness of God*

> The bedrock of Islam: **Allāh is One, unique, indivisible, and worthy of worship alone.** Part of [[Islamic Concepts MOC]].

---

## Definition

**Tawḥīd** (توحيد) literally means "making one" or "asserting oneness." In Islamic theology, it is the belief that God (*Allāh*) is:

- **One** (*Aḥad*, [[112 Al-Ikhlaas]] 112:1)
- **Unique** — nothing resembles Him ([[042 Ash-Shura]] 42:11)
- **Eternal, without beginning or end** ([[112 Al-Ikhlaas]] 112:2)
- **Self-Sufficient** (*al-Ṣamad*) — needing nothing, needing Him
- **Creator, Sustainer, and Sovereign** of all existence

> [!quote] Qur'an 112:1–4 — The essence of Tawhid
> > "Say: He is Allah, the One! Allah, the Self-Sufficient. He begets not, nor is He begotten. And there is none comparable unto Him."
> > — [[112 Al-Ikhlaas|Al-Ikhlaas]] (The Purity of Faith)

---

## The Three Categories of Tawhid

Classical Islamic scholarship divides Tawhid into three interrelated categories, each established by distinct Qur'anic arguments:

### 1. Tawhid al-Rubūbiyyah — Oneness of Lordship

God alone is the Creator, Sustainer, and Governor of the universe. He brings life and death, sends rain, controls the heavens and the earth.

> [!quote] Qur'anic evidence
> - "Allah is the Creator of all things, and He is the Guardian over all things." ([[039 Az-Zumar]] 39:62)
> - "Such is Allah, your Lord. There is no god except Him, the Creator of all things, so worship Him." ([[006 Al-An'aam]] 6:102)
> - "Blessed be He in Whose Hand is the Sovereignty, and He is Able to do all things." ([[067 Al-Mulk]] 67:1)

Even the polytheists of Mecca acknowledged God as the Creator — their error was in associating others in worship while admitting His Lordship.

> [!quote] Qur'an 29:61
> > "If you asked them: 'Who created the heavens and the earth and subjected the sun and the moon?' they would surely say: 'Allah.' Then how are they deviating?"
> > — [[029 Al-Ankaboot]] 29:61

### 2. Tawhid al-Ulūhiyyah — Oneness of Worship

God alone is worthy of worship (*ibādah*). All acts of devotion — prayer, sacrifice, trust, love, fear, hope, supplication — must be directed to Him alone. **This is the core meaning of *lā ilāha illa Allāh*** ("there is no god but Allah").

> [!quote] Qur'anic evidence
> - "And your God is One God; there is no god except Him, the Beneficent, the Merciful." ([[002 Al-Baqara]] 2:163)
> - "Worship Allah and associate nothing with Him." ([[004 An-Nisaa]] 4:36)
> - "Say: My prayer, my sacrifice, my life and my death are for Allah, Lord of the Worlds — no partner has He." ([[006 Al-An'aam]] 6:162–163)
> - "And We sent no messenger before you but We revealed to him: There is no god except Me, so worship Me." ([[021 Al-Anbiyaa]] 21:25)

### 3. Tawhid al-Asmā' wa al-Ṣifāt — Oneness of Names and Attributes

God's names and attributes are unique to Him. Muslims affirm the names and attributes God has described for Himself in the Qur'an and through His Prophet — without:
- **Taḥrīf** (distorting them)
- **Ta'ṭīl** (denying them)
- **Takyīf** (asking "how")
- **Tashbīh** (comparing them to creation)

> [!quote] Qur'anic evidence
> - "To Allah belong the Most Beautiful Names, so call upon Him by them." ([[007 Al-A'raaf]] 7:180)
> - "There is nothing like unto Him, and He is the Hearing, the Seeing." ([[042 Ash-Shura]] 42:11)
> - "He is Allah, the Creator, the Maker, the Fashioner. To Him belong the Most Beautiful Names." ([[059 Al-Hashr]] 59:24)

## Tawhid vs. Shirk

The opposite of tawhid is **shirk** (associating partners with God) — the only sin the Qur'an says God will not forgive unless repented from:

> [!quote] Qur'an 4:48
> > "Allah forgives not that partners be associated with Him; but He forgives what is less than that to whom He wills."
> > — [[004 An-Nisaa]] 4:48

Forms of shirk include:
- Worship of idols, celestial bodies, or created beings
- \[Riyāʾ\] — performing acts of worship to be seen by others
- Placing ultimate trust or fear in anything other than God
