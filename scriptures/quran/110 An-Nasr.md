backstory: Revealed in Medina in 630 CE (8 AH), shortly after the conquest of Mecca. Named after "Divine Support" or "Victory" (An-Nasr — v1). The last surah revealed in full, signalling the completion of the Prophet's mission. The Prophet recited it with great emotion, and the Companions understood his death was near.
narrative: (1) When God's victory and conquest come (v1); (2) And you see people entering God's religion in multitudes (v2); (3) Then glorify your Lord with praise and ask His forgiveness — He is ever ready to accept repentance (v3).
subject: The Victory (the conquest of Mecca), people entering Islam in throngs, the command to glorify and seek forgiveness.
motivation: To announce that the mission is complete — victory has come, faith is spreading — and the Prophet's task now turns from struggle to glorification and seeking forgiveness, a sign that his life was drawing to a close.

# 110. An-Nasr — *Divine Support*

> [[Quran Index]] · Medinan · 3 ayat · *trans. Pickthall (public domain)*

*In the name of Allah, the Beneficent, the Merciful.*

**1.** When Allah's succour and the triumph cometh
**2.** And thou seest mankind entering the religion of Allah in troops,
**3.** Then hymn the praises of thy Lord, and seek forgiveness of Him. Lo! He is ever ready to show mercy.
