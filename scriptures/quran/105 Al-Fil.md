backstory: Revealed in Mecca, named after "The Elephant" (Al-Fil — v1). Refers to the Year of the Elephant (c. 570 CE), when Abraha al-Ashram, the Christian Ethiopian viceroy of Yemen, marched on Mecca with an elephant to destroy the Kaaba. The army was destroyed by birds carrying stones of baked clay (ababil). This was the year of the Prophet's birth.
narrative: (1) Did your Lord not deal with the people of the elephant? (v1); (2) He made their plan go astray (v2); (3) He sent flocks of birds (ababil) against them, pelting them with stones of baked clay (v3-4); (4) He made them like eaten chaff (v5).
subject: The Year of the Elephant, Abraha's invasion of Mecca, the miracle of the birds (ababil) and the clay stones (sijjil), the protection of the Kaaba.
motivation: To recall the miracle that saved Mecca from the Ethiopian invasion — a lesson to the Quraysh that God alone protected the Sacred House, and to reinforce that they owe their security to God, not their own power.

# 105. Al-Fil — *The Elephant*

> [[Quran Index]] · Meccan · 5 ayat · *trans. Pickthall (public domain)*

*In the name of Allah, the Beneficent, the Merciful.*

**1.** Hast thou not seen how thy Lord dealt with the owners of the Elephant?
**2.** Did He not bring their stratagem to naught,
**3.** And send against them swarms of flying creatures,
**4.** Which pelted them with stones of baked clay,
**5.** And made them like green crops devoured (by cattle)?
