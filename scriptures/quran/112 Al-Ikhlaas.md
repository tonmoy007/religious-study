backstory: Revealed in Mecca, named after "Sincerity" or "Purity of Faith" (Al-Ikhlaas). Described as equivalent to one-third of the Quran (according to hadith). A response to the Meccans who asked the Prophet about the nature of his Lord. The definitive statement of tawhid (divine unity).

subject: The oneness of God, God as As-Samad (the Eternal, the Self-Sufficient), the negation of begetting and being born, the incomparability of God.
motivation: To answer the question "What is your Lord like?" with four concise statements that define God's absolute unity, self-sufficiency, and transcendence — the core of Islamic theology.

# 112. Al-Ikhlaas — *Sincerity*

> [[Quran Index]] · Meccan · 4 ayat · *trans. Pickthall (public domain)*

*In the name of Allah, the Beneficent, the Merciful.*

**1.** Say: He is Allah, the One!
**2.** Allah, the eternally Besought of all!
**3.** He begetteth not nor was begotten.
**4.** And there is none comparable unto Him.
