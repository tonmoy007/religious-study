backstory: Revealed in Mecca, named after "Abundance" (Al-Kawthar — v1). The shortest surah in the Quran (3 verses). Revealed to console the Prophet after his infant son Abdullah died, leading the Meccans to taunt him as "abtar" (one cut off, without posterity). Al-Kawthar is a river in Paradise given to the Prophet.
narrative: (1) We have given you abundance (Al-Kawthar) (v1); (2) So pray to your Lord and sacrifice (v2); (3) Your hater — he is the one cut off (v3).
subject: Abundance (the River Kawthar in Paradise), the command to pray and sacrifice, the refutation of the accusation that the Prophet is "cut off" (abtar).
motivation: To respond to the Meccan taunt that the Prophet was "cut off" (without male heir) by declaring that it is the hater who is truly cut off, while the Prophet has been given infinite abundance.

# 108. Al-Kawthar — *Abundance*

> [[Quran Index]] · Meccan · 3 ayat · *trans. Pickthall (public domain)*

*In the name of Allah, the Beneficent, the Merciful.*

**1.** Lo! We have given thee Abundance;
**2.** So pray unto thy Lord, and sacrifice.
**3.** Lo! it is thy insulter (and not thou) who is without posterity.
