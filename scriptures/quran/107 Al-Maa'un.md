backstory: Revealed in Mecca, named after "Almsgiving" or "Small Kindnesses" (Al-Maa'un — v7). Condemns religious hypocrisy — those who pray but are careless in prayer and refuse small acts of kindness. Often paired with 108 Al-Kawthar.
narrative: (1) Have you seen the one who denies the religion? (v1); (2) He repels the orphan and urges not the feeding of the poor (v2-3); (3) Woe to the praying ones who are heedless of their prayer and show off (v4-6); (4) They withhold even the smallest kindness (al-maa'un) (v7).
subject: Denial of religion, mistreatment of the orphan and the poor, hypocritical prayer, withholding small kindnesses (the loan of household items).
motivation: To define true religion not by prayer alone but by social conscience — the one who "denies the religion" is the one who repels the orphan and refuses the smallest kindness.

# 107. Al-Maa'un — *Almsgiving*

> [[Quran Index]] · Meccan · 7 ayat · *trans. Pickthall (public domain)*

*In the name of Allah, the Beneficent, the Merciful.*

**1.** Hast thou observed him who belieth religion?
**2.** That is he who repelleth the orphan,
**3.** And urgeth not the feeding of the needy.
**4.** Ah, woe unto worshippers
**5.** Who are heedless of their prayer;
**6.** Who would be seen (at worship)
**7.** Yet refuse small kindnesses!
