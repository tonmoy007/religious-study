# 📜 The Talmud (Soncino English Translation)

> Part of [[Scriptures Library]] → Judaism
> Full English text of the **Babylonian Talmud** (*Talmud Bavli*), Soncino translation, public domain via Halakhah.com.

The Talmud is the central text of Rabbinic Judaism — a vast record of rabbinic debate, legal analysis, and storytelling spanning the 3rd–6th centuries CE. It is composed of two layers:

- **Mishnah** (c. 200 CE) — the first written codification of Jewish Oral Law, arranged by subject into 6 orders and 63 tractates.
- **Gemara** (c. 200–500 CE) — rabbinic commentary and discussion on the Mishnah, recording debates from the academies of Palestine and Babylonia.

The **Babylonian Talmud** (the version translated here) is more extensive and authoritative than the **Jerusalem Talmud** (*Talmud Yerushalmi*). It comprises ~2,500,000 words across 2,711 folio pages.

> [!tip] Structure
> Each tractate is a single note, divided into folio pages (e.g., Berakoth 2a–64b). Folio references are preserved in headings so you can cross-reference with any standard edition.

## About the Translation

The **Soncino Talmud** was the first complete English translation of the Babylonian Talmud, published between 1935 and 1948 under the editorship of Rabbi Dr. Isidore Epstein. Translators included Maurice Simon (Berakoth), Jacob Shachter (Shabbath), and other leading scholars of the period.

**Key features of this edition:**
- Preservation of the original folio divisions (2a, 2b, 3a…) — standard for all academic citation.
- Mishnah texts differentiated in bold.
- Extensive footnotes explaining legal background, textual variants, and cross-references.
- Hebrew and Aramaic terms transliterated consistently (Soncino system).
