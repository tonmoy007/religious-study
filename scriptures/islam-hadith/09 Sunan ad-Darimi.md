# Sunan ad-Darimi

> [[Hadith Library]] · ~3,406 hadith · **No complete English translation exists**

> [!warning] No complete English translation available
> Sunan ad-Darimi has **not been translated into English** in its entirety. This is the only one of the "Nine Books" of hadith that remains untranslated. The other eight are fully populated in the vault.

**About this collection:**

**Sunan ad-Darimi** (سنن الدارمي) was compiled by **'Abd Allah ibn 'Abd al-Rahman al-Darimi** (181–255 AH / 797–869 CE), a Persian scholar from Samarkand. He was a teacher of both **Imam Muslim** and **Abu Dawud**, placing him in the same generation as Bukhari. His *Sunan* contains approximately **3,406 hadiths** arranged by subject (fiqh chapters).

Notable features:
- Known for strong **chain criticism** — al-Darimi frequently comments on the reliability of narrators.
- Includes many **sayings of the Companions and Successors** (not just the Prophet), making it a bridge between hadith and early Islamic scholarly opinion.
- Contains important hadiths on **Qur'anic sciences** (recitation, tafsir, virtues of the Qur'an).

**Status in Islamic scholarship:**
While not among the "Six Books" (Kutub al-Sittah) that constitute the core Sunni hadith canon, Sunan ad-Darimi is widely cited as a **supplementary collection** and is valued for its unique narrations and critical methodology.

### Arabic Text (Sunnah.com)
The full Arabic text with English chapter headings is available at [sunnah.com/darimi](https://sunnah.com/darimi). There are 23 books (kutub) covering purification, prayer, zakat, fasting, Hajj, sacrifices, hunting, food, drinks, dreams, marriage, divorce, punishments, vows, oaths, blood-money, jihad, expeditions, trade, permission, heart-softeners, inheritances, wills, and virtues of the Qur'an.

### Partial AI Translation (HadithUnlocked)
A volunteer-driven project at [hadithunlocked.com/darimi](https://hadithunlocked.com/darimi) is incrementally producing English translations. Most are AI-generated (tagged `[Machine]`) with some human-contributed translations and a few adapted from parallel narrations in other collections. The authentication ratings (e.g., "Sound Chain," "Weak Chain") follow the assessments of Shaykh Shu'ayb al-Arna'ut.

### If You Read Arabic
The Arabic text is fully accessible on Sunnah.com. If you can read Arabic, you can study the collection directly by navigating its 23 books and ~3,400 hadiths there.
