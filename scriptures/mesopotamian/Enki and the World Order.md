# 📖 Enki and the World Order

A Sumerian poem celebrating **Enki**, the god of wisdom and fresh water, as he organizes the world and assigns each deity their domain. The **Anunnaki** are described as decreeing the fates of mankind and doing homage to Enki.

> [!important] Anunnaki Key Passages
> - **Line 207**: The Anunnaki "decree the fates of mankind" (twice stated)
> - The Anunnaki "do homage" to Enki and sing hymns in his honor
> - The Anunnaki "take up their dwellings" among the people of Sumer
> - Includes the phrase: "palil da-nun-na-ke4-ne-me-en" — "I am the prefect of the Anunnaki" (spoken by Enki)

---

## Translation (selected passages)

The poem opens with a hymn to Enki, describing his majesty and his role as the organizer of civilization.

**1-8.** The true great lord, the lord of heaven and earth, the self-reliant, father Enki, engendered like a bull, born as a great wild bull, the one who is praised by the great mountain Enlil, the beloved of holy An, the king who built the me- shrines in the abzu, who rises over all the lands, the great marshland-stand erected in Eridug, whose shadow covers heaven and earth, a forest of vineyards covering the land with its shade. Enki, the lord of abundance, **the one who is the prefect of the Anunnaki**.

**9-17.** Nudimmud, the foremost, the great steward of E-kur, the steward of heaven and earth. You are the great house, the abzu, built with the right decor, the great fashioner of heaven and earth. Enki, when you lift your eyes across the lands, the powerful one is born, the stag is born, the ibex is born, the mountain sheep is born. You make the ...... abundantly flourish, the interior of the mountains. Where you pass, no man may enter.

**18-35.** Like a halhal-sieve you examine the interior of all the lands. You determine the days, you let the months enter the house, you complete the years. You complete the years, and you give the decisions in the assembly. You make straight the decisions which are far removed from days. Father Enki, you are the lord of the jeweled beard, the lord of abundance. When you fasten the ...... at your chest, the ..... is full of abundance, prosperity follows behind you to the ground.

**83-84.** "I am the lord whose word is true, I am the one who goes before. I am the prefect of the Anunnaki."

**207.** The great Anunnaki who assign the fates ... (detailed passage describes how the Anunnaki sit in assembly and decree the fates of mankind, while Enki oversees the distribution of domains and offices.)

The poem continues with Enki blessing the cities of Sumer, assigning crafts and industries to various gods, and establishing the divine order of civilization — agriculture, animal husbandry, building, and the arts.

---

## Key Themes

1. **Enki as Organizer**: Enki travels through Sumer, assigning each deity's role — a cosmic division of labor
2. **The Anunnaki's Role**: They are the assembly of the great gods who "decree the fates" — the supreme judicial authority in the cosmos
3. **Human Society as Divine Order**: Cities, kingship, crafts, and agriculture are all established by Enki as part of the world order
4. **The Me**: The divine powers/ordinances that underpin civilization, which Enki controls
