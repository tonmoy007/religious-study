# 🏛️ Mesopotamian Scriptures — Index

Full ancient Mesopotamian religious and mythological texts. These are the primary sources for understanding the **Anunnaki**, the Igigi, and the pantheon of Sumer, Akkad, Assyria, and Babylon.

> [!info] About these texts
> The Anunnaki (𒀭𒀀𒉣𒈾, "those of royal blood") are the high deities of the Mesopotamian pantheon — descendants of An (sky) and Ki (earth). They appear in these texts as creators, judges, fate-decreers, and sometimes as underworld deities. These translations are public domain or openly licensed.

---

## 📜 Primary Anunnaki Texts

| # | Text | Period | Language | Key Anunnaki Content |
|---|------|--------|----------|---------------------|
| 1 | **[[Enūma Eliš (The Babylonian Creation Epic)]]** | ~1200 BCE | Akkadian | Marduk assigns 600 Anunnaki (300 heaven, 300 underworld); they build Esagila |
| 2 | **[[Atrahasis Epic]]** | ~1700 BCE | Akkadian | Anunnaki impose labor on Igigi; creation of humans; the Great Flood |
| 3 | **[[Enki and Ninmah]]** | ~1800 BCE | Sumerian | Anuna (Anunnaki) gods born; creation of humans from clay |
| 4 | **[[Enki and the World Order]]** | ~1800 BCE | Sumerian | Anunnaki decree the fates of mankind; do homage to Enki |
| 5 | **[[Inanna's Descent to the Netherworld]]** | ~1800 BCE | Sumerian | 7 Anunnaki as underworld judges; Inanna's trial and death |
| 6 | **[[Epic of Gilgamesh]]** | ~2100–1200 BCE | Akkadian/Sumerian | Anunnaki brandish torches at the Flood (TXI); weep with Ishtar over humanity (TXI); settle destiny of mortals (TX); Gilgamesh called "Judge of the Anunnaki" (TVIII) |
